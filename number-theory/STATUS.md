## Coverage Notes

Number theory — the study of integers and their properties — ranges from elementary arithmetic (divisibility, primes, congruences) through algebraic structures (number fields, ideal class groups) to deep analytic machinery (the Riemann zeta function, L-functions, the prime number theorem). This directory covers the full hierarchy: foundational divisibility and modular arithmetic, group-theoretic structure (primitive roots, discrete logarithm), quadratic reciprocity, Diophantine equations (including the history of Fermat's Last Theorem), algebraic number theory (rings of integers, class field theory foundations), analytic number theory, the Riemann Hypothesis and its connections to random matrix theory, and computational number theory as applied directly to modern cryptography.
