# Microcanonical Ensemble

## The Big Picture

The microcanonical ensemble is the foundational ensemble: an isolated system with fixed energy
E, volume V, and particle number N. No energy or particles are exchanged with any reservoir.
The fundamental postulate says all accessible microstates are equally probable. Entropy S = k ln О©
then follows immediately. The second law becomes a counting theorem: systems evolve toward
macrostates with the most microstates, because those are overwhelmingly more probable.

## Counting Microstates вҖ” Classical Phase Space

For a classical system of N particles in 3D with Hamiltonian H(q, p):

**Phase space volume** in the shell [E, E + ОҙE]:

    О©(E) = (1 / hВібҙә N!) вҲ«_{E < H < E+ОҙE} dВібҙәq dВібҙәp

The factors:
- **hВібҙә**: Heisenberg uncertainty principle вҖ” each phase space cell has volume hВі per dimension. This makes О© dimensionless.
- **N!**: identical particles are indistinguishable вҖ” permuting them gives the same physical microstate.

**Cumulative phase space volume** ОҰ(E) = number of states with H < E:

    ОҰ(E) = (1 / hВібҙә N!) вҲ«_{H < E} dВібҙәq dВібҙәp

So О©(E) = (вҲӮОҰ/вҲӮE) ОҙE (with the energy shell width ОҙE, which drops out of all physical quantities since we take logs).

## Ideal Gas вҖ” Microcanonical Calculation

The ideal gas is the paradigmatic exactly-solvable microcanonical system. Hamiltonian:

    H = ОЈбөў pбөўВІ/(2m)     (N particles, 3D, no interactions)

**Phase space integral**: The energy constraint H = E defines a 3N-dimensional hypersphere of radius R = вҲҡ(2mE) in momentum space, with surface area proportional to R^{3N-1}.

    ОҰ(E) = V^N / (hВібҙә N!) Г— (volume of 3N-dimensional sphere of radius вҲҡ(2mE))
          = V^N / (hВібҙә N!) Г— ПҖ^{3N/2} (2mE)^{3N/2} / О“(3N/2 + 1)

Taking the surface area (Оҙ-function on energy shell):

    О©(E) вҲқ V^N E^{3N/2 - 1} / (hВібҙә N!) Г— (2ПҖm)^{3N/2} / О“(3N/2)

**Entropy** (using Stirling: ln(N!) вүҲ N ln N вҲ’ N):

    S = k_B ln О© = Nk_B [ln(V/N) + (3/2) ln(2ПҖmE/3Nk_B) + (5/2)] + const

This is the **Sackur-Tetrode equation** for the entropy of an ideal gas.

**Temperature from 1/T = вҲӮS/вҲӮE|_{N,V}**:

    1/T = k_B (3N/2) / E    вҹ№    E = (3/2) Nk_BT    вң“ (equipartition)

**Pressure from P/T = вҲӮS/вҲӮV|_{N,E}**:

    P/T = Nk_B/V    вҹ№    PV = Nk_BT    вң“ (ideal gas law)

Both of these emerge from pure counting вҖ” no dynamics, no forces, just geometry of phase space.

## The Density of States

The density of states g(E) is the number of microstates per unit energy:

    g(E) = dО©/dE = dОҰ/dE

For N non-interacting quantum particles, the energy levels are discrete, and:

    g(E) = ОЈ_n Оҙ(E вҲ’ E_n)

For a 3D ideal gas, the single-particle density of states (energy range [Оө, Оө+dОө]):

    gвӮҒ(Оө) = (V / 4ПҖВІ) (2m/в„ҸВІ)^{3/2} вҲҡОө = (3N/2) N(Оө)/Оө ... depends on normalization

The **many-body** density of states grows as:

    g(E) вҲқ E^{3N/2 - 1}    (for N ideal gas particles)

This enormous growth with E is why the entropy S = k ln О© grows with energy, and why
systems absorb heat without decreasing entropy: О© grows so fast that even transferring
energy to a system dramatically increases the number of accessible microstates.

```
DENSITY OF STATES g(E) FOR DIFFERENT SYSTEMS:

  3D ideal gas:      g(E) вҲқ E^{3N/2 - 1}  (polynomial, huge exponent)
  1D harmonic osc:   g(E) = const          (equally spaced levels)
  2D harmonic osc:   g(E) вҲқ E              (linearly increasing)
  3D harmonic osc:   g(E) вҲқ EВІ             (quadratically increasing)
  Spin-1/2 chain:    g(E) = C(N, N/2+E/2О”) (combinatorial, peaks at E=0)

  The rate of growth of g(E) with E determines the heat capacity C_V.
  Slow growth вҶ’ small C_V. Fast growth вҶ’ large C_V.
```

## The Second Law from Counting

The argument below is maximum likelihood estimation in disguise: maximizing О©_total = О©_A(E_A) Г— О©_B(E вҲ’ E_A) over E_A is maximizing a product of probabilities (a likelihood). Taking logarithms converts the product to a sum вҖ” the log-likelihood вҖ” and the optimality condition вҲӮ ln О©_A/вҲӮE_A = вҲӮ ln О©_B/вҲӮE_B is the score equation. Temperature equality at equilibrium is the MLE solution.

**Setup**: Two systems A and B, initially isolated at energies E_A and E_B, then allowed to exchange energy (total E = E_A + E_B is fixed).

The total number of microstates:

    О©_total(E_A) = О©_A(E_A) Г— О©_B(E вҲ’ E_A)

Equilibrium = the value of E_A that **maximizes** О©_total.

Setting вҲӮln О©_total/вҲӮE_A = 0:

    вҲӮ ln О©_A / вҲӮE_A = вҲӮ ln О©_B / вҲӮE_B    вҹ№    1/T_A = 1/T_B    вҹ№    T_A = T_B

```
SECOND LAW AS PROBABILITY:

  Before contact:
  E_A = E_A^{init}  (one particular split)
  E_B = E вҲ’ E_A^{init}

  After contact:
  System explores ALL energy splits consistent with E_A + E_B = E.
  The probability of split E_A is:
  P(E_A) вҲқ О©_A(E_A) Г— О©_B(E вҲ’ E_A)

  в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
  в”Ӯ  О©_total peaks ENORMOUSLY at E_A = E_A^{eq}.           в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ  For N ~ 10ВІВі, the peak is so sharp (width ~ N^{-1/2}) в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ  that deviations from equilibrium are never observed.  в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ                                                        в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ  The second law is NOT a fundamental law.              в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ  It is an overwhelming probabilistic tendency.         в”Ӯ
  в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
```

**Quantifying the peak**: For two ideal gas systems each with N particles:

    ln О©_total(E_A) = const + (3N/2) ln E_A + (3N/2) ln(E вҲ’ E_A)

This is a sum of logs peaked at E_A = E/2 with Gaussian width О”E_A/E ~ 1/вҲҡN.

For N = 10ВІВі: relative width вүҲ 10вҒ»В№В№В·вҒө. The deviation from equilibrium is thermodynamically unobservable.

## Negative Temperature and Population Inversion

The microcanonical ensemble allows formal **negative temperatures** when О©(E) is a decreasing function of E.

For a spin-1/2 system in a magnetic field (energy bounded above):

```
SPIN-1/2 SYSTEM: N spins, each Вұ1 in field B
  Energy: E = вҲ’ОјB ОЈбөў sбөў вҲҲ [вҲ’NОјB, +NОјB]

  О©(E) = C(N, N/2 + E/2ОјB)  (binomial coefficient)
  Peaks at E = 0, falls off on both sides.

  For E < 0 (most spins aligned with field):
  вҲӮS/вҲӮE > 0  вҹ№  T > 0   (normal)

  For E > 0 (most spins anti-aligned, INVERTED POPULATION):
  вҲӮS/вҲӮE < 0  вҹ№  T < 0   (negative temperature)

  Negative T is HOTTER than +вҲһ.  Order:
  0 K < ... < 300 K < ... < +вҲһ = вҲ’вҲһ < ... < вҲ’300 K < 0 K
  (cyclic in ОІ = 1/kT, not in T itself)
```

**Laser physics**: Population inversion (negative temperature spin system coupled to photon field) is the mechanism behind laser gain. The energy flows from the negative-T spin system to the positive-T photon field вҖ” from the "hotter" to the "colder" system, consistent with entropy maximization.

## Microcanonical vs. Canonical вҖ” When They Differ

```
ENSEMBLE EQUIVALENCE (thermodynamic limit N вҶ’ вҲһ):
  In the thermodynamic limit, all ensembles give identical results.
  They differ for:

  1. SMALL SYSTEMS (N ~ 1вҖ“100):
     Fluctuations are large. Microcanonical: exact fixed E.
     Canonical: energy fluctuates by ~вҲҡN relative to N вҶ’ O(1/вҲҡN) relative.
     For N = 10, this matters.

  2. FIRST-ORDER PHASE TRANSITIONS:
     Canonical: forbidden region of вҹЁEвҹ© (latent heat gap).
     Microcanonical: S(E) has a convex dip вҖ” negative heat capacity is possible
     in finite systems. Caloric curve T(E) can be non-monotone.

  3. LONG-RANGE INTERACTIONS (gravity, plasma):
     Ensemble non-equivalence persists even in the thermodynamic limit.
     Self-gravitating systems can have negative heat capacity in the
     microcanonical ensemble (gravo-thermal catastrophe).
```

**Negative heat capacity in the microcanonical ensemble**: For self-gravitating clusters (star clusters, galaxy clusters), adding energy (injecting kinetic energy) causes the system to contract and heat up, radiating more energy than injected. The Jeans instability and gravo-thermal catastrophe are microcanonical phenomena with no canonical analog.

## Quantum Microcanonical вҖ” Counting Discrete Levels

For quantum systems, the energy spectrum is discrete. The microcanonical ensemble counts quantum states in [E, E+ОҙE]:

    О©(E) = number of energy eigenstates with E_n вҲҲ [E, E+ОҙE]

**Harmonic oscillator ladder** (1D, energy spacing в„ҸПү):

    E_n = в„ҸПү(n + 1/2),    n = 0, 1, 2, ...
    g(E) = 1/в„ҸПү  (constant density of states)
    S = k_B ln(E/в„ҸПү + const) = k_B ln(n)

**3D harmonic crystal (Einstein model)**: 3N oscillators at the same frequency Пү.

    О©(E) = C(E/в„ҸПү + 3N вҲ’ 1, 3N вҲ’ 1)  (distributing n quanta among 3N oscillators)

At high T (k_BT >> в„ҸПү): О© grows polynomially вҶ’ equipartition вҶ’ C_V = 3Nk_B (Dulong-Petit).
At low T (k_BT << в„ҸПү): О© ~ e^{-в„ҸПү/k_BT} вҶ’ quantum freeze-out вҶ’ C_V вҶ’ 0.

**The Debye model** improves on Einstein by using a spectrum of frequencies Пү_k (phonons) up to a Debye cutoff Пү_D. The density of states goes as g(Пү) вҲқ ПүВІ for Пү < Пү_D. This gives C_V вҲқ TВі at low T вҖ” observed in all crystalline solids.

**Why C_V вҲқ TВі follows from g(Пү) вҲқ ПүВІ**: The total energy U = вҲ«вӮҖ^{Пү_D} в„ҸПү Г— g(Пү) Г— n_BE(Пү) dПү where n_BE = 1/(e^{ОІв„ҸПү}вҲ’1). At low T (ОІв„ҸПү_D >> 1), the upper limit extends to вҲһ and the substitution x = ОІв„ҸПү gives U вҲқ T^4 вҲ«вӮҖ^вҲһ xВі/(e^xвҲ’1) dx, so C_V = вҲӮU/вҲӮT вҲқ TВі. The exponent 3 = d+1вҲ’1 for d = 3 with g(Пү) вҲқ Пү^{d-1}. The g(Пү) вҲқ ПүВІ density of states from the table above directly determines the low-T power law.

## Common Confusion Points

**The energy shell width ОҙE drops out**: The entropy is S = k_B ln О© where О© counts states in a shell [E, E+ОҙE]. Changing ОҙE changes О© by an additive O(ln ОҙE) term, negligible compared to O(N). So thermodynamic results are independent of ОҙE.

**N! is not optional for classical identical particles**: Without N!, the entropy of an ideal gas is not extensive (Gibbs paradox): mixing two identical gases would increase entropy, which is wrong. The N! factor is a classical approximation to the quantum indistinguishability that forbids permuted states from being distinct.

**Microcanonical negative heat capacity is real, not pathological**: In gravitational systems, energy and temperature can move in opposite directions. Adding energy causes collapse and heating. This is not an error вҖ” it is the gravo-thermal catastrophe, responsible for core collapse of globular clusters. The canonical ensemble conceals this by coupling to a heat bath that prevents the instability.

**The second law holds statistically, not absolutely**: In a finite system at finite time, the entropy can decrease by a small amount. The fluctuation theorems (Jarzynski, Crooks) quantify exactly how probable such violations are: P(О”S > 0)/P(О”S < 0) = e^{|О”S|/k_B}. For macroscopic О”S, the ratio is e^{10ВІВі} вҖ” violations are never observed in practice but are logically possible.
