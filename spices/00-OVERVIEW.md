# 00 — Spices: Overview

## Trade, Power, and Flavor — Why Spices Shaped the Modern World

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- What counts as a spice vs herb vs flavoring: dried plant parts (seed, bark, root, stigma, berry) vs fresh leaves; volatile oils as the active agents; distinction from dyes and medicines (though heavy overlap)
- Why spices mattered before refrigeration: preservation (salt, vinegar, smoking), masking spoilage, medicine (humoral theory — "hot" spices for "cold" conditions), luxury signaling (pepper as currency, saffron as royal dye), religious ritual (frankincense, myrrh)
- Spice geography: origin map — almost all Old World spices from South/Southeast Asia (monsoon tropics), capsicums uniquely from Americas; climate requirements (tropical, specific soil); why spices couldn't be grown in Europe
- Trade eras:
  - Overland Silk Road (ancient–1453): Arab intermediaries → Venice/Genoa monopoly on European end; Ottoman conquest of Constantinople drives search for sea routes
  - Portuguese maritime breakthrough: Bartolomeu Dias rounds Cape (1488), Vasco da Gama reaches Kozhikode (1498); Estado da India + cartaz system
  - Dutch VOC dominance (1602–1799): first joint-stock corporation, Banda Islands massacre, nutmeg/clove monopoly, Run Island exchange for Manhattan; spice as driver of capitalism's origin
  - British EIC displacement of Dutch; commodity market era — spices become cheap bulk goods
  - Modern: global commodity markets (CBOT for pepper), origin certification, fair trade, terroir claims (Kampot pepper, Malabar pepper)
- Economic significance: pepper once worth its weight in silver; Columbus's fatal geographic error (sought spice islands, found Caribbean); spice trade financed the Age of Exploration; Dutch Golden Age funded by nutmeg
- Spice adulteration: ancient problem (lead in pepper, brick dust in saffron); modern food fraud; spectroscopic authentication
- Decision cheat sheet: spice families, origin regions, key compounds, historical chokepoints
