# 03 — Turmeric, Ginger & Galangal

## The Zingiberaceae Family — Rhizome Botany, Curcumin, Bioactive Chemistry

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Zingiberaceae family: monocot family of ~1,600 species; characteristically aromatic rhizomes (underground stems, not roots); tropical distribution centered on South/Southeast Asia; includes turmeric, ginger, galangal, cardamom, grains of paradise
- **Turmeric** (*Curcuma longa*): native to South Asia (likely Bengal/Assam origin); cultivated for rhizomes; the yellow-orange color from curcuminoids (curcumin ~2–5% of dry weight, demethoxycurcumin, bisdemethoxycurcumin)
- Curcumin chemistry: diferuloylmethane; phenylpropanoid pathway biosynthesis; keto-enol tautomerism; vibrant yellow from extended conjugation; poor bioavailability (hydrophobic, rapid metabolism) — the piperine enhancement story (20× via CYP450 inhibition); nanoformulation research
- Turmeric in history: Sanskrit texts 4000 BCE; Ayurvedic medicine (anti-inflammatory in traditional use); Hindu religious significance (auspicious yellow); spice, dye, and preservative simultaneously; modern Western "superfood" market vs traditional South Asian everyday spice — cultural appropriation dynamics
- Clinical evidence debate: curcumin trials — promising in vitro, disappointing in vivo; bioavailability problem; PAINS (pan-assay interference compounds) — curcumin as canonical example of a problematic research compound
- **Ginger** (*Zingiber officinale*): probably original in Maritime Southeast Asia; cultivated so long it no longer exists in wild; fresh vs dried have different flavor profiles due to chemistry transformation
- Ginger chemistry: fresh = gingerols (6-gingerol, 8-gingerol, 10-gingerol — pungent, similar structure to capsaicin, both activate TRPV1); dried = shogaols (dehydration converts gingerols → shogaols, more pungent and stable); heat treatment → zingerone (milder, spicy-sweet)
- Ginger in trade: ancient trade item across Indian Ocean; Roman spice trade; crystallized ginger and ginger beer as English colonial products; anti-nausea evidence (motion sickness, chemotherapy nausea — reasonable clinical support, mechanism uncertain)
- **Galangal**: greater galangal (*Alpinia galanga*) and lesser galangal (*A. officinarum*); Southeast Asian aromatics (Thai, Indonesian, Vietnamese cuisine); galangal ≠ ginger (different flavor profile — more piney, medicinal, less sharp); galangin as key compound; 1-acetoxychavicol acetate; medieval European use as medicine (Hildegard von Bingen)
- Grains of paradise (*Aframomum melegueta*): West African Zingiberaceae; used as cheap pepper substitute in medieval Europe; thermogenic properties; craft beer/spirits revival
