## Piperine — The Active Compound

```
PIPERINE (C17H19NO3)
  Piperidine alkaloid — nitrogen-containing heterocycle
  ~5–9% of black pepper dry weight
  Biosynthesis: piperic acid + piperidine

MECHANISM:
  Piperine ≠ capsaicin mechanism
  - Activates TRPA1 + TRPV1 (at higher concentrations)
  - Inhibits CYP3A4 and P-glycoprotein (drug-metabolizing enzymes)
  → Piperine increases bioavailability of many drugs/compounds

  Most famous interaction:
  PIPERINE + CURCUMIN → 20× bioavailability increase for curcumin
  (via CYP450 inhibition — slows first-pass metabolism)
  This is why traditional Indian cooking combines black pepper + turmeric
  — discovered empirically thousands of years before the biochemistry

DRUG INTERACTION NOTE:
  Piperine can affect absorption of: phenytoin, propranolol,
  theophylline, rifampicin, cyclosporine — clinically significant
  at supplement doses, not typical culinary use
```

## Long Pepper — The Forgotten Precursor

```
PRE-1500 WORLD: Long pepper (Piper longum) > Black pepper in value
  Longer, more complex flavor; perceived as more "medicinal"
  Classical antiquity texts reference "pepper" = often long pepper

Post-Portuguese: Black pepper flooded markets at lower prices
  Black pepper's simpler harvest + higher yield won economically
  Long pepper collapsed from dominance to niche product
  Now: craft/artisan revival; Indian āyurvedic medicine (pippali)
```

## Salt — Geology and Chemistry

Salt is not a spice (it has no flavor compound beyond NaCl itself), but it is the foundational food technology, predating all spices.

"Himalayan pink salt" marketing vs chemistry:
  Pink color from iron oxide (Fe₂O₃) trace impurity
  NaCl purity ~97–99% — trace minerals negligible nutritionally
  From Khewra mine, Pakistan — not the Himalayas proper
  Price premium: marketing, not chemistry

## Salt Preservation Chemistry

```
OSMOTIC DEHYDRATION:
  High extracellular [NaCl] → water leaves bacteria via osmosis
  Water activity (aₓ) below 0.85 → most pathogens cannot grow
  Salt-tolerant organisms (halophiles) survive → selective for:
    Lactobacillus (fermentation) — kimchi, sauerkraut, pickles
    Halobacterium (not harmful) — salt cod

DRY CURE vs BRINE:
  Dry cure: salt packed directly on meat → draws moisture out
           → forms its own brine → equilibrates
  Brine: meat submerged in salt solution → faster, more uniform

  Salt concentration spectrum:
  2–3%: Lacto-fermentation (kimchi brine)
  5–10%: Mild preservation (olives, pickles)
  20%+: Full preservation (salt cod, gravlax)

SALT IN FERMENTATION:
  Salt selects Lactobacillus over competing bacteria
  → L. produces lactic acid → pH drops → further protection
  This is lacto-fermentation — kimchi, sauerkraut, miso, cheese
  The salt isn't preserved product itself — it creates conditions
  for organisms that DO the preserving
```

## Salt Roads and Political Economy

```
VIA SALARIA (Rome):
  One of oldest Roman roads (300+ BCE)
  Rome → Adriatic coast salt flats
  "Via Salaria" = Salt Road, literally
  Control of salt = control of protein preservation = power

SAHARAN SALT TRADE:
  Timbuktu = nexus of salt (north) + gold (south)
  Tuareg traders on trans-Saharan routes
  Salt bars used as currency across sub-Saharan Africa
  Equal weight exchange: salt (north) ↔ gold (south)
  Mali empire founded on this arbitrage

GABELLE (France):
  Medieval–Revolutionary salt tax
  State-owned salt monopoly with mandatory purchase quotas
  Hated tax — among grievances leading to 1789 Revolution
  Different regions had different gabelle rates → massive smuggling

BRITISH INDIA:
  Salt manufacture monopoly → Indians had to buy British salt
  Gandhi's Salt March (1930): 240-mile walk to sea to make salt
  Brilliant political choice: universal, tangible, undeniable injustice
  Became symbol of Indian independence movement
```

## Roman Salarium — The Salary Myth

The claim that Roman soldiers were "paid in salt" (hence *salarium* → *salary*) is partly mythology:

```
WHAT'S DOCUMENTED:
  "Salarium" derives from "sal" (salt)
  Roman soldiers received "salarium" — but likely as salt allowance
  or money TO BUY salt, not salt itself as wages

WHAT'S DISPUTED:
  "Paid in salt" as literal wage payment: no strong documentary evidence
  Pliny's Natural History hints at it but ambiguously
  The phrase may have been metaphorical from the start

WHAT'S CERTAIN:
  Salt's economic significance in Rome was real and massive
  Salt tax (via salinarum) was a major revenue source
  Control of salt pans (saltus) was politically significant
```

## Modern Salt — Commercial Reality

| Product | What it actually is |
|---------|---------------------|
| Table salt | Finely ground halite, iodized (KI added), anti-caking agents |
| Kosher salt | Coarser crystal, no iodine, no anti-caking agents. Draws blood from meat (kashering). No religious content in the salt itself. |
| Sea salt | Solar-evaporated seawater. Trace mineral profile varies by source but nutritionally similar to table salt |
| Fleur de sel | First delicate crystals that form on surface of evaporation ponds. Premium texture, quick-dissolving, finishing salt |
| Himalayan pink salt | Rock salt from Khewra Mine, Pakistan. Iron oxide pigment. ~97% NaCl. |
| "Low sodium" salt | KCl substitution for NaCl. Works for mild reduction. Metallic taste at high substitution. |

**Iodized salt — the public health story:**
- Goiter (thyroid enlargement from iodine deficiency) was endemic in landlocked populations
- US began iodizing salt 1924 (Michigan first)
- One of the cheapest and most effective public health interventions in history
- Iodine deficiency is still the leading preventable cause of intellectual disability globally

## Common Confusion Points

**Long pepper vs black pepper:** Pre-Portuguese, "pepper" often meant long pepper (*P. longum*). Black pepper won commercially due to higher yield and lower price, not superior flavor.

**"Sichuan pepper" is not pepper:** *Zanthoxylum* spp. (citrus/rue family). Numbness from hydroxy-alpha-sanshool acting on TRPV1/TRPA1 simultaneously. The *má* (numb) in Chinese *mála* (numb-hot). Completely unrelated to *Piper* genus.

**Piperine ≠ capsaicin mechanism:** Both cause "heat" sensation but bind different receptor sites and have different structural classes. Capsaicin = vanilloid alkaloid binding TRPV1 transmembrane domain. Piperine = piperidine alkaloid with a more complex binding pattern.

**Kosher salt is not blessed:** The name comes from the kashering process (drawing blood from meat for kosher dietary laws). The salt itself is chemically identical to other NaCl products — just coarser ground for better blood absorption. Rabbinical blessing not involved.
