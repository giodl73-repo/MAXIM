# 01 — Pepper & Salt

## The Two Universal Spices — Flavor, Preservation, and Political Economy

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Black pepper** (*Piper nigrum*): origin in Kerala (Malabar Coast, India); climbing vine needing support trees and high humidity; drupes harvested green → dried to wrinkle (black), or fully ripe → hulled (white), or freeze-dried green; long pepper (*P. longum*) the dominant variety in classical antiquity — why it was displaced
- Pepper in antiquity: found in Ramesses II's nostrils (1213 BCE); Alaric the Visigoth demanded 3,000 lbs of pepper as ransom for Rome (408 CE); Roman pepper trade volume and value; Arab dhow trade across Indian Ocean
- Piperine (alkaloid, ~5–9%): the primary pungent compound; different receptor than capsaicin (not TRPV1 — acts on different pathway); piperine enhancing curcumin bioavailability (20× increase) — why turmeric + black pepper traditional combination
- Venice and the pepper monopoly: overland route, Levantine ports, Venetian control of European distribution; why this drove Portuguese maritime exploration; price differential between Kozhikode and Venice (60×)
- Post-Vasco da Gama: Portuguese Estado da India; eventual loss of monopoly; pepper becomes cheap — irony of exploration success
- Modern: India, Vietnam, Indonesia, Brazil as top producers; Kampot (Cambodia) and Malabar as premium terroir designations; Sichuan pepper (*Zanthoxylum*) — not a true pepper, causes numbing via hydroxy-alpha-sanshool on TRPV1 + TRPA1
- **Salt** (NaCl): not a spice but the foundational preservative; geology — evaporite deposits (Wieliczka, Poland; Khewra, Pakistan — Himalayan pink salt; Detroit salt mines under the city); sea salt from solar evaporation (fleur de sel, Maldon)
- Salt preservation chemistry: osmotic dehydration killing bacteria; brine vs dry-cure; role in fermentation (lacto-fermentation — kimchi, sauerkraut — salt selects Lactobacillus)
- Salt roads and political power: Via Salaria (Rome); Saharan salt trade (Timbuktu = salt from north + gold from south); gabelle (French salt tax, cause of Revolution grievances); British salt monopoly in India (Gandhi's Salt March 1930)
- Roman salarium: etymology of "salary"; soldiers paid in salt — historical accuracy debated; but salt's value as currency across sub-Saharan Africa documented
- Modern salt: iodized salt (goiter prevention, 1920s — one of public health's greatest wins); kosher salt (coarser grain for drawing blood), fleur de sel vs Himalayan — marketing vs chemistry (trace minerals vs NaCl purity)
