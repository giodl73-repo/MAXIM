# Rankean Positivism: Archives, Quellenkritik, and Historicism

## The Big Picture

Leopold von Ranke (1795-1886) established the professional historian's craft as it was practiced from the mid-19th century through most of the 20th. His central claims: history must be based on primary sources (preferably archival documents), sources must be subjected to systematic criticism (Quellenkritik), and each historical epoch has its own internal logic that must be understood on its own terms (historicism). These were all improvements over Enlightenment practices, but each had built-in limits that later schools exploited.

```
RANKEAN REVOLUTION

  BEFORE RANKE:                     RANKE'S STANDARDS:
  Chronicles and narratives          Primary archival documents
  based on earlier chronicles        (not chronicles = secondary)

  Philosophical history              Specific events, not generalized
  (principles abstracted from        philosophical claims about human nature
  multiple cases)

  Humanist rhetoric:                 Scholarly apparatus: footnotes,
  argue from examples                source citations, critical apparatus

  Patriotic or providential          Attempt at detachment: understand
  frameworks imposed                 each epoch on its own terms

  Trained through reading            Seminar method: students trained
  classical texts                    through source criticism (archival work)

  RESULT: Modern academic historical profession
    PhD training -> seminar -> archival research -> monograph
    This structure created and Ranke dominated; still partially operational
```

## Leopold von Ranke (1795-1886)

```
RANKE'S FAMOUS FORMULA

  "wie es eigentlich gewesen"

  TRANSLATION DEBATES:
    Traditional: "as it actually/really was"
    Revisionist (Krieger): "as it essentially was" (eigentlich = essentially)
    The distinction matters: "actually" implies photographic recovery of fact;
    "essentially" implies informed understanding of character/nature

  Ranke meant: the historian should not impose present frameworks
  (Enlightenment progress; contemporary politics) but understand the past
  through its own logic, its own documents, its own internal coherence

  QUELLENKRITIK (source criticism):
    Not invented by Ranke but systematized into academic standard
    EXTERNAL CRITICISM: Is the document authentic? When was it written?
      Who wrote it? Has it been altered?
    INTERNAL CRITICISM: What is the reliability of the content?
      Is the author in a position to know? Do they have a motive to distort?
      How does the account compare to other sources?

    Ranke famously worked through the Venetian diplomatic dispatches
    (relazioni) for Papacy and 16th-century Italian history
    -> Archives as the primary locus of historical research

  HISTORICISM:
    Each historical epoch (Zeitalter) stands in immediate relationship to God
    Has its own intrinsic value; cannot be judged merely as step to present
    "All epochs are equally immediate to God"
    -> Counter to Enlightenment teleology: no epoch is merely transition to next
    -> Each epoch should be understood in its own terms
    This is not the same as relativism: some things really happened; some sources
    are better than others; historical truth is attainable (within limits)

  SEMINAR METHOD:
    Ranke established the historical seminar at Berlin (from 1825)
    Students trained to read documents critically, in original languages
    Became the model for university history training worldwide
    Exported to US via Johns Hopkins (Herbert Baxter Adams, 1876)
    -> Created the academic profession as we know it
```

## German Historical School: Droysen and Dilthey

Ranke's near-contemporaries developed the methodological foundations in more philosophically sophisticated directions:

```
DROYSEN AND THE THEORY OF HISTORICAL METHOD (Historik)

  Johann Gustav Droysen (1808-1884):
    "Historik" (lectures, 1857ff): first systematic theory of historical method
    Key distinction: Verstehen (understanding) vs. Erklaren (explaining)
    Natural sciences: Erklaren = explain by universal causal laws
    Human sciences (Geisteswissenschaften): Verstehen = understand from within
    History belongs to the human sciences: empathetic understanding
    not causal-law subsumption

    Historical inquiry: "investigation" (Forschung) + "presentation" (Darstellung)
    -> Cannot cleanly separate research method from narrative presentation
    Anticipates Hayden White's claim about form and content

DILTHEY AND HERMENEUTICS

  Wilhelm Dilthey (1833-1911):
    "Critique of Historical Reason" (parallel to Kant's critiques)
    Human sciences require a different epistemological foundation than natural science
    Erlebnis (lived experience) -> Ausdruck (expression) -> Verstehen (understanding)
    Understanding: always interpretive; always circular (hermeneutic circle)
    Hermeneutic circle: understand whole text by reading parts;
                        understand parts by reference to whole
    Historical knowledge: always from within tradition; no view from nowhere

    Significance: Dilthey's framework became the epistemological basis for
    all humanistic disciplines opposed to natural-science imperialism
```

## Rankean Limits: The Built-in Problems

```
WHAT RANKE'S METHOD CANNOT DO

1. UNDOCUMENTED POPULATIONS:
   The poor, women, enslaved people, pre-literate cultures:
   largely absent from archives
   Archives record what literate bureaucracies chose to record
   -> Social history later compensated (material culture, quantitative data)

2. DOCUMENTARY FETISHISM:
   Over-privileging written documents vs. material culture, oral tradition
   Archaeology, numismatics, art history: treated as auxiliary
   Quantitative data: not primary documents; mostly excluded until 20th century

3. STRUCTURE VS. EVENT:
   Ranke's method excels at reconstructing specific events and decisions
   Structural forces (climate, disease, economics, geography): invisible
   Braudel's Annales revolt is partly against this event-focus

4. APPARENT DETACHMENT IS NOT ACTUAL DETACHMENT:
   Ranke claimed to transcend politics; actually wrote for Prussian state
   His "universal history" privileged Protestant German states
   His archival access was to European state archives -- excludes non-European

5. HERMENEUTIC PROBLEM:
   The historian always brings frameworks to documents
   The "impartial" reading Ranke claimed is impossible
   Droysen already saw this; Gadamer made it systematic theory
```

## Positivism in History: Buckle and Statistical History

A separate strand: applying natural science methods to history:

```
H.T. BUCKLE (1821-1862)

  "History of Civilization in England" (1857-1861)
  Thesis: history follows regular laws discoverable by statistical analysis
  Evidence: French homicide statistics showed remarkable constancy year to year
  -> If individual acts seem random but aggregate statistics are stable,
     human behavior follows laws as regular as natural laws
  -> History should become a science of these laws

  BUCKLE'S SOURCES:
    Quetelet (Belgian statistician): "social physics" -- social phenomena
    follow Gaussian distributions like physical phenomena
    Comte's positivism: hierarchy of sciences; sociology at the top

  CRITIQUE:
    Buckle conflates statistical regularity with determinism
    Statistical stability of aggregate does not eliminate individual agency
    Aggregate patterns can be described without reducing explanation to laws

  LEGACY:
    Opened quantitative direction in history
    Direct precursor to 20th-century social science history (cliometrics)
    Buckle himself was not a professional historian: no university post,
    no training -- yet most widely read English historian of his day
```

## The Positivist Legacy in 20th-Century History

```
SOCIAL SCIENCE HISTORY (1950s-1970s)

  Ranke's insistence on primary sources + Buckle's quantification ->
  Social Science History Association (1975)
  Cliometrics (from Clio, muse of history + metrics): quantitative economic history
  Key figures: Robert Fogel, Douglass North
  Methods: counterfactual analysis, statistical regression, econometric models

  FOGEL AND ENGERMAN: "Time on the Cross" (1974)
    Thesis: American slavery was economically efficient system
    Method: systematic analysis of plantation records, slave health data
    Controversy: conclusions widely disputed; methodology also attacked
    -> Showed both power and limits of quantitative history
    Fogel later won Nobel Prize in Economics (1993)

  HISTORICAL DEMOGRAPHY:
    Cambridge Group for the History of Population and Social Structure
    Peter Laslett, E.A. Wrigley: reconstruction of English population from
    parish records (births, marriages, deaths)
    Established that English household size was small and nuclear -- not extended
    Counter to Marxist and Parsonian assumptions about pre-modern family

  ANNALES QUANTITATIVE:
    Ernest Labrousse: price and wage series; conjunctural economic history
    Pierre Chaunu: serial history (systematic analysis of repetitive sources
    over long time periods: notarial records, inquisition files, wills)
    -> Both inspired by Annales; neither purely Rankean
```

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| Concept | Ranke's Position | Later Critique |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Primary sources | Archives are the gold standard | Archives survive unequally; not neutral |
| Source criticism | Systematic external + internal criticism | Accepted but expanded (material, oral) |
| Historicism | Each epoch on its own terms | Risk of relativism; paralysis of judgment |
| Detachment | Historian can transcend their own time | Impossible; better to acknowledge perspective |
| Political history | Kings, diplomats, battles, treaties | Neglects structure, society, economy |
| German archives | Accessible, well-organized, central | Most of world history has no such archives |

## Common Confusion Points

**Ranke's formula does not mean "just the facts"** -- "Wie es eigentlich gewesen" is a methodological ideal about subordinating the historian's present-mindedness to the past's own logic, not a naive positivist claim about recovering raw facts. Ranke was deeply aware that all history is interpretation; his point was about the direction of the interpretive effort.

**Historicism is not historical relativism** -- Herder's and Ranke's historicism (each epoch on its own terms) does not imply that all historical claims are equally valid. It implies that the historian should not measure the past by present standards -- a methodological caution, not a claim about truth. Ranke was a Christian believer who thought historical understanding approached the divine logos.

**Quellenkritik did not originate with Ranke** -- Systematic textual criticism was developed by classical philologists (Bentley, Wolf) and biblical scholars (Eichhorn's source criticism of the Pentateuch, de Wette) before Ranke applied it specifically to archives. Ranke transferred philological methods from texts to documents.

**The seminar model exported unevenly** -- When the German PhD model was exported to the United States (Johns Hopkins, 1876), it was adapted to American conditions. The British historical tradition remained more essay-based and less strictly archival until the 20th century. French academic history had its own parallel development.
