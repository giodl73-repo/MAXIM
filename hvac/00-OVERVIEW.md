# 00 — HVAC: Overview

## Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning — Load, Equipment, Distribution, Controls

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- HVAC's three functions, often treated independently but interdependent: (1) **Heating** — replace heat lost through building envelope; (2) **Cooling** — remove heat gained through envelope/people/equipment/solar; (3) **Ventilation** — replace indoor air with outdoor air for IAQ (indoor air quality); mechanically separate in some systems (furnace + AC + ERV), integrated in others (heat pump with ventilation)
- The load vs capacity framework: load = BTU/h required by physics of building envelope; capacity = BTU/h equipment can deliver; proper sizing requires calculation, not rules of thumb; oversizing causes short cycling (comfort problems, humidity issues, increased maintenance), undersizing causes inability to maintain setpoint on design day
- **Manual J** (ACCA): residential load calculation standard; accounts for: climate zone, insulation levels, window area/orientation/U-factor, infiltration, internal gains (people, appliances), latent vs sensible loads; must be performed before equipment selection; rule of thumb "400 sq ft per ton" is NOT a substitute
- Psychrometrics: the thermodynamics of moist air; sensible heat (raises dry-bulb temperature), latent heat (phase change — evaporation/condensation of water); both must be controlled; AC systems cool AND dehumidify (condensation on coil); dedicated dehumidifiers for cases where cooling isn't needed
- Refrigerant-based systems: heat pumps and air conditioning move heat via refrigerant phase changes; evaporation absorbs heat, condensation releases heat; reversing valve enables heat pump (same equipment heats or cools)
- Building envelope interaction: HVAC equipment size must match envelope; better envelope (more insulation, better windows, air sealing) → smaller HVAC equipment needed; design sequence: envelope first, then HVAC sizing; envelope improvements make HVAC upgrades more effective
- Code framework: International Mechanical Code (IMC), International Fuel Gas Code (IFGC), International Energy Conservation Code (IECC) for efficiency requirements; ASHRAE 62.2 (residential ventilation), ASHRAE 90.1 (commercial energy efficiency); local amendments; equipment AHRI-certified ratings
