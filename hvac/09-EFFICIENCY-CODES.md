# 09 — Efficiency & Codes

## SEER2, AFUE, HSPF2, IECC, IRA Tax Credits, Manual J/D/S

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **DOE efficiency ratings change (2023)**: DOE updated test procedures for residential HVAC January 1, 2023; new test conditions are more representative of actual operation (higher external static pressure for AC, lower temperature for heat pumps); SEER → SEER2, EER → EER2, HSPF → HSPF2; numbers under new test procedure are ~4-7% lower (not lower efficiency equipment — harder test); do not compare old SEER to new SEER2 directly; minimum SEER2: 13.4 (North), 14.3 (South/Southwest) for single-stage
- **New minimum efficiency standards (2023)**:
  - Central AC: 13.4 SEER2 (North), 14.3 SEER2 (Southeast/Southwest), 13.4 EER2 minimum
  - Heat pumps: 14.3 SEER2, 7.5 HSPF2 minimum nationally
  - Gas furnaces: 80% AFUE minimum nationally (2023 DOE attempted 92% AFUE national minimum for gas furnaces — blocked by courts); regional minimums may apply
  - Equipment sold before 2023 using old ratings can be installed after 2023 — affects replacement inventory
- **Energy Star** thresholds (voluntary, above minimum):
  - Central AC: SEER2 ≥ 16 (variable speed), SEER2 ≥ 15.2 (two-stage)
  - Heat pumps: SEER2 ≥ 15.2, HSPF2 ≥ 8.1
  - Furnaces: ≥ 95% AFUE
  - Tax credit qualification (Inflation Reduction Act 2022): Energy Star required for most credits
- **IECC (International Energy Conservation Code)**: building energy efficiency requirements for new construction; climate zones 1 (hot/humid — Miami) through 8 (subarctic — Alaska); zones determine: minimum insulation levels (wall, ceiling, basement, slab), maximum window U-factor and SHGC, air sealing requirements (blower door test — ACH50 limit), duct sealing (duct blaster test), mechanical system efficiency minimums; updated every 3 years; state adoption lags (many states on 2015 or 2018 IECC)
- **Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) 2022 tax credits** (§25C, residential): 30% of cost up to limits:
  - Heat pumps (ASHP): up to $2,000/year (Energy Star certified)
  - Heat pump water heaters: up to $2,000/year (Energy Star)
  - Central AC: up to $600 (highest efficiency tiers)
  - Gas furnace: up to $600 (95% AFUE, gas/propane only, not natural gas in some interpretations — verify current guidance)
  - Insulation, air sealing: up to $1,200/year
  - Windows: up to $600/year
  - Home energy audit: up to $150
  - Credits are non-refundable, apply against tax liability; not refundable if tax bill is zero; Inflation Reduction Act Rebates (HEAR — High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate Act) are separate, income-qualified upfront rebates through state programs (rollout began 2024)
- **Manual J/D/S triad** (ACCA — Air Conditioning Contractors of America):
  - Manual J: heating and cooling load calculation (room-by-room sensible and latent loads)
  - Manual S: equipment selection (match equipment capacity to Manual J loads — allows for some oversizing but limits it; selects equipment at AHRI-rated conditions closest to design conditions)
  - Manual D: duct design (sizes duct system to deliver Manual J airflow to each room)
  - These three must be performed sequentially; most replacement equipment is installed without any of them (rule of thumb → chronic problems); increasingly required by code for new construction and in some jurisdictions for replacements
- **Typical replacement mistakes**: (1) replacing equipment same size as old equipment without load calc (original equipment may have been oversized); (2) no duct evaluation (old duct system may be undersized for new equipment); (3) mismatched indoor/outdoor units (mixed brands/models that aren't AHRI-matched — lower real efficiency than rated, warranty issues); (4) not replacing refrigerant when switching from R-22 to R-410A equipment (different oil, different pressures); (5) SEER2 number selected for marketing without considering realistic operating conditions
