## Paths

### The Formal Knowledge Path
`mathematicians-logicians/` → `physicists-astronomers/` → `computing-pioneers/`
*Mathematics provides the language; physics provides the phenomena that demand new mathematical structures (calculus was forced by mechanics, Riemannian geometry by general relativity, linear algebra by quantum mechanics, probability by statistical mechanics); computing pioneers take the mathematical logic of Turing and Shannon and instantiate it in machines. Gödel and Turing are the hinge: incompleteness and undecidability are the same result in two registers — the limits of formal systems and the limits of computation are identical.*

### The World-Changing Path
`political-reformers/` → `social-reformers/` → `visionaries/`
*Political reformers change institutions; social reformers change the conditions those institutions govern (slavery, gender, labor, public health — the substance rather than the structure of power); visionaries project what institutions and conditions could become. The sequence moves from the constitutionally achievable to the humanly possible to the speculatively possible — each generation's reforms become the prior generation's vision.*

### The Ideas-Into-Form Path
`philosophers-thinkers/` → `writers-poets/` → `artists-architects/`
*Philosophy provides the conceptual vocabulary that literary and visual culture works through over time: Plato's idealism shapes Augustine and Dante; Kant's aesthetics shapes German Romanticism; existentialism shapes Sartre's theater and Beckett's drama; Ruskin and Morris shape Arts and Crafts architecture. Ideas move from abstraction into narrative into built form — and built form feeds back into the philosophical problems it was meant to answer.*

| Computing & Software | `computing-pioneers/` ↔ `computing/` — the pioneers section explains why technical decisions were made. Thompson and Ritchie's Unix design philosophy is not separable from their personalities and circumstances. Dijkstra's correctness obsession is the human origin of `formal-methods/`. |
| History & Ideas | `philosophers-thinkers/` ↔ `philosophy/` (the philosophical tradition is the content; the people are the thread that makes it a story); `political-reformers/` ↔ `political-history/` (structural history and biographical history are complementary lenses on the same events — Skocpol's comparative analysis and Lincoln's biography illuminate different things about the same crisis); `explorers/` ↔ `historical-geography/` (exploration is geography in motion — Cook's surveys are the data that `historical-geography/` analyzes). |
| Social Sciences | `social-reformers/` ↔ `sociology/` and `public-health/` — Nightingale invented epidemiological data visualization; Carson invented regulatory environmentalism; Snow's cholera map is the founding document of spatial epidemiology. The reformers section is applied social science with names attached. `political-reformers/` ↔ `political-science/` — the practitioners instantiate what the theorists describe, and sometimes the same person does both (Locke, Madison, Rawls). |
