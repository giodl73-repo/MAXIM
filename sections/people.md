# People

*12 directories · ~1,200 pages · P·I, P·II, P·III*

The People section profiles the individuals who built the disciplines catalogued throughout this library. Where other sections teach the ideas, this section teaches through biography — showing how knowledge was discovered, by whom, in what context, and at what cost.

Organized by domain to mirror the structure of the encyclopedia itself: scientists alongside the science, builders alongside the engineering, thinkers alongside the philosophy.

## How to Use This Section

The People section is a **cross-index into the rest of the library**. Each directory profile focuses on:

- **What they discovered or built** — the intellectual/technical contribution
- **How they did it** — method, context, tools, collaborators
- **What resisted them** — institutional friction, prior paradigms, personal obstacles
- **Connections to the field** — bridging biography to the relevant subject directory

Read a biography here, then follow the link to the field directory for the technical depth. The bridge runs both ways: studying a field cold, then reading about its founders, fixes the ideas in narrative memory.

---

## Style Note

People entries do not dumb down. The learner has MIT depth in mathematics and computer science — biographies of Gödel, Turing, Noether, and Grothendieck can and should engage with the actual mathematics. Biographical narrative does not require avoiding technical precision.
