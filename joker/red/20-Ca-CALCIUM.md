### What the Diagram Shows

**Below the unconformity:**

- **A** (M[U]DSTONE) -- the lowest unit. Fine-grained, dark, massive. Extends across the full width of the exposure.
- **B** (SA[N]DSTONE) -- medium-grained, quartz-rich. Rests on A. Tilted at roughly 30 degrees along with C.
- **C** (LIM[E]STONE) -- grey carbonate rock that fizzes with acid. Rests on B. Also tilted at roughly 30 degrees.
- **D** (B[A]SALT DIKE) -- a narrow vertical column of dark, fine-grained igneous rock, marked `*` in the diagram. It passes through A, B, and C as a continuous body. It terminates at the unconformity surface. It does not appear anywhere above the unconformity.

**The angular unconformity** -- an erosion surface. Below it, beds B and C are tilted. Above it, beds are horizontal.

**Above the unconformity:**

- **E** (CHE[R]T) -- hard, glassy, conchoidal fracture. Lies directly on the unconformity surface. Horizontal.
- **F** (SIL[T]STONE) -- fine-grained, slightly gritty. Lies above E. Horizontal.
- **G** (S[H]ALE) -- very fine-grained, fissile, dark. The uppermost unit. Horizontal.
