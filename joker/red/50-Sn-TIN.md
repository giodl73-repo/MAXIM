## The Puzzle

A drawdown was recovered from a nineteenth-century Lyon workshop -- the kind of atelier where Jacquard looms turned punched cards into silk. Every cell in the grid represents one interlacement point where a single warp thread crosses a single weft thread. Two states. Nothing else.

The textiles section explains what the two states mean, how a drawdown encodes them, and how the Jacquard loom's punch cards controlled each thread individually. The connection between looms and computing is not metaphor. It is engineering history.
