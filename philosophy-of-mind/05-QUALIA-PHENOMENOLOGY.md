## Qualia: Definition and Properties

```
QUALIA DEFINED
===============

Qualia (singular: quale) = the intrinsic, subjective, qualitative
properties of conscious experience.

CLASSIC EXAMPLES:
  - The redness you experience when seeing a red apple
  - The painfulness of a toothache
  - The taste of coffee
  - The smell of roses
  - The sound of middle C

PROPOSED PROPERTIES OF QUALIA (Dennett's list to critique):
  1. INEFFABLE: Cannot be fully communicated to others
     "I can't describe what red looks like to you"
  2. INTRINSIC: Non-relational properties of the experience itself
     Not the relation to stimuli; the experience as such
  3. PRIVATE: Only accessible to the subject having them
     You can't verify that my red is your red
  4. DIRECTLY APPREHENSIBLE: Known to the subject immediately
     No inference required to know you're in pain

DENNETT'S VIEW:
  Qualia as traditionally conceived don't exist.
  Each of the four properties is incoherent or empirically false.
  The concept of an "intrinsic, ineffable, private, immediately
  apprehensible feel" is philosophically confused.
  (This is his eliminativist move about qualia specifically.)

CHALMERS' VIEW:
  Qualia are real and irreducible.
  They are the phenomenal properties that generate the hard problem.
  Any theory of mind that can't account for qualia is incomplete.
```

## The Knowledge Argument: Mary's Room

```
FRANK JACKSON'S KNOWLEDGE ARGUMENT (1982)
==========================================

MARY'S SCENARIO:
  Mary is a brilliant neuroscientist.
  She has lived her entire life in a black-and-white room.
  She has never seen colors.
  BUT: she knows ALL physical facts about color perception:
  - Wavelengths of light
  - Retinal cone responses (L, M, S cones)
  - Neural pathways from eye to V4 to visual cortex
  - Every neuroscience paper ever written about color vision
  - Every functional fact about color discrimination behavior

  Mary is OMNISCIENT about the physical/functional facts of color.

THE QUESTION:
  When Mary leaves the room and sees red for the first time,
  does she learn something NEW?

JACKSON'S ANSWER: YES.
  She learns "what red LOOKS LIKE."
  She knew all the physical facts but didn't know the phenomenal fact.
  Therefore: phenomenal facts are NOT physical facts.
  Therefore: physicalism is incomplete.

FORMAL ARGUMENT:
  P1: Mary knows all physical facts about color.
  P2: Mary learns something new when she sees red.
  Conclusion: There are facts about color that are not physical.
  => Phenomenal properties are non-physical.
```

### Physicalist Responses to Mary

```
PHYSICALIST RESPONSES TO MARY'S ROOM
======================================

1. ABILITY HYPOTHESIS (Lewis, Nemirow):
   Mary doesn't gain new PROPOSITIONAL KNOWLEDGE.
   She gains new ABILITIES:
   - Ability to recognize red
   - Ability to imagine red
   - Ability to remember what red looks like

   "What red looks like" is not a proposition.
   It's a capacity.
   Therefore: no non-physical fact is learned.
   Jackson's premise 2 is ambiguous: "learning something" could be
   gaining an ability, not gaining propositional knowledge.

2. PHENOMENAL CONCEPT STRATEGY (see 03-HARD-PROBLEM.md):
   Mary gains a NEW CONCEPT of a physical fact she already knew.
   Before: she knows the physical description of the C-fiber state.
   After: she has a phenomenal concept of the same state.
   Two concepts; one fact.
   Like gaining "H2O" concept after already knowing "water" concept.

3. "OLD FACT, NEW PERSPECTIVE" (Bigelow & Pargetter):
   Mary knew the fact under one mode of presentation.
   She now knows it under a new mode of presentation (phenomenal).
   The fact is the same; the mode of access is new.

4. "MARY WAS WRONG" (eliminativism):
   Mary's alleged new knowledge is an illusion.
   There is no phenomenal knowledge distinct from functional knowledge.
   What she experiences as "learning what red looks like" is just
   her cognitive system re-routing information in a new way.
```

## The Inverted Spectrum Thought Experiment

```
INVERTED SPECTRUM (Locke, Wittgenstein, Shoemaker)
====================================================

SCENARIO:
  Suppose your internal experience of "red" (the quale)
  is systematically different from mine.
  Where you have the experience "red" produces in me,
  I have the experience "green" produces in you.
  And vice versa.

  We BOTH call the apple "red."
  We have been trained by ostension in a community:
  "This [apple] is red."
  Our verbal behavior is identical.
  Our functional organization is identical (same input-output).
  But our inner experiences are inverted.

  +--------+                    +--------+
  | Your   |  apple --> "red"   | My     |  apple --> "red"
  | qualia |  grass --> "green" | qualia |  grass --> "green"
  +--------+                    +--------+
  Verbally identical.           But subjectively inverted?

IMPLICATIONS:
  1. Against functionalism: same functional organization, different qualia.
     If qualia can vary independently of function, qualia are not
     functional states (contra functionalism).

  2. Against behaviorism: no behavioral test can detect the inversion.
     Qualia are not accessible from behavior.

  3. Against type identity: the same brain state type could (if the
     scenario is possible) be associated with different qualia.

FUNCTIONALIST RESPONSE:
  The scenario is incoherent.
  What makes a quale "red" IS its functional role.
  There is no "intrinsic redness" over and above the functional properties.
  A spectrum inversion would require functional differences too.
  If you react to green wavelengths in all the ways I react to red,
  you DO see what I call red, by definition.

CHALMERS' RESPONSE:
  The scenario IS coherent.
  Qualia have intrinsic properties not captured by functional role.
  Two functionally identical systems could have inverted qualia.
  This is another form of the zombie argument.
```

## Absent Qualia

```
ABSENT QUALIA (Block)
======================

SCENARIO:
  Suppose there is a system that is functionally identical to a
  conscious system in every respect -- same inputs, same outputs,
  same internal functional transitions.
  But it has NO qualia at all.
  It's a philosophical zombie with full functional equivalence.

IMPLICATION FOR FUNCTIONALISM:
  If absent qualia are possible, then qualia are not functional states.
  Functionalism defines mental states by functional role.
  If the same functional role can exist without qualia, qualia are
  additional to functional states.

BLOCK'S NATION OF CHINA (revisited as absent qualia):
  The functional organization of a human brain is replicated by
  1.3 billion people in coordinated communication.
  Does the organization-as-a-whole have qualia?
  Intuition: no. The organization is present; the qualia are absent.
  This suggests qualia can be absent from functional organization.
```

## Husserlian Phenomenology

### Intentionality of Consciousness

```
HUSSERL: INTENTIONALITY (Logical Investigations, 1901; Ideas, 1913)
====================================================================

HUSSERL'S FUNDAMENTAL CLAIM:
  Consciousness is always "intentional" in the technical sense:
  consciousness is ALWAYS consciousness OF something.
  There is no contentless experience.
  Every act of consciousness has an intentional OBJECT.

  Seeing: seeing something
  Thinking: thinking about something
  Fearing: fearing something
  Even imagining: imagining something (even non-existent)

NOESIS / NOEMA DISTINCTION:
  NOESIS: the ACT of consciousness (the experiencing)
          e.g., the perceiving, the wishing, the judging

  NOEMA: the CONTENT of consciousness (what is experienced)
          e.g., the perceived apple, the wished-for peace

  The noema is not the object itself (the real apple)
  but the MEANING through which we intend the object.
  Same real object can be intended through different noemata:
  "The morning star" and "the evening star" = same object (Venus)
  but different intentional content (noema).

  Husserl's key move: bracket the question of whether the object
  exists ("epoché" or phenomenological reduction).
  Study the structure of consciousness itself, regardless of
  whether the intended objects are real.

CS BRIDGE — NOESIS/NOEMA AS METHOD/RETURN-TYPE SEPARATION:
  The noesis/noema distinction separates the act of intending
  from the content intended — a clean separation between process
  and data that formal systems handle explicitly.

  Noesis (act) ≈ the method call — the computational event,
  the operation-as-happening, with its own causal context.

  Noema (content) ≈ the return type / semantic value — what
  the act is about, independent of the specific act instance.

  The same noema (the meaning "Paris") can be the content of
  very different noetic acts: perceiving Paris, remembering Paris,
  imagining Paris, fearing Paris. The content is invariant across
  different act types, just as a return type specifies what a method
  produces independently of how it's implemented.

  Husserl's epoché (bracket existence; study structure of content)
  is the philosopher's analog of interface testing: specify and test
  the contract without committing to any particular implementation.
  What matters is what the consciousness is *about*, not whether
  the object exists — just as interface testing verifies behavior
  against a specification without instantiating a concrete class.

  This separation also maps to the observer pattern:
  the noematic content is the observable state;
  different noetic acts are different observers registering
  the same observable. Husserl's key insight is that multiple
  acts with the same content can cohere as being about the same
  thing — this is intentional identity, and it is what allows
  thought to refer stably across different mental episodes.
```

### Time-Consciousness

```
HUSSERL ON TIME (Lectures on the Consciousness of Internal Time, 1928)
=======================================================================

PROBLEM: A melody is heard as a melody, not as isolated notes.
How? Each note is experienced as:
  - PRIMAL IMPRESSION: the note NOW
  - RETENTION: the just-past notes, still held in consciousness
  - PROTENTION: anticipation of notes about to come

  C -- D -- E -- F (melody)
  When we hear F:
  - F is primary impression (now)
  - E is retained (just-past, fading)
  - D is retained more faintly
  - G (or next note) is protended (anticipated)

  Consciousness is not a series of isolated "nows."
  It has a temporal thickness: a living present with
  retention of the immediate past and anticipation of the
  immediate future.

  This gives temporal FLOW and UNITY of experience.
  NOT a reconstruction after the fact.
  A structure of living present.
```

## Merleau-Ponty: Embodied Phenomenology

```
MERLEAU-PONTY: THE PHENOMENOLOGY OF PERCEPTION (1945)
=======================================================

CORE CLAIM: The body is not an object that the mind inhabits.
The body is the SUBJECT of experience.

BODY-OBJECT vs. BODY-SUBJECT:
  Body-object: the body as thing-in-the-world, studied from outside.
               My doctor's view of my body.

  Body-subject: the body as the lived basis of experience.
                My body as I experience it from the inside.
                "I can" (pouvoir) rather than "I have a body."

MOTOR INTENTIONALITY:
  The body has its own intentionality prior to conscious thought.
  A skilled pianist doesn't think "now move this finger."
  The body "knows" how to play; it has a pre-reflective skill.
  Skills are embodied -- stored in the body, not in a Cartesian mind.

  Heidegger's "ready-to-hand": using a hammer without attending to it.
  When the hammer breaks (present-at-hand), we attend to it.
  Normal tool use is pre-reflective, embodied.
  Consciousness only becomes focal when things break down.

PHANTOM LIMB PHENOMENON:
  Amputees experience sensations in missing limbs.
  Why? Merleau-Ponty: the BODY SCHEMA (motor/sensory self-representation)
  persists after the anatomical limb is gone.
  Body schema ≠ body image (conscious representation of one's body).
  Body schema is pre-reflective, embodied, operative.
  Phantom limb is the body schema not updated to the new anatomy.

IMPLICATIONS FOR AI:
  Cognition, for Merleau-Ponty, is grounded in embodied
  sensorimotor engagement with the world.
  A system without a body has no lived experience in this sense.
  LLMs have no body schema, no motor intentionality, no lived world.
  Whether this matters for their cognition is the Dreyfus critique
  (see 08-EMBODIED-COGNITION.md).
```

## Neural Correlates of Consciousness

```
NEURAL CORRELATES OF CONSCIOUSNESS (NCC)
==========================================

OPERATIONAL APPROACH (Crick & Koch):
  Find the minimal neural mechanisms sufficient for any experience.
  Correlate neural activity with reported phenomenal states.

RESEARCH PARADIGMS:
  Binocular rivalry: two different images to two eyes.
  Brain alternates between images every few seconds.
  When you see image A: certain neural pattern.
  When you see image B: different neural pattern.
  Phenomenal content changes without physical input changing.
  NCC = the changing neural pattern.

GLOBAL WORKSPACE THEORY (Dehaene/Baars):
  Consciousness = information broadcast to a "global workspace"
  accessible to multiple cognitive systems.
  Unconscious processing: activity in specialized areas without broadcast.
  Conscious processing: ignition -- widespread cortical activation.
  This explains why we're not conscious of most brain activity.

INTEGRATED INFORMATION THEORY (Tononi):
  Consciousness = phi (PHI), a measure of integrated information.
  System has phi > 0 iff: the system as a whole contains more
  information than the sum of its parts.
  High phi = high consciousness.
  Predicts: cerebellar cortex has low phi (many neurons, low integration)
  despite neuron count. Consistent with patient observations?
  (See 09-AI-CONSCIOUSNESS.md for IIT implications for AI.)

HIGHER-ORDER THEORIES (HOT):
  Consciousness requires a higher-order state representing the first-order state.
  Being conscious of seeing red = having a thought about seeing red.
  "I see red" is a conscious state because I have a higher-order
  representation that I am in that state.
  Unconscious states: first-order states not accompanied by HOT.
```

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| If debating... | The key move is... |
|---|---|
| Whether Mary learns something new (knowledge argument) | Distinguish propositional knowledge from ability: Lewis/Nemirow say she gains the ability to recognize, imagine, and remember red — not a new proposition. Jackson himself eventually accepted this. |
| Whether inverted spectra are possible | Ask whether the spectrum inversion is functionally detectable. Functionalists say no: what makes a quale "red" IS its functional role, so undetectable inversion = no real inversion. Chalmers: the scenario is coherent because qualia have intrinsic properties beyond functional role. |
| Whether absent qualia refute functionalism | Grant Block's point: if the Nation of China has the right functional organization but no qualia, qualia are not functional states. The functionalist must deny that the thought experiment is coherent — i.e., deny that absent qualia are genuinely possible for a system with the right functional organization. |
| Whether LLMs have qualia | The hard problem applies: no behavioral or functional test settles this. IIT gives a tentative "low phi" verdict; GWT gives "some access consciousness analog, no phenomenal consciousness verdict." Neither is conclusive. |
| Whether phenomenology (Husserl) is relevant to AI | Husserl's intentionality is essential: consciousness is always consciousness *of* something — it has a directedness and a content structure. LLMs can be described as having intentional states (they process inputs that are *about* things), but whether the noetic act (the processing event) has phenomenal character is the hard problem again. |
| Whether embodiment is necessary for qualia | Merleau-Ponty: qualia emerge in the context of sensorimotor engagement — the redness of red is tied to the lived body's relationship to light sources, colored surfaces, and contexts of use. Disembodied systems might have functional analogs without the sensorimotor grounding that constitutes the phenomenal character. |

## Common Confusion Points

**"Mary's Room proves physicalism is false, full stop."**
The argument has several serious physicalist responses (ability hypothesis, phenomenal concepts strategy, old fact/new perspective). Jackson himself later *retracted* the anti-physicalist conclusion, adopting the ability hypothesis. The argument is influential and disputed, not conclusive.

**"Qualia are what colors look like."**
Qualia are the subjective experience of colors, not the colors themselves. The qualia of redness is what it's like to experience red, not the wavelength 700nm or even the functional representation of red. The distinction is between the object, the functional state, and the phenomenal character of the experience.

**"Phenomenology (Husserl/Merleau-Ponty) is the same as phenomenal consciousness (Chalmers)."**
Related but distinct projects. Chalmers uses "phenomenal consciousness" to refer to the what-it's-like aspect of experience (qualia, hard problem). Husserlian phenomenology is a broader method of investigating the structure of consciousness from the first-person perspective — including intentionality, time, intersubjectivity. They study overlapping territory with different methods.

**"Merleau-Ponty's body-subject is just the body-image (how we think about our body)."**
Body IMAGE = conscious, conceptual representation of one's body (this is what becomes distorted in body dysmorphic disorder).
Body SCHEMA = pre-reflective, operative sensorimotor self-representation (this is what persists in phantom limb syndrome despite absent body image of the limb).
They are empirically dissociable: body schema can be updated (tool use extends body schema in skilled users) independently of body image.

**"The inverted spectrum is empirically testable."**
No. By definition, inverted spectrum subjects behave identically and report identically to non-inverted subjects. Any test you could run is a behavioral or functional test. The scenario is precisely designed so that no behavioral test can distinguish it. This is why it's a philosophical argument about the independence of qualia from function, not an empirical prediction.
