# Elements and Compounds — The Universal Framing

"Even if aliens found this, they would understand it."

---

## The Principle

The periodic table is the only organizational system that any civilization discovering chemistry would independently derive. It is not cultural. It is not linguistic. It is structural.

The Two Jokers adopt this as their organizing framework:

- **Red Joker = Elements.** Individual puzzles. Each one isolates a single section, a single skill, a single insight. Atomic, indivisible.
- **Black Joker = Compounds.** Synthesis puzzles. Each one combines elements. The compound has properties neither element has alone. Water is not hydrogen. Water is not oxygen. Water is what happens when they bond.

---

## Red Joker — The Elements

### Puzzle numbering = Atomic numbers

Each puzzle is assigned an element. The atomic number IS the puzzle number. The element's properties describe the puzzle's character.

This means the puzzle count is not arbitrary. It is not "13 because there are 13 sections" or "30 because we had room." **The number of puzzles = however many elements the library earns.** We write puzzles until we run out of great ideas, then we see which element we've reached.

### Element ↔ Puzzle mapping (conceptual)

The mapping should feel natural — the element's real properties should resonate with the puzzle's character.

| Atomic # | Element | Properties | Puzzle character |
|----------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Hydrogen** | Simplest, most abundant, lightest | The first puzzle. The on-ramp. The simplest mechanism. |
| 2 | **Helium** | Noble, inert, rises | A puzzle that lifts you — light, playful, warm-up |
| 6 | **Carbon** | Bonds with everything, basis of life | A puzzle that connects to multiple sections |

| 47 | **Silver** | Reflective, precious, photosensitive | Arts/Photography puzzle |
| 79 | **Gold** | Incorruptible, rare, beautiful | The hardest, most rewarding puzzle |

### Difficulty curve follows the periodic table

- **Period 1 (H, He)**: Warm-ups. Simple mechanisms. Teach the solver how the book works.
- **Period 2 (Li → Ne)**: Core puzzles. One per section. The main experience.
- **Period 3 (Na → Ar)**: Companion puzzles. Deeper dives into sections with the best content.
- **Period 4+ (K → ...)**: Expert puzzles, physical interludes, challenge problems.

## Black Joker — The Compounds

### Puzzle numbering = Chemical formulas

Each Black Joker puzzle is a compound whose constituent elements are Red Joker puzzles. To solve the compound, you use knowledge/skills/answers from the element puzzles it contains.

### Example compounds

| Compound | Formula | Elements combined | Puzzle character |
|----------|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Water** | H₂O | Hydrogen (warm-up) + Oxygen (first wow) | The most fundamental synthesis — combining simplicity with power |
| **Salt** | NaCl | Sodium + Chlorine | Preservation — what endures when everything else decays |
| **Carbon dioxide** | CO₂ | Carbon + Oxygen | The exhale of combustion — what's left after the fire |
| **Steel** | Fe + C | Iron + Carbon | Civilization's backbone — the compound stronger than either element |
| **Bronze** | Cu + Sn | Copper + Tin | The first alloy — the first time combining was better than choosing |
| **Quartz** | SiO₂ | Silicon + Oxygen | The timekeeper — crystal structure that counts eons |
| **Ammonia** | NH₃ | Nitrogen + Hydrogen | The invisible essential — fertilizer, life's quiet enabler |

### The Grid as The Periodic Table

The Black Joker's opening page (The Grid — 52 blank cells) IS a periodic table. 13 rows × 4 columns. The solver doesn't know that at first. But once they understand the element framing from the Red Joker, the shape of The Grid resonates — "this IS a table of elements. The 52 archetypes are the library's elements."

### Compound puzzles use collected keywords

The 52 keywords from The Grid are the "atoms." The synthesis puzzles are "reactions" — combine keywords from different rows/columns to form new meaning. The compound's properties (stable? explosive? corrosive? essential?) describe what happens when those sections' knowledge combines.

## Why This Works

1. **Universal.** Any intelligence that discovers chemistry finds this table. No cultural assumptions.
2. **Scalable.** 26 puzzles or 118 puzzles — the framework holds. Add more elements as you write more puzzles.
3. **Natural difficulty curve.** Hydrogen is simple. Uranium is complex. The periodic table IS a difficulty gradient.
4. **Self-documenting.** "What puzzle is #14?" "Silicon." "What's Silicon about?" "Computing, obviously." The element IS the mnemonic.
5. **Ties to the encyclopedia.** The library literally has `periodic-table/` as a directory. The puzzle framework references the content it lives alongside.
6. **Beautiful.** A puzzle book organized like the periodic table. An encyclopedia organized like a deck of cards. Systems within systems.

## The Numbering Systems

### Red Joker: Atomic numbers
Puzzle numbers ARE atomic numbers. Non-sequential. Gaps signal the system.
`1 · 6 · 7 · 8 · 11 · 12 · 13 · 14 · 15 · 16 · 17 · 19 · 20 · 22 · 24 · 25 · 26 · 27 · 28 · 29 · 30 · 33 · 47 · 50 · 79 · 82`

### Black Joker: Molecular weights (rounded)
Puzzle numbers ARE molecular weights. Well-spaced. Instantly confirmable.
`17 · 18 · 40 · 41 · 44 · 58 · 60 · 80 · 96 · 97 · 98 · 100 · 101 · 106 · 120 · 136 · 143 · 151 · 160 · 198 · 224 · 239 · 267 · 310 · ...`

**18 IS water. 58 IS salt. 100 IS limestone.** A chemist sees the number and knows the compound. The puzzle number confirms the identification — the same way atomic number 26 confirms iron.

Both numbering systems are the FIRST number any chemist learns about a substance. Universal. Alien-readable.

Both books have exactly 26 puzzles. The symmetry is structural, not cosmetic.

## Skipped Numbers — The First Signal

Red Joker puzzles are NOT numbered 1 through 26. They carry their element's ACTUAL atomic number. The sequence reads something like:

**1 · 6 · 7 · 8 · 11 · 12 · 13 · 14 · 15 · 16 · 17 · 19 · 20 · 22 · 24 · 25 · 26 · 27 · 28 · 29 · 30 · 33 · 47 · 50 · 79 · 82**

A solver who knows chemistry sees atomic numbers immediately. A solver who doesn't will notice the gaps and wonder: "Why is this book numbered like this?" The numbering IS the first puzzle — figuring out the system. The periodic table on the inside cover (or in the encyclopedia's `periodic-table/` directory) is the Rosetta Stone.

**Why these 26?** Each element was chosen because its properties match the puzzle's character AND the element is significant to human civilization. These aren't obscure lanthanides. These are the elements that built the world: carbon, iron, copper, gold, silicon, tin, lead. The elements you'd want to know about if you were rebuilding.
