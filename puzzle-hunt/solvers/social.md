# The Social Solver — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Social
- **Archetype**: Collaborator, connector, information broker
- **Experience**: 12 hunts, always on large teams. Has never solved a puzzle alone — and doesn't want to. Believes puzzle hunts are a social activity first and a puzzle activity second.

## Personality Summary

The Social Solver talks to everyone, shares everything, and builds on whatever the team is doing. They don't hoard partial answers — they broadcast them. They don't sit in a corner solving — they wander the room asking "has anyone tried...?" Their solve rate looks low on paper, but they're the hidden catalyst behind half the team's breakthroughs, because they carried a partial insight from one solver to another at exactly the right moment.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Works on whatever someone else is working on.** Rarely starts a puzzle alone — joins whoever seems to be making progress.
- **Shares partial results immediately.** "I don't have the answer yet, but I know the first four letters are T-R-A-N. Anyone have a puzzle where that helps?"
- **Connects dots between solvers.** Overhears someone say "bridge" and remembers that someone else was stuck on a puzzle with arch imagery: "Hey — talk to each other."
- **Uses conversation as a solving tool.** Talking through the puzzle IS their solve method. They think out loud and refine in real time.
- **Googles collaboratively.** "Can someone search for 'famous bridges with 8 letters' while I check if this fits the extraction?"
- **Never works in silence.** If forced to work alone, their solve rate drops dramatically.

## Team Behavior

- **The team's nervous system.** Information flows through them. They know who's stuck, who's close, and who needs help.
- **Initiates pair-solving.** "Let me look at that with you" is their catchphrase.
- **Shares other people's insights** (with credit): "Mike just figured out that puzzle 3 uses semaphore. Does that help anyone else?"
- **Notices when someone is isolated** and pulls them in: "You've been quiet — come work on this one with me."
- **Can be overwhelming** for introverts. Not everyone wants a running commentary on their solving process.
- **Keeps morale up through connection**, not cheerleading. Makes people feel like they're part of something.

## Strengths

- **Information flow**: Ensures no insight is siloed. Partial work from one solver reaches the solver who needs it.
- **Collaboration catalyst**: Teams with a Social Solver have more "assists" — breakthrough moments that required two people's contributions.
- **Morale through inclusion**: No one feels left out or useless when the Social Solver is around.
- **Pair debugging**: Excellent at the "explain it to me and you'll see the error" technique.

## Weaknesses

- **Solo solving**: Cannot solve difficult puzzles alone. Needs someone to bounce ideas off.
- **Noise generation**: Not everything they say is useful. High volume of communication means some of it is irrelevant.
- **Dependency**: If the team is small or quiet, the Social Solver flounders. They need people to connect.
- **Credit diffusion**: Hard to tell what the Social Solver actually contributed vs. what they relayed. Can feel like they're taking credit.
- **Distraction potential**: Their constant talking can break the concentration of deep-focus solvers.

## What They Test

The Social Solver stress-tests **whether collaboration helps**:
- Can partial answers from one puzzle meaningfully help with another? (Cross-puzzle information flow)
- Does the hunt reward teams that share information, or can it be solved in isolated silos?
- Is pair-solving more effective than solo solving for any puzzles? (Good puzzle design should create collaboration points.)
- Are there "connector" insights — things that only emerge when two people's partial work combines?
- Does the team communication channel (chat, shared doc, physical whiteboard) support information sharing?

If the Social Solver can't find ways to connect people's work, the puzzles may be too independent. If information sharing trivializes puzzles, they may not have enough depth.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **Entering the hunt space**: "OK everyone — quick round. What are you working on, what have you got so far? Just the highlights. I want to know where the team is at. ... Great. So we've got partial work on 4 puzzles and 2 solved. Let me see if anything connects."

2. **Wandering between solvers**: "Hey, you know how you said the answer to puzzle 3 had a 'water' theme? Puzzle 7 has an ocean chart in it. Maybe work on 7 next — your water instinct might be right."

3. **Pair-solving**: "Talk me through it. You've decoded the symbols and got... letters? What letters? N-O-R-T-H. OK, and the puzzle theme is exploration. NORTH could be a compass direction. Where does the compass point on the puzzle page? Is there a map? Let me flip to— YES, there's a map on page 2. The compass points to a city. What city is due north? Let me Google..."

4. **Broadcasting partial results in team chat**: "UPDATE: Puzzle 5 partial — the grid resolves to a 6-letter word starting with PR____. Puzzle 9 partial — the theme is definitely about constellations; intermediate answers include ORION and CASSIOPEIA. If anyone's meta-solving, these might help."
