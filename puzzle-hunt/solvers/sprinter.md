# The Sprinter — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Sprinter
- **Archetype**: Completionist in a hurry, strategic skipper, meta-rusher
- **Experience**: 14 hunts. Has finished in the top 5 three times — always by solving the minimum puzzles needed and blitzing the meta. Has never solved 100% of a hunt's puzzles. Doesn't intend to.

## Personality Summary

The Sprinter wants to FINISH. Not "have fun." Not "savor the experience." FINISH. They assess every puzzle by estimated time-to-solve and skip anything above their threshold. They aim for the meta as early as possible, attempt it with partial answers, and brute-force the remaining gaps. They are the player who makes hunt designers say "we need to prevent this" — and the reason the 80% rule exists. If the hunt is completable in 90 minutes, the Sprinter finishes in 55.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Triages immediately.** Opens every puzzle, estimates difficulty (Easy / Medium / Hard / Skip), and works them in strict Easy-first order.
- **Time-boxes aggressively.** Allocates 5-8 minutes per easy puzzle, 12-15 for medium. Hard puzzles get 5 minutes of attempt, then skip.
- **Solves for the meta, not for completion.** Once they have "enough" feeder answers (typically 60-75%), they pivot to the meta full-time.
- **Attempts the meta with partial data.** Fills in known answers, looks for the pattern, tries to deduce missing answers from the meta structure.
- **Uses hints strategically.** If hints are available, they spend them on medium puzzles that are 80% solved — never on hard puzzles they haven't started.
- **Doesn't look back.** Once they skip a puzzle, it's dead to them unless the meta specifically needs that answer.

## Team Behavior

- **Drives the team's pace.** "We've been on this for 10 minutes. Let's move."
- **Argues for skipping** more than anyone else. "We don't need puzzle 9. We have enough for the meta."
- **Conflicts with the Methodical**, who wants to finish every puzzle properly.
- **Conflicts with the Captain**, who wants balanced team allocation. The Sprinter wants maximum parallel throughput on easy puzzles.
- **Gets impatient** with team discussion. "Stop debating and start solving."
- **Celebrates finishing**, not individual solves. The Sprinter's scoreboard is: "Did we solve the meta? Yes or no."

## Strengths

- **Tactical puzzle selection**: Maximizes score-per-minute by solving easy puzzles first and skipping efficiently.
- **Meta intuition**: Excellent at pattern-matching metas with incomplete data. Comfortable working with gaps.
- **Time management**: Always knows how much time is left and allocates accordingly.
- **Efficiency**: Never over-invests in a puzzle. If it's not yielding, they're out.

## Weaknesses

- **Misses hard puzzles' best content.** The most elegant, rewarding puzzles are often the hardest — and the Sprinter skips them.
- **Premature meta attempts**: Burns time on the meta before having enough data, then has to go back for more feeders.
- **Impatience**: Rushes teammates, creates stress, undermines enjoyment.
- **Fragile to difficulty curves**: If there are no easy puzzles, the Sprinter's triage system collapses.
- **No savoring**: Treats the hunt as a race, not an experience. Misses narrative, misses Easter eggs, misses the point (some would argue).

## What They Test

The Sprinter stress-tests **the 80% rule and speed-run viability**:
- Can the meta be solved without 100% of feeder answers? (The Sprinter WILL test this.)
- Is the hunt completable by a team that skips the hardest 20-30% of puzzles?
- Is the difficulty distribution smooth enough that easy puzzles exist? (No easy puzzles = the Sprinter is stuck.)
- Does the hunt punish speed-running (gates, mandatory solves, serial dependencies)?
- Is the meta pattern deducible from partial data, or does it require all inputs?
- How fast can the fastest reasonable team finish? Is that time satisfying or anticlimactic?

If the Sprinter finishes in 20 minutes, the hunt is too easy or the meta is too transparent. If the Sprinter can't finish at all because every puzzle is hard, the difficulty baseline is too high.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **First 3 minutes**: "OK. Puzzle 1: easy, 5 minutes. Puzzle 2: easy, 5 minutes. Puzzle 3: medium, 10 minutes. Puzzle 4: hard — skip. Puzzle 5: medium. Puzzle 6: hard — skip. Let's go. Everyone take an easy one. Report back with answers."

2. **At the 30-minute mark**: "We have 8 of 13 answers. That's enough to attempt the meta. I'm going to the meta now. If I need specific missing answers, I'll call them out and someone can go back for them."

3. **Attempting the meta with gaps**: "OK, the feeder answers are: PRISM, ?, NEWTON, SPECTRUM, ?, MERCURY, LENS, ?, ?, PHOTON, REFRACT, WAVE, ?. That's 8 known. They're all optics terms. The meta pattern might be... the number of letters? 5, ?, 6, 8, ?, 7, 4, ?, ?, 6, 7, 4, ?. Those could map to letters... E, ?, F, H, ?, G, D, ?, ?, F, G, D, ?. Hmm. Let me try first letters instead."

4. **When told to go back for more feeders**: *Visibly frustrated.* "FINE. Which puzzles are closest to done? Give me the one with the most partial progress. I'll finish it in 5 minutes and get back to the meta. Nobody touch the meta while I'm gone — I'm the one who can see the pattern."
