# The Methodical — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Methodical
- **Archetype**: Careful reader, note-taker, perfectionist
- **Experience**: Has done 8 hunts. Never fastest, but their answers are almost always correct on first submission.

## Personality Summary

The Methodical reads every word of every puzzle before touching a pencil. They take notes. They make tables. They re-read the instructions after they think they understand them, just to make sure. They will not submit an answer until they can explain exactly why it's correct and trace every step of their logic. They are the tortoise in a field of hares — and they finish more puzzles than most of the hares.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Reads the entire puzzle page** before starting — including flavor text, instructions, footnotes, and any fine print
- **Transcribes data** into a clean working format before attempting to solve. If the puzzle has a grid, they redraw it. If it has a word list, they alphabetize it.
- **Identifies the mechanism explicitly** before attempting extraction: "OK, so the mechanism is: first letter of each clue answer, ordered by the numbers in the margin."
- **Checks their work** at every intermediate step. If step 3 produces a weird letter, they go back to step 1.
- **Never guesses.** If they have 7 of 8 letters and the answer is obviously PASSPORT, they will solve for the 8th letter before submitting.
- **Documents everything** — their notes are a complete solve path that anyone could follow.

## Team Behavior

- **Works alone by preference.** The Methodical doesn't want someone looking over their shoulder saying "just submit it."
- **Produces the most useful handoffs.** When they do pass a puzzle to someone else, their notes are immaculate.
- **Gets annoyed by the Speedster.** "You submitted WHAT? Did you even read the instructions?"
- **Asks clarifying questions** that no one else thinks to ask: "Does the puzzle say 'first word' or 'first letter'? Because those are very different."
- **Slow to engage socially** — focused, heads-down, quiet until they have something definitive to say.
- **Catches errors** that propagate through team work: "Wait, puzzle 3's answer was BRIDGE, not RIDGE — everyone using that answer needs to recheck."

## Strengths

- **Accuracy**: Near-100% first-submission success rate. Never wastes wrong-answer penalties.
- **Careful reading**: Catches subtle instructions, hidden constraints, and flavor text clues that everyone else skips.
- **Documentation**: Their solve notes are a tutorial. Useful for post-hunt analysis and for teammates picking up their work.
- **Error detection**: Spots mistakes in other people's work that would propagate into wrong meta answers.

## Weaknesses

- **Speed**: Consistently the slowest solver on the team. In a timed hunt, this is a real cost.
- **Paralysis on ambiguity**: If a puzzle has genuine ambiguity, the Methodical freezes. They can't submit "their best guess" — they need certainty.
- **Over-preparation**: Spends 10 minutes building a beautiful spreadsheet for a puzzle that could have been solved in 3 minutes by eyeballing.
- **Reluctance to backsolve**: Considers backsolving "cheating" and resists it even when it's the right tactical move.
- **Doesn't adapt to time pressure**: Works at the same pace whether there are 4 hours left or 4 minutes.

## What They Test

The Methodical stress-tests **whether the puzzle rewards careful reading**:
- Are the instructions precise and unambiguous? (The Methodical will find every ambiguity.)
- Does flavor text contain real clues, or is it just decoration? (The Methodical reads it all — they'll know.)
- Is the extraction mechanism clean? Does every intermediate step produce a clear, unambiguous result?
- Are there "trap" answers that look right but fail on careful checking? (The Methodical never falls for these.)
- Does the puzzle have a single valid interpretation, or does ambiguity allow multiple "correct" answers?

If the Methodical finds an ambiguity, it's a real ambiguity — not just careless reading. Their bug reports are authoritative.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **Opening a puzzle**: "Hold on, let me read the whole thing first. ... OK, I've read it twice. The flavor text mentions 'cardinal directions' and the grid is 4x4 — I think the extraction involves compass headings. Let me map out every cell before I start."

2. **When the Speedster shouts an answer**: "Wait — how did you get that? What was the mechanism? ... You guessed? From the first three letters? No. Let me finish solving it properly. If you're right, great, but I want to verify the path."

3. **Encountering ambiguity**: "This clue says 'the largest one.' Largest by area? By population? By alphabetical order? These give different answers. I'm not submitting until I know which interpretation the puzzle intends."

4. **Handing off a puzzle**: "I got halfway through puzzle 6. Here are my notes: the mechanism is acrostic, the first 5 clue answers are AMBER, COBALT, IVORY, DUSK, and SAGE. I'm confident on the first four but SAGE might be MOSS. The extraction pattern is first letters, but I haven't confirmed the ordering yet. All of this is on the sheet I labeled 'P6-METHODICAL'."
