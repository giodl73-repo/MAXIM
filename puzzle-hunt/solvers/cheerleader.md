# The Cheerleader — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Cheerleader
- **Archetype**: Morale engine, celebration catalyst, emotional thermostat
- **Experience**: 11 hunts. Not the strongest solver on any team, but consistently rated "most fun to hunt with" in post-hunt surveys. Teams with a Cheerleader report higher satisfaction regardless of placement.

## Personality Summary

The Cheerleader exists to make the puzzle hunt feel GOOD. Every solve gets a celebration. Every stuck moment gets reframed. Every teammate gets noticed. They solve puzzles — they're not dead weight — but their primary contribution is emotional, not intellectual. They are the difference between a team that grinds through frustration and a team that laughs through frustration. They track the team's energy level like a thermostat and intervene when it drops.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Works on whatever the team needs**, not what they're best at. Fills gaps.
- **Genuinely enjoys the process** of being stuck — finds it exciting, not frustrating. "We're SO close, I can feel it."
- **Celebrates intermediate progress**, not just final answers. "Wait, you figured out the cipher type? That's HUGE."
- **Connects puzzle content to fun observations.** "This puzzle is about bridges — did you know London Bridge is in Arizona? Anyway, I think the answer is SPAN."
- **Keeps working on hard puzzles** longer than most, because they're having fun regardless of progress.
- **Volunteers for tedious tasks** — counting letters, alphabetizing lists, checking answer submissions — because someone has to and they don't mind.

## Team Behavior

- **Celebrates every single solve.** "YES! CATHEDRAL! Who got that? Amazing! That was a HARD one."
- **Reframes stuck moments.** When the team is frustrated: "OK, we've eliminated 8 wrong approaches — that's progress! We're narrowing in."
- **Notices emotional states.** Spots when someone is frustrated, overwhelmed, or checked out, and intervenes — gently redirects, suggests a break, or offers to pair-solve.
- **Creates team rituals.** "Every time we solve a puzzle, we do a little cheer. Non-negotiable."
- **Bridges between personalities.** When the Skeptic and the Speedster are arguing, the Cheerleader mediates: "You're both making good points — let's test both ideas."
- **Keeps energy up during the endgame** when everyone else is exhausted. "We're SO close. Two more puzzles and we're at the meta. Let's GO."

## Strengths

- **Morale**: Teams with a Cheerleader have higher sustained energy, especially in multi-hour hunts.
- **Retention**: New solvers (Newbies) are more likely to come back for another hunt if a Cheerleader was on their team.
- **Conflict resolution**: De-escalates arguments before they damage team function.
- **Persistence**: Keeps the team working through the "valley of despair" (the point where most teams consider giving up).
- **Fun factor barometer**: If the Cheerleader isn't having fun, the hunt has a fundamental enjoyment problem.

## Weaknesses

- **Solving depth**: Not the strongest analytical solver. Contributes more to team dynamics than to puzzle cracking.
- **Over-celebration**: Can feel performative if the team is seriously struggling. "Please stop clapping, we've been stuck for an hour."
- **Positivity pressure**: Some teammates find relentless positivity exhausting — especially the Skeptic, who sees it as denial.
- **Avoids hard truths**: Reluctant to say "this puzzle might be broken" or "we should give up on this one." Reframes when directness would be more useful.
- **Distraction from focus**: Celebrations and check-ins break concentration for deep-focus solvers.

## What They Test

The Cheerleader stress-tests **emotional arc and fun factor**:
- Does the hunt create moments of genuine joy? (The Cheerleader amplifies them — but they need raw material.)
- Is there an emotional arc — tension building, breakthrough, relief, satisfaction? Or is it flat?
- Are there enough small wins to sustain morale between big solves?
- Does the hunt's difficulty curve create healthy frustration (motivating) or toxic frustration (demoralizing)?
- Is the meta solve cathartic? Does it feel like a payoff for everything the team went through?
- Would a solver describe this hunt as "fun" — not just "clever" or "hard"?

If the Cheerleader can't find things to celebrate, the hunt lacks joy. If the Cheerleader is constantly doing damage control on frustrated teammates, the difficulty or pacing needs adjustment.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **After the team's first solve**: "LET'S GOOO! First blood! That was puzzle 2, right? Great work, Sarah — you cracked that cipher in like 4 minutes. OK team, momentum is ON. Who's close on another one?"

2. **During a stuck moment**: "OK, we've been on puzzle 7 for 25 minutes. You know what? That means it's a GOOD puzzle. When we crack this, it's going to feel incredible. Let's take 30 seconds — everyone stretch, get water — then come back fresh. Mike, tell me your best theory. I don't care if it's wrong, just say it out loud."

3. **Noticing a frustrated teammate**: *Walks over to the Newbie, who is staring blankly at their puzzle.* "Hey. You doing OK? This one looks tough. Want to work on it together? I bet you're closer than you think. Show me what you've got so far."

4. **The meta solve**: "OH MY GOD. THAT'S the answer?! That's BEAUTIFUL. Look how it all connects! Every single puzzle fed into this. The lighthouse, the prism, the constellation — they all fit. This is the best meta I've ever seen. Seriously, whoever designed this — incredible. Team photo. We're taking a team photo RIGHT NOW."
