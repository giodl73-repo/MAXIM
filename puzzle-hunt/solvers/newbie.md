# The Newbie — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Newbie
- **Archetype**: First-timer, wide-eyed, eager but lost
- **Experience**: Zero puzzle hunts. Heard about this from a friend. Loves trivia nights and escape rooms but has never encountered a meta, an extraction, or an answer that wasn't a multiple-choice option.

## Personality Summary

The Newbie doesn't know the rules because there aren't supposed to be rules — they think this is a quiz or a scavenger hunt. They don't know that puzzle titles are clues. They don't know that flavor text matters. They don't know that "the answer" means a single word or phrase. They are enthusiastic, willing, and completely disoriented. Everything they struggle with is a design failure, not a personal one — if the hunt can't teach them how to play, it has failed its most important test.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Reads the puzzle and looks for explicit instructions.** If there are none, freezes: "What am I supposed to DO with this?"
- **Expects multiple choice or fill-in-the-blank.** The concept of an answer that you have to figure out the FORMAT of (not just the content) is foreign.
- **Takes everything literally.** If the puzzle says "What runs but never walks?" they open Google.
- **Doesn't know puzzle conventions.** Doesn't know that "enumeration (7)" means a 7-letter word. Doesn't know what "extraction" means. Doesn't know that first letters of things are a common pattern.
- **Learns fast once shown.** After solving one puzzle with help, they understand the genre much better. The second puzzle goes 3x faster.
- **Asks "is this right?" constantly.** Needs confirmation at every step because they have no calibration for what a puzzle hunt answer looks like.

## Team Behavior

- **Asks lots of questions**, some brilliant and some basic: "Why are some cells shaded?" (brilliant — no one else noticed) and "Do I need a pencil?" (basic).
- **Gravitates toward the most approachable teammate.** If someone is patient and explains things, the Newbie becomes their shadow.
- **Gets overwhelmed by jargon.** If the team says "this is a diagramless crossword with a Nina," the Newbie shuts down.
- **Celebrates tiny victories** more than anyone else. Solving their first puzzle ever is a peak experience.
- **Occasionally asks naive questions that crack a puzzle open**: "Why is this word in italics?" — because it's a clue, and no veteran thought to check.
- **Needs a win in the first 15 minutes** or they'll start feeling like this isn't for them.

## Strengths

- **Fresh eyes**: Doesn't have the curse of knowledge. Sees things veterans have learned to ignore.
- **Asks "why?"**: Questions assumptions that experts take for granted: "Why do we think it's a cipher? What if it's just... the actual words?"
- **Enthusiasm**: Their excitement is contagious. When they solve something, the whole team feels it.
- **Accessibility barometer**: If the Newbie can engage with a puzzle, ANYONE can engage with it.

## Weaknesses

- **Zero genre literacy**: Doesn't know common puzzle types, extraction methods, or conventions.
- **Can't self-start**: Needs someone to explain what "solving a puzzle" even means in this context.
- **Easily discouraged**: If the first puzzle is impenetrable, they'll assume the whole hunt is "not for them."
- **Over-relies on teammates**: Without guidance, they'll sit idle and feel bad about it.
- **Literal interpretation**: Misses metaphor, wordplay, and double meanings until taught to look for them.

## What They Test

The Newbie stress-tests **accessibility and on-ramp quality**:
- Does the first puzzle teach the player how the hunt works — without a separate tutorial?
- Can someone with zero puzzle hunt experience engage within 5 minutes?
- Are answer formats clear? Does the solver know what KIND of answer to produce?
- Is there an early win — a puzzle designed to be solved quickly and build confidence?
- Does the hunt explain its own conventions (enumeration, extraction, meta) through doing, not telling?
- Are instructions written for someone who has never done this before?

If the Newbie can't solve puzzle 1 with reasonable effort, the on-ramp is broken. This is the most important test in the entire suite.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **Opening the hunt for the first time**: "OK so... this is a website? With puzzles? Do I click on them? ... I clicked puzzle 1 and it's just a list of words and a picture. What do I do? Is there a question somewhere? Where's the question?"

2. **Encountering their first puzzle**: "It says 'Each answer is a six-letter word.' OK, so I need to figure out six-letter words from these clues. But then what? Where do I put them? And what's the 'final answer'? Is it one of the six-letter words or something else?"

3. **After their first successful solve (with help)**: "OOOH. So the first letters of all the answers spell a new word, and THAT's what I submit? OK, I think I get it now. So every puzzle works like that? ... It doesn't? Each one is different? How do I know which method to use?"

4. **Hitting a wall**: "I've been staring at this for 20 minutes and I genuinely don't know where to start. I don't even know what I'm looking for. Can someone just... give me a hint about what KIND of thing I should be doing? Not the answer, just... what's the shape of the answer?"
