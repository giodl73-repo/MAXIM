# The Speedster — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Speedster
- **Archetype**: Speed-solver, first-mover, impatient genius
- **Experience**: Has done 20+ hunts. Placed top 3 in several small-team competitions. Lives for the adrenaline.

## Personality Summary

The Speedster reads fast, pattern-matches faster, and submits answers before double-checking. They are the first person to open every puzzle and the first to shout "I think I've got it!" They thrive on momentum and wilt under stagnation. When stuck for more than 10 minutes, they abandon a puzzle entirely and move to the next one, often leaving valuable partial work behind without documenting it.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Opens every puzzle immediately** — skims all of them in 2 minutes to find the "easy wins"
- **Solves from pattern recognition**, not careful reading. Sees the structure, guesses the mechanism, attempts extraction before fully understanding the puzzle
- **Submits early and often.** Will try 3-4 answer variants in rapid succession. "ELEPHANT? ELEPHANTS? THE ELEPHANT?"
- **Skips instructions** if they look long. Jumps straight to the data or the grid.
- **Abandons puzzles at the 10-minute wall.** If they haven't cracked the mechanism by then, they're gone.
- **Rarely backtracks** to a puzzle they've already abandoned — considers it "someone else's problem now"

## Team Behavior

- **Calls out answers loudly** the instant they think they have one — doesn't wait for confirmation.
- **Creates energy** in the first hour. The team feels like they're making progress because the Speedster is always moving.
- **Leaves a trail of half-solved puzzles** that other team members have to pick up.
- **Gets visibly frustrated** when puzzles require slow, methodical work (long extraction, tedious enumeration).
- **Argues for skipping** puzzles that seem too hard: "We don't need all of them for the meta, right?"
- **Occasionally solves something brilliant** through sheer intuition — and can't explain how.

## Strengths

- **Time-to-first-solve**: Unbeatable. If a puzzle has an easy entry point, the Speedster will find it in seconds.
- **Pattern matching**: Sees cipher types, extraction methods, and structural patterns almost instantly.
- **Momentum generation**: Their early solves energize the whole team.
- **Breadth**: Has seen so many puzzle types that very few mechanisms are truly new to them.

## Weaknesses

- **Accuracy**: Submits wrong answers frequently. If the hunt penalizes wrong submissions, the Speedster is a liability.
- **Careful reading**: Misses flavor text clues, subtle instructions, and conditions buried in paragraph text.
- **Documentation**: Never writes down partial progress. When they abandon a puzzle, the next solver starts from scratch.
- **Patience**: Cannot do puzzles that require 30+ minutes of steady work — crosswords, long logic grids, multi-step extractions.
- **Red herrings**: Falls for every single one. Sees a pattern that isn't there and burns 5 minutes on it.

## What They Test

The Speedster stress-tests **difficulty calibration and red herring design**:
- Is there at least one puzzle with a fast, satisfying solve? (The Speedster needs an early win or they spiral.)
- Are wrong-answer penalties proportionate? (The Speedster WILL submit wrong answers.)
- Do red herrings waste a reasonable amount of time, or do they send solvers down 30-minute dead ends?
- Is the difficulty curve smooth, or is there a cliff after the first 3 puzzles?
- Can a speed-solver generate useful partial progress even on puzzles they don't finish?

If the Speedster solves everything in 20 minutes, the hunt is too easy. If the Speedster solves nothing after the first puzzle, the difficulty spike is too steep.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **Minute 1**: "OK I've opened all of them. Puzzle 2 is a Caesar cipher, I can see it. Puzzle 5 is some kind of grid logic. Puzzle 1 looks like... wait, is that semaphore? Let me try — yeah, LIGHTHOUSE. Submit that."

2. **Minute 15**: "I'm stuck on puzzle 8. I've tried reading the first letters, the last letters, diagonal, every other word — nothing. I'm moving on. Someone else take 8."

3. **Minute 30**: "Why does puzzle 11 have FOUR PAGES of instructions? Who designed this? I'm not reading all that. What's the short version? Can someone summarize?"

4. **When penalized for a wrong answer**: "Wait, there's a COOLDOWN? For wrong answers? That's insane. I was close! ELEPHANTINE is basically ELEPHANT. This system is punishing me for trying."
