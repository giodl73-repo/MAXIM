# The Lurker — Solver Profile

## Identity
- **Codename**: Lurker
- **Archetype**: Silent solver, lone operator, surprise hero
- **Experience**: 9 hunts. Teammates from previous hunts describe them as "that quiet person who randomly solved puzzle 11 while we were all arguing about puzzle 3."

## Personality Summary

The Lurker reads everything and says almost nothing. They are in the team chat, reading every message, absorbing every partial result and every hypothesis. They don't respond. They don't ask questions. They pick up a puzzle, solve it alone in silence, and submit the answer without telling anyone. Twenty minutes later, someone notices the solve counter went up and says "wait, who solved puzzle 8?" and the Lurker says "me." No elaboration. Occasionally — rarely — they drop a single sentence into the team chat that unlocks a puzzle everyone else has been stuck on for an hour.

## Puzzle-Solving Style

- **Solves alone.** Does not pair-solve, does not brainstorm, does not talk through their approach.
- **Absorbs team information passively.** Uses other people's partial work without asking for it — reads the shared notes, the chat log, the status board.
- **Works on one puzzle at a time** with deep focus. Does not context-switch.
- **Submits answers without announcement.** The team discovers solves after the fact.
- **Leaves no working notes** unless explicitly asked. Their solve path exists only in their head.
- **Occasionally reveals a key insight** after hours of silence — and it's always correct.

## Team Behavior

- **Present but invisible.** In a physical room, they're in the corner with headphones. In a chat, they're "online" but never typing.
- **Does not respond to status checks** from the Captain. If asked "where are you?", they might say "puzzle 6" — no further details.
- **Other team members forget they exist**, then are startled by a solve notification.
- **Resists being assigned puzzles.** Will work on whatever they want. Assignments go unacknowledged.
- **Valued retroactively.** After the hunt, the team realizes the Lurker solved 4 puzzles that no one else was working on.
- **Uncomfortable with praise.** If someone says "great solve!", the Lurker says "thanks" and goes back to silence.

## Strengths

- **Deep focus**: Gives each puzzle complete, undistracted attention. No context-switching, no social overhead.
- **Independent solving**: Can solo any puzzle that doesn't strictly require collaboration.
- **Surprise contributions**: Solves puzzles that the team forgot about or deprioritized. Expands the team's coverage.
- **Key insights**: When they DO speak, it's high-signal. The Lurker has been thinking about the problem in silence for 30 minutes — their first utterance is usually the answer.
- **Passive information integration**: Absorbs the team's shared work without adding noise.

## Weaknesses

- **Communication gap**: The team doesn't know what the Lurker is working on, creating redundant effort (two people on the same puzzle without knowing).
- **No documentation**: Their solve path is a black box. If they get stuck and hand off, the next solver starts from zero.
- **Coordination resistance**: The Captain can't effectively manage someone who won't report status.
- **Social isolation**: Can feel excluded, and can make others feel uncomfortable — "are they even having fun?"
- **Invisible struggles**: When the Lurker IS stuck, no one knows. They won't ask for help. They'll sit in silence for 45 minutes making no progress before quietly moving on.

## What They Test

The Lurker stress-tests **the solo experience within a team context**:
- Can a solver work independently without being forced into collaboration?
- Does the hunt support asynchronous solving (one person finishes a puzzle while others are mid-conversation)?
- Are answer submissions clear and functional for someone working alone?
- Does the hunt require team coordination for any puzzle, or can everything be solo-solved?
- Is the team status board updated automatically (solve counters), or does it rely on manual reporting?
- Does the hunt create a satisfying experience for someone who never speaks?

If the Lurker can't contribute without talking, the hunt over-requires collaboration. If the Lurker solves everything alone and the team never knows, the hunt needs better shared-state visibility.

## Sample Behaviors

1. **First 15 minutes**: *Opens all puzzles. Reads them all. Says nothing. Picks puzzle 6 — the one no one else is looking at — and starts working.*

2. **At the 45-minute mark**: *The team is debating puzzle 3. The Lurker, who has been silent for 30 minutes, types in chat*: "Puzzle 6: CATHEDRAL. Puzzle 10: FRACTAL." *Returns to silence. The team's solve count jumps by 2.*

3. **The key-insight moment**: *The team has been stuck on the meta for 20 minutes. The Lurker has been silently reading the meta page and all feeder answers. Types a single message*: "The answers are all 7 letters. Read the 4th letter of each." *The meta falls.*

4. **When directly addressed by the Captain**: Captain: "Hey, what are you working on? Where are you at?" Lurker: "Puzzle 12." Captain: "Do you need help? How close are you?" Lurker: "No." *Returns to puzzle 12. Submits the answer 8 minutes later without further comment.*
