# Design Principles — The Two Jokers

The rules that survived three rounds of expert review, one blind asset audit, and the first test failures. Each principle has a name, a source, and a test.

---

## The Riven Standard

*Source: Rand Miller (Round 3), confirmed by Oxygen test (26.3/30)*

> "The puzzle IS what the section does, not an obstacle overlaid on it."

The Star Chart puzzle IS astronomy — identifying objects, plotting coordinates, reading the sky. The Codon puzzle IS molecular biology. The Logic Grid IS political science reasoning. If you could remove the encyclopedia and the puzzle would still work, the puzzle is wrong.

**Test:** Could a practitioner of this field recognize their own work in the puzzle?

---

## Solving = Proving Understanding

*Source: Jonathan Blow (profile research)*

> "Solving the puzzle is showing the game that you understood what the puzzle was about."

The solver doesn't just find the answer — they DEMONSTRATE comprehension of the material. The answer is proof of engagement. If the solver can reach the answer without understanding the content, the puzzle has zero information content.

**Test:** Does the solver know more about the section AFTER solving than before?

---

## The Dinner Party Test

*Source: Mike Selinker (Round 2)*

> "Would you tell someone about this puzzle at dinner?"

Not every puzzle needs to be a showstopper. But at least 5 of 26 per book should be the kind of thing where the solver says "you will not believe what I just did." The Codon puzzle ("the answer is written in DNA"), the Star Chart ("I drew the answer in the sky"), the Anamorphic Drawing ("I tilted the book and the word appeared").

**Test:** In one sentence, does the puzzle make a non-puzzler say "wait, really?"

---

## The Book Test

*Source: Dana Young (Round 2)*

> "Can you solve it sitting in a chair with only the book and a pencil?"

The Red Joker is a book. Not a kit. Not a craft project. Scissors, flashlights, and pins are Black Joker territory. Red Joker puzzles can ask you to write, draw, trace, tilt, and fold — but not cut, build, or assemble.

**Test:** Does it require any tool besides a pencil and the book itself?

---

## Blame the Player

*Source: Rand Miller (profile research)*

> "Once you understand the solution and look back, you should blame yourself for not finding it — not blame us."

If a solver says "how was I supposed to know that?" — the puzzle failed. If they say "I should have seen that" — it succeeded. Every clue must be fair in retrospect.

**Test:** After revealing the answer, does the solver feel respect or resentment?

---

## The 80% Rule

*Source: Dan Katz (Round 1, Round 3)*

> "A good metapuzzle should be solvable with any 80% of the answers."

The Red Joker meta crossword should be crackable with 10-11 of 13 Tier 1 answers. The Black Joker should be attemptable with 40 of 52 Grid entries. Rigid systems that require 100% completion punish the solver for the hardest puzzles.

**Test:** What happens if the solver skips the two hardest puzzles?

---

## Reading Reward ≥ 4

*Source: Peter Sarrett (Round 2)*

> "Does the puzzle make you a better reader of the encyclopedia?"

Scored 1-5. A puzzle at 1 can be solved without reading the encyclopedia at all. At 3, you need to skim for keywords. At 5, you must genuinely understand the material. Every puzzle should score ≥ 4.

**Test:** Can the solver solve it by Ctrl+F alone? If yes, Reading Reward < 4.

---

## No Over-Scaffolding

*Source: Carbon test failure (20/30 REVISE)*

> "A worksheet that does all the work is not a puzzle. It's a coloring book."

The Carbon puzzle handed the solver a complete codon table, pre-grouped codons, step-by-step instructions, and a predictable answer. Three testers called it "tutorial, not a puzzle." Worksheets should provide SPACE to work, not INSTRUCTIONS for how to work.

**Test:** If you removed the worksheet, would the solver still know what to do?

---

## Surprise the Answer

*Source: Carbon test failure — "GENETIC is the most predictable word for a DNA puzzle"*

> "The answer word should make the solver pause, not nod."

If the puzzle is about DNA and the answer is GENETIC, there's no surprise. If the puzzle is about DNA and the answer is ALCHEMY, the solver's reaction shifts from "obviously" to "wait — alchemy is written in DNA?" The surprise IS the reward.

**Test:** Could the solver guess the answer from the puzzle's topic alone?

---

## One Aha, Not Zero, Not Three

*Source: Wei-Hwa Huang (Round 1)*

> "No ahas is dull, one aha is fun, two ahas is a stretch, three ahas is a slog."

The aha is the moment the solver sees the mechanism. After that, extraction should be mechanical (but not mindless — see No Over-Scaffolding). Puzzles with zero ahas are lookup tables. Puzzles with three ahas are chains of unrelated insights.

**Test:** Can you name the single aha moment? If you need a list, there are too many.

---

## The Periodic Table Is Not Decoration

*Source: Mark Gottlieb (Round 3), Dan Katz (Round 3 — A+)*

> "The numbering system is the single best structural idea in the entire project."

Puzzle numbers ARE atomic numbers (Red) and molecular weights (Black). The gaps are the first puzzle. The overlap at 17 is a cross-book discovery. If the periodic table framing were removed and the puzzles still worked identically, the framing is decoration. It should be structural.

**Test:** Does knowing the element/compound name help you solve or understand the puzzle?

---

## Discovered, Never Explained

*Source: Blind asset review (8/12 on Card Identity System)*

> "The suit-element mapping, the tarot correspondences, the archetype system — let the solver find them. Never explain."

The deepest layers of the hunt (suit = element, rank = tarot archetype, The Fool = Card 0) are for the solver who goes looking. Not every solver will find them. That's fine. The hunt works without them. But the solver who discovers them feels like they've found a secret room.

**Test:** Would explaining this layer IMPROVE or DIMINISH the experience?

---

## Three Deduction Tools, Not a Scavenger Hunt

*Source: Lucas Pope (Obra Dinn), Wei-Hwa Huang (Round 3 — "deductively thin")*

> "The Grid must be solvable through deduction, not just searching."

The Black Joker's Grid gives the solver three reference systems: Card Backs (fingerprints — WHO), Concept Index (connections — WHERE), Bill of Materials (contents — WHAT). Multiple overlapping clue systems mean multiple paths to each identification. The solver who uses all three can DEDUCE which archetype hides where. The solver who just greps will miss the experience.

**Test:** Can a solver identify at least 10 archetypes through deduction alone, without brute-force searching?

---

## No Deliberate Errors

*Source: User (absolute rule)*

> "The encyclopedia is a source of truth. The puzzle books do not contradict it."

Every fact in every puzzle is correct. Every quote is real. Every chemical formula is verified. Every historical relationship is documented. The puzzle may be TRICKY but never WRONG. A solver who fact-checks any clue should find it confirmed.

**Test:** Is every factual claim in the puzzle verifiable in the encyclopedia or a standard reference?

---

## The Voice Rules (Joker)

*Source: JOKER-VOICE.md*

1. No exclamation marks.
2. No questions.
3. Short sentences (rarely > 15 words).
4. Present tense.
5. No meta-commentary ("this puzzle is about...").
6. The solver is "you."

**Test:** Read the Joker intro aloud. Does it sound like a person standing next to you in a quiet library?

---

## Principles in Priority Order

When principles conflict, higher beats lower:

1. **No Deliberate Errors** — non-negotiable
2. **Blame the Player** — fairness above all
3. **The Riven Standard** — the puzzle IS the field
4. **Solving = Proving Understanding** — the answer is proof
5. **No Over-Scaffolding** — trust the solver
6. **Surprise the Answer** — the word matters
7. **Reading Reward ≥ 4** — engagement required
8. **One Aha** — clean mechanism
9. **The Book Test** — it's a book, not a kit
10. **The Dinner Party Test** — some puzzles should be legendary
11. **The 80% Rule** — the meta is robust
12. **Discovered, Never Explained** — hidden depth rewards curiosity
13. **The Periodic Table Is Not Decoration** — the framing is structural
14. **Three Deduction Tools** — the Grid is deduction, not search
15. **The Voice Rules** — the Joker is consistent
