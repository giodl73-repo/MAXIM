# Dan Katz — Round 2 Review: Puzzle Pool Ranking

**Lens:** Structure & Pacing (puzzle selection for the full Red Joker lineup + Black Joker moments)
**Date:** 2026-02-26
**Input:** PUZZLE-POOL.md (89 briefs), V3-CHANGES.md, TWO-JOKERS.md
**Task:** Rank candidates per section, pick the Dream 13, flag eliminations.

---

## Preamble

V3 addressed most of my Round 1 structural complaints. The crossword meta is the right call — it passes the 80% rule (solve with 10-11 of 13), enables backsolving via crossing constraints, and provides built-in confirmation. The freed answer words are stronger across the board. The card-archetype integration is no longer wallpaper — it is load-bearing in the meta grid. Good.

What V3 did NOT do is fix the mechanism monoculture I flagged in Round 1. The pool gives us the raw material to fix it. The question is whether the panel picks for variety or picks for comfort. I am picking for variety.

**My selection criteria, in order:**
1. Does this puzzle produce a *qualitatively different* solve experience from its neighbors?
2. Does solving require engaging with the section's content, not just scanning for hidden signals?
3. Is the mechanism genre-accessible (solvable by a puzzle-naive reader who reads carefully)?
4. Is the extraction clean (one aha, then mechanical)?
5. Is it fun? Would I tell someone about it afterward?

2-4 (Birdsong as Morse) is clever — spectrograms where long notes = dashes and short notes = dots — but it is another Morse puzzle. If 10-1 already includes Morse as one of its ten encoding systems, a standalone Morse puzzle elsewhere in the hunt is redundant.

### Top 5 Picks

**1. X1 -- Paper + Light (Raiders of the Lost Ark)**

This is the legendary moment. Build a 3D polyhedron from a cut-out template, shine a flashlight through it, shadows spell the answer. This is the single most memorable puzzle concept in the entire pool. It requires physical construction, it requires light, and it produces a result that feels like magic. This is Black Joker meta territory — the culminating "WOW" moment.

**Risk:** High execution difficulty. The polyhedron geometry must be precise enough that imperfect cuts still produce readable shadows. Test this with paper prototypes before committing.

**2. X8 -- Punch Card Overlay**

Punch holes at marked positions, overlay on a text-heavy page, visible words are the answer. This is a physical puzzle with a clean mechanism and a satisfying payoff. The text page can come from any section of the encyclopedia, making it a true cross-section tool. And the act of punching holes in a book — DESTROYING a page to solve a puzzle — is a transgressive moment that solvers will remember.

**Why it ranks high:** The punch card is a computing concept. The overlay is a physical act. The text underneath is encyclopedia content. Three layers, zero ambiguity in the mechanism.

**3. X2 -- Color Overlay**

Hold two pages together up to light, the overlap reveals a hidden word. Simpler than X1 but more reliable — no cutting, no assembly, just alignment and light. This works well as a mid-Black-Joker puzzle: a moment of physical discovery that does not require tools.

**4. X15 -- Phenakistoscope / Zoetrope**

Cut out a disc, spin it, the animation reveals a word invisible when stationary. This is the deepest connection to the encyclopedia's content — it IS a lesson in persistence of vision from `cinema-film/` and `optics/`. The word only exists in motion. This is the kind of puzzle that makes people understand why the encyclopedia exists: knowledge is not just information, it is *capability*.

**Risk:** Requires precise printing and cutting. Animation quality degrades with imprecise construction.

**5. X3 -- Fold the Page**

Fold the page in half — distant elements meet and form a word. This is the simplest physical puzzle in the pool, and that simplicity is its strength. No tools, no light, no cutting. Just fold. The aha of seeing scattered letters align is instant and gratifying. This works as an early Black Joker puzzle — the moment where the solver realizes the book is not just a book.

### Honorable Mentions

- **X10 (Tangram Letter Assembly):** Strong concept, but cutting tangram pieces is fiddly and letter shapes from tangrams are often ambiguous.
- **X9 (Mirror Puzzle):** Leonardo da Vinci mirror-writing is thematically rich, but "find a mirror" is a barrier. Keep as a bonus, not a required puzzle.
- **X4 (Golden Ratio Spiral):** Beautiful concept for Black Joker "Spectrum" puzzle — one phenomenon (phi) across four sections. But it is a concept, not a mechanism. The brief does not explain how phi "decrypts a final message."

### Bottom of the Pool

- **X5 (Fibonacci Structural Sequence):** Not a puzzle. A meta-structural principle. Interesting as an Easter egg but should not take a slot.
- **X12 (Five Senses Quintet):** Not a puzzle. An organizing principle. Same issue.
- **X16 (Sundial):** Requires sunlight. No.
- **X17 (Stereo Pair):** Requires crossing your eyes or a stereoscope. Too many solvers physically cannot do this.
- **X14 (Mountain Range Frequency):** Overengineered — elevation profiles as waveforms with measurable frequencies mapped to musical notes mapped to letters. Three encoding layers is two too many.

## Closing Note

The V3 design fixed the structural problems I cared most about: the meta now passes the 80% rule, the card system is load-bearing, and the answer words are thematically strong. The remaining work is mechanism selection, and the pool gives us the raw material to build a hunt with genuine variety.

The single most important change I am recommending is the swap of Q1 (Crossword) for Q2 (Visual Rebus) in the Arts slot. A crossword is a delivery mechanism for trivia. A visual rebus is a puzzle about *perception* — which is what the Arts section is actually about. If the hunt's thesis is "the content IS the mechanism," then every section's puzzle should use the section's signature mode of thinking. The Visual Rebus does. The crossword does not.

The second-most-important change is swapping 2-1 (Organism Identification + Diagonal) for 2-3 (Dichotomous Key). A diagonal read is fragile and arbitrary. A dichotomous key is the natural world's fundamental tool. The Life Sciences puzzle uses the genetic code. The Computing puzzle uses a cipher. The Natural World puzzle should use a classification key. Pattern: the section's tool IS the puzzle's tool.

The three Black Joker picks are ordered by memorability. Paper + Light is the moment that people describe to their friends five years later. Punch Card Overlay is the moment that makes people gasp ("you want me to punch HOLES in this book?"). Fold the Page is the moment that changes how the solver thinks about every subsequent page.

Thirteen puzzles. Thirteen different solve experiences. One hunt that uses its own content as its own mechanism. That is a hunt worth finding.

-- Dan Katz
