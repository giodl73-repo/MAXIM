# 01 — Nuclear Physics for Engineers

## Binding Energy, Cross Sections, Decay, Fission

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Nuclear structure: protons, neutrons, nuclide notation AZX, isotopes/isobars/isotones
- Nuclear sizes: R = R₀·A^(1/3) ≈ 1.2A^(1/3) fm; nuclear density ~2.3×10¹⁷ kg/m³
- Binding energy: B = (Z·mₚ + N·mₙ − M)·c², mass defect, atomic mass units (u)
- Semi-empirical mass formula (Bethe-Weizsäcker): volume/surface/Coulomb/asymmetry/pairing terms
- Binding energy per nucleon curve: iron-56 maximum, fusion left, fission right
- Q-value: Q = (mass_reactants − mass_products)·c² — energy release or absorption
- Radioactive decay law: dN/dt = −λN, N(t) = N₀e^{−λt}, T_{1/2} = ln2/λ
- Activity: A = λN in Becquerels (Bq) or Curies (Ci = 3.7×10¹⁰ Bq)
- Decay chains: secular/transient equilibrium, branching ratios, Bateman equations
- Alpha decay: Geiger-Nuttall rule, tunneling, energy spectrum, range in matter
- Beta decay: β⁻ (n→p+e⁻+ν̄_e), β⁺ (p→n+e⁺+ν_e), electron capture, neutrinos
- Gamma decay: isomeric transitions, internal conversion, gamma spectroscopy, Compton/photoelectric/pair production
- Interaction of radiation with matter: linear attenuation coefficient, half-value layer, KERMA, dose
- Nuclear reactions: conservation laws (A, Z, energy, momentum), threshold energy
- Cross sections: definition σ [barns], microscopic vs macroscopic Σ = nσ, mean free path λ = 1/Σ
- Neutron cross sections: scattering, absorption, fission — 1/v region, resonances, fast region
- Fission: spontaneous vs induced; neutron-induced 235U and 239Pu fission
- Fission fragment distribution: bimodal mass distribution, energy release (~200 MeV per fission)
- Prompt and delayed neutrons: ν̄ ≈ 2.4 for U-235, delayed fraction β ≈ 0.0065
- Fusion: D-T reaction Q=17.6 MeV, Lawson criterion, confinement approaches (tokamak, ICF)
- Nuclear data libraries: ENDF/B, JEFF, JENDL, TENDL — where cross section data comes from
