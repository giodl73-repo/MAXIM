# 04 — Reactor Types

## PWR, BWR, CANDU, Advanced Designs, SMRs, Gen IV

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Reactor classification matrix: moderator × coolant × fuel type × spectrum (thermal vs fast)
- Light Water Reactors (LWR): worldwide ~75% of operating fleet; H₂O as moderator + coolant
- Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR):
  - Primary/secondary loop separation via steam generator
  - Pressurizer: maintains ~155 bar, 345°C (subcooled liquid)
  - Control: soluble boron (chemical shim) + control rods + burnable absorbers
  - Fuel: 3–5% enriched UO₂ pellets in Zircaloy cladding; 4-loop Westinghouse, VVER
- Boiling Water Reactor (BWR):
  - Single-loop design: steam generated in reactor vessel; drives turbine directly
  - Lower pressure (~70 bar), lower temperature (~285°C), steam quality ~12–15%
  - Control: control rods from below + jet pumps (no boron in coolant during operation)
  - GE BWR families: BWR/1 through BWR/6, ABWR, ESBWR
- CANDU (CANada Deuterium Uranium):
  - Heavy water (D₂O) moderator and coolant; natural uranium fuel
  - Horizontal pressure tubes; on-power refueling — high capacity factor
  - Positive void coefficient (design compensated); CANDU 6, ACR-1000
- RBMK (Soviet graphite-moderated): positive void coefficient; Chernobyl accident cause
- VVER vs PWR: hexagonal fuel bundles, horizontal steam generators, horizontal ECCS injection
- Generation III/III+ designs: passive safety emphasis, 60-year design life, AP1000, EPR, APR1400
- Small Modular Reactors (SMRs): <300 MWe, factory-built modules, NuScale Power Module, Kairos KP-FHR
- Generation IV designs (2040+):
  - Sodium-cooled Fast Reactor (SFR): breeder potential, used in BN-800/BN-1200 (Russia), IFR
  - Molten Salt Reactor (MSR): liquid fuel, online reprocessing, Thorium potential, ThorCon, Terrestrial
  - Very High Temperature Reactor (VHTR): 900–1000°C, hydrogen production, TRISO fuel
  - Gas-cooled Fast Reactor (GFR): helium coolant, fast spectrum
  - Lead-cooled Fast Reactor (LFR): low pressure, natural circulation, BREST
  - Supercritical Water-cooled Reactor (SCWR): higher efficiency (~45%)
- Fusion reactors: ITER (D-T, Q=10 goal), SPARC/Commonwealth Fusion, NIF inertial confinement
- Fuel cycles: once-through (US), limited reprocessing (France), thorium cycle potential (India)
