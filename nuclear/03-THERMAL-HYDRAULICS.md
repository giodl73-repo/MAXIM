# 03 — Nuclear Thermal Hydraulics

## Heat Generation, Coolant Flow, DNB, Reactivity Feedback

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Heat generation in fuel: power density q''' [W/m³], flux-power coupling q''' = Σ_f·φ·Q_f
- Radial temperature profile in fuel rod: q''' = k_f·∇²T → parabolic profile
- Gap conductance: UO₂-cladding gap, gas composition, contact pressure effects
- Cladding temperature: inner/outer cladding temperature difference, oxidation limits
- Coolant energy balance: single channel model, mixed mean temperature rise
- Single-phase forced convection: Dittus-Boelter, Gnielinski — internal flow correlations
- Flow regimes in heated channel: subcooled nucleate boiling, saturated boiling, post-CHF
- Critical heat flux (CHF): W-3 correlation (PWR), Zuber-Griffith model, CHF mechanisms
- Departure from Nucleate Boiling Ratio (DNBR): design limit ≥ 1.3, hot channel factors
- Two-phase flow: quality x, void fraction α, slip ratio, flow regime maps
- Two-phase pressure drop: Lockhart-Martinelli correlation, Martinelli-Nelson
- Boiling crisis: film boiling (Leidenfrost), transition boiling, stable film boiling
- Hot channel factors: nuclear heat flux factor F_N_Q, total hot channel factor F_Q
- Flow distribution in rod bundles: COBRA-type subchannel analysis
- Reactivity-temperature feedback: dρ/dT_fuel (Doppler), dρ/dT_mod (moderator temp)
- Void coefficient: dρ/dα_void — negative in LWRs, positive in RBMK (accident contributor)
- Power-to-flow mismatch: transient analysis, peak centerline fuel temperature
- Natural circulation: driving head = ρ_cold·g·H − ρ_hot·g·H, decay heat removal
- Loss of Coolant Accident (LOCA) thermal response: blowdown, refill, reflood phases
- Emergency Core Cooling: high/low pressure injection, accumulator discharge, residual heat removal
- Fuel performance: PCI (pellet-cladding interaction), fission gas release, cladding creep
