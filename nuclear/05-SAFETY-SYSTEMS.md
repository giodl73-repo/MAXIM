# 05 — Nuclear Safety Systems

## Defense in Depth, LOCA, Passive Safety, Accident Lessons

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Safety philosophy: defense in depth (DiD), IAEA principles, deterministic vs probabilistic safety
- Five barriers: fuel matrix, cladding, primary coolant boundary, reactor pressure vessel, containment
- Single failure criterion: any single active or passive failure must not prevent safety function
- Redundancy and diversity: trains, separation, 2-out-of-3 logic, diverse actuation systems
- Safety classification: safety-related vs non-safety, IEEE 323/344 qualification
- Reactor protection system (RPS): trip logic, ATWS (anticipated transient without scram)
- Design basis accidents (DBAs): LOCA, main steam line break, control rod ejection, loss of feedwater
- Large-break LOCA: blowdown phase, refill, reflood; peak cladding temperature (PCT) < 1204°C
- Small-break LOCA: pressurizer heater effects, natural circulation challenges
- Emergency Core Cooling System (ECCS): high-pressure injection, accumulator (passive), low-pressure injection
- Passive safety features: AP1000 passive core cooling, passive containment cooling, gravity-driven
- Core Damage Frequency (CDF): probabilistic risk assessment (PRA), Level 1/2/3 PRA
- Large Early Release Frequency (LERF): severe accident management
- Severe accident progression: core heat-up, fuel damage, melt progression, vessel failure
- Hydrogen generation: Zircaloy oxidation, detonation risk, passive autocatalytic recombiners (PAR)
- Containment design: dry containment, ice condenser, pressure suppression (BWR), filtered venting
- Accident management: emergency operating procedures (EOPs), severe accident management guidelines (SAMGs)
- Three Mile Island (1979): stuck-open PORV, operator response, lessons learned (ECCIs, training)
- Chernobyl (1986): power excursion in RBMK, graphite fire, lack of containment, AZ-5 positive feedback
- Fukushima Daiichi (2011): loss of AC power + tsunami, station blackout, hydrogen explosions, melt
- Post-Fukushima requirements: FLEX strategy, portable ECCS equipment, hardened venting
- WANO/IAEA peer reviews: OSART, SALTO, WANO peer review program
- Radiation protection (RadProt): dose limits (50 mSv/yr occupational, 1 mSv/yr public), ALARA
- Effluent control: liquid/gaseous releases, dose to critical group, 10 CFR 50 Appendix I limits
