# 02 — Reactor Physics

## Criticality, Neutron Transport, Neutron Economy, Kinetics

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Neutron life cycle: birth (fast) → slowing down → thermal absorption or leakage
- Four-factor formula: k∞ = η·ε·p·f
  - η: thermal fission factor (neutrons per absorption in fuel)
  - ε: fast fission factor (>1, accounts for fast fissions in U-238)
  - p: resonance escape probability (avoid capture during slowing down)
  - f: thermal utilization (fraction absorbed in fuel vs all materials)
- Six-factor formula: k_eff = k∞·P_FNL·P_TNL (leakage probabilities)
- Material buckling B²_m = (k∞ − 1)/M² ; geometric buckling for simple shapes
- Critical condition: B²_m = B²_g — sets critical size
- Neutron moderation: collision-by-collision, average lethargy gain, slowing-down power
- Moderator comparison: H₂O, D₂O, graphite, beryllium — moderating ratio
- Fermi age theory: τ = age to thermal, slowing-down density q(u)
- Neutron diffusion equation: D∇²φ − Σ_a·φ + S = 0, diffusion length L² = D/Σ_a
- One-group diffusion theory: bare sphere/cylinder/slab critical dimensions
- Two-group theory (thermal and fast): coupling equations, reflector savings
- Multigroup transport: Boltzmann transport equation, discrete ordinates (S_N), Monte Carlo (MCNP)
- Reactivity: ρ = (k_eff − 1)/k_eff, in pcm (per cent mille) or $
- Reactivity worth: control rods (differential and integral worth), boron worth
- Delayed neutrons: six precursor groups, effective delayed neutron fraction β_eff
- Point kinetics equations: inhour equation, reactor period T, prompt jump approximation
- Feedback mechanisms: Doppler (fuel temp), void, moderator temp coefficients
- Xenon poisoning: 135Xe from 135I decay, xenon transient after shutdown, xenon oscillations
- Samarium poisoning: 149Sm from 149Pm, equilibrium, post-shutdown transient
- Burnup and transmutation: actinide buildup (239Pu, 240Pu, 241Pu), fission product accumulation
- Fuel management: reload enrichments, power peaking factors, flux flattening, burnable absorbers
