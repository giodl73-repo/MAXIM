# 10 — Epidemiology & Public Health

## R₀, Herd Immunity, Outbreak Investigation, Surveillance

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Epidemiological measures: incidence (new cases/time) vs prevalence (existing cases/population), attack rate, case fatality rate vs infection fatality rate
- Study designs: randomized controlled trial / cohort / case-control / cross-sectional / ecological — strengths/limitations, confounding, bias
- Measures of association: relative risk (cohort), odds ratio (case-control), NNT, attributable risk
- R₀ (basic reproduction number): definition, herd immunity threshold = 1 − 1/R₀, how control measures reduce Re
- Herd immunity thresholds: measles (R₀ 12–18 → 94% threshold), polio, COVID-19
- Outbreak investigation: case definition → case count → epidemic curve (point/propagated/continuous) → hypothesis generation → analytic study → control measures → communication
- Transmission routes: contact (direct/indirect), droplet, airborne, fecal-oral, vector-borne, vertical — implications for control
- Surveillance systems: passive vs active, syndromic surveillance, IHR (International Health Regulations)
- Screening principles: Wilson-Jungner criteria, sensitivity vs specificity trade-off, positive predictive value depends on prevalence
- Vaccines and population immunity: WHO Expanded Programme on Immunization, cold chain, vaccine hesitancy, herd effects
- Global burden of disease: GBD study, DALY calculation, leading causes by region, demographic transition, NCDs vs communicable
