# 01 — Bacterial Disease

## Gram Classification, Virulence Factors, Key Pathogens, Resistance

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Gram staining: gram+ (thick peptidoglycan) vs gram− (thin PG + outer membrane + LPS) — implications for treatment
- Bacterial virulence factors: adhesins, toxins (exotoxins A-B structure / endotoxin/LPS), capsule, biofilm, immune evasion
- Exotoxin mechanisms: AB toxins (cholera/anthrax/diphtheria/botulinum), superantigens (TSST-1), pore-forming (Staphylococcal α-toxin)
- Key pathogens by system:
  - Respiratory: S. pneumoniae, S. pyogenes, M. tuberculosis, B. pertussis, Legionella
  - GI: Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter, H. pylori, C. difficile, ETEC/EPEC/EHEC
  - Urogenital: E. coli (UTI), N. gonorrhoeae, Chlamydia
  - Systemic/blood: S. aureus (MRSA), S. epidermidis, Streptococcus viridans (endocarditis)
  - Neurological: N. meningitidis, Listeria, T. pallidum
- Antibiotic resistance mechanisms: β-lactamase (ESBL, carbapenemase), efflux pumps, target modification (PBP2a in MRSA), ribosomal methylation
- ESKAPE pathogens (priority drug-resistant organisms)
