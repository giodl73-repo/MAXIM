# 09 — Genetic & Developmental Disease

## Chromosomal Abnormalities, Single-Gene Disorders, Birth Defects

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Chromosomal disorders: numerical (aneuploidy — trisomy 21/18/13, monosomy X/Turner, XXY/Klinefelter), structural (deletions/duplications/translocations/inversions)
- Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome): Robertsonian translocation mechanism, clinical features, mosaicism
- Inheritance patterns: autosomal dominant (AD), autosomal recessive (AR), X-linked recessive/dominant, Y-linked, mitochondrial, codominant
- Key AD diseases: Huntington's, Marfan's (FBN1), NF1/NF2, ADPKD, familial hypercholesterolemia, achondroplasia (FGFR3 activating mutation)
- Key AR diseases: cystic fibrosis (CFTR ΔF508), PKU (PAH deficiency), sickle cell (HbS), thalassemias, Gaucher's/Tay-Sachs (lysosomal storage), Wilson's (ATP7B), hemochromatosis (HFE)
- X-linked recessive: Duchenne MD (dystrophin frameshift), hemophilia A/B, G6PD deficiency, fragile X (CGG repeat)
- Imprinting disorders: Prader-Willi (paternal 15q11 loss) vs Angelman (maternal 15q11 loss), Beckwith-Wiedemann
- Mitochondrial diseases: maternal inheritance, heteroplasmy, MELAS/MERRF/Leber's
- Developmental birth defects: neural tube defects (folic acid/SHH), cardiac malformations (GATA4/Nkx2.5), teratogens (thalidomide/isotretinoin/alcohol/rubella)
- Newborn screening: PKU (Guthrie test), congenital hypothyroidism, CAH, galactosemia, MSUD
