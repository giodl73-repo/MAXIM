# 03 — Fungal, Parasitic & Prion Disease

## Fungi, Protozoa, Helminths, Prions

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Fungi:**
  - Superficial: tinea (dermatophytes), Candida intertrigo, pityriasis versicolor
  - Systemic endemic: Histoplasma (Ohio/Mississippi valley, intracellular macrophage), Coccidioides (SW USA/spherules), Blastomyces
  - Opportunistic: Aspergillus (angioinvasive, immunocompromised), Candida (systemic), Cryptococcus neoformans (meningitis + India ink, AIDS)
  - Antifungal targets: cell wall (echinocandins/glucan synthase), ergosterol synthesis (azoles/polyenes)
- **Protozoa:**
  - Malaria: Plasmodium life cycle (mosquito/liver/RBC stages), P. falciparum severe malaria, chloroquine resistance
  - Trypanosomes: T. brucei (sleeping sickness/tsetse fly), T. cruzi (Chagas/reduviid bug/Romana's sign)
  - Leishmaniasis: cutaneous/mucocutaneous/visceral (kala-azar) forms, sandfly vector
  - Toxoplasma: cat fecal cycle, congenital infection, reactivation in AIDS (ring-enhancing brain lesions)
  - Giardia/Cryptosporidium/Entamoeba: common GI protozoa, cyst transmission
- **Helminths:**
  - Nematodes (roundworms): Ascaris, hookworm, Strongyloides, Trichinella, Toxocara, filariae (Wuchereria/Brugia/Onchocerca)
  - Trematodes (flukes): Schistosoma (portal hypertension/bladder cancer), liver flukes, lung flukes
  - Cestodes (tapeworms): Taenia solium (neurocysticercosis — leading cause of acquired epilepsy globally), Echinococcus
- **Prions:** misfolded PrP^Sc → autocatalytic conversion of PrP^C, no nucleic acid; CJD (sporadic/variant/familial/iatrogenic), Kuru, fatal familial insomnia, Gerstmann-Sträussler-Scheinker; no effective treatment
