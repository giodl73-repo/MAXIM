# 06 — Metabolic & Endocrine Disease

## Diabetes, Metabolic Syndrome, Thyroid, Adrenal

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Type 1 diabetes: autoimmune β-cell destruction, absolute insulin deficiency, DKA, HLA-DR3/4 association
- Type 2 diabetes: insulin resistance → β-cell exhaustion, pathophysiology of insulin resistance (ectopic lipid, ceramide, IRS-1 serine phosphorylation), progression
- DKA vs HHS: pathophysiology, laboratory distinction, management principles
- Metabolic syndrome: visceral adiposity, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia (high TG, low HDL), HTN, hyperglycemia — unifying mechanisms
- Thyroid disorders: hypothyroidism (Hashimoto's — anti-TPO/anti-Tg; primary hypothyroidism), hyperthyroidism (Graves' — TSI stimulating antibodies; toxic multinodular goiter; thyroiditis)
- Thyroid hormone physiology: T3 vs T4, deiodinase conversion, receptor-nuclear effects
- Adrenal insufficiency: primary (Addison's — autoimmune, all three cortical layers) vs secondary (ACTH deficiency) vs tertiary; adrenal crisis
- Cushing's syndrome: exogenous steroids vs endogenous (ACTH-dependent: pituitary/ectopic; ACTH-independent: adrenal tumor)
- Phaeochromocytoma: catecholamine-secreting tumor, hypertensive crises, 10% rule
- Obesity: adipokines (leptin/adiponectin), gut hormones (GLP-1/PYY/ghrelin), set-point theory
- Hyperuricemia/gout: uric acid metabolism, xanthine oxidase, monosodium urate crystal deposition, allopurinol
