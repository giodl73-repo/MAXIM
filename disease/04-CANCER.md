# 04 — Cancer

## Oncogenes, Tumor Suppressors, Hallmarks, Staging, Metastasis

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Hallmarks of cancer (Hanahan & Weinberg): sustaining proliferation, evading growth suppressors, enabling replicative immortality, activating invasion/metastasis, inducing angiogenesis, resisting cell death + emerging hallmarks (reprogramming metabolism, evading immune destruction, tumor-promoting inflammation)
- Proto-oncogenes → oncogenes: gain-of-function mutations; Ras (GTPase), Myc (TF), Her2 (RTK), BCR-ABL (fusion kinase)
- Tumor suppressors: loss-of-function, two-hit hypothesis (Knudson); Rb (G1/S checkpoint), p53 (genome guardian, mutated in 50% of cancers), BRCA1/2 (DNA repair), APC (Wnt pathway)
- Cell cycle checkpoint failures: CDK/cyclin complexes, CKI inhibitors, Rb/E2F axis
- DNA repair pathways and cancer: MMR deficiency (MSI-high, Lynch syndrome), BRCA/HR deficiency (PARP inhibitor sensitivity), XP/BER/NER
- Epigenetics: methylation silencing of TSGs, histone modifications, oncogenic fusion TFs
- Metastasis: local invasion (MMP/EMT) → intravasation → circulation → extravasation → colonization; organotropism
- Tumor staging: TNM system (T=size/invasion, N=nodes, M=metastasis)
- Cancer epidemiology: leading cancers by incidence/mortality, risk factors, screening principles
- Liquid biopsy: ctDNA, CTCs, applications in monitoring
