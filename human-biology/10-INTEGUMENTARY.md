# 10 — Integumentary System

## Skin Layers, Appendages, Thermoregulation, Wound Healing

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Skin layers: epidermis (5 strata: basale/spinosum/granulosum/lucidum/corneum), dermis (papillary/reticular), hypodermis
- Cell types: keratinocytes (90%), melanocytes (UV protection, Fitzpatrick scale), Langerhans cells (immune), Merkel cells (touch)
- Skin appendages: hair follicles (anagen/catagen/telogen cycle), sebaceous glands (sebum, acne), eccrine/apocrine sweat glands, nails
- Thermoregulation: vasodilation/vasoconstriction, sweating (evaporative cooling), piloerection, shivering
- Sensory receptors: Meissner (light touch/papillary ridges), Pacinian (vibration/deep pressure), Ruffini (stretch), free nerve endings (pain/temp)
- Skin barrier: stratum corneum lipid bilayers, tight junctions, antimicrobial peptides
- Vitamin D synthesis: UVB → 7-dehydrocholesterol → pre-D3 → D3 → liver 25-OH → kidney 1,25-OH (calcitriol)
- Wound healing: four phases — hemostasis (platelets/fibrin), inflammation (neutrophils/macrophages), proliferation (fibroblasts/angiogenesis/re-epithelialization), remodeling (collagen cross-linking)
- Skin as immune organ: Langerhans cells, IgA secretion, SALT (skin-associated lymphoid tissue)
- Burns: classification (1st/2nd/3rd degree), rule of nines, fluid resuscitation (Parkland formula)
