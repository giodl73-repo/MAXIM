# 06 — Health Psychology & Stress

## HPA Axis, Allostatic Load, Stress-Illness Pathways, Well-Being Science

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Stress response physiology: fast (sympathoadrenal — catecholamines in seconds) vs slow (HPA axis — cortisol in minutes); fight-flight-freeze; the stress response is adaptive for acute stressors but damaging when chronic
- HPA axis: hypothalamus (CRH) → anterior pituitary (ACTH) → adrenal cortex (cortisol); negative feedback loop (cortisol → hippocampus + hypothalamus); diurnal rhythm (peak at waking); HPA dysregulation in depression
- Allostatic load (McEwen/Stellar 1993): cumulative wear and tear from repeated stress activation; allostasis (achieving stability through change) vs homeostasis; allostatic load biomarkers (cortisol/DHEA-S/blood pressure/BMI/inflammatory markers/immune function); ACEs (Adverse Childhood Experiences) as allostatic load accelerant
- Stress-illness pathways: neuroimmunological (stress → SNS → cytokines → inflammation), cardiovascular (repeated BP spikes → endothelial damage), cognitive (cortisol → hippocampal atrophy → memory impairment), behavioral (sleep disruption/substance use/diet changes), telomere shortening (Blackburn/Epel — Nobel 2009)
- Cohen's work on common cold: prospective psychosocial stress exposure → nasal inoculation → dose-response relationship between stress and infection susceptibility; most rigorous human stress-illness evidence
- Resilience: Bonanty's four trajectories (chronic/recovered/delayed/resilient), protective factors (social support/self-efficacy/cognitive flexibility/meaning-making), resilience vs recovery distinction; post-traumatic growth
- Positive psychology (Seligman): from disease model to flourishing; PERMA (Positive emotions/Engagement/Relationships/Meaning/Accomplishment); evidence base for interventions (gratitude journaling/signature strengths/best possible self: small-moderate effects, often short-lived); critique of happiness industry
- Mindfulness: Jon Kabat-Zinn MBSR (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction), meta-analytic evidence (moderate effects on anxiety/depression/pain, largely non-inferiority to active treatments), neurological correlates (default mode network deactivation), commercial distortion of original Buddhist construct
- Sleep as health behavior: sleep stages (N1/N2/N3/REM), sleep deprivation effects (immune/cognitive/emotional/metabolic), sleep debt myth, circadian rhythm (SCN, melatonin, light entrainment), sleep hygiene evidence (CBT-I vs pharmacological)
- Occupational health: work-related stress models (Karasek demand-control, Siegrist effort-reward imbalance), presenteeism vs absenteeism, burnout prevention (JD-R model — see organizational/04)
