# 04 — Organizational Psychology

## Selection Validity, Job Performance, Burnout, Engagement, JD-R Model

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- I-O psychology scope: personnel selection, performance management, training/development, work motivation, leadership, job design, organizational climate/culture, work-life, occupational health
- Validity types: criterion validity (predictive/concurrent), construct validity, content validity; incremental validity over existing predictors; validity generalization (Schmidt/Hunter — meta-analysis shows validity generalizes across jobs)
- Selection battery evidence (Schmidt/Hunter 1998, updated 2016): GMA (general mental ability) single best predictor (r~0.51 with job performance), structured interviews (r~0.58 — situational + behavioral questions), work samples (r~0.54), conscientiousness (r~0.24), unstructured interviews (r~0.20), education (r~0.10), reference checks (r~0.26)
- Job performance models: Campbell's model (8 factors — task performance/non-task/communication/supervision/management/organizational citizenship/will comply with organizational policies/facilitation), OCB (Organizational Citizenship Behavior — beyond-role behaviors), counterproductive work behavior
- Structured interviews: behavioral (STAR method — past behavior predicts future), situational (hypothetical scenarios — what would you do?), panel vs single interviewer, rating scale anchoring; why reduce bias
- Burnout (Maslach): three dimensions — exhaustion (energy depletion), cynicism/depersonalization (detachment from work), reduced efficacy; MBI (Maslach Burnout Inventory); opposite = engagement (vigor/dedication/absorption)
- Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) model: demands (physical/cognitive/emotional — deplete resources → exhaustion) vs resources (social support/autonomy/feedback — buffer demands → engagement); health impairment vs motivational process
- Work motivation (applied): intrinsic motivation, self-determination theory applied to work design (see organizational-behavior/01), job crafting (proactive shaping of tasks/relationships/meaning), psychological empowerment
- Job design: Hackman-Oldham job characteristics model (skill variety/task identity/task significance/autonomy/feedback → psychological states → outcomes); job enrichment vs job enlargement; telework design
- Performance management: rating formats (BARS/BOS/graphic scales), rater bias (halo/leniency/recency), 360-degree feedback, calibration sessions, OKR/goal-setting integration; evidence on annual reviews vs continuous feedback
