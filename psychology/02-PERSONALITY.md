# 02 — Personality Psychology

## Big Five Factor Structure, Heritability, Dark Triad, Stability, Assessment

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Big Five (OCEAN) origins: lexical hypothesis (personality encoded in language), factor analysis of trait adjectives (Allport/Odbert → Cattell → Fiske → Goldberg → Costa/McCrae NEO-PI-R); five robust factors across languages and methods
- Big Five dimensions: Openness to Experience (intellect/creativity/curiosity), Conscientiousness (self-discipline/organization/goal-direction), Extraversion (sociability/positive affect/assertiveness), Agreeableness (cooperation/trust/altruism), Neuroticism (emotional instability/anxiety/negative affect); facets within each
- Validity of Big Five: predicts job performance (Conscientiousness strongest — r~0.24), academic achievement (C), relationship quality (A), mental health (N), longevity (C), political orientation (Openness → liberal); incremental validity over IQ
- Heritability: ~40-60% heritable for each dimension; shared environment matters little after adolescence; gene × environment interaction; GWAS findings (small effects, many variants)
- Stability across lifespan: mean-level changes (increase in C and A, decrease in N into middle age — "personality maturation"), rank-order stability increases with age (~0.75 over 40-year interval); personality change possible but trait-consistent
- MBTI critique: based on Jungian typology (not empirical factor analysis), forced dichotomies vs continuous dimensions, poor test-retest reliability (~50% different type after 5 weeks), weak predictive validity; despite popularity, not endorsed by personality researchers
- Dark Triad (Paulhus/Williams 2002): Narcissism (grandiosity/entitlement/exploitativeness), Machiavellianism (cynicism/strategic manipulation/low emotionality), Subclinical Psychopathy (impulsivity/thrill-seeking/callousness); correlate with each other; differential prediction of outcomes; Dark Tetrad adds everyday sadism
- Assessment methods: self-report (NEO-PI-R), informant report (more accurate for some traits), behavioral observation, experience sampling; response biases (social desirability, self-enhancement)
- Personality in organizations: personality assessment in hiring (validity, legal constraints, faking), job-personality fit, personality and leadership (see organizational-behavior/02)
