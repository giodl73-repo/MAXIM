# 01 — Social Psychology

## Conformity, Obedience, Attribution, Attitude Change, Social Identity

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Conformity (Asch line studies 1951): informational vs normative social influence; ~37% conformity rate on unambiguous perceptual tasks; factors (unanimity, group size, anonymity); cultural variation (collectivist societies higher conformity); minority influence (Moscovici — consistency matters)
- Obedience to authority (Milgram 1963): 65% delivered maximum shock; situational factors (physical proximity, legitimate authority, incremental escalation, diffusion of responsibility); critique and replications (Jerry Burger 2009 — similar rates); Abu Ghraib as real-world analog
- Bystander effect and diffusion of responsibility: Kitty Genovese myth vs reality (Darley/Latané — pluralistic ignorance + diffusion of responsibility); intervention increases when: victim clearly needs help, one bystander, similarity to victim
- Fundamental Attribution Error (FAE): overattribute behavior to dispositional factors, underestimate situational; Jones/Harris 1967 (essay study); actor-observer asymmetry; correspondence bias; culture modulates (less FAE in East Asian populations)
- Cognitive dissonance (Festinger 1957): inconsistency between cognitions → aversive arousal → reduce dissonance (change belief/behavior/add cognition); Festinger-Carlsmith study ($1 vs $20 payment); self-perception theory alternative (Bem)
- Attitude structure and change: Tri-component (affect/cognition/behavior), attitude-behavior gap, persuasion routes (ELM — central/systematic vs peripheral/heuristic routes), when attitude change is durable
- Social identity theory (Tajfel/Turner): social categorization → social identity → in-group favoritism + out-group derogation; minimal group paradigm; self-esteem maintenance; intergroup contact hypothesis (Allport's conditions)
- Stereotypes, prejudice, discrimination: stereotype as cognitive schema (efficiency vs accuracy tradeoff), implicit bias (IAT — predictive validity debated), aversive racism, stereotype threat (Steele/Aronson — effect on test performance; meta-analytic support moderate)
- Prosocial behavior: altruism vs reciprocal altruism, empathy-altruism hypothesis (Batson), kin selection vs group selection debate, situational determinants of helping
