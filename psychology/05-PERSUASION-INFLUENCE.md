# 05 — Persuasion & Influence

## Cialdini Six Principles, ELM, Inoculation Theory, Disinformation

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Cialdini's six principles (Influence, 1984) — with mechanisms not just labels:
  - Reciprocity: we feel obligated to return favors; uninvited gifts create obligation; concession-and-retreat (door-in-the-face); mechanism: evolutionarily adaptive norm enforcement
  - Commitment/Consistency: once we commit, we align future behavior; foot-in-the-door; public > private commitment; written > verbal; mechanism: cognitive dissonance + self-perception
  - Social proof: in uncertainty, we follow others; pluralistic ignorance; canned laughter; bystander effect link; mechanism: heuristic for correct action under uncertainty
  - Authority: we defer to perceived experts; white coat effect; credentials vs substance; mechanism: genuine epistemic efficiency + exploitation
  - Liking: we comply with people we like (similar/attractive/familiar/cooperative); mere exposure effect; mechanism: halo effect + reduced cognitive scrutiny
  - Scarcity: we value what's rare or losing availability; reactance (Brehm); FOMO engineering; mechanism: loss aversion + uniqueness motivation
  - Pre-suasion (Cialdini 2016): priming context before the message; attention directs focus → focus directs perception of importance
- Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM, Petty/Cacioppo): central/systematic route (effortful processing of argument quality → durable attitude change) vs peripheral/heuristic route (cue processing — source attractiveness/consensus → surface change); motivation + ability determine route; implications for when to use statistics vs stories
- Inoculation theory (McGuire 1961, updated Lewandowsky/van der Linden): pre-emptive exposure to weakened form of misinformation + refutation → resistance to subsequent misinformation; prebunking vs debunking; psychological vaccine analogy; SIREN model
- Propaganda techniques: repetition (illusory truth effect), false balance (both-sidesism), firehose of falsehood (overwhelm, not persuade), deepfake/synthetic media, astroturfing, amplification networks
- Online influence: algorithmic amplification (outrage/engagement optimization), echo chambers vs cross-cutting exposure debate (Bail et al. — exposure to opposing views can backfire), coordinated inauthentic behavior, platform design as persuasion architecture
- Nudge theory (Thaler/Sunstein): default effects (opt-out vs opt-in organ donation), choice architecture, libertarian paternalism; EAST framework (Easy/Attractive/Social/Timely); limits and backfire effects (reactance)
- Dark patterns: UI deception taxonomy (trick questions/roach motel/privacy zuckering/confirmshaming/misdirection) — FTC regulation trajectory
