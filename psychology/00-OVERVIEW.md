# 00 — Psychology Overview

## Subfields, Methods, Replication Crisis: What Survived

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Subfields: experimental/cognitive (overlaps cognitive-science/), social, personality, clinical/abnormal, developmental (covered in cognitive-science/06), organizational/industrial-organizational (I-O), health, neuropsychology, forensic
- Methods: RCT (gold standard but ethical limits), quasi-experiment, survey (construct validity problems), case study, experience sampling/ESM, psychophysiological (EEG/fMRI — see neuroscience/), longitudinal cohort
- Replication crisis: the Open Science Collaboration (2015) — ~37% of results replicated in psychology; priming effects largely failed (social priming, ego depletion); power posing collapsed; what survived (Big Five, cognitive dissonance, fear conditioning, Milgram-ish effects, certain CBT outcomes)
- Effect size context: Cohen's d in psychology averages ~0.4, much smaller than most hypothesize; publication bias inflates; registered reports as reform
- History: Wundt's experimental psychology (1879 Leipzig lab) → structuralism vs functionalism → behaviorism (Watson/Skinner — stimulus-response, mind as black box) → cognitive revolution (1956) → social cognitive → evolutionary → neuroscience integration
- Key tensions: nature vs nurture (false dichotomy — gene-environment interaction), universal vs culture-specific, conscious vs unconscious, lab vs ecological validity
- WEIRD problem: psychological science based 96% on Western/Educated/Industrialized/Rich/Democratic populations; generalizability crisis parallel to replication crisis
- Ethical landmarks: Tuskegee (ongoing harm without consent), Milgram obedience studies, Stanford Prison Experiment (Zimbardo — methodology critiques), Belmont Report (1979 — respect/beneficence/justice), IRB system
- Index of modules: social psychology, personality, clinical/abnormal, organizational, persuasion/influence, health/stress
