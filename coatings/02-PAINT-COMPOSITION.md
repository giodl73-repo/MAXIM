# 02 — Paint Composition

## Binder, Pigment, Solvent, Additives — What's Actually in a Can

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Four components: (1) binder/vehicle — forms the film, holds pigment; (2) pigment — color + hiding power + sometimes corrosion inhibition; (3) solvent/carrier — enables application, evaporates; (4) additives — dozens of functional chemicals in small amounts
- **Binders**:
  - Acrylic latex: acrylic polymer (polyacrylate/polymethacrylate) in water emulsion; most common for architectural paint; excellent UV resistance, color retention, flexibility; water cleanup; "100% acrylic" vs "vinyl-acrylic" (VAE — cheaper, less durable)
  - Alkyd (oil-modified polyester): polyester backbone esterified with fatty acids from drying oils; solvent-based; superior flow/leveling and adhesion; slower dry; used for trim, doors; "alkyd" from "alcohol" + "acid" (polyol + polyacid + fatty acid); waterborne alkyd = emulsified alkyd in water (compromise properties)
  - Epoxy: two-part or single-part; excellent chemical resistance, adhesion to metal; floor coatings, garage floors, industrial; typically not UV stable (chalks outdoors)
  - Polyurethane: one-part (moisture-cure) or two-part (isocyanate + polyol); floor finishes, industrial; excellent abrasion resistance; one-part slightly more forgiving to apply
  - Specialty: silicone (high-temp coatings, >1000°F), chlorinated rubber (pool paint), bituminous (below-grade waterproofing)
- **Pigments**: titanium dioxide (TiO₂, hiding/white), iron oxides (color + UV resistance), extenders (calcium carbonate/talc/clay — bulk, rheology, reduces cost, adds porosity to flat sheens); pigment volume concentration (PVC) critical — below critical PVC = paint film is strong; above CPVC = porous, weak, flat; flat paint has PVC near/above CPVC → microscopically porous → poor scrub resistance → matte appearance
- **Sheen levels** (decreasing PVC → increasing gloss): Flat (PVC ≥ CPVC, ~85-90 PVC%), Matte, Eggshell (~35-40%), Satin (~25-35%), Semi-gloss (~10-25%), Gloss (<10%); higher gloss = harder film, more scrub resistant, shows surface imperfections more; flat hides imperfections, less durable; appropriate sheen by location (kitchen/bath semi-gloss, living room eggshell/satin)
- **Additives**: thickeners/rheology modifiers (HEUR — hydrophobically modified ethylene oxide urethane — give pseudoplastic behavior: thick at rest, thin under brush/roller = "flow and leveling"); surfactants (stabilize emulsion, wet substrate); coalescents (temporary plasticizer to lower MFFT — evaporate after film forms; Texanol = common coalescent, VOC concern); biocides (in-can biocide prevents microbial growth in wet paint; dry-film biocide prevents mildew on painted surface); defoamers; UV stabilizers; freeze-thaw stabilizers; driers (oil-based, metal soaps — cobalt/manganese naphthenate accelerate oxidative drying)
- **Interior vs exterior formulation**: exterior requires UV stabilizers (HALS — hindered amine light stabilizers), mildewcides, greater flexibility (thermal cycling), harder binder for dirt pickup resistance; interior optimized for washability, color consistency, lower odor, anti-blocking (stacked surfaces don't stick)
- **Reading a spec sheet**: pot life (2-part products), dry time (touch, recoat, full cure — all different), coverage (sq ft/gallon — theoretical vs practical), VOS content, ASTM testing standards cited (D2486 scrub resistance, D3359 adhesion, D4587 QUV weathering)
