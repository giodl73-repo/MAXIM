# 00 — Coatings: Overview

## Paint, Stain, Varnish, Adhesive, Sealant — Surface Science Taxonomy

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Why surfaces need protection: corrosion (electrochemical oxidation of metals), biological attack (mold/fungi/termites in wood), UV degradation (photolysis of polymers), mechanical wear, water intrusion (freeze-thaw cycling in masonry), aesthetic maintenance
- Coatings taxonomy:
  - **Paint**: pigmented, opaque, film-forming; hides substrate; decorative + protective
  - **Stain**: penetrating (no surface film) or semi-transparent (thin film); shows substrate texture/grain; wood-primary
  - **Varnish**: unpigmented film-forming coating; protects while showing substrate; furniture/floor/marine
  - **Lacquer**: fast-drying solvent-release coating (vs varnish which polymerizes); furniture/automotive
  - **Adhesive**: bonds two substrates; load-bearing application of chemistry
  - **Sealant/caulk**: fills gaps and joints; accommodates movement; prevents infiltration
  - **Industrial coating**: specialized — corrosion protection (zinc-rich primer), chemical resistance (epoxy/vinyl ester), thermal resistance, electrical properties
- Film formation mechanisms:
  - **Solvent evaporation** (lacquer, shellac): polymer dissolved in solvent, solvent evaporates, polymer deposits; thermoplastic — re-soluble in same solvent; fast, but weaker than cross-linked
  - **Oxidative polymerization** (oil paint, alkyd): unsaturated fatty acids crosslink in air via oxygen; "drying oils" — linseed, tung, danish; slow, continues for years; produces tough, hard film; can yellow (linseed); cannot re-dissolve
  - **Coalescence** (latex/water-based): polymer particles in water emulsion; water evaporates, particles coalesce into film; minimum film formation temperature (MFFT) — below this temperature, particles won't fuse; plasticizers lower MFFT; modern water-based coatings use this
  - **Chemical crosslinking** (2-part epoxy, 2-part polyurethane, moisture-cure): reactive components mix → polymerization reaction; pot life (working time after mixing); highest performance but complex application
- Application methods: brush (cut-in work, accuracy), roller (large areas, texture from nap), spray (HVLP — high volume low pressure; airless sprayer — 2000+ PSI; electrostatic), dip/flow coat (industrial), powder coating (electrostatic + heat cure)
- VOC (volatile organic compound) regulations: solvent-based coatings release VOCs on drying; EPA and state (California CARB most stringent) VOC limits drive formulation to water-based; measured in grams/liter; flat paint typically <50g/L, gloss may be higher; "zero VOC" typically <5g/L
