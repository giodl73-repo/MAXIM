# 03 — Transport Phenomena

## Momentum, Energy, and Mass Transfer (Bird-Stewart-Lightfoot Framework)

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- BSL framework: unified treatment of momentum, energy, mass transfer via analogies
- Shell balance method: derive governing equations from first principles for simple geometries
- Molecular transport: Newton's law (viscosity), Fourier's law (conductivity), Fick's law (diffusivity)
- Dimensionless analogies: Re-Pr-Sc-Nu-Sh-St — knowing one often gives others
- Momentum transfer (viscous flow)
  - Navier-Stokes equations in Cartesian, cylindrical, spherical coordinates
  - Fully developed laminar flow: Poiseuille in tube, Couette flow, flow between plates
  - Turbulent flow: Reynolds decomposition, turbulent viscosity, mixing length
  - Boundary layer equations: Prandtl approximation, Blasius solution
- Energy transfer (conduction and convection)
  - Energy equation: conduction + convective transport + viscous dissipation
  - Forced convection in tubes: entry length, fully developed profiles, Dittus-Boelter
  - Free convection: buoyancy-driven flow, Grashof number, vertical plate correlations
  - Radiation: view factors, radiosity method (see heat transfer for fuller treatment)
- Mass transfer (diffusion and convection)
  - Fick's first and second laws; binary diffusivity table (gas, liquid, solid)
  - Steady and unsteady diffusion: equimolar counter-diffusion, diffusion through stagnant film
  - Convective mass transfer coefficient k_c; analogies to heat transfer (Chilton-Colburn j-factor)
  - Packed beds: pressure drop (Ergun equation), mass transfer correlations
  - Interphase mass transfer: two-film theory, overall mass transfer coefficients, K_ya
- Coupled transport: drying (heat and mass simultaneously), reactive absorption, membrane separation
- Dimensional analysis: Buckingham π theorem applied to transport problems
- Transport in biological systems: oxygen transport, drug delivery, biofilm mass transfer
