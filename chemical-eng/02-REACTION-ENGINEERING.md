# 02 — Chemical Reaction Engineering

## Kinetics, Reactor Design, Selectivity, Catalysis

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Rate of reaction: -r_A, intrinsic rate, units, stoichiometric relationships
- Power-law kinetics: rate = k·[A]^m·[B]^n, determining m and n from data
- Arrhenius equation: k = A·exp(-Ea/RT), activation energy, pre-exponential factor
- Elementary vs non-elementary reactions; rate-determining step
- Integrated rate laws: zero, first, second order — concentration vs time
- Batch reactor (BR): design equation, conversion X, sizing from -r_A vs X data
- Continuous stirred tank reactor (CSTR): steady-state design equation, X at exit
- Plug flow reactor (PFR): differential form, Levenspiel plot (1/−r_A vs X), PFR/CSTR comparison
- Space time τ, space velocity (GHSV/LHSV), reactor sizing
- Multiple reactor configurations: CSTRs in series vs single PFR, optimal sequencing for non-monotonic rates
- Parallel reactions (selectivity): point selectivity S, overall yield Y, temperature and concentration effects
- Series reactions: optimum time for intermediate, tubular vs mixed reactors
- Non-isothermal reactors: energy balance for batch/CSTR/PFR, adiabatic temperature rise
- Stability of CSTR: heat generation vs removal, multiple steady states, ignition/extinction
- Heterogeneous catalysis: Langmuir adsorption isotherm, BET surface area, pore diffusion
- Effectiveness factor η: Thiele modulus φ, internal diffusion limitation, external mass transfer
- Weisz-Prater criterion for diffusion limitation; Mears criterion for external heat transfer
- Residence time distribution (RTD): E(t), F(t) curves; pulse and step experiments
- Ideal flow models: mixed flow, plug flow; non-ideal: dispersion model (Pe), tanks-in-series (N)
- Polymerization reactors: chain growth vs step growth, molecular weight distribution
