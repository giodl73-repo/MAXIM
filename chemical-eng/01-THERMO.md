# 01 — Chemical Engineering Thermodynamics

## Phase Equilibria, Equations of State, Chemical Potential, Fugacity

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Fundamental relations: dG = VdP − SdT, chemical potential μᵢ = (∂G/∂nᵢ)_{T,P,nⱼ}
- Gibbs phase rule: F = C − P + 2 (degrees of freedom = components − phases + 2)
- Equations of state: ideal gas pV = nRT, van der Waals, Redlich-Kwong, Peng-Robinson
- Compressibility factor Z = pV/nRT; corresponding states principle; acentric factor ω
- Fugacity and fugacity coefficient: f = φP, departure functions from ideal gas
- Activity and activity coefficient: aᵢ = γᵢxᵢ, excess Gibbs energy models
- Activity coefficient models: Margules, van Laar, Wilson, NRTL, UNIQUAC
- Vapor-liquid equilibrium (VLE): Raoult's law (ideal), modified Raoult's law, γ-φ formulation
- P-x-y and T-x-y diagrams: bubble/dew point calculations, flash calculations (Rachford-Rice)
- Azeotropes: positive/negative deviation, pressure-sensitive vs pressure-insensitive
- Liquid-liquid equilibrium (LLE): tie lines, plait point, ternary diagrams
- Solid-liquid equilibrium: melting point depression, eutectic systems
- Thermodynamics of mixtures: mixing rules for equation of state, Kay's rule for pseudocriticals
- Chemical reaction equilibrium: equilibrium constant K_eq, van't Hoff equation (d ln K/dT)
- Gibbs energy minimization: simultaneous phase and reaction equilibrium
- Residual properties: departure functions, residual enthalpy and entropy from EOS
- Heat of mixing, volume of mixing: ideal solution, excess properties
- Thermodynamic consistency testing: Gibbs-Duhem equation, area test
