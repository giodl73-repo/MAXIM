# Dance — Landscape and Taxonomy

## The Big Picture

Dance is simultaneously a physical discipline, a formal notation system, a cultural archive, and a compositional art. Its full landscape spans from codified classical vocabularies to spontaneous improvisation, from biomechanical training science to political protest.

## Choreographic Structure: The Grammar of Movement

Movement sequences in dance follow compositional logic analogous to musical form:

```
COMPOSITIONAL HIERARCHY

  MOTIF (smallest unit -- distinctive gesture or movement quality)
     |
     v
  PHRASE (grammatical sentence -- motif + development, 4-32 counts)
     |
     v
  SECTION (paragraph -- grouped phrases, single mood/dynamic)
     |
     v
  WORK (complete piece -- sections organized by formal structure)

FORMAL STRUCTURES:
  ABA (ternary)         -- most common; departure/return
  Rondo (ABACADA)       -- theme returns between episodes
  Through-composed      -- no repetition; continuous development
  Canon                 -- same material offset in time (like musical canon)
  Accumulation          -- phrase 1, phrase 1+2, phrase 1+2+3... (Trisha Brown)
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Modern dance" is not contemporary dance.**
Modern dance is a specific historical movement (Isadora Duncan through Graham through Cunningham, roughly 1900-1960s). Contemporary dance is what is made now. All modern dance is old; not all contemporary dance descends from modern dance.

**Ballet vocabulary is French but the technique codified in Russia.**
The language is French (from Louis XIV's academy), but the technical system was systematized by Marius Petipa in imperial Russia and further codified by Vaganova (Soviet-era method).

**Postmodern dance rejected expression, not structure.**
Cunningham, Rainer, et al. rejected the idea that movement should express emotion or narrative -- they did not reject formal rigor. Cunningham's work is extremely technically demanding.

**Labanotation is not the same as Laban Movement Analysis (LMA).**
Labanotation is a score-writing system for specific movement sequences. LMA is a qualitative analysis framework (Effort, Space, Shape, Body) for describing movement qualities. Same inventor (Rudolf Laban), different tools.

**Butoh is not just dark aesthetic.**
Butoh (ankoku-butoh = "dance of darkness/anguish") emerged directly from post-WWII Japan as a phenomenological response to trauma. Hijikata Tatsumi's first work (1959) was a direct provocation against both Western dance import and traditional Japanese form. It is a philosophical practice, not a style choice.
