# 03 — Heat Transfer

## Conduction, Convection, Radiation, Heat Exchangers

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Fourier's law: q = −kA dT/dx, thermal conductivity values table (metals, polymers, air, water)
- 1D steady-state conduction: plane wall, cylinder, sphere — thermal resistance analogy
- Composite walls: series/parallel thermal resistance networks, contact resistance
- Extended surfaces (fins): fin efficiency, fin effectiveness, common fin geometries
- 2D conduction: shape factors, numerical methods (FDM), isotherms and heat flux lines
- Transient conduction: lumped capacitance (Biot number criterion), semi-infinite solid
- Charts: Heisler charts for plane wall/cylinder/sphere transient solutions
- Newton's law of cooling: q = hA(T_s − T_∞), convection coefficient h
- Dimensionless groups: Nusselt Nu, Reynolds Re, Prandtl Pr, Grashof Gr, Rayleigh Ra
- External forced convection: flat plate (Churchill-Ozoe/Churchill-Bernstein), cylinders, spheres
- Internal forced convection: entry length, fully developed Nusselt (Dittus-Boelter, Gnielinski)
- Natural convection: vertical/horizontal surfaces, enclosures, correlations
- Boiling: pool boiling curve (nucleate/transition/film), critical heat flux, Leidenfrost point
- Condensation: film condensation (Nusselt analysis), dropwise condensation
- Radiation fundamentals: blackbody radiation, Stefan-Boltzmann law, Planck distribution
- Surface properties: emissivity, absorptivity, reflectivity, transmissivity, Kirchhoff's law
- Radiation exchange: view factors, reciprocity, summation rule
- Radiation networks: radiosity, enclosure analysis, grey-diffuse surface assumption
- Heat exchangers: LMTD method, NTU-effectiveness method, fouling factor
- Heat exchanger types: shell-and-tube, plate, finned tube, cross-flow
- Combined modes: radiation + convection in electronic cooling, furnace problems
