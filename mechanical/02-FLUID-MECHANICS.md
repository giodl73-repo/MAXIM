# 02 — Fluid Mechanics

## Statics, Navier-Stokes, Pipe Flow, External Flow, Turbulence

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Fluid properties: density, viscosity (dynamic/kinematic), surface tension, compressibility
- Fluid statics: pressure variation ∂p/∂z = −ρg, manometry, hydrostatic forces on planes and curves
- Buoyancy and stability: Archimedes' principle, metacenter, floating body stability
- Fluid kinematics: streamlines, pathlines, streamfunction, velocity potential
- Reynolds Transport Theorem (RTT): bridge between Lagrangian and Eulerian descriptions
- Conservation equations: continuity (mass), momentum (Euler + Navier-Stokes), energy
- Bernoulli equation: derivation, applicability, extensions (with losses)
- Dimensional analysis: Buckingham Π theorem, important dimensionless groups (Re, Fr, We, Ca)
- Pipe flow: laminar Hagen-Poiseuille, turbulent Colebrook-White, Moody chart, roughness
- Minor losses: entrance, exit, bends, valves — loss coefficients K
- Pipe networks: series/parallel, Hardy-Cross method
- External flow: drag on spheres/cylinders/flat plates, Blasius boundary layer, drag coefficient table
- Lift generation: airfoil pressure distribution, circulation, Kutta condition
- Turbulence: Reynolds decomposition, turbulent stresses, mixing length theory, log-law
- Turbulence models in CFD: k-ε, k-ω SST, Large Eddy Simulation (LES)
- Open channel flow: Manning equation, Froude number, hydraulic jump, gradually varied flow
- Compressible flow: speed of sound, Mach number, isentropic relations, normal shocks
- Turbomachinery: velocity triangles, Euler turbomachinery equation, specific speed
- Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD): finite volume, mesh types, boundary conditions, convergence
