# 04 — Machine Design

## Stress, Fatigue, Gears, Bearings, Shafts, Fasteners

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Design process: requirements, concept generation, concept selection, detailing, optimization
- Stress and strain: normal/shear, Hooke's law, biaxial stress state, Mohr's circle for 2D stress
- Failure theories for ductile materials: von Mises criterion (distortion energy), Tresca criterion
- Failure theories for brittle materials: max normal stress, Mohr's theory
- Stress concentration factors: Kt tables for notches, holes, fillets; theoretical vs fatigue
- Factor of safety: design by analysis, design by code, probabilistic design
- Fatigue: S-N (Wöhler) curve, endurance limit (steels), fatigue limit (aluminum), surface finish factor
- Fatigue criteria: Goodman diagram, Gerber parabola, Soderberg line — mean + alternating stress
- Fracture mechanics in design: critical crack size, inspection intervals, leak-before-break
- Shaft design: combined bending/torsion, ASME and maximum shear stress criteria, DE-Goodman
- Keys, splines, couplings: keyway stress concentration, fit types, flexible couplings
- Rolling element bearings: ball vs roller, load-life equation (L10 = (C/P)^p × 10^6 rev), selection
- Journal (plain) bearings: hydrodynamic lubrication, Sommerfeld number, Petroff equation
- Lubrication regimes: boundary, mixed film, full hydrodynamic, elastohydrodynamic (EHD)
- Gear geometry: module, diametral pitch, pressure angle, involute profile, gear ratio
- Spur, helical, bevel gears: contact ratio, tooth load, AGMA design standard
- Worm gears: lead angle, self-locking condition, efficiency
- Springs: compression/extension spring design, rate, fatigue of springs
- Screws and bolts: thread standards (ISO/UNC), bolt preload, joint separation, bolt group
- Welds: joint types, throat area, allowable stress, fatigue of welds
- Tolerances and fits: ISO system (H7/h6, H7/p6), GD&T, interchangeability
