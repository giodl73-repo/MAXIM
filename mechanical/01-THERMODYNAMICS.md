# 01 — Thermodynamics

## Laws, Cycles, Entropy, Exergy

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Zeroth law: thermal equilibrium, temperature as property
- First law: dU = δQ − δW, energy balance for open and closed systems
- Enthalpy H = U + PV, steady-flow energy equation
- Second law: Kelvin-Planck, Clausius statements, irreversibility
- Entropy: dS ≥ δQ/T, entropy generation, entropy as microscopic disorder (Boltzmann S=k ln Ω)
- Third law: entropy → 0 as T → 0 K, absolute entropy reference
- Carnot cycle: reversibility, maximum efficiency η = 1 − T_c/T_h, entropy diagram
- Ideal gas: pV = nRT, specific heats c_p/c_v, γ = c_p/c_v, isentropic relations
- Rankine cycle (steam power): boiler, turbine, condenser, pump; T-s diagram; superheat/reheat
- Brayton cycle (gas turbine): compressor work, turbine work, thermal efficiency, back work ratio
- Otto cycle (spark ignition): compression ratio, ideal efficiency
- Diesel cycle: constant pressure combustion, efficiency comparison
- Stirling cycle: external combustion, ideal regenerator, zero-emission potential
- Vapor compression refrigeration: COP, refrigerants (HFCs, CO₂, ammonia), enthalpy-entropy chart
- Exergy analysis: available work, exergy destruction, second-law efficiency
- Combustion thermodynamics: stoichiometry, adiabatic flame temperature, excess air
- Phase equilibrium: Clausius-Clapeyron, phase diagrams (P-T, P-v, T-s), triple point, critical point
- Mixtures: Dalton's law, psychrometrics, humidity ratio, dew point, HVAC applications
