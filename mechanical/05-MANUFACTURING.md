# 05 — Manufacturing Processes

## Machining, Forming, Casting, Joining, Additive Manufacturing

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Manufacturing process selection: Taylor's tool life equation, machinability, cost drivers
- Machining fundamentals: chip formation, cutting forces (Merchant model), tool materials
- Turning: parameters (speed/feed/depth), surface roughness, insert grades
- Milling: peripheral vs face milling, climb vs conventional, end mills, cutters
- Drilling and reaming: chisel edge, pilot holes, reamer allowances
- Grinding: abrasive types, bond types, wheel spec, thermal damage, plunge vs traverse
- Non-traditional machining: EDM, laser cutting, waterjet, ECM, ultrasonic
- Metal forming fundamentals: plasticity, flow stress, strain hardening, anisotropy
- Forging: open die, closed die, ring rolling, flashless forging, forgability
- Rolling: flat rolling (reduction, draft, forward slip), profile rolling
- Sheet metal: bending (springback, K-factor), deep drawing (LDR), stretch forming, stamping
- Extrusion: direct vs indirect, hot vs cold, extrusion ratio, die design
- Drawing: wire/tube drawing, reduction per pass
- Casting processes: sand casting, die casting, investment casting, centrifugal casting
- Solidification: nucleation, dendrite growth, shrinkage, porosity, riser design
- Powder metallurgy: pressing, sintering, HIP — for carbides, ceramics, net shape
- Welding processes: SMAW, GMAW/MIG, GTAW/TIG, SAW, electron beam, laser welding
- Weld metallurgy: HAZ, residual stresses, distortion, cracking types (hot/cold)
- Joining: brazing, soldering, adhesive bonding, mechanical fastening
- Additive manufacturing: FDM, SLA/DLP, SLS, DMLS/SLM, EBM, DED — advantages and limits
- Surface finishing: plating, anodizing, painting, shot peening, hard coatings (PVD/CVD)
- Geometric tolerancing: IT grades, surface roughness (Ra, Rz), form tolerances, GD&T
