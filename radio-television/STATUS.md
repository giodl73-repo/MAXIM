## Coverage Notes

Radio and television broadcasting represent the first mass electronic media — technologies that shaped the 20th century politically, culturally, and commercially. This directory covers the electromagnetic physics of radio wave propagation and modulation, the invention history from Maxwell's equations through Marconi's transatlantic transmission to the Audion tube, the creation of regulatory frameworks (FCC, ITU spectrum allocation), the golden age of network broadcasting, the parallel development of television technology (mechanical to electronic, monochrome to color, analog to digital), the cable and satellite revolution that shattered network monopolies, spectrum policy and digital transition, and the current streaming disruption that has fragmented the broadcast audience into algorithmically curated niches.
