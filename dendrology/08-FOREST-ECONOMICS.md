# Forest Economics: Timber Markets, Carbon Credits, Certification

## The Big Picture

Forests generate multiple streams of economic value — timber, non-timber forest products, carbon sequestration, biodiversity, water regulation, recreation — but markets exist for only some of them. The mismatch between full economic value and what markets pay drives deforestation.

```
FOREST VALUE STREAMS

  MARKETED                          NOT (FULLY) MARKETED
  +------------------------+        +---------------------------+
  | Timber (stumpage)      |        | Carbon sequestration      |
  | Pulpwood / fiber       |        | Biodiversity (option val.)|
  | Non-timber products    |        | Water regulation          |
  | (nuts, bark, sap,      |        | Flood buffering           |
  |  mushrooms, game)      |        | Soil stabilization        |
  | Recreation fees        |        | Pollination services      |
  | Carbon credits (VCM)   |        | Climate buffering         |
  +------------------------+        +---------------------------+

  PROBLEM: Conversion to agriculture/development competes with
           low-return marketed value; non-marketed value is lost

  SOLUTION ATTEMPTS:
  REDD+ (payments for avoided deforestation)
  Ecosystem service payments (PES)
  Carbon markets (voluntary + compliance)
  Eco-certification premiums (FSC)
```

## Section 1 — Timber Markets and Stumpage

### Price Chain: Standing Tree to Retail Lumber

```
  [STANDING TREE (stumpage)]
       |
       | Harvesting cost (felling, skidding, loading): $30–80/m³
       v
  [LOG AT ROADSIDE (log price)]
       |
       | Transport: $10–30/m³
       v
  [LOG AT MILL GATE (delivered log price)]
       |
       | Sawmill processing: $80–150/m³ (kerf, chip, sawdust losses: ~50%)
       v
  [DIMENSIONAL LUMBER / CANTS (mill door price)]
       |
       | Drying (kiln): $30–80/m³
       | Planing/grading: $20–50/m³
       v
  [DRESSED LUMBER (wholesale price)]
       |
       | Distribution markup: 20–40%
       v
  [RETAIL LUMBER (board-foot price)]

  STUMPAGE is the residual value after subtracting all costs from retail
  Stumpage = retail price - (harvesting + transport + processing + profit)
  Stumpage can be NEGATIVE on marginal sites (loggers paid to harvest)
```

## Section 2 — Carbon Credit Markets

### Two Market Types

```
  COMPLIANCE MARKET:
    +----------------------------+
    | Cap-and-trade systems      |
    | EU ETS, CA-Quebec, RGGI    |
    | California forestry offsets|
    | Regulated by government    |
    | Higher price stability     |
    | Limited to approved types  |
    +----------------------------+

  VOLUNTARY CARBON MARKET (VCM):
    +-----------------------------+
    | No regulatory obligation    |
    | Corporate net-zero targets  |
    | Biodiversity co-benefits    |
    | Verra VCS, Gold Standard,   |
    | CAR, ACS, ACR               |
    | Lower prices; quality varies|
    +-----------------------------+

  FORESTRY CREDITS PRIMARILY IN VCM
  Compliance markets accept limited forestry; often political constraints
```

### Verra VCS (Verified Carbon Standard)

The dominant voluntary carbon standard for forest projects.

```
  VCS CREDIT TYPES (forestry):
  +-- REDD+ (Reduced Emissions from Deforestation and Degradation)
  |   Baseline: what would have happened without project
  |   Credit = baseline emissions - actual emissions
  |   Additionality: must prove deforestation would have occurred
  |
  +-- Improved Forest Management (IFM)
  |   Extending rotation ages, switching harvest systems
  |   Credited for additional carbon stored vs. BAU management
  |
  +-- Afforestation/Reforestation (A/R)
  |   Planting trees on previously non-forested land
  |   Credited for carbon accumulation in growing stand
  |
  +-- Avoided Conversion of Forests (ACF)
  |   Protecting forests from non-forest land use conversion

  VCS PROCESS:
  Project Design Document (PDD)
  --> Third-party Validation (Verra-accredited verifier)
  --> Registration in Verra registry
  --> Implementation + monitoring
  --> Verification (every 5 yr)
  --> Issuance of Verified Carbon Units (VCUs): 1 VCU = 1 tonne CO2e

  CREDIT PRICE (VCM, 2024): $5–$25/tonne CO2e typical range
  Nature-based high-integrity (REDD+ with co-benefits): $15–40+
```

### Permanence and Buffer Pools

The fundamental risk in forest carbon credits: what if the forest is cut down or burns after credits are issued?

```
  PERMANENCE PROBLEM:
  Credits are issued for 30–100 year carbon storage
  But forest can be destroyed (fire, harvest, pest)

  SOLUTION — BUFFER POOL:
  Project withholds % of credits to buffer pool (15–40% typical)
  Buffer covers non-permanence events across entire portfolio
  Verra AFOLU Non-Permanence Risk Tool determines buffer % required

  REVERSAL EVENTS:
  +-- Accidental (wildfire): buffer pool absorbs loss
  +-- Force majeure (insect outbreak): project-specific rules
  +-- Intentional reversal (owner harvests protected area):
      owner must replace from elsewhere or buffer absorbs
```

## Section 3 — REDD+ (Reduced Emissions from Deforestation and Degradation)

REDD+ is the UNFCCC-recognized framework for paying developing countries to reduce deforestation. The "+" refers to sustainable forest management and carbon stocks enhancement.

```
  REDD+ STRUCTURE:

  [UNFCCC PARIS AGREEMENT]
  Article 5: REDD+ recognized as mitigation approach
       |
       v
  [NATIONAL LEVEL: FREL (Forest Reference Emission Level)]
  Country establishes historical deforestation rate as baseline
  Measured in Mg CO2e/yr from historical forest cover change
       |
       v
  [SUBNATIONAL / PROJECT LEVEL]
  Individual projects within national framework
  Nested approach: project within jurisdiction within country
       |
       v
  [RESULTS-BASED PAYMENTS]
  UNFCCC Green Climate Fund, bilateral agreements, voluntary markets
  Payment for verified emission reductions vs. FREL

  ADDITIONALITY TEST:
  Would deforestation have occurred without REDD+ payment?
  Threat must be real, credible, documented
  Most contested part of REDD+ methodology
```

### High-Profile REDD+ Controversies

| Issue | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| "Ghost credits" (2023 Guardian/Zeit investigation) | Verra's REDD+ projects credited for forest that was never at risk of deforestation; ~90% of REDD+ rainforest credits not legitimate by independent analysis |
| Leakage | Protecting one area displaces deforestation elsewhere; displacement rate can be 5–90% |
| Indigenous rights | REDD+ projects have removed indigenous communities from ancestral lands |
| Additionality | Difficult to prove counterfactual; business-as-usual baselines often inflated |

## Section 4 — NPV of Rotation Analysis

Forest investment analysis requires discounting future cash flows from a multi-decade investment.

### Basic Rotation NPV

```
  NPV OF SINGLE ROTATION:

  NPV = -C0 + R_t × e^(-i×t) - annual_costs × Σ(e^(-i×k)) for k=1 to t

  Where:
    C0    = establishment cost (planting, site prep): $500–3000/ha
    R_t   = revenue at harvest age t: volume × stumpage price
    i     = discount rate (real, inflation-adjusted): typically 4–8%
    annual_costs = taxes, protection, management: $20–100/ha/yr

  EXAMPLE: Douglas-fir plantation, Pacific NW
  C0 = $1,500/ha (site prep + planting)
  Annual costs = $40/ha/yr
  Rotation = 50 yr
  Volume at harvest = 600 m³/ha
  Stumpage price = $80/m³
  Gross revenue = $48,000/ha

  NPV (at 5% real discount, 50 yr):
  PV of $48,000 received in 50 yr = $48,000 × e^(-0.05×50)
                                  = $48,000 × 0.0821 = $3,940/ha
  PV of annual costs ($40 × annuity factor at 5%, 50yr)
                                  = $40 × 18.26 = $730/ha
  PV of establishment           = $1,500/ha

  NPV = $3,940 - $730 - $1,500 = +$1,710/ha

  LEV (land expectation value, infinite rotations):
  LEV = NPV_single_rotation × e^(i×t) / (e^(i×t) - 1)
      = $1,710 × (e^(0.05×50)) / (e^(0.05×50) - 1)
      = $1,710 × 12.18 / 11.18 = $1,862/ha
```

### Carbon Premium Impact

If the forest qualifies for carbon credits under IFM (extended rotation):

```
  EXTENDED ROTATION (70 yr vs. 50 yr):
  Additional volume: 100 m³/ha
  Additional stumpage: $8,000/ha (discounted 20 yr more = $3,000 PV)
  Additional carbon: 150 t CO2e/ha stored
  At $15/t = $2,250/ha (received over 20 yr; discounted ~$1,400 PV)

  Net change: PV timber gain + PV carbon - extra annual costs
  = $3,000 + $1,400 - $800 = +$3,600/ha increase in rotation NPV

  CONCLUSION: Carbon credits can flip the economics of longer rotations
              from negative to strongly positive at modest credit prices
```

## Section 5 — Payment for Ecosystem Services (PES)

PES programs compensate landowners for maintaining ecosystem services beyond timber production.

```
  PES DESIGN PRINCIPLES (Wunder 2005/2015):
  1. Voluntary transaction
  2. Well-defined environmental service (or land use as proxy)
  3. At least one ES buyer
  4. At least one ES provider/seller
  5. Conditionality (payment only if service delivered — monitoring required)

  EXAMPLES:
  +-- Costa Rica: PSA program (1997); government pays landowners $64–130/ha/yr
      to maintain forest cover; funded by water user fees + fuel tax + foreign aid
  +-- New York City watershed (Catskills/Delaware): pays farmers to manage land
      in watershed to avoid building $8 billion filtration plant
  +-- China: Sloped Land Conversion Program (Grain for Green): pays farmers
      to convert marginal cropland to forest; 9 M ha enrolled
  +-- US Conservation Reserve Program: pays farmers to retire cropland;
      some forest enrollment; $80–300/acre/yr
```

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| Question | Answer/Concept |
|----------|----------------|
| What is stumpage? | Net value of standing timber: retail price minus all harvesting + processing costs |
| Why does economic rotation differ from biological rotation? | Opportunity cost of capital tied in standing timber shortens optimal harvest age |
| What is 1 VCU worth? | 1 tonne CO2 equivalent; market price $5–40 depending on quality, co-benefits |
| Why do REDD+ credits have low integrity concerns? | Additionality is hard to prove; baselines inflated; leakage unaccounted |
| What is the buffer pool? | Reserve of unissued credits to cover non-permanence events (fire, harvest) |
| How do carbon payments change rotation age decisions? | By monetizing additional carbon sequestration of longer rotations, they increase NPV of delay |
| What is LEV (Land Expectation Value)? | NPV of all future timber rotations on a site; the maximum price to pay for bare land |

## Common Confusion Points

**Carbon credits do not equal permanent carbon storage.**
A VCU represents a commitment to store carbon for the credit period (typically 30–100 yr). If the forest burns or is harvested, the credit must be replaced from buffer pool or by the project developer. Credits are not absolute permanent guarantees.

**Board feet and cubic meters measure different things.**
Board foot (BF) = 144 in³ = 12"×12"×1" — a unit of volume for sawn lumber. Cubic meter is volumetric measure of log. Conversion depends on species, grade, and mill efficiency. Rule of thumb: ~400 BF/m³ for rough-cut softwood, but varies 10–20%.

**Stumpage can be zero or negative on marginal sites.**
If harvest and transport costs exceed the delivered log value, stumpage is zero or the landowner pays loggers to remove timber. This is common in remote, low-value stands or where only small-diameter trees exist.

**REDD+ payments go to countries, not necessarily to forest owners.**
The UNFCCC structure pays national governments for national-level REDD+ results. Whether that payment reaches local communities or landowners depends entirely on national policy — often it does not, creating equity problems.
