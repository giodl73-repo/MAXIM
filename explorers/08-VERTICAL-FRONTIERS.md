## Maurice Herzog (1919–2012) and Louis Lachenal (1921–1955)

### Bio Snapshot

**Herzog**: French alpinist. Led the 1950 French Annapurna expedition.
**Lachenal**: Chamonix mountain guide. The most technically skilled climber on the expedition. Arguably the better mountaineer of the two.

### Annapurna — First 8,000m Summit (June 3, 1950)

```
THE ANNAPURNA EXPEDITION (1950)
==================================

  Context:
    No 8,000m peak had ever been climbed.
    14 peaks exist above 8,000m (all in Himalayas/Karakoram).
    Nepal had only recently opened to foreigners (1949).

  The French team: 8 climbers. Goal: either Annapurna (8,091m) or
  Dhaulagiri (8,167m). Neither had been surveyed closely.
  They had to find and map the routes during the expedition itself.

  Approach to summit camp:
    They found an approach route late.
    The summit was reached early in the monsoon season.
    Risk: monsoon storms imminent.

  THE SUMMIT DAY (June 3, 1950):
    Herzog and Lachenal leave Camp V. No supplemental oxygen.
    Herzog: willing to risk everything for the summit.
    Lachenal: wanted to turn back, then agreed to continue.
    Summit: 2 PM. Photographs. Flag. No shelter, no time to rest.

  THE DESCENT — FROSTBITE AND NEAR-DEATH:
    Herzog dropped his gloves at the summit. Immediately frostbitten hands.
    Bivouac in a crevasse. Snow fell on them.
    Lachenal: severe frostbite on feet. Fell into a crevasse.
    Herzog: hallucinating. Hands and feet destroyed by frostbite.

    The retreat: multiple bivouacs in worsening weather.
    Medical care at base camp: improvised surgery. No painkillers.
    Evacuation: carried out on stretchers by Sherpas and porters.
    In hospital: Herzog lost all his toes and most of his fingers.
    Lachenal: lost toes; less severe. Later died in a ski accident (1955).

  SIGNIFICANCE:
    First of the fourteen 8,000m peaks to be summited.
    Proved that the highest mountains could be climbed.
    The other 13 were all summited within the next decade.
    Opened the high-altitude mountaineering era.
```

**The controversy**: Lachenal's diary, published decades after his death, reveals that he did not share Herzog's romantic attitude. He was a professional guide who felt pressured to continue by the expedition's dynamics. The heroic narrative in Herzog's book (*Annapurna*, 1951 — the best-selling mountaineering book ever) is somewhat at odds with Lachenal's private account.

## Edmund Hillary (1919–2008) and Tenzing Norgay (1914–1986)

### Bio Snapshot

**Hillary**: New Zealand beekeeper. No formal mountaineering training — learned on New Zealand glaciers. Joined the 1953 British Everest expedition as a member.

**Tenzing Norgay**: Nepali Sherpa. Born in Nepal (of Tibetan heritage). Made 7 previous Everest attempts. The most experienced high-altitude climber in the 1953 expedition.

### Everest Summit (May 29, 1953)

```
THE 1953 BRITISH EVEREST EXPEDITION
======================================

  Leader: John Hunt.
  Two summit attempts planned:
  Attempt 1: Evans + Bourdillon. Reached South Summit (8,760m). Turned back.
  Attempt 2: Hillary + Tenzing.

  MAY 28: Clinton Camp established at ~8,500m. Last night before summit.
           Hillary and Tenzing. No tent. Slept in sleeping bags.
           Temperature: -27°C.

  MAY 29, 1953:
    6:30 AM: depart.
    Supplemental oxygen (closed-circuit sets, 3L/min flow).
    The "Hillary Step": a 12-meter vertical rock wall near summit.
    Hillary chimneyed up it. Tenzing followed.
    11:30 AM: SUMMIT.
    Time at summit: 15 minutes.
    Photographs: Tenzing holding flags (Hillary's camera). No photo of Hillary.
    Hillary's account: looked for footprints above (Mallory+Irvine question).
    Found none.

  WHO STEPPED ON SUMMIT FIRST?
    Both maintained the issue was "not important."
    Tenzing's son (writing posthumously) said Tenzing told him Hillary went first.
    Hillary's private letters say the same.
    This was kept ambiguous for decades as a diplomatic matter.

  RETURN:
    Base camp knew: Hillary gave an "O.K." signal from the South Col.
    James Morris (Times correspondent) relayed the news to London.
    Arrived June 2 — Queen Elizabeth II's coronation day.
    "Queen crowns Everest" — the front pages.
```

**Tenzing's legacy**: A Sherpa from a poor family became one of the most celebrated men on Earth in 1953. He trained the Indian mountaineering program. The Tenzing Norgay National Adventure Award (India's highest adventure award) is named for him. His daughter later said he was deeply affected by the celebrity, finding it at odds with his private, spiritual life.

## Reinhold Messner (1944–present)

### Bio Snapshot

South Tyrolean (Italian) mountaineer. Began climbing in the Dolomites as a child. Technical rock and ice climber. Transformed high-altitude mountaineering from aided ascents to "fair means" alpinism — no fixed ropes pre-placed along the route, no supplemental oxygen.

### The Eight-Thousanders Project (1970–1986)

```
MESSNER'S FOURTEEN 8,000m PEAKS
=================================

  Nanga Parbat (8,126m): June 27, 1970. First 8,000m peak.
    First ascent of Rupal Face (hardest route at the time).
    Brother Günther died on descent.
    Messner: severe frostbite.

  Key records:
    1978: First Everest without supplemental oxygen (with Habeler).
    1980: First Everest solo (without O2). Monsoon season. North Face.
    1982: First "triple crown" in one year: 3 × 8,000m in one calendar year.
    1986: K2 and Lhotse — completedall 14 eight-thousanders.
           First person to climb all 14.
           All without supplemental oxygen.

  SIGNIFICANCE OF NO SUPPLEMENTAL OXYGEN:
    With O2: effective altitude reduced by ~1,000m.
    Above 8,000m without O2: in the "Death Zone."
    O2 partial pressure insufficient for sustained aerobic exertion.
    Body slowly dies. Cells fail. Judgment impaired.
    Messner proved it possible. Previously considered impossible.

    "Without supplemental oxygen, I'm not really at 8,000m —
    I'm at 9,000m." The body is working twice as hard.

    The cognitive impairment at extreme altitude:
    At 8,500m without O2: equivalent to breath-holding for 30 seconds.
    Continuously.
    Messner reported experiences he could not explain rationally —
    later called the "third man factor" (imagined companion).
```

## Jacques Cousteau (1910–1997)

### Bio Snapshot

French naval officer and oceanographer. Co-inventor of the aqualung (scuba) with Émile Gagnan (1943). Ship Calypso: 48 years of oceanographic expeditions. 120+ documentary films. *The Silent World* (1956 film, co-directed with Louis Malle) won the Palme d'Or and Academy Award. Made the deep ocean familiar to tens of millions of people.

### The Aqualung and Scuba Diving

```
THE AQUALUNG (1943)
====================

  PROBLEM (pre-1943):
    Underwater breathing apparatuses required:
    (A) Hose connected to a surface air supply — limited range.
    (B) "Closed-circuit" rebreathers — complex, dangerous (pure O2 toxic > 6m).

  COUSTEAU-GAGNAN SOLUTION:
    On-demand regulator: valve opens ONLY when the diver inhales.
    Pressure at mouth = ambient water pressure (not tank pressure).
    This was the key: the regulator matches breathing effort to depth.
    Tank of compressed air → regulator → diver breathes at ambient pressure.
    At 10m: 2 atm. Regulator delivers air at 2 atm. Diver breathes normally.

  AQUALUNG:
    Tank: compressed air (not pure O2 — avoids oxygen toxicity).
    Regulator: Cousteau-Gagnan demand valve.
    Result: scuba diving. Can dive to 40–50m safely.

  LIMITATIONS:
    Nitrogen narcosis below ~30m (similar to alcohol intoxication).
    Decompression sickness (the bends): if ascent too fast, dissolved N2
    forms bubbles in blood → fatal. Requires decompression stops.
    These limits eventually pushed technical diving to mixed gases
    (trimix, heliox) for deeper diving.
```

**The Calypso expeditions**: Cousteau's research vessel (a converted minesweeper) allowed him to dive, film, and document the ocean from 1950 to 1996. He filmed coral reefs (and their degradation), shark behavior, ocean floor topography, and ancient shipwrecks. His 1970s television series (*The Undersea World of Jacques Cousteau*) reached hundreds of millions of viewers.

**Environmental advocacy**: By the 1970s, Cousteau had shifted from exploration to advocacy — documenting ocean pollution, coral bleaching, and overfishing. He was among the first to describe ocean degradation at scale.

## Common Confusion Points

**"Hillary stood on Everest first."**
Almost certainly — Tenzing's son and Hillary's own private letters indicate Hillary was first on the summit. But both maintained polite ambiguity for decades because of the symbolic importance of Tenzing for Nepal/India. The diplomatic non-answer "we reached the summit together" was a deliberate choice.

**"Messner was just stronger than other climbers."**
His physiological capacity for O2 deprivation is extraordinary — he did extensive physiological testing. But the approach was also different: smaller teams, no fixed ropes, no supplemental oxygen, faster movement. He was doing "alpine style" ascents on peaks that previous expeditions had taken months to siege with hundreds of porters. It was a philosophical and logistical change as much as a physical one.

**"Cousteau invented diving."**
Helmeted dive suits had existed since the 1820s (Augustus Siebe). Surface-supply diving was used for underwater construction for over a century. Cousteau made autonomous underwater breathing practical for individual divers with the demand regulator — freeing divers from hoses and enabling filming, science, and sport diving.
