# Atomic Theory — Dalton, Avogadro, Gay-Lussac, Berzelius

## The Question: What Are Elements Made Of?

Lavoisier had defined elements operationally (can't be decomposed). But what ARE
they? Dalton's atomic theory answered: elements consist of atoms — indivisible
particles characteristic of each element.

```
THE ATOMIC THEORY PROBLEM
===========================

LAVOISIER'S QUESTION (1789): What is an element?
  Answer: Operationally — something that can't be decomposed.

DALTON'S QUESTION (1803): Why do elements combine in fixed ratios?
  Observation: When carbon and oxygen combine, they form two compounds:
    "fixed air" (CO₂): 12 parts carbon to 32 parts oxygen (3:8 by mass)
    "carbonic oxide" (CO): 12 parts carbon to 16 parts oxygen (3:4 by mass)
  The oxygen ratio is exactly 2:1.

LAW OF MULTIPLE PROPORTIONS (Dalton):
  When two elements form more than one compound, the masses of one element
  that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in a ratio of small integers.

  → This makes sense IF matter is made of discrete atoms!
  → Atoms combine in whole-number ratios.

AVOGADRO'S QUESTION (1811): Why do gas volumes combine in simple ratios?
  Dalton's model doesn't explain why equal volumes of gases contain
  equal numbers of "particles" under the same conditions.
  Avogadro's answer: Molecular gases (H₂, O₂, N₂) — atoms come in pairs.
```

## John Dalton (1766–1844)

### Who He Was

English meteorologist and chemist, Manchester. Color-blind (the condition is
still called "daltonism" in some languages). Quaker. Never married. Worked
with extreme simplicity — his laboratory was his lodgings.

### The Contribution: Atomic Theory

**The Five Postulates**

```
DALTON'S ATOMIC THEORY (1803–1808)
=====================================

1. All matter is composed of extremely small indivisible particles called ATOMS.
2. Atoms of a given element are identical in mass and properties.
   Atoms of different elements differ in mass and properties.
3. Atoms cannot be created, subdivided, or destroyed.
4. Atoms of different elements combine in simple whole-number ratios to form
   compound "atoms" (molecules, in modern terms).
5. In a chemical reaction, atoms are rearranged — they are neither created
   nor destroyed.

WHY REVOLUTIONARY:
  - Postulate 2: explained why elements have characteristic properties
  - Postulate 4: explained law of definite proportions
    (every sample of CO₂ is 12C to 32O = 3:8 by mass, always)
  - Postulate 4 + multiple proportions: ratio of oxygen in CO to CO₂ is 1:2
    because CO = 1 oxygen, CO₂ = 2 oxygens per carbon atom

WHAT WAS WRONG:
  - Atoms are NOT indivisible (have electrons, protons, neutrons — discovered 1897–1932)
  - Atoms of the same element are NOT identical (isotopes — same element,
    different neutron count, different mass)
  - Atomic mass ratios Dalton measured were mostly wrong (confused molecules
    with atoms: thought water was HO, not H₂O)
```

**Dalton's Atomic Weights**

Dalton assigned atomic weights based on combining masses.
He set hydrogen = 1 (lightest known element).

```
DALTON'S ATOMIC WEIGHT TABLE (1803) — SELECTED
===============================================

  Hydrogen: 1
  Carbon: 5 (actual: 12)
  Oxygen: 7 (actual: 16)
  Nitrogen: 5 (actual: 14)
  Phosphorus: 9 (actual: 31)
  Sulfur: 13 (actual: 32)

WHY SO WRONG:
  He assumed the simplest possible formulas.
  Water: HO (wrong), so mass of oxygen from water mass/H ratio → 7 not 16.
  Actually water is H₂O — need 2 hydrogen per 1 oxygen.
  His numbers were wrong but his METHOD was right.

Avogadro's work (1811) and Cannizzaro's later clarification (1858)
fixed the formula determination problem.
```

## Amedeo Avogadro (1776–1856)

### Who He Was

Italian lawyer and physicist. Professor at Turin. Published his hypothesis in
1811 in a French journal. Ignored for ~50 years.

### The Contribution: Molecules and Avogadro's Hypothesis

**The Gay-Lussac Law (Background)**

Gay-Lussac observed (1808):
- 2 volumes hydrogen + 1 volume oxygen → 2 volumes water vapor
- 1 volume nitrogen + 1 volume oxygen → 2 volumes nitric oxide
- 1 volume nitrogen + 3 volumes hydrogen → 2 volumes ammonia

Volumes combine in simple integer ratios. Why?

Dalton's explanation: failed — he couldn't explain why 1 volume H + 1 volume O
shouldn't give 1 volume of water (not 2).

**Avogadro's Hypothesis (1811)**

```
AVOGADRO'S HYPOTHESIS
======================

"Equal volumes of all gases, at the same temperature and pressure,
contain equal numbers of molecules."

This solves Gay-Lussac's puzzle:
  2 volumes H₂ + 1 volume O₂ → 2 volumes H₂O

  2n H₂ molecules + n O₂ molecules → 2n H₂O molecules
  2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O (each H₂ is two hydrogen atoms bonded together)

KEY INSIGHT: Dalton thought H and O were single atoms in elemental form.
  Avogadro proposed: hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen are DIATOMIC (H₂, O₂, N₂).
  This resolved all the volume ratio paradoxes.

WHY IGNORED:
  Dalton and Berzelius (the authorities) rejected it.
  Dalton: "same atoms repel each other — can't bond together."
  The dispute was resolved by Cannizzaro at Karlsruhe (1860) using Avogadro's
  method systematically. Two years after Avogadro died.
```

**Avogadro's Number**

Avogadro himself never calculated what we now call Avogadro's number.
It's named for him because his hypothesis is the conceptual foundation:
a mole of anything contains N_A particles.

N_A = 6.022 × 10²³ mol⁻¹ (Avogadro's constant)

This was first estimated by Loschmidt (1865) and measured precisely by Perrin
(1908–1909) using Brownian motion — simultaneously confirming the atomic theory
(which Ernst Mach still denied in 1905).

## Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac (1778–1850)

### Who He Was

French chemist and physicist. Made balloon ascents to high altitude to study
the atmosphere. Discovered the volume ratios of combining gases. Also discovered
(with Thenard) that some compounds contain two different proportions of the same
element — a key observation for Dalton's law of multiple proportions.

### The Contribution: Gas Laws and Combining Volumes

**Gay-Lussac's Law (1802)**

P ∝ T at constant V and n (gas pressure proportional to absolute temperature)

Combined with Boyle's (PV = const) and Charles's (V ∝ T) laws:
PV = nRT — the ideal gas law.

**Law of Combining Volumes (1808)**

```
GAY-LUSSAC'S COMBINING VOLUMES LAW
=====================================

When gases react at the same temperature and pressure, the volumes of
gaseous reactants and products are in simple whole-number ratios.

Examples:
  H₂ + Cl₂ → 2 HCl     (1:1:2 volume ratio)
  2 H₂ + O₂ → 2 H₂O    (2:1:2)
  N₂ + 3 H₂ → 2 NH₃    (1:3:2)

This is now understood via:
  - Avogadro's equal volumes = equal molecules
  - Stoichiometry follows from atomic composition
```

## Jöns Jacob Berzelius (1779–1848)

### Who He Was

Swedish chemist. Dominated chemistry in the first half of the 19th century.
Introduced the modern chemical symbols (H, O, Fe, Cu...), determined atomic
weights with great precision, discovered several elements (cerium, thorium,
selenium, silicon), and developed dualistic electrochemical theory.

### The Contribution: Chemical Symbols and Accurate Atomic Weights

**Chemical Symbols (1813)**

```
CHEMICAL NOTATION — BERZELIUS'S INVENTION
==========================================

Before Berzelius: Lavoisier's circle/square symbols, Dalton's dots-in-circles.
  Not printable, not memorable, not universal.

Berzelius (1813):
  Use the first letter (or first two letters) of the element's Latin name.
    H = Hydrogen (Hydrogenium)
    O = Oxygen (Oxygenium)
    N = Nitrogen (Nitrogenium)
    Fe = Iron (Ferrum)
    Pb = Lead (Plumbum)
    Hg = Mercury (Hydrargyrum)
    Na = Sodium (Natrium)
    K = Potassium (Kalium)

Compounds:
  H₂O, CO₂, H₂SO₄, NaCl
  The modern chemical formula notation.

This system (with modifications) is still in use worldwide.
Every periodic table uses Berzelius's symbols.
```

**Atomic Weights (1810–1826)**

Berzelius determined atomic weights with unprecedented accuracy:

```
BERZELIUS'S ATOMIC WEIGHTS (selected)
======================================

Element    Berzelius    Modern
------     ---------    ------
Oxygen     16.026       15.999
Sulfur     32.09        32.06
Chlorine   35.41        35.45
Carbon     12.25        12.011
Calcium    40.97        40.078

He used oxygen = 16 as reference (rather than Dalton's hydrogen = 1).
His values for many elements were accurate to within 0.5%.
He analyzed over 2,000 compounds to determine these weights.
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Dalton's atomic theory is correct"** — The core insight (discrete atoms in fixed
ratios) is correct. But the specific postulates have been modified: atoms are
divisible (into electrons, protons, neutrons), atoms of the same element are not
identical (isotopes exist), and Dalton's own atomic weights were wrong. The theory
is correct at its conceptual level; incorrect in its details.

**"Avogadro was ignored because he was obscure"** — He published in a prominent
French journal. He was ignored because Dalton and Berzelius — the most influential
chemists — rejected his hypothesis on theoretical grounds they thought were sound.
Science is a social enterprise; authority matters. Cannizzaro's 1860 lecture at
the Karlsruhe congress finally convinced chemists to adopt Avogadro's view.

**"Mole is Avogadro's invention"** — The mole is a unit of amount. Avogadro's
hypothesis is the conceptual basis for it (equal volumes = equal moles). The
mole was formalized later; N_A was first estimated by Loschmidt in 1865. The
IUPAC redefined the mole in 2019: exactly 6.02214076 × 10²³ elementary entities
(fixed exact number, not derived from measurement).

**"Berzelius created the modern periodic table"** — He created the symbols and
accurate atomic weights that ENABLED Mendeleev to see the periodic pattern.
Without Berzelius's weights, Mendeleev couldn't have organized elements by
atomic weight. The table is Mendeleev's; the foundation data is Berzelius's.
