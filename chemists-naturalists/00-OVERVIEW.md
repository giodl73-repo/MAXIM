# Chemists and Naturalists — Landscape and Roster

## The Landscape

Chemistry and natural history share a family resemblance: both are about
cataloging and understanding the diversity of matter and life. The figures here
range from the classifiers (Linnaeus giving order to biological chaos) through
the discoverers of chemical elements and reactions to the molecular biologists
who unified chemistry and biology in the 20th century.

## Selection Criteria

These are figures who:
1. **Discovered or defined fundamental concepts** — atomic theory, organic chemistry,
   evolution, the periodic table, the chemical bond
2. **Changed the conceptual framework** — not just new facts but new ways of thinking
3. **Bridged disciplines** — the chemists who influenced biology, the naturalists
   who made geography quantitative
4. **Leave artifacts still in daily use** — Avogadro's number, Arrhenius equation,
   Mendeleev's table, the double helix

## Why This Matters for a Technologist

The connections between chemistry/natural history and technology are direct:

```
CHEMISTRY IN TECHNOLOGY
=========================

Concept                    Chemist(s)              Where it appears
-----------------------    ----------------        ----------------
Arrhenius equation         Arrhenius               Semiconductor reliability (MTTF models)
  (rate ∝ exp(-Ea/kT))                             Battery degradation models
                                                   Chemical vapor deposition in chip fab
Periodic table             Mendeleev               Materials selection for electronics
  (element properties)                             Semiconductor dopants (boron, arsenic)
Nitrogen fixation          Haber + Bosch           Global food supply (fertilizers)
                                                   ~50% of nitrogen in human bodies
                                                   came from Haber-Bosch process
Polymer chemistry          Staudinger (later)      All plastic in every device
DNA double helix           Crick+Watson+Franklin   Bioinformatics, genetic algorithms,
                                                   DNA storage of data (research)
X-ray crystallography      Bragg, Hodgkin           Drug design, protein structure
Chemical bond theory       Lewis, Pauling           Materials science, drug-receptor binding
pH, ionization             Arrhenius, Henderson    Battery chemistry, semiconductor fab
Chirality                  Pasteur                 Drug design (thalidomide tragedy)
```
