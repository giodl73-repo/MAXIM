# Organic Chemistry — Wöhler, Kekulé, Pasteur, Liebig

## The Vital Force and Its Death

Before 1828, organic compounds (carbon-based compounds from living things) were
thought to require a special "vital force" — the energy of life — to synthesize
them. Inorganic chemistry (minerals, salts) obeyed different rules than organic
chemistry (proteins, sugars, fats). This was vitalism.

```
THE VITAL FORCE DOCTRINE
==========================

VITALISM:
  Organic compounds can only be made by living organisms.
  They contain a "vital force" absent in dead matter.
  Chemistry (inorganic) cannot produce them.

  Practical implication: Organic chemistry is descriptive (identify
  what organisms make) not synthetic (make it yourself).

WÖHLER'S EXPERIMENT (1828):
  Ammonium cyanate (inorganic salt) → urea (organic compound).
  Same atoms, different arrangement. No vital force needed.

  NH₄OCN → CO(NH₂)₂
  Silver cyanate + ammonium chloride → urea (+ silver chloride)

  First synthesis of an organic compound from inorganic starting materials.
  VITAL FORCE IS DEAD.

CONSEQUENCES:
  - Organic compounds are molecules governed by the same chemistry as inorganic
  - Organic chemistry = chemistry of carbon compounds (not "life chemistry")
  - Synthesis of organic compounds becomes possible
  - The pharmaceutical industry becomes conceivable
```

## Friedrich Wöhler (1800–1882)

### Who He Was

German chemist, Göttingen. Student of Berzelius. Close friend of Liebig
(the two maintained a warm correspondence for decades, unusual in competitive
19th-century chemistry).

### The Contribution: Synthesis of Urea and Aluminum

**Urea Synthesis (1828)**

```
WÖHLER'S UREA SYNTHESIS
=========================

EXPERIMENT:
  Heated ammonium cyanate (NH₄CNO) → got white crystals.
  "I can no longer, so to speak, hold my chemical water and must tell you
  that I can make urea without needing a kidney, and indeed without the aid
  of any animal, man or dog." (Letter to Berzelius, 1828)

Urea CO(NH₂)₂:
  - Major nitrogen waste product of mammalian metabolism
  - Excreted in urine (Latin: urina → urea)
  - Previously only obtainable from urine

ISOMERS: Wöhler also recognized that ammonium cyanate and urea have
  the same formula (CH₄N₂O) but different properties — one of the first
  recognized cases of isomerism.

HISTORICAL NOTE: The death of vitalism was not instantaneous.
  Many chemists continued to believe organic compounds required
  "organic sources" for decades. Wöhler's synthesis was one step
  in a gradual transition.
```

**Isolation of Aluminum (1827)**

Wöhler first isolated pure aluminum metal by reduction with potassium.
Before this, aluminum was more expensive than gold (Napoleon served
honored guests with aluminum utensils; lesser guests got silver).
The Hall-Héroult process (1886) made aluminum cheap by electrolysis.

## Justus von Liebig (1803–1873)

### Who He Was

German chemist, Giessen. Created the modern chemistry laboratory — the first
teaching laboratory where students learned by doing experiments (the "Giessen
model" spread worldwide). Developed agricultural chemistry. Invented bouillon
cubes (Liebig's Extract of Meat).

### The Contribution: Organic Analysis and Agricultural Chemistry

**Organic Analysis (1831)**

```
LIEBIG'S COMBUSTION ANALYSIS
==============================

PROBLEM: Determine the molecular formula of an organic compound.
  What is the ratio of C, H, O, N in a compound?

LIEBIG'S METHOD (1831):
  Burn a known mass of the compound in a tube with excess oxygen.
  Pass the products through absorbents:
    KOH/NaOH pellets absorb CO₂ (→ weight increase = mass of CO₂ = mass of C)
    CaCl₂ absorbs H₂O (→ weight increase = mass of H₂O = mass of H)
    Difference in mass → oxygen content

  From masses:
    Carbon: mass(CO₂) × 12/44
    Hydrogen: mass(H₂O) × 2/18
    Oxygen: by difference

THIS ENABLED:
  - Determining molecular formulas of thousands of organic compounds
  - Building the systematic classification of organic chemistry
  - Understanding the relationship between structure and properties

Liebig used this to analyze ~300 compounds by 1840.
The method is still used (with modern instruments) in organic chemistry.
```

**Agricultural Chemistry (1840)**

Liebig's *Organic Chemistry in its Applications to Agriculture and Physiology*
(1840) founded agricultural chemistry:
- Plants get carbon from CO₂ in air, not from humus in soil
- Plants require nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium from soil (NPK — still used)
- The soil element present in the least relative amount limits plant growth
  (Liebig's law of the minimum — a precursor to limiting factors in ecology)
- Artificial fertilizers are possible (realized by Haber-Bosch, File 09)

## August Kekulé (1829–1896)

### Who He Was

German chemist, Bonn. Studied architecture before switching to chemistry.
Developed the theory of carbon tetravalence (carbon makes 4 bonds) and
the structure of benzene.

### The Contribution: Carbon Valence and Benzene

**Tetravalent Carbon (1858)**

```
KEKULÉ'S CARBON THEORY
========================

PROBLEM (1850s): What determines molecular structure?
  We know atoms combine in fixed ratios.
  But HOW do they connect?

KEKULÉ (1857–1858), independently COUPER (1858):
  Carbon is TETRAVALENT: forms exactly 4 bonds.
  Carbon atoms can bond to each other (carbon chains).

  H-C-H   →  Methane CH₄: carbon with 4 H
  |
  H

  H₃C-CH₃   →  Ethane C₂H₆: two carbons bonded, each with 3H

THIS EXPLAINS:
  - Why organic chemistry has such vast diversity:
    carbon chains can be long, branched, cyclic
  - Homologous series: CH₄, C₂H₆, C₃H₈, C₄H₁₀ (alkanes: n carbons, 2n+2 hydrogens)
  - Why organic compounds have specific H/C ratios

Combined with the concept of DOUBLE and TRIPLE bonds (from analysis of
alkenes and alkynes), the framework for all organic structural chemistry was set.
```

**The Benzene Ring (1865)**

```
THE BENZENE PROBLEM
====================

Benzene: molecular formula C₆H₆.
  Carbon makes 4 bonds. With 6 carbons and 6 hydrogens:
  Each carbon must use some bonds to bond to carbons, not hydrogens.

  Predicted from tetrahedral carbon: should be highly reactive (double bonds).
  Observation: Benzene is STABLE, substitutes rather than adds.

KEKULÉ'S DREAM (1865):
  He claimed he dreamed of a snake biting its own tail → benzene ring.
  (Possibly apocryphal or embellished; nevertheless historically cited.)

KEKULÉ'S STRUCTURE:
  Alternate single and double bonds in a 6-membered ring:

      H       H
       \     /
    H-C₁ = C₆-H
       |         |
    H-C₂ = C₅-H    → two Kekulé structures
       \     /       (resonance)
        C₃ = C₄
        H       H

MODERN UNDERSTANDING (Pauling's resonance theory):
  Neither Kekulé structure is correct.
  Benzene is a resonance hybrid — the electrons are delocalized around the ring.
  All C-C bonds are equivalent (length 1.40 Å, between single 1.54Å and double 1.34Å).
  Often drawn as a hexagon with a circle inside.

  The stability of benzene = aromatic stabilization = delocalization energy ≈ 150 kJ/mol.

APPLICATIONS:
  Benzene ring = aromatic ring — present in:
    - All amino acids with aromatic side chains (Phe, Tyr, Trp, His)
    - DNA bases (purines and pyrimidines contain aromatic rings)
    - Most pharmaceuticals
    - Aspirin, acetaminophen, ibuprofen, caffeine — all contain aromatic rings
```

## Louis Pasteur (1822–1895)

### Who He Was

French chemist and microbiologist. Disproved spontaneous generation (life from
non-life). Developed germ theory of disease. Invented pasteurization and vaccines
for chicken cholera, anthrax, and rabies. One of the most consequential scientists
in history for practical impact on human welfare.

His chemistry work came first: stereochemistry and chirality (1848–1853).

### The Contribution: Chirality and Stereochemistry

**The Discovery of Molecular Chirality (1848)**

```
TARTARIC ACID AND CHIRALITY
=============================

CONTEXT:
  Tartaric acid: a natural acid from wine fermentation.
  Racemic acid: found as a byproduct — appeared to be identical to tartaric.
  PROBLEM: Polarimetry showed tartaric acid rotates light.
    Racemic acid: NO rotation. Yet same formula, same chemical reactions.

Pasteur (1848):
  Carefully crystallized sodium ammonium racemate salts.
  Examined crystals under microscope.
  Found TWO types of crystals, mirror images of each other:
    Left-handed (hemihedral) crystals
    Right-handed (hemihedral) crystals
  These had never been noticed before.

  Separated the two crystal types by hand.
  Dissolved each in water.
  Measured with polarimeter:
    Left-handed crystals → rotated light LEFT
    Right-handed crystals → rotated light RIGHT
    Mixed 50:50 → no rotation (racemic acid = 50:50 mixture!)

CONCLUSION:
  Molecules can be mirror images of each other (enantiomers).
  They have identical chemical formulas and most properties.
  They differ in how they interact with polarized light and biological systems.

  Pasteur exclaimed to his lab assistant: "I have just made a great discovery."
  He was 25.
```

**Why Chirality Matters**

```
CHIRALITY IN BIOLOGY AND MEDICINE
===================================

BIOLOGICAL HOMOCHIRALITY:
  Life uses L-amino acids (left-handed) exclusively in proteins.
  Life uses D-sugars (right-handed) in DNA, RNA, glucose.
  The origin of this homochirality is an open question.

PHARMACEUTICAL IMPLICATIONS:
  Two enantiomers of a drug often have DIFFERENT biological effects:
    S-ibuprofen: anti-inflammatory
    R-ibuprofen: inactive (wasted, or potentially harmful)

  Thalidomide (1950s):
    R-thalidomide: anti-nausea (prescribed to pregnant women)
    S-thalidomide: teratogenic (causes limb defects)
    Worse: the body can interconvert the two forms in vivo.
    10,000+ children born with thalidomide-caused defects.
    Led to strict chirality requirements in drug approval.

  Enantiopure drugs ("chiral drugs"): manufactured to be one enantiomer only.
    Esomeprazole (Nexium) vs omeprazole (Prilosec): one is enantiopure.
    L-DOPA for Parkinson's (D-DOPA is toxic).

  Drug development: chirality must be considered at every step.
  Asymmetric synthesis (producing enantiopure compounds): Nobel Prize 2001.
```

**Germ Theory and Spontaneous Generation**

Pasteur's famous swan-neck flask experiments (1861) disproved spontaneous generation:
broth in a swan-neck flask (neck curves down to prevent dust) stays sterile indefinitely,
but breaks the neck, exposes to air → contamination. Life comes only from life.

This established the germ theory of disease: infectious diseases are caused by
microorganisms. The consequences for medicine (antisepsis, vaccines, antibiotics)
are immeasurable.

## Common Confusion Points

**"Wöhler killed vitalism with one experiment"** — The urea synthesis was one step.
Many chemists continued to believe organic compounds ultimately required living
organisms for decades. Vitalism died gradually as more and more organic compounds
were synthesized: acetic acid (Kolbe, 1845), fats (Berthelot, 1854), many more.
The synthesis of increasingly complex compounds made vitalism increasingly untenable.

**"Kekulé's benzene ring was completely correct"** — The ring structure was right;
the alternating double bonds were not. Benzene's bonds are all equivalent (delocalized),
not alternating long and short. The Kekulé structures are the two contributors to the
resonance hybrid. Pauling's resonance theory (1920s-1930s) and molecular orbital theory
give the correct description.

**"Chirality is just an academic chemical curiosity"** — It is a medical safety issue.
The thalidomide disaster (10,000+ children harmed) directly resulted from the
failure to consider enantiomers in drug development. The FDA now requires chiral
drugs to be evaluated for both enantiomers. Most drugs are now developed as enantiopure
compounds, which requires asymmetric synthesis chemistry.

**"Pasteur was primarily a microbiologist"** — His early work was pure chemistry:
crystallography, stereochemistry, the relationship between molecular asymmetry and
optical rotation. He pivoted to microbiology because his chemistry work on
fermentation (1857) led him to realize fermentation was caused by microorganisms —
not chemical processes. The germ theory is the chemical theory's offspring.
