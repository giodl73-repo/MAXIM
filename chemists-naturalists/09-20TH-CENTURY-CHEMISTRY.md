# 20th Century Chemistry — Haber, Bosch, Curie, Hodgkin, Crick+Watson+Franklin

## The Transformation: Chemistry Enters Technology at Scale

The 20th century saw chemistry move from laboratory science to planetary
engineering. The figures here represent:
- Nitrogen fixation (feeding ~half of humanity)
- Radioactivity (medical diagnosis, nuclear weapons, power)
- X-ray crystallography (all modern drug design)
- DNA structure (the basis of all molecular biology)

```
20TH CENTURY CHEMISTRY — SCALE
=================================

SCALE OF IMPACT:

Haber-Bosch process (1909–1913):
  ~150 million tons of ammonia synthesized per year.
  ~50% of nitrogen in human bodies comes from this process.
  Without it: the Earth cannot feed ~4 billion of its current population.

Radioactivity (Curie, 1898–1910):
  All nuclear medicine (PET, SPECT, cancer radiotherapy) derives from here.
  Nuclear power plants: ~10% of world electricity.
  Nuclear weapons: existential risk of the 20th century.

X-ray crystallography (Hodgkin, 1930s–1960s):
  Every protein structure in the PDB (220,000+ structures as of 2024)
  was determined by crystallography or cryo-EM (which crystallography
  enabled conceptually).
  All structure-based drug design depends on this.

DNA structure (Watson + Crick + Franklin, 1953):
  All of molecular biology, genetic engineering, PCR, genome sequencing,
  CRISPR, mRNA vaccines — built on the double helix.
```

## Fritz Haber (1868–1934)

### Who He Was

German chemist. Jewish. Nobel Prize in Chemistry, 1918 (for ammonia synthesis).
His life is one of the most ethically complex in scientific history: he
invented the process that feeds the world AND directed Germany's chemical
weapons program in WWI. He pioneered chlorine and mustard gas deployment.
His wife Clara (also a chemist) shot herself the night after he returned
from supervising a chlorine attack. He died in Basel in 1934, fleeing
Nazi Germany (which was persecuting Jews, despite his WWI service).

### The Contribution: Nitrogen Fixation

**The Problem: Feeding Humanity (pre-1909)**

```
THE NITROGEN PROBLEM
=====================

ALL LIFE REQUIRES NITROGEN for:
  Amino acids (proteins) → every enzyme, structural protein
  Nucleic acids (DNA, RNA) → every gene
  Chlorophyll → all photosynthesis

WHERE NITROGEN COMES FROM:
  Atmosphere: 78% N₂ gas.
  But N₂ is extremely stable (triple bond: bond energy 945 kJ/mol).
  Plants cannot use atmospheric N₂ directly.
  They need "fixed" nitrogen: NH₃, NO₃⁻, or NH₄⁺.

NATURAL NITROGEN FIXATION (limited):
  Lightning (small contribution)
  Nitrogen-fixing bacteria: Rhizobium (in legume root nodules),
    Azotobacter, cyanobacteria
  These provide ~120 million tons N per year globally.

THE 1900 PROBLEM:
  By 1900, the world population (~1.6 billion) was approaching the limit
  of what natural nitrogen fixation + Peruvian guano (depleted by 1870s)
  + Chilean nitrate deposits could support.
  Crookes (1898 Royal Institution lecture): "the world will face famine
  within 30 years unless chemistry solves the nitrogen problem."

HABER'S SOLUTION:
  Fix atmospheric N₂ by reacting it directly with H₂.
```

**The Haber-Bosch Process (1909–1913)**

```
HABER-BOSCH PROCESS
=====================

REACTION:
  N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃     ΔH = -92 kJ/mol (exothermic)

THERMODYNAMICS:
  The reaction is exothermic (favors products at low temperature).
  But ΔS < 0 (4 moles gas → 2 moles gas — entropy decreases).
  ΔG = ΔH - TΔS:
    At high T: TΔS term dominates → ΔG becomes positive → no reaction
    At low T: ΔG is negative → thermodynamically favored
  → Thermodynamics wants LOW temperature.

KINETICS:
  At low temperature: reaction rate is negligible (N≡N triple bond too stable)
  → Kinetics wants HIGH temperature.

HABER'S COMPROMISE (1909):
  Temperature: 400–500°C (kinetically fast enough; thermodynamically OK)
  Pressure: 150–200 atm (high pressure favors fewer moles of gas → products)
  Catalyst: iron with potassium and aluminum oxide promoters

BOSCH'S ENGINEERING (1910–1913):
  Carl Bosch (BASF) scaled this up from laboratory to industrial scale.
  The engineering challenge: steel vessels at 200 atm that don't fail
  when hydrogen embrittlement attacks the metal.
  Bosch solved the materials problem (using soft iron lining inside hard steel).
  First industrial plant: Oppau, Germany, 1913.

  Bosch won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1931 (with Bergius) for
  high-pressure chemistry.

SCALE TODAY:
  ~150 million tons NH₃/year
  ~1.4% of global energy consumption goes to Haber-Bosch
  ~50% of nitrogen in human tissue came from this process
  Without it: Earth can support ~4 billion people on natural nitrogen alone.
  Current population: ~8 billion. The difference = Haber-Bosch.
```

## Marie Curie (1867–1934) and Irène Joliot-Curie (1897–1956)

### Who They Were

**Marie Curie**: Polish-born French physicist and chemist. First woman to win
a Nobel Prize. Only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences:
Physics (1903, shared with Pierre Curie and Becquerel) and Chemistry (1911).
Died of aplastic anemia from lifetime radiation exposure.

**Irène Joliot-Curie**: Marie and Pierre's daughter. Nobel Prize in Chemistry,
1935 (shared with her husband Frédéric Joliot-Curie) for discovery of
artificial radioactivity. Also died of leukemia from radiation exposure.

### Marie Curie's Contribution: Radioactivity as Atomic Property

**Discovering Polonium and Radium (1898)**

```
MARIE CURIE'S RADIOACTIVITY WORK
===================================

CONTEXT: Henri Becquerel had discovered that uranium emits radiation (1896).
  Nobody knew what the radiation was or why uranium was special.

CURIE'S APPROACH:
  She used an electrometer (precision instrument co-developed by Pierre)
  to measure ionization of air caused by radiation.
  This gave QUANTITATIVE measurements of radioactivity.

KEY INSIGHT (1898):
  The amount of radiation from uranium compounds is proportional only
  to the AMOUNT OF URANIUM PRESENT — not to chemical form.

  ConclusionS:
  (1) Radioactivity is a property of the ATOM, not the chemical bond.
  (2) It is not a surface or molecular effect.
  This was revolutionary: it meant atoms have internal structure that
  can change spontaneously — against all of classical physics.

PITCHBLENDE (uranium ore):
  Curie measured pitchblende and found it was MORE radioactive than
  pure uranium alone. → There must be OTHER radioactive elements in it.

ISOLATION WORK (1898):
  Marie and Pierre processed tons of pitchblende by hand.
  Separation of polonium: 1898 (named for Poland, Marie's homeland).
  Separation of radium: 1898 (announced); isolated 1902 (0.1 g from 1 ton ore).

  Half-lives:
    Polonium-210: 138 days
    Radium-226: 1600 years

WHAT CURIE COULDN'T EXPLAIN:
  Why atoms emit radiation. Where the energy comes from.
  Rutherford and Soddy explained this (1902): radioactive decay is
  transmutation of one element into another, releasing energy.
  The energy comes from mass (E = mc²).
```

**Medical and Technological Legacy**

```
RADIUM/RADIOACTIVITY IN MEDICINE
===================================

EARLY MEDICAL USE (early 1900s):
  Radium applied directly to tumors → radium therapy (brutal but sometimes
  effective — ionizing radiation kills rapidly dividing cells preferentially)

  X-ray machines (Röntgen, 1895) + radioactive isotopes → radiation medicine

MODERN DESCENDANTS:
  Radiotherapy: X-ray, electron, proton, carbon ion beams for cancer
  Brachytherapy: radioactive seeds (I-125, Ir-192) implanted in tumors
  Nuclear medicine:
    PET (positron emission tomography): F-18 glucose → maps metabolic activity
    SPECT: Tc-99m → most common nuclear medicine scan (10 million/year in US)
    Thyroid treatment: I-131 (kills thyroid tissue selectively)
    Bone scan: Tc-99m + bisphosphonate

CURIE'S WWI MOBILE X-RAY UNITS:
  Marie Curie developed "petites Curies" — mobile X-ray vehicles.
  ~18 units, ~200 fixed stations, ~1 million soldiers X-rayed during WWI.
  She trained 150 women as X-ray technicians.
```

### Irène Joliot-Curie's Contribution: Artificial Radioactivity

```
ARTIFICIAL RADIOACTIVITY (1934)
=================================

IRÈNE AND FRÉDÉRIC JOLIOT-CURIE (1934):
  Bombarded aluminum with alpha particles (from polonium):
    ²⁷Al + ⁴He → ³⁰P + n
    (aluminum + alpha particle → phosphorus-30 + neutron)

  Phosphorus-30 is RADIOACTIVE (half-life 2.5 minutes):
    ³⁰P → ³⁰Si + β⁺ + ν   (positron emission)

  After removing the alpha source, the phosphorus continued to emit radiation.
  First ARTIFICIALLY PRODUCED radioactive isotope.

SIGNIFICANCE:
  Before 1934: only naturally occurring radioactive elements existed.
  After: any element can potentially be made radioactive by nuclear bombardment.

  This opened:
  - Medical isotopes on demand (Tc-99m, F-18, etc.)
  - Neutron-induced fission discovery (Fermi, 1934 — who found the
    Joliot-Curie paper and immediately followed up, eventually leading to
    nuclear fission and reactors)
  - Isotope labeling in biochemistry (radioactive tracers)
  - The Manhattan Project ultimately depended on this work chain.
```

## Dorothy Crowfoot Hodgkin (1910–1994)

### Who She Was

British chemist and crystallographer. Nobel Prize in Chemistry, 1964 —
only the third woman to win the Chemistry Nobel (after Marie and Irène Curie).
Determined the 3D structures of penicillin, vitamin B12, and insulin using
X-ray crystallography. Crippled by rheumatoid arthritis from her 20s but
continued working for decades.

### The Contribution: X-Ray Crystallography of Biological Molecules

**How X-Ray Crystallography Works**

```
X-RAY CRYSTALLOGRAPHY
======================

PRINCIPLE (Bragg's Law, 1913):
  X-rays scattered by the electron clouds of atoms.
  If atoms are arranged in a regular crystal lattice, scattered X-rays
  interfere constructively at specific angles:

  nλ = 2d·sin(θ)

  where:
    n = integer (order of reflection)
    λ = X-ray wavelength (~0.5–2.5 Å)
    d = spacing between crystal planes
    θ = angle of incidence

  From the pattern of constructive interference (diffraction pattern):
  → infer the arrangement of atoms in 3D.

THE PHASE PROBLEM:
  You measure INTENSITY of diffracted beams (|F|²).
  You need PHASE (angle) of each beam to reconstruct the 3D structure.
  Phases are lost in the measurement.
  Solving the phase problem is the hard part of crystallography.

  Methods: isomorphous replacement (heavy atoms), anomalous dispersion,
  molecular replacement. Hodgkin pioneered the early methods.

COMPLEXITY OF THE PROBLEM:
  Small molecule: 10s of atoms; days of calculation
  Penicillin: 39 non-hydrogen atoms; Hodgkin used early computers (1945)
  Vitamin B12: 181 atoms; Nobel Prize work
  Insulin: 800+ atoms; determined 1969 after 35 years of work
```

**The Three Great Structures**

```
HODGKIN'S THREE STRUCTURES
============================

1. PENICILLIN (1945)
   Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin (1928); didn't know structure.
   Hodgkin determined structure: bicyclic ring system containing
   a beta-lactam (4-membered ring with nitrogen and carbonyl).
   The beta-lactam is responsible for antibiotic activity:
   it inhibits transpeptidase, blocking bacterial cell wall synthesis.
   Most chemists thought a 4-membered ring was too strained to be stable.
   Hodgkin proved it existed. The entire penicillin class of antibiotics
   (ampicillin, amoxicillin, methicillin) follows from this structure.

2. VITAMIN B12 (1956) — Nobel Prize Work
   B12 (cobalamin): 181 non-hydrogen atoms, a cobalt ion in the center.
   Most complex natural product structure determined to that date.
   Hodgkin used early electronic computers (the first use of computers
   in structural crystallography).
   Deficiency: pernicious anemia (neurological damage).
   Synthesis: Woodward + Eschenmoser total synthesis (1972) — 100 steps,
   required Woodward-Hoffmann rules (orbital symmetry — another Nobel).
   Structure determination preceded synthesis by 16 years.

3. INSULIN (1969)
   Began studying insulin crystals: 1934.
   Structure determination completed: 1969.
   35 years of work.
   Insulin: 51 amino acids in 2 chains (A and B) connected by disulfide bonds.
   The 3D structure showed how the two chains fold and the disulfides form.
   Critical for understanding insulin action (receptor binding, diabetes treatment).
   Recombinant insulin (from E. coli, 1982) was designed using this structure.
```

## Crick, Watson, and Franklin — DNA Structure (1953)

### Who They Were

**Francis Crick** (1916–2004): British physicist turned molecular biologist.
Worked on the theoretical framework for X-ray diffraction of helical molecules.
Later work: genetic code (codons), central dogma, consciousness.

**James Watson** (b. 1928): American biologist. Had written up his thesis
on phage genetics. Arrived in Cambridge in 1951. Co-discovered the double helix.
Later headed the Human Genome Project before resigning after racist statements.

**Rosalind Franklin** (1920–1958): British chemist and crystallographer.
Produced the sharpest X-ray diffraction images of DNA ever taken.
Her Photo 51 (1952) showed the B-form of DNA. Died of ovarian cancer
at 37, four years before the Nobel Prize was awarded (which excluded her
posthumously — the Nobel is not awarded posthumously).

### The Contribution: The Double Helix

**X-Ray Evidence for DNA Structure**

```
ROAD TO THE DOUBLE HELIX
==========================

CONTEXT (1950–1952):
  Avery, MacLeod, McCarty (1944): DNA, not protein, carries genetic information.
  Chargaff (1950): A = T and G = C in any DNA sample
    (Chargaff's rules — key data point).
  Franklin (1952): Photo 51 — the sharpest diffraction image of B-form DNA.

PHOTO 51 (Franklin, 1952):
  B-form DNA (fully hydrated):
    - Cross-shaped diffraction pattern → helical structure
    - 34 Å repeat (pitch of helix)
    - 3.4 Å rise per residue (bases 3.4 Å apart)
    - 10 bases per turn
  A-form DNA (dehydrated): Franklin was focusing on this more tractable form.

HOW WATSON SAW PHOTO 51:
  Wilkins (Franklin's King's College colleague) showed Watson Photo 51
  in January 1953, without Franklin's knowledge or permission.
  Watson immediately understood: it was a double helix.

  Whether this was a decisive input vs. corroboration of what Watson
  already suspected is historically contested.

PAULING'S WRONG TRIPLE HELIX (February 1953):
  Pauling published a triple-helix model with the phosphates inside.
  Watson and Crick (Cambridge) knew it was wrong (wrong ionization).
  Competitive pressure intensified.
```

**The Double Helix Structure (April 25, 1953)**

```
THE DOUBLE HELIX
=================

WATSON AND CRICK (Nature, April 25, 1953):
  "We wish to suggest a structure for the salt of deoxyribose
  nucleic acid (DNA). This structure has novel features which
  are of considerable biological interest."

  (One of the most understated openings in scientific history.)

THE STRUCTURE:
  Two polynucleotide chains coiled around a common axis.
  Anti-parallel: one chain runs 5'→3', the other 3'→5'.
  Right-handed helix.
  Sugar-phosphate backbone on the OUTSIDE.
  Bases on the INSIDE.

  Base pairing (Chargaff's rules explained):
    A pairs with T (2 hydrogen bonds)
    G pairs with C (3 hydrogen bonds)
    Purine always pairs with pyrimidine → constant helix width (20 Å)

  Dimensions:
    Diameter: 20 Å (2 nm)
    Pitch: 34 Å per turn (3.4 nm)
    10 base pairs per turn, 3.4 Å per base pair

THE FAMOUS UNDERSTATEMENT:
  "It has not escaped our notice that the specific pairing we
  have postulated immediately suggests a possible copying mechanism
  for the genetic material."

  This one sentence: the entire basis of molecular biology.
  A → T, G → C pairing means each strand can serve as a template.
  DNA replication: unwind the helix, each strand serves as template
  for synthesis of a complementary strand.
  PCR, DNA fingerprinting, Sanger sequencing — all from this pairing.
```

**The Central Dogma (Crick, 1958)**

```
CRICK'S CENTRAL DOGMA (1958, formalized 1970)
===============================================

DNA → RNA → Protein

  Replication: DNA → DNA (DNA polymerase)
  Transcription: DNA → RNA (RNA polymerase)
  Translation: RNA → Protein (ribosome + tRNA + aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases)

"Central Dogma": information flows from nucleic acid to protein, not
  from protein back to nucleic acid.

REVERSE TRANSCRIPTASE EXCEPTION (Baltimore + Temin, 1970 — Nobel 1975):
  RNA viruses (retroviruses, HIV) carry reverse transcriptase:
  RNA → DNA (reverse transcription)
  The central dogma's primary arrow is correct; reverse is exceptional.

GENETIC CODE (Nirenberg, Khorana, Holley — Nobel 1968):
  Codon = 3 nucleotides → 1 amino acid
  64 codons → 20 amino acids + 3 stop codons (degenerate code)
  Code is essentially universal across all life.

ALL OF MOLECULAR BIOLOGY DESCENDS FROM:
  Double helix structure (1953)
  Central dogma (1958)
  Genetic code (1961–1966)

In technology:
  PCR (Mullis, 1983 — Nobel 1993): copies DNA exponentially; COVID tests
  Sanger sequencing (1977): first genome sequencing method
  NGS (next-generation sequencing, 2000s): $1,000 genomes
  CRISPR (Doudna + Charpentier, 2020 Nobel): precise genome editing
  mRNA vaccines (COVID-19): use the RNA → protein pathway
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Haber is a hero because he fed the world"** — He is also responsible for
pioneering chemical weapons in WWI (chlorine, phosgene, mustard gas attacks).
He supervised the first large-scale chemical weapons deployment at Ypres (April
22, 1915), resulting in thousands of deaths. His Nobel Prize (1918, awarded
despite the WWI chemical weapons) was controversial. His story is not a simple
heroism narrative; it is a direct confrontation with the dual-use problem in
chemistry. Haber's own formulation: "During peace time a scientist belongs to
the world, but during war time he belongs to his country."

**"Watson and Crick discovered DNA"** — Miescher discovered DNA (nuclein, 1869).
Avery showed DNA carries genetic information (1944). Watson and Crick determined
the 3D structure of DNA. These are completely different contributions from
different eras.

**"Franklin's contribution was stolen"** — The historical assessment is nuanced.
Wilkins showed Watson Photo 51 without her consent (or likely knowledge).
Watson was already building helical models; Photo 51 confirmed the B-form
dimensions decisively. Franklin's contribution was essential. Whether it was
"theft" in the strong sense depends on what information Watson already had
and whether Photo 51 was the crucial step or a confirming step. The consensus
among historians: Franklin did not receive appropriate credit, and the Nobel
Prize committee's failure to include her (even considering the "no posthumous"
rule, they could have cited her) was a failure.

**"Hodgkin was peripheral to the DNA story"** — Her contributions are
independent and parallel. She solved penicillin before DNA, vitamin B12 after
DNA, and insulin 16 years after DNA. She trained many crystallographers.
Her techniques (heavy atom methods, early computer use) were adopted by the
field. Her insulin structure (1969) took 35 years — started in 1934, when Watson
was 6 years old.

**"Marie Curie discovered radioactivity"** — Becquerel discovered that uranium
emits radiation (1896). Marie Curie showed that radioactivity is an atomic
property, discovered two new elements, and isolated them. The conceptual shift
from "uranium emits something" to "radioactivity is an intrinsic atomic property
that can be measured and quantified" — that's Marie Curie's contribution.
