# Evolution and the Naturalists — Darwin, Wallace, Lamarck, Haeckel, Huxley

## The Question: Why Do Species Exist Where They Do, and How Did They Get There?

Natural history had accumulated millions of observations by 1800. The
classification of species (Linnaeus), the identification of biogeographic
patterns (Buffon), and the fossil record (Cuvier, Agassiz) all demanded
explanation. The 19th century delivered it.

```
THE EVOLUTIONARY PROBLEM (pre-Darwin)
=======================================

FACTS ACCUMULATED BY 1800:
  - ~100,000 known species, described and classified
  - Fossil record shows extinction and succession (Cuvier, 1810s)
  - Biogeography: different regions have different species,
    even with similar climates (Buffon's question)
  - Domestication shows species CAN change under selection (dog breeds,
    pigeon varieties, crop plants)
  - Comparative anatomy: limbs of different vertebrates are homologous
    (same bones, different proportions) — suggesting common origin

EXPLANATIONS BEFORE DARWIN:
  Creationism (mainstream): each species separately created, fixed.
  Catastrophism (Cuvier): successive creations after mass extinctions.
  Lamarck (1809): species transform through inheritance of acquired characters.

DARWIN + WALLACE (1858–1859): natural selection as mechanism.

THE MECHANISM WAS THE MISSING PIECE:
  It wasn't enough to say "species change."
  You need to explain HOW and WHY — a mechanism that is:
    (1) consistent with known genetics/inheritance
    (2) powerful enough to produce observed diversity
    (3) consistent with the fossil record
    (4) testable

  Natural selection provided all four.
```

## Jean-Baptiste Lamarck (1744–1829)

### Who He Was

French naturalist. Director of invertebrate zoology at the Natural History
Museum, Paris. Named the word "biology" (along with Treviranus, 1802).
Extensive work on classification of invertebrates. Died blind and impoverished.
His career was damaged by Cuvier (a more politically powerful rival) during his lifetime.

### The Contribution: First Systematic Theory of Evolution

**Lamarckism (1809)**

```
LAMARCK'S THEORY (Philosophie Zoologique, 1809)
=================================================

WHAT LAMARCK PROPOSED:
  1. Life has an intrinsic tendency toward greater complexity
     (a "vital force" driving toward higher organization).
  2. Organisms respond to environmental needs — new needs produce
     new behaviors, new use of organs.
  3. USE AND DISUSE: Organs that are used become stronger/larger;
     organs that are not used degenerate.
  4. INHERITANCE OF ACQUIRED CHARACTERISTICS: Changes produced by
     use/disuse are inherited by offspring.

EXAMPLE (his famous giraffe example):
  Giraffes stretching to reach higher leaves → longer neck muscles.
  Offspring inherit the slightly longer neck.
  Over many generations: long-necked giraffe.

WHAT WAS WRONG:
  Inheritance of acquired characteristics does not occur (in general).
  The mechanism of inheritance (genes, chromosomes) was not known to
  Lamarck. There is no mechanism by which muscle use in a parent
  changes the DNA in gametes.

WHAT WAS RIGHT:
  Species DO change over time (a heretical claim in 1809).
  Environment drives the change (correct — but mechanism is wrong).
  The giraffe's long neck did evolve for feeding efficiency (correct),
  but via natural selection, not use-inheritance.

MODERN NUANCE:
  Epigenetics has revived limited forms of "inheritance of acquired
  characters" — methylation patterns CAN be transmitted across a few
  generations in some organisms. This does not rehabilitate Lamarck's
  full theory, but the relationship between phenotype and genotype is
  more complex than the "Neo-Darwinian synthesis" assumed.
```

## Charles Darwin (1809–1882)

### Who He Was

English naturalist. Cambridge-educated (BA theology, but deeply interested
in natural history). Five-year voyage on HMS Beagle (1831–1836). Spent the
next 23 years accumulating evidence before publishing *On the Origin of
Species* (1859). Waited partly because Wallace sent him an independent
summary of the same theory (1858), prompting joint publication.
Died respected, buried in Westminster Abbey.

### The Contribution: Natural Selection as the Mechanism of Evolution

**Natural Selection (1858–1859)**

```
DARWIN'S ARGUMENT (Origin of Species, 1859)
=============================================

FOUR OBSERVATIONS:
  1. Organisms produce more offspring than can survive (overproduction).
  2. Resources are limited (competition).
  3. Offspring vary (heritable variation exists in every population).
  4. Some variants survive and reproduce better than others.

LOGICAL CONCLUSION (Wallace reached it independently):
  The variants that survive better will have more offspring.
  Their traits will increase in frequency in the population.
  Over time: the population changes.
  Given enough time and geographical isolation: new species arise.

DARWIN'S EVIDENCE:
  Artificial selection (pigeon breeding, dog breeds):
    Showed dramatic change in organisms under deliberate selection.
    Argued natural selection could do the same.

  Biogeography:
    Galápagos finches: 13 species from one ancestor, each adapted to
    a different food source on different islands.
    Island species resemble nearby mainland species, not distant ones
    with similar climates. (Buffon's pattern + an explanation.)

  Fossil record:
    Successive strata show successive species — with transitions.
    Extinction is real; species are not permanent.

  Comparative anatomy:
    Homologous structures (same bones in human arm, bat wing, whale flipper)
    → common ancestry.
    Vestigial organs (human coccyx, whale pelvis) → descent from organisms
    that used them.

  Embryology:
    Embryos of vertebrates are nearly indistinguishable early —
    revealing shared ancestry before divergence.
```

**The Problem Darwin Couldn't Solve: Inheritance**

```
DARWIN'S INHERITANCE PROBLEM
==============================

Darwin knew selection worked on variation, but didn't know the MECHANISM
of inheritance. He proposed "pangenesis" (particles from all body cells
collected in gametes) — wrong.

The real problem (Fleeming Jenkin, 1867):
  If inheritance is "blending" (offspring average their parents' traits),
  then any new beneficial mutation would be halved in the next generation,
  quartered in the following one, and diluted to nothing in a few generations.
  Natural selection can't preserve variation if it's immediately blended away.

DARWIN NEVER RESOLVED THIS.

THE RESOLUTION: Mendelian genetics (1866, but ignored until 1900).
  Genes are discrete (not blending).
  A mutation either survives as a discrete variant or goes extinct —
  it doesn't get diluted.

The Modern Synthesis (Fisher, Haldane, Wright, Dobzhansky, Mayr, ~1930–1950)
united Darwin's selection + Mendelian genetics + population genetics:
  dq/dt = q(1-q) · s  (rate of allele frequency change under selection s)
  This is the mathematical foundation of evolutionary biology.
```

**Descent with Modification**

```
KEY CONCEPTS IN DARWIN

COMMON DESCENT:
  All life shares a common ancestor.
  The "tree of life" — species diverge from shared ancestral populations.
  Darwin's only diagram in Origin was a branching tree.

ADAPTATION:
  Features that improve reproductive success spread by natural selection.
  "Fitness" = reproductive success (not strength or "fitness" in common sense).
  Adaptation is NOT directed or purposeful — it is selection on random variation.

GRADUALISM:
  Evolution occurs by accumulation of small changes (mostly).
  Darwin resisted sudden jumps (saltations).
  Modern: punctuated equilibrium (Gould + Eldredge, 1972) suggests
  evolution is episodic — long stasis punctuated by rapid (geologically)
  change. Both patterns are real.

SEXUAL SELECTION:
  Second mechanism: competition for mates.
  Peacock tails, deer antlers — costly signals favored because they
  attract mates, even at survival cost.
  Darwin's Descent of Man (1871) introduced this.

WHAT DARWIN DID NOT KNOW:
  The mechanism of heredity (genes, DNA)
  The source of variation (mutations)
  The age of the Earth precisely (Kelvin's calculation gave ~100My;
  actual ~4.5By — Kelvin's estimate was too short for Darwin's comfort)
```

## Alfred Russel Wallace (1823–1913)

### Who He Was

Welsh naturalist. Self-educated (unlike Darwin's Cambridge training).
Collected extensively in the Amazon (1848–1852, lost his collections when
the ship sank on the return voyage) and the Malay Archipelago (1854–1862).
Independently discovered natural selection (1858) — his letter to Darwin
prompted the joint presentation to the Linnean Society.

### The Contribution: Natural Selection (Independent) and Biogeography

**Natural Selection (1858)**

```
WALLACE'S PAPER (1858)
=======================

Wallace, while ill with fever in the Moluccas (modern Indonesia), worked
out the theory of natural selection in two hours (by his account) and
wrote it up in three days.

His key paper: "On the Tendency of Varieties to Depart Indefinitely
from the Original Type" (1858).

CONTENTS: Essentially Darwin's argument:
  - Populations grow geometrically; resources grow arithmetically
  - Competition → selection of fit individuals
  - Variation + selection → change over time → new species

His paper, sent to Darwin in June 1858, arrived as Darwin was working
on Origin of Species. Darwin had been developing the theory for 20 years.

JOINT PRESENTATION:
  Darwin and Wallace jointly presented at the Linnean Society on July 1, 1858.
  Neither was present. Neither paper created immediate sensation.
  Darwin's Origin (1859) — a full book with massive evidence — created the revolution.

CREDIT:
  Darwin gets primary credit for: (1) 20 years of evidence accumulation,
  (2) the book that convinced the scientific community.
  Wallace's contribution was genuine and independent.
  Wallace himself gave Darwin primary credit throughout his life.
```

**The Wallace Line (1859–1869)**

```
THE WALLACE LINE
=================

During 8 years in the Malay Archipelago, Wallace collected ~125,000 specimens.
He noticed a sharp faunal boundary between:

  WESTERN side (Bali, Borneo, Malaya):
    Asian fauna — tigers, orangutans, Asian birds, etc.

  EASTERN side (Lombok, Sulawesi, New Guinea, Australia):
    Australasian fauna — marsupials, birds of paradise, cockatoos

  BALI and LOMBOK are separated by 35 km of water.
  Their faunas are completely different.

THE WALLACE LINE:
  A biogeographic boundary running between Bali and Lombok, north through
  the Makassar Strait between Borneo and Sulawesi.

WHY:
  During ice ages (sea level lower), Borneo/Bali were connected to Asia.
  Australia/New Guinea were connected to each other but not to Asia.
  The deep Makassar Strait has always been a barrier.
  Fauna evolved separately on each side for millions of years.
  The boundary persists today even though sea levels have changed.

SIGNIFICANCE:
  This is the founding observation of biogeography as a science.
  Wallace inferred the geological history of the region from faunal patterns —
  a decade before plate tectonics.
  The Wallace Line is still drawn on every biogeography map.
```

## Ernst Haeckel (1834–1919)

### Who He Was

German biologist and naturalist, Jena. One of Darwin's most prominent
advocates in Germany. Coined many terms still in use: ecology, phylogeny,
phylum, ontogeny, Protista, Monera. Made stunning anatomical illustrations
of marine microorganisms (radiolarians, medusae).

### The Contribution and Controversy: Recapitulation, Ecology, and Phylogenetics

**Biogenetic Law (1866) — Influential but Wrong**

```
HAECKEL'S BIOGENETIC LAW
==========================

"Ontogeny recapitulates phylogeny."

WHAT IT CLAIMS:
  The embryonic development of an individual (ontogeny) replays the
  evolutionary history of its species (phylogeny).

  A human embryo passes through a fish stage (gill slits), then an
  amphibian stage, then a reptile stage, then a mammal stage.

HAECKEL'S EVIDENCE:
  Embryos of vertebrates do look very similar at early stages.
  Human embryos have pharyngeal arches (sometimes called "gill slits").
  Human embryos have a tail (the coccyx is its remnant).

WHAT WAS WRONG:
  The strong version ("replay" of ancestors) is incorrect.
  Embryos are not small adults of ancestral species.
  What is shared is: COMMON DEVELOPMENTAL PATHWAYS from common ancestors.

  The pharyngeal arches in human embryos are not fish gills;
  they become jaw muscles, ear bones, thyroid gland, etc.
  The resemblance reflects shared genetic programs, not replay.

HAECKEL'S FORGERIES:
  His embryo comparison illustrations (1868, 1874) exaggerated the
  similarities between embryos of different species.
  Modern analysis: he fabricated or heavily modified some illustrations.
  The genuine biological observation (early embryo similarity) is real;
  the "recapitulation" interpretation is the oversimplification.

THE MODERN SYNTHESIS:
  "Evolutionary developmental biology" (evo-devo) has rehabilitated
  a softer version: embryonic development is constrained by evolutionary
  history (Hox genes are shared across animals; see developmental biology files).
  But the strong recapitulation claim is false.
```

**Ecology (Term Coined, 1866) and Phylogenetics**

```
HAECKEL'S LASTING CONTRIBUTIONS
==================================

ECOLOGY:
  Haeckel coined "ecology" (Oekologie) in 1866:
  "the whole science of the relationships of the organism to the
  surrounding outer world."
  The field he named became one of the most important in biology.

PHYLOGENY:
  Haeckel drew the first evolutionary trees (phylogenetic trees)
  showing the branching pattern of life's history.
  His trees were speculative (little molecular data available)
  but established the concept that evolutionary history could be
  represented as a branching diagram.

  Modern phylogenetics (molecular clocks, Bayesian trees, maximum
  likelihood methods) fills in what Haeckel could only sketch.

COINAGE: Haeckel coined or popularized:
  Ecology, phylogeny, ontogeny, phylum, Protista, Monera, Metazoa,
  and the phrase "biogenetic law."
  Many are still in daily use (ecology, phylogeny, Protista).
```

## Thomas Henry Huxley (1825–1895)

### Who He Was

English biologist and comparative anatomist. Self-educated in natural history.
"Darwin's bulldog" — the foremost public defender of evolutionary theory.
Won the famous 1860 Oxford debate against Bishop Wilberforce (the details are
disputed, but the general character of the exchange is historical).
Coined the term "agnostic" (1869).

### The Contribution: Advocacy, Comparative Anatomy, and Science Education

**The Oxford Debate (1860)**

```
THE 1860 OXFORD DEBATE
========================

June 30, 1860 — British Association for the Advancement of Science, Oxford.

Bishop Samuel Wilberforce (coached by Richard Owen, Darwin's anatomical
rival) challenged evolutionary theory.

Wilberforce reportedly asked Huxley whether he was descended from an ape
on his grandfather's or grandmother's side.

Huxley reportedly replied (paraphrased — the exact words are disputed):
  "I would rather be descended from an ape than from a man who misuses
  his talents to ridicule an argument he hasn't understood."

SIGNIFICANCE:
  Not primarily as a scientific debate (it wasn't resolved that day).
  Rather as a cultural turning point: Huxley demonstrated that
  evolutionary theory could be defended publicly and confidently
  against ecclesiastical authority.
  The Victorian public was watching; many scientists who had been
  cautious now felt comfortable declaring support for Darwin.
```

**The Archaeopteryx Argument (1868)**

```
ARCHAEOPTERYX AND TRANSITIONAL FOSSILS
=========================================

Archaeopteryx lithographica:
  Found in Bavarian limestone, 1861.
  Had: feathers, wishbone (furcula) → bird
  Had: teeth, long bony tail, claws on wings → dinosaur

Huxley argued (1868): This is a transitional form.
  Birds are descended from dinosaurs (specifically, theropod dinosaurs).
  "Archaeopteryx is the grandfather of all birds."

He was RIGHT — but 150 years early.
  The dinosaur-bird connection was not widely accepted until the 1970s-90s
  (Ostrom, 1973; Bakker; confirmed by Chinese feathered dinosaur fossils
  from 1990s-2000s).
  Birds ARE living theropod dinosaurs. Huxley's 1868 conclusion was correct.

This is also a case study in science sociology:
  A correct claim (bird-dinosaur connection) was dismissed for a century,
  then resurrected when new evidence (Chinese fossils) arrived.
```

**Science Education and Agnosticism**

Huxley fought for science in public education and served on the London School
Board. Coined "agnosticism" (1869) to describe the position that empirical
science cannot address metaphysical questions — a careful philosophical
distinction between what science can and cannot claim. This term and concept
remain in active use.

## Common Confusion Points

**"Darwin invented evolution"** — He invented the mechanism (natural selection).
The idea that species change over time had been proposed by Lamarck (1809),
Erasmus Darwin (Charles's grandfather), and others. What Darwin provided was
a credible, evidence-backed mechanism. Lamarck's theory was real evolution;
it just had the wrong mechanism.

**"Survival of the fittest"** — Herbert Spencer's phrase (1864), adopted by
Darwin in later editions of Origin. Misleading: "fittest" does not mean
strongest or most capable in everyday terms. "Fitness" in evolutionary
biology means reproductive success. A bacterium that produces 1,000 offspring
in a day is more "fit" than a blue whale that produces one calf every few years,
in the technical sense.

**"Evolution has been disproved / is just a theory"** — "Theory" in science
means "explanatory framework supported by extensive evidence" (like the theory
of gravity or the germ theory of disease). The evidence for common descent
is overwhelming: molecular phylogenetics (DNA sequence comparisons), the fossil
record, comparative anatomy, direct observation of evolution in real time
(bacteria, viruses, the peppered moth, cichlids). The mechanism debate (how fast,
how gradual, role of genetic drift vs. selection) is active; the fact of common
descent is not in scientific dispute.

**"Haeckel was mostly wrong"** — He coined two of the most important biological
terms still in use (ecology, phylogeny), drew the first evolutionary trees,
and made foundational contributions to marine invertebrate biology. His biogenetic
law was wrong as stated; his illustration doctoring was a genuine scientific
misconduct. Both coexist: significant contributions and significant errors.

**"Wallace was just Darwin's subordinate"** — Wallace independently discovered
natural selection, spent 8 years in the field making original observations
Darwin couldn't have made, founded biogeography as a quantitative science,
and wrote extensively on evolutionary theory after Origin. He is a major figure
who happens to share his biggest discovery with Darwin.
