## Coverage Notes

Abstract algebra studies algebraic structures — sets equipped with operations satisfying axioms — at increasing levels of generality. This directory covers the full hierarchy from groups through rings to fields and modules, with Galois theory as the capstone connecting field extensions to group theory. The progression moves from concrete examples (integers mod n, symmetric groups, matrix groups) through the isomorphism theorems, into polynomial rings and field extensions, culminating in Galois theory's resolution of the quintic. Additional modules cover representation theory (critical for physics and chemistry), category theory (the unifying language of modern mathematics), and applications in error-correcting codes, crystallography, and cryptography.
