# 06 — Political Economy

## Trade Theory, Sanctions, Currency Wars, Developmental State, IPE Theories

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Trade theory: Ricardo comparative advantage (even if one country is absolutely better at everything, both gain from specialization), Heckscher-Ohlin (factor endowments drive trade pattern), Stolper-Samuelson (trade affects factor distribution — imports hurt scarce factor), New Trade Theory (Krugman — economies of scale + imperfect competition explain intra-industry trade)
- Political constraints on free trade: concentrated losses vs diffuse gains, trade adjustment assistance, infant industry argument (Hamilton/List), strategic trade policy (Boeing/Airbus subsidies), national security exceptions (GATT Article XXI)
- Global value chains: fragmentation of production, "factory world" (China), "factory Europe" (CEE assembly), trade in value added vs gross trade, reshoring/friendshoring post-2020
- Economic sanctions: unilateral vs multilateral, comprehensive vs targeted (smart sanctions against individuals), smart sanctions evolution post-Iraq, financial sanctions (SWIFT exclusion), effectiveness debate (Hufbauer et al. vs skeptics), secondary sanctions extraterritoriality
- Currency and monetary politics: Nixon shock 1971 (end of Bretton Woods gold convertibility), floating exchange rates, dollar hegemony (reserve currency advantages — exorbitant privilege), currency manipulation accusations, capital controls (Mundell-Fleming trilemma — open capital account + fixed exchange rate + independent monetary policy: pick two)
- Washington Consensus vs alternatives: Williamson's 10 policy prescriptions (privatization/liberalization/fiscal discipline), East Asian developmental state (Korea/Japan/Taiwan — industrial policy, directed credit, export discipline), China's state capitalism model, post-2008 rethinking of capital account liberalization
- Resource politics: oil as political commodity (OPEC, petrostates, resource curse/Dutch disease), rare earth concentration, food as geopolitical lever (Russian grain deal, Ukraine exports), water as future conflict driver
- Financial globalization: capital account liberalization benefits vs risks (sudden stops, contagion), Keynesian capital controls revival (Ostry/IMF 2010), offshore financial centers, global tax competition and OECD Pillar Two (15% minimum corporate tax)
