# 05 — International Institutions

## UN System, WTO, IMF/World Bank, NATO, EU Supranational Logic

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- UN system architecture: Security Council (P5 veto, 10 elected members, binding resolutions under Chapter VII), General Assembly (one state one vote, non-binding resolutions), Secretariat (Secretary-General), ICJ (voluntary jurisdiction, contentious cases vs advisory opinions), specialized agencies (WHO/UNESCO/FAO/IAEA/ILO)
- UN peacekeeping: Chapter VI (consent-based) vs Chapter VII (enforcement), blue helmets, R2P (Responsibility to Protect — 2005 World Summit, Libya implementation controversy), Security Council reform debates
- WTO/GATT: GATT 1947 origins, Uruguay Round → WTO 1995, MFN principle, National Treatment, bound tariff schedules, Dispute Settlement Body (DSB) panels + Appellate Body, retaliation authorization; Appellate Body crisis (US blocking appointments 2017-2023)
- IMF: Article IV surveillance (macroeconomic monitoring), Stand-By Arrangements/Extended Fund Facility (conditionality debates — Washington Consensus vs post-Washington Consensus), Special Drawing Rights (SDRs), Global Financial Safety Net, governance (quota-based voting)
- World Bank Group: IBRD (middle-income country loans), IDA (concessional/low-income), IFC (private sector), MIGA (investment guarantees); development conditionality; environmental/social safeguards
- NATO: collective defense (Article 5 — an attack on one is an attack on all), consensus decision-making (every ally has veto), integrated military command (SACEUR), enlargement (from 12 to 32 members), 2% GDP defense spending pledge
- EU supranational logic: pooled sovereignty, directly effective law (Van Gend en Loos 1963), supremacy of EU law (Costa v ENEL 1964), direct election of Parliament (1979), Euro as currency union without fiscal union (Mundell OCA theory and its violation), Brexit as test of membership logic
- Regional organizations: ASEAN (non-interference principle, ASEAN Way), AU (R2P more developed than UN), Mercosur, SCO (Shanghai Cooperation Organisation), BRICS expansion
- Regime theory (Krasner): "implicit or explicit principles, norms, rules, and decision-making procedures around which actors' expectations converge in a given area"; hegemonic stability vs cooperation under anarchy debate
