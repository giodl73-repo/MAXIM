# 02 — Nuclear Deterrence

## MAD, Credibility, Extended Deterrence, First-Strike Stability, Arms Control

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Deterrence basics: deter by denial (make attack unable to succeed) vs deter by punishment (threaten unacceptable costs); rational deterrence theory (expected costs > expected benefits), credibility requirement
- MAD (Mutual Assured Destruction): both sides maintain secure second-strike capability; assured destruction = ability to absorb first strike and retaliate; Schlesinger Doctrine → SIOP; McNamara/Wohlstetter debate
- Credibility problem: "would you really trade New York for Paris?"; resolve (will to use) + capability (technical ability); declaratory vs operational policy; positive control (authorization) vs negative control (accident prevention)
- Extended deterrence: US nuclear umbrella over NATO/Japan/South Korea; coupling (visible linkage of US strategic forces to ally defense), flexible response (Kennedy administration abandoning massive retaliation), forward deployment
- First-strike stability: crisis stability (neither side benefits from striking first in a crisis); counterforce vs countervalue targeting; launch-on-warning vs ride-out; ICBM vulnerability (silo-based = use-them-or-lose-them pressure)
- Nuclear weapons effects: blast/heat/radiation/EMP; Glasstone/Dolan Effects of Nuclear Weapons; nuclear winter hypothesis (Turco/Sagan); limited nuclear war possibility
- Arms control: SALT I/II (offensive arsenals), ABM Treaty (missile defenses as stabilizing), START I/II/New START (strategic warhead limits), INF Treaty (intermediate range), NPT (Non-Proliferation Treaty, 191 parties), CTBT (Comprehensive Test Ban — not in force)
- Proliferation logic: why states pursue nuclear weapons (security/status/domestic politics); why they stop (negative security assurances, alliance guarantees, economic costs, leadership change); Iraq/Libya/South Africa/Ukraine denuclearization cases
- Current landscape: US-Russia new START expired 2026, China opaque buildup (~400→1500 warheads trajectory), North Korea operational ICBMs, Iran threshold state, India-Pakistan subcontinent risk
- Technology and deterrence: hypersonic glide vehicles (reduce warning time), autonomous systems (human control), cyber (early warning system vulnerability), AI (decision compression)
