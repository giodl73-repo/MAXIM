# 00 — Political Science Overview

## Subfields, Levels of Analysis, Schools of Thought, Methods

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Subfields: International Relations (IR), Comparative Politics, Political Theory/Philosophy, American Politics, Public Policy/Administration, Political Economy
- Levels of analysis (Waltz): individual (leaders/psychology), state (domestic politics/regime type), system (anarchic international structure/distribution of power); which level explains what
- Methodology debates: positivism vs interpretivism; qualitative (case studies/process tracing/comparative) vs quantitative (regression/experiments/formal modeling); scientific vs humanistic vision of political science; credibility revolution in political science (parallel to economics)
- Key theoretical traditions: realism (power/security/anarchy), liberalism (institutions/interdependence/democracy), constructivism (identity/norms/ideas), Marxism/critical theory (class/material interests/structure), feminism (gender/power), English School (society of states/international society)
- Power: hard (military/economic coercion) vs soft (attraction/legitimacy) vs smart power; Joseph Nye's framework; sharp power (authoritarian influence operations)
- The state: Weber's definition (monopoly on legitimate use of force in territory), sovereignty (Westphalian 1648), failed states, state formation theories
- Democratic peace theory: democracies rarely fight each other; evidence and causal mechanisms (liberal institutions/trade/norms); critique (selection effects, definition of democracy)
- Polity and democracy metrics: Freedom House scores, Polity5, Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem); democratic backsliding measurement
- Index of modules: IR theory, nuclear deterrence, geopolitics, comparative politics, institutions, political economy
