# 03 — Geopolitics

## Mackinder, Mahan, Great-Power Competition, Indo-Pacific, Belt and Road

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Mackinder's Heartland Theory (1904): "Who rules Eastern Europe commands the Heartland; who rules the Heartland commands the World-Island; who rules the World-Island commands the World"; pivot area = Eurasian heartland; land power vs sea power competition
- Mahan's Sea Power (1890): command of the sea as basis of national power; naval strength = merchant marine + navy + bases; chokepoints; US/UK as maritime powers vs continental Russia; Mahanian strategy → US Pacific fleet
- Spykman's Rimland (1944): critique of Mackinder — rimlands (coastal Eurasia) more important than heartland; whoever controls rimland rules Eurasia; containment strategy intellectual foundation
- Brzezinski's Grand Chessboard (1997): Eurasia as central arena; Eurasian Balkans (Central Asia), key pivots (Ukraine/Azerbaijan/Turkey/Iran/South Korea), US as first truly global power; China containment
- Contemporary great-power competition: US unipolarity (1991-2008) → multipolar contestation; China's "peaceful rise" → Wolf Warrior diplomacy; Russia 2022 Ukraine invasion as revisionism; India as swing state; Thucydides Trap debate
- Belt and Road Initiative (BRI): ~140 countries, infrastructure lending, debt-trap diplomacy debate (Hambantota port), geopolitical vs economic interpretation, quality vs scale
- Indo-Pacific as strategic concept: US INDOPACOM, Quad (US/India/Japan/Australia), AUKUS (nuclear submarines), Taiwan Strait as potential flashpoint (deterrence calculus), South China Sea (UNCLOS vs Chinese 9-dash line, artificial islands)
- Critical geography: Strait of Hormuz (21M bbl/day oil), Strait of Malacca (40% of world trade), Bosporus (Turkish control of Black Sea access), GIUK gap (NATO anti-submarine), Suez/Panama chokepoints
- Arctic competition: ice-free Arctic sea routes (NSR shortens Europe-Asia 40%), Arctic Council, Russian military buildup (Murmansk, icebreaker fleet), resource claims, Article 76 UNCLOS continental shelf extensions
- Sanctions as geopolitical tool: SWIFT exclusion (Russia 2022), export controls (chips to China), secondary sanctions extraterritoriality, dollar weaponization and de-dollarization counter-response
