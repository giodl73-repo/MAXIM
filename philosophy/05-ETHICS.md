# 05 — Ethics

## Metaethics, Normative Theories, Applied Ethics, AI Ethics

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Metaethics: what is the nature of moral claims? Are they objective or subjective?
- Moral realism: moral facts exist independently of minds; Platonism, naturalism, non-naturalism
- Moral anti-realism: error theory (Mackie — all moral claims false), expressivism (Ayer, Blackburn), relativism
- Quasi-realism (Blackburn): earning the right to talk as if moral realism is true
- Moral epistemology: how do we know moral truths? Intuition, reason, reflective equilibrium
- Normative theories: systematic frameworks for determining right action
- Consequentialism (Bentham, Mill, Singer): maximize good outcomes; utility; problems (demandingness, separateness of persons)
- Act vs rule consequentialism; preference vs hedonic utilitarianism
- Deontology (Kant): duty-based ethics; categorical imperative (universalizability, humanity formula)
- Perfect vs imperfect duties; moral absolutism; trolley problem as deontological test
- Rights-based theories: negative vs positive rights; Nozick's libertarianism
- Virtue ethics (Aristotle): character-based; eudaimonia (flourishing); virtues as golden mean
- Contemporary virtue ethics: MacIntyre, Foot — virtues as biological flourishing
- Contractualism (Rawls, Scanlon): principles that could not be reasonably rejected
- Rawls' veil of ignorance; original position; difference principle; justice as fairness
- Moral psychology: Haidt's social intuitionism; moral dumbfounding; dual-process theories
- Parfit's population ethics: total vs average utilitarianism; repugnant conclusion; non-identity problem
- Applied ethics — AI:
  - Alignment problem: specifying human values for AI systems; value learning
  - Moral status of AI: can AI suffer? Moral patiency; Bostrom's orthogoality thesis
  - Autonomous weapons: just war theory applied to killer robots; accountability gap
  - Surveillance and privacy: panopticon, informational self-determination
  - Algorithmic fairness: definitions of fairness (demographic parity, equalized odds); incompatibility theorems
- Applied ethics — bioethics: autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice (Beauchamp-Childress)
- Applied ethics — environmental: future generations, non-human animals, ecosystem value
- Political philosophy: Hobbes/Locke/Rousseau social contract; liberty; equality; distributive justice
