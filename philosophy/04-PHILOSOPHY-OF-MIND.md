# 04 — Philosophy of Mind

## Consciousness, Qualia, Functionalism, Intentionality, AI Consciousness

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- The mind-body problem: how does mind relate to matter? Dualism vs monism
- Cartesian substance dualism: res cogitans vs res extensa; the interaction problem
- Property dualism: one substance (matter) with two kinds of properties (physical + mental)
- Identity theory: mental states = brain states (Place, Smart); type vs token identity
- Functionalism: mental states defined by causal role, not physical substrate — Putnam's breakthrough
- Multiple realizability: same mental state can be realized in different physical systems; consciousness in silicon?
- The hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers): why is there something it is like to be me?
- Easy problems: explaining cognitive functions (attention, memory, report) — tractable
- Explanatory gap (Levine): even if we explain all function, why should that feel like anything?
- Qualia: the subjective character of experience — redness of red, painfulness of pain
- Inverted qualia: could your red be my green while behavior is identical?
- Absent qualia (philosophical zombie): behavior-identical being with no inner experience
- Physicalist responses: eliminativism (Churchland), illusionism (Frankish), higher-order theories
- Integrated Information Theory (IIT, Tononi): consciousness = Φ (integrated information); controversial
- Global Workspace Theory (Baars, Dehaene): consciousness = global broadcast in neural workspace
- Predictive processing (Clark, Friston): brain as prediction engine; active inference
- Intentionality: aboutness of mental states; how can thoughts be about things?
- Brentano's thesis: intentionality is the mark of the mental
- Searle's Chinese Room: syntax is not sufficient for semantics; blocks strong AI
- Functionalist reply to Chinese Room: systems reply, robot reply
- Turing test: behavioral criterion for intelligence — criticisms and defenses
- Philosophy of AI: can machines be conscious? Moral status of AI; substrate independence
- Consciousness and quantum mechanics: Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR hypothesis; criticisms
- Free will: compatibilism vs libertarianism vs hard determinism; neuroscience and agency
