## The Philosophy Map

```
PHILOSOPHY
│
├── LOGIC & FORMAL METHODS          ← foundations of all rigorous argument
│   ├── Propositional logic
│   ├── Predicate (first-order) logic
│   ├── Modal logic (necessity, possibility)
│   └── Proof theory + incompleteness (Gödel)
│
├── EPISTEMOLOGY                    ← theory of knowledge
│   ├── What is knowledge? (JTB + Gettier)
│   ├── Skepticism and its responses
│   ├── Scientific method and confirmation
│   └── Social epistemology
│
├── METAPHYSICS                     ← fundamental nature of reality
│   ├── Ontology (what exists?)
│   ├── Causation and laws of nature
│   ├── Time and persistence
│   └── Modality (necessity, possibility, possible worlds)
│
├── PHILOSOPHY OF MIND              ← consciousness, cognition
│   ├── Mind-body problem
│   ├── Consciousness and qualia
│   ├── Intentionality and mental content
│   └── AI and machine consciousness
│
├── ETHICS                          ← right action, value, obligation
│   ├── Metaethics (nature of moral claims)
│   ├── Normative ethics (consequentialism, deontology, virtue)
│   ├── Applied ethics (AI, bioethics, climate)
│   └── Political philosophy
│
└── PHILOSOPHY OF SCIENCE           ← foundations of scientific inquiry
    ├── Demarcation problem
    ├── Scientific explanation
    ├── Realism vs anti-realism
    └── Paradigm shifts and scientific revolutions
```

## Why Philosophy Matters for an Engineer/Scientist

**Planned coverage — key themes:**
- Gödel's incompleteness theorems: limits of formal systems → limits of any axiomatic approach
- Halting problem as philosophical corollary: undecidability from the inside (MIT TCS bridge)
- Bayesian epistemology: rationality as coherent probability updating — foundation of ML
- Philosophy of causation: correlation vs causation, counterfactual theories, causal graphs (Pearl)
- Philosophy of probability: frequentist vs Bayesian interpretations — matters for statistics
- Philosophy of mind and AI: can machines think? Searle's Chinese Room vs functionalism
- Ethics of AI: alignment, value learning, moral status of AI systems, liability
- Philosophy of physics: what does quantum mechanics mean? (Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Pilot Wave)
- Scientific realism: do theoretical entities (quarks, fields) exist?
- Paradigm shifts (Kuhn): normal science, anomalies, revolutions — understanding scientific progress
- Logic and PL theory bridge: propositions-as-types (Curry-Howard) — already in 23-PL-THEORY.md
- Decision theory: expected utility, Newcomb's problem, game theory foundations
