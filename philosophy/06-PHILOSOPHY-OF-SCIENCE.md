# 06 — Philosophy of Science

## Demarcation, Explanation, Realism, Paradigm Shifts, Quantum Interpretation

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- The demarcation problem: what distinguishes science from non-science / pseudoscience?
- Logical positivism: verificationism — meaning = method of verification (Vienna Circle)
- Popper's falsificationism: science proceeds by bold conjectures + attempts at refutation
- Problems with falsificationism: the Duhem-Quine thesis (auxiliary hypotheses), Lakatos's response
- Lakatos's research programmes: protective belt, hard core, progressive vs degenerating programmes
- Kuhn's Structure of Scientific Revolutions: normal science, anomalies, crisis, revolution, paradigm shift
- Incommensurability: paradigms can't be fully compared; is science rational progress?
- Feyerabend's epistemological anarchism: "anything goes"; science is not uniquely rational
- Scientific explanation: covering-law model (Hempel-Oppenheim, deductive-nomological)
- Problems with D-N: irrelevance (height/shadow), asymmetry (pendulum vs period)
- Causal-mechanical explanation (Salmon): explanation = identifying causal structure
- Unification explanation (Friedman, Kitcher): explanation = reducing number of independent patterns
- Scientific realism: mature science is approximately true; entities it posits exist
- Structural realism: only the structure (mathematical relations), not the nature, is preserved across theory change
- Anti-realism: van Fraassen's constructive empiricism — aim is empirical adequacy, not truth
- No-miracles argument: realism best explains success of science
- Pessimistic meta-induction: past theories (caloric, phlogiston, ether) were false → current theories probably false
- Underdetermination: empirically equivalent theories; theory choice involves non-evidential factors
- Model-based science: models as mediators (Morrison & Morgan); idealization; Ptolemy's spheres were "just a model"
- Quantum mechanics interpretations:
  - Copenhagen (Bohr): wave function is not real; measurement creates fact
  - Many-worlds (Everett): all branches are real; decoherence explains apparent collapse
  - Pilot wave / de Broglie-Bohm: deterministic, nonlocal hidden variables
  - QBism: probabilities are agent's degrees of belief; quantum mechanics is a user's manual
  - Relational QM (Rovelli): quantum states are relative to observers
- Philosophy of spacetime: absolute vs relational space (Newton vs Leibniz); conventionality of simultaneity
- Philosophy of biology: species concept, natural selection as explanation, reductionism vs emergence in biology
- Philosophy of social science: hermeneutics, verstehen, values in social science, critical realism
