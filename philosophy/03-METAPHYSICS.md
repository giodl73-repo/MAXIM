# 03 — Metaphysics

## Ontology, Causation, Time, Modality, Identity, Laws of Nature

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- What is metaphysics? Aristotle's "first philosophy"; the study of being qua being
- Ontology: what kinds of things exist? Particular vs universal, abstract vs concrete
- Universals debate: realism (universals exist independently), nominalism (only particulars), trope theory
- Properties: monism, dualism; sparse vs abundant views; natural properties
- Existence and quantification: Quine's criterion "to be is to be the value of a variable"
- Causation: regularity theory (Hume), counterfactual theory (Lewis), mechanism, powers
- Counterfactual analysis: □→ operator, closest possible world where C doesn't obtain
- Causal graphs and structural equations: Pearl's SCM — formalization of causation (ML bridge)
- Laws of nature: Humean regularity vs DTA (dispositional/causal essentialism), Armstrong's universals
- Necessity and possibility: de dicto vs de re, metaphysical vs logical vs physical necessity
- Possible worlds: Lewisian modal realism vs ersatzism (abstract possible worlds)
- Modality in physics: could the constants of nature have been different? Anthropic reasoning
- Personal identity: psychological continuity theory (Locke, Parfit), biological view, narrative identity
- Parfit's Reasons and Persons: no persistent self; implications for ethics and rationality
- Time: A-theory (McTaggart's A-series: past/present/future) vs B-theory (eternal block universe)
- Time and physics: block universe (special relativity), presentism vs eternalism, time travel
- Persistence: endurantism (3D through time) vs perdurantism (4D worm), temporal parts
- Mereology: parts and wholes, composition, Ship of Theseus, composition as identity
- Abstract objects: mathematical Platonism (Frege, Gödel) vs nominalism (Field's fictionalism)
- Philosophy of mathematics: do numbers exist? If so, how do we know about them?
- Emergence: strong vs weak emergence; consciousness, life, complex systems
- Ground and dependence: metaphysical grounding, priority, fundamentality — recent literature
