# Greek & Roman Mythology

## The Big Picture

Greek mythology is the most extensively documented polytheistic tradition in the Western world, preserved in epic poetry (Homer, ~800 BCE), genealogical theology (Hesiod, ~700 BCE), tragedy (Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides, 5th c. BCE), and hundreds of other texts. Roman mythology absorbed Greek mythology wholesale after ~200 BCE, renaming most deities while preserving their stories.

## Major Myth Cycles

### The Trojan War Cycle

```
CAUSE: Judgment of Paris — Eris (Discord) throws golden apple
"For the Fairest" → Hera, Athena, Aphrodite compete
Paris (Trojan prince) judges → Aphrodite wins (bribes with
Helen, most beautiful woman in world)

ABDUCTION: Paris takes Helen from Sparta (husband: Menelaus)

THE WAR (10 years):
Year 1-9: Siege of Troy, no progress
Year 10: Achilles' rage (Iliad begins here)
  Agamemnon takes Briseis from Achilles
  → Achilles withdraws from battle
  → Greeks suffer
  → Patroclus (Achilles' friend/beloved) enters battle in Achilles' armor
  → Patroclus killed by Hector (Troy's champion)
  → Achilles returns in grief, kills Hector
  → Priam ransoms Hector's body (Iliad ends)

AFTER ILIAD:
  Achilles killed by Paris (arrow in heel — Achilles' one vulnerability)
  Odysseus devises the Trojan Horse
  Troy falls, burns
  Helen returned to Menelaus

KEY SOURCES: Homer's Iliad, cyclic epics (Cypria, Aethiopis, etc.)
```

### The Odyssey

```
10-year return journey of Odysseus (Ulysses) from Troy to Ithaca

MAJOR EPISODES:
  Cyclops Polyphemus → Odysseus blinds him → Poseidon's wrath
  Land of the Dead (Nekuia) → meets ghosts incl. Tiresias, Achilles
  Circe → turns men to pigs, then advisor
  Scylla and Charybdis → monster cliff vs. whirlpool
  Island of Helios → men eat forbidden cattle → all die
  Calypso's island → 7 years detained (nymph in love)
  Phaeacians → hospitality + the story told in flashback (Books 9-12)
  Return to Ithaca → disguised as beggar
  Suitors' contest → stringing Odysseus's bow → slaughter of suitors
  Reunion with Penelope

THEMES: cunning (metis) over strength, hospitality (xenia), homecoming
```

### The Labors of Heracles (Hercules)

```
WHY: Hera drives Heracles mad; he kills his wife Megara and children
PENANCE: Twelve Labors assigned by King Eurystheus

1.  Nemean Lion             — invulnerable skin; strangled
2.  Lernaean Hydra          — grows two heads per cut; Iolaus cauterizes
3.  Ceryneian Hind          — golden-antlered, sacred to Artemis; captured
4.  Erymanthian Boar        — captured alive
5.  Augean Stables          — cleaned in one day by diverting rivers
6.  Stymphalian Birds       — man-eating birds driven off with rattle
7.  Cretan Bull             — captured, brought to Eurystheus
8.  Mares of Diomedes       — man-eating horses; fed Diomedes to them
9.  Girdle of Hippolyta     — Amazon queen's belt; Hera causes conflict
10. Cattle of Geryon        — three-bodied giant; long journey
11. Apples of the Hesperides— golden apples; Atlas holds up sky while H. gets them
12. Cerberus               — brought from underworld alive

DEATH: poisoned tunic from centaur Nessus (Deianira's mistake)
APOTHEOSIS: burns on pyre, ascends to Olympus, reconciles with Hera
```

### Perseus

```
BIRTH: Zeus + Danaë (imprisoned in bronze tower)
        Acrisius (grandfather) casts them to sea in chest
        Rescued by Dictys on Seriphos island

QUEST: Kill Medusa (one of three Gorgons — gaze turns to stone)
  Helpers: Athena (shield-mirror), Hermes (winged sandals+harpe),
           Graeae sisters for info (steal their one eye)
  Kills: Medusa (beheads via reflection in shield)
  From Medusa's blood: Pegasus (winged horse) + Chrysaor spring

RETURN: Saves Andromeda from sea monster (Cetus)
         Rescues mother from Polydectes by showing Medusa's head
         → turns him to stone

IRONY: Accidentally kills grandfather Acrisius with discus at games
        (prophecy fulfilled despite all precautions)

FOUNDED: Mycenae
```

### Oedipus

```
ORACLE: Oedipus will kill his father and marry his mother

HISTORY:
  Birth: King Laius of Thebes, told of oracle, exposes infant
  Rescue: shepherds give to Corinthian king/queen, raised as prince
  Adult Oedipus hears oracle, flees Corinth to avoid it
  Crossroads: kills old man (unknown: Laius, his actual father)
  Thebes: solves Sphinx's riddle → becomes king, marries widow Jocasta
    (unknown: his actual mother)
  Children: Eteocles, Polyneices, Antigone, Ismene

DISCOVERY: Plague; Oedipus investigates → discovers truth
  Jocasta hangs herself
  Oedipus blinds himself, goes into exile with Antigone

SEQUELS:
  Seven Against Thebes: sons fight over throne, both die
  Antigone: buries Polyneices against Creon's edict, dies for it

FREUDIAN SIGNIFICANCE: Freud named the Oedipus complex after this myth
  (the ambivalence of the child toward same-sex parent/opposite-sex parent)
  Whether Sophocles' play was "about" this is hotly debated
```

### Orpheus and Eurydice

```
Orpheus: greatest musician ever (son of Apollo and Muse Calliope)
Music so powerful it charmed rocks, trees, rivers, wild beasts

STORY:
  Eurydice (wife) dies from snakebite on wedding day
  Orpheus descends to underworld
  His music moves Hades and Persephone → granted to take Eurydice back
  CONDITION: walk back without looking behind until reaching upper world
  At the last moment Orpheus turns to check she follows → she returns to death

AFTERMATH:
  Orpheus wanders, refuses all women
  Maenads (Dionysus's frenzied followers) tear him apart
  His head floats down river singing; oracle at Lesbos

THEMES: art's power, loss, the one rule that cannot be broken
  Orphism: mystery cult promising afterlife salvation
```

## Eleusinian Mysteries

```
Location: Eleusis (near Athens)
Deity: Demeter and Persephone (and Iacchus/Dionysus)

MYTH FOUNDATION:
  Persephone abducted by Hades while picking flowers
  Demeter searches, earth withers (autumn/winter)
  Zeus negotiates: Persephone returns but ate pomegranate seeds
  → must spend 1/3 year in underworld (winter) + 2/3 with mother (spring/summer)

THE MYSTERIES (initiatory rites):
  LESSER MYSTERIES: spring, Athens — purification, preparation
  GREATER MYSTERIES: September, Eleusis
  — 9 days
  — Fasting, purification, procession from Athens to Eleusis
  — The dromena (things done), legomena (things said), deicnymena (shown)
  — The exact central revelation was never written — initiates swore secrecy
  — Promised initiates: better afterlife, loss of fear of death

  Cicero: "We have been given a reason not only to live in joy but to die in hope"

Participants included: Plato, Hadrian, Cicero, Marcus Aurelius
Running: 1900+ years (c. 1600 BCE → 392 CE, abolished by Christian Emperor Theodosius)
```

## Greek vs. Roman: Key Differences

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           GREEK vs. ROMAN MYTHOLOGY — DIFFERENCES                │
│                                                                  │
│  1. ROMAN GODS LESS ANTHROPOMORPHIC originally                   │
│     — Early Roman religion = numina (divine powers/presences)    │
│     — Abstract gods of function: Janus (doorways), Terminus      │
│       (boundaries), Saturn (planting time), Consus (grain)       │
│     — Imported Greek forms + personalities ~200 BCE              │
│                                                                  │
│  2. ROMAN FOUNDATION MYTHS:                                      │
│     — Romulus + Remus (suckled by she-wolf Lupa)                 │
│     — Romulus kills Remus, founds Rome                           │
│     — Aeneas: Trojan hero, escapes Troy, founds Latin bloodline  │
│       (Virgil's Aeneid, ~19 BCE — Rome's national epic)          │
│                                                                  │
│  3. ROMAN HOUSEHOLD RELIGION:                                    │
│     — Lares (household/ancestor spirits) — shrines in homes      │
│     — Penates (pantry/provisions gods)                           │
│     — Genius (divine creative force of the paterfamilias)        │
│     These are specifically Roman, no Greek equivalent            │
│                                                                  │
│  4. DEIFICATION OF EMPERORS:                                     │
│     — Julius Caesar → divus Julius (deified after assassination) │
│     — Augustus established imperial cult                         │
│     — Later emperors routinely deified                           │
│     — Caligula/Domitian claimed living divinity (controversial)  │
│                                                                  │
│  5. JANUS — uniquely Roman:                                      │
│     Two-faced god of beginnings, transitions, doorways           │
│     January named after him; no Greek equivalent                 │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Common Confusion Points

**Apollo is Greek AND Roman** — uniquely, Apollo was imported by Rome without a name change. Most other Greek deities got Roman names (Zeus→Jupiter), but Apollo remained Apollo.

**Hercules vs. Heracles**: Heracles is the Greek name; Hercules is the Roman name. The myths are largely the same but Rome gave Hercules a larger cult presence and civic importance.

**Hades is not evil**: Hades is stern, just, and impartial — the dead must go somewhere. He's not a devil figure; he simply rules the dead. He rarely leaves the underworld and is not the antagonist of Greek mythology the way Satan is in Christian thought.

**Homer's Iliad doesn't tell the whole Trojan War**: The Iliad covers about 50 days of the 10th year of the war. The fall of Troy (the Trojan Horse) is NOT in the Iliad — it's in the cyclic epics (lost) and referenced in the Odyssey. The complete story requires multiple sources.

**Myth vs. Tragedy**: Aeschylus, Sophocles, and Euripides took existing myths and reinterpreted them for dramatic purposes. Their versions are not the "original" or "authoritative" myth. Euripides in particular was known for revisionist takes (his Medea is especially unsympathetic to Medea compared to earlier tellings).
