# Egyptian Mythology

## The Big Picture

Egyptian religion is one of the world's longest-lived: 3,000+ years from c. 3000 BCE to 392 CE (Theodosius closes pagan temples). It produced the **oldest religious texts in the world** (Pyramid Texts, 2400 BCE), an extraordinarily complex theology, and the most elaborate system of afterlife preparation in any ancient culture.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              EGYPTIAN COSMOLOGICAL SCHOOLS                        │
│                                                                  │
│  HELIOPOLIS (On):                                               │
│  The Ennead — "Nine" — the primary creation theology            │
│  Atum → Shu+Tefnut → Geb+Nut → Osiris+Isis+Set+Nephthys        │
│  Ra/Atum as sun-creator; solar theology dominant                │
│                                                                  │
│  MEMPHIS:                                                        │

│                                                                  │
│  HERMOPOLIS:                                                     │
│  The Ogdoad — "Eight" primordial pairs (chaos before creation)  │

│  Kuk+Kauket (darkness) — the eight chaos forces                │
│  Creation: primordial mound rises from chaos waters; first light │
│                                                                  │
│  THEBES (New Kingdom dominance):                                │
│  Amun becomes "king of gods"; syncretized as Amun-Ra            │

│                                                                  │
│  AMARNA HERESY (Akhenaten, 1353–1336 BCE):                     │
│  Akhenaten (Amenhotep IV) suppresses all gods → worship Aten only│

│  Reversed immediately after Akhenaten's death                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## The Heliopolitan Ennead

The nine primary deities of the Heliopolitan tradition, the most widely disseminated Egyptian theology:

```
ATUM (or Ra-Atum, Ra) — the self-created first god
  Creator; self-generated from the primordial waters Nun
  Masturbation myth: Atum produces Shu and Tefnut from himself
  (some versions: spits them out; others: masturbation)
  Solar deity: the setting sun as old man; midday as Ra-Horakhty

SHU — Air, atmosphere, light between earth and sky
  Son of Atum; holds up Nut (sky) from Geb (earth)
  The eternal separation of sky from earth

TEFNUT — Moisture, rain, dew
  Wife of Shu; twin of Shu; often depicted as lioness
  The Eye of Ra narrative: Tefnut as the sun's violent side

GEB — Earth (male — unusual; most earth deities are female)
  Son of Shu and Tefnut; father of the Osiris generation
  Depicted lying flat (the earth surface); green (vegetation)
  The Earth's rumbling: Geb laughing

NUT — Sky (female — the sky is her arched body)
  Swallows Ra each evening; gives birth to him each dawn
  Stars = her body
  Five children: Osiris, Horus the Elder, Set, Isis, Nephthys
  (on the five extra days Thoth won from the moon in dice)

OSIRIS — Death, resurrection, fertility, kingship of the dead
  First-born of Nut and Geb; first king of Egypt; civilizing deity
  Myth: murdered by Set → core myth of Egyptian religion

ISIS — Magic, motherhood, healing, throne
  Wife and sister of Osiris; the quintessential magical deity
  Her name means "throne"

SET — Chaos, desert, storms, foreigners, the necessary antagonist
  Murders Osiris; wars with Horus; but also PROTECTS Ra's solar barque
  from Apep (serpent of chaos) — ambiguous figure

NEPHTHYS — Mourning, transition, hidden things
  Sister of Isis; wife of Set; helps Isis reassemble Osiris's body
```

## The Osiris Myth — The Central Narrative

The most important narrative in Egyptian religion, encoding the theology of kingship, death, resurrection, and cosmic order (ma'at):

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    THE OSIRIS MYTH                                │
│                                                                  │
│  GOLDEN AGE:                                                     │
│  Osiris is the first king; teaches agriculture, laws, civilization│
│  Isis rules while Osiris civilizes the world                    │
│                                                                  │
│  FIRST MURDER:                                                   │
│  Set tricks Osiris into lying in an ornate chest                │
│  Seals it, throws it into the Nile → floats to Byblos (Lebanon) │
│  Isis searches → finds the chest embedded in a tree             │
│  (Isis as nurse to Byblos's queen; nearly burns the prince      │
│   immortal in fire; interrupted)                                │
│  Brings Osiris body home                                        │
│                                                                  │
│  SECOND MURDER AND DISMEMBERMENT:                               │
│  Set finds body → dismembers it into 14 (or 42) pieces          │
│  Scatters across Egypt                                          │
│                                                                  │
│  ISIS REASSEMBLES OSIRIS:                                        │
│  Isis and Nephthys gather pieces (all but the phallus,          │
│  eaten by Nile fish — Isis makes a golden replacement)          │
│  Isis breathes life into Osiris with her wings                  │
│  Conceives Horus (post-mortem conception — miraculous)          │
│                                                                  │
│  OSIRIS IN THE UNDERWORLD:                                       │
│  Osiris cannot fully return to life; becomes king of the dead   │
│  (the Duat — Egyptian underworld)                               │
│  His resurrection is partial: he is the first mummy             │
│  Embalming = re-enacting Isis/Anubis's preparation of Osiris   │
│                                                                  │
│  THE CONTENDINGS OF HORUS AND SET:                              │
│  Horus grows up (protected by Isis in papyrus swamps)           │
│  Challenges Set for Osiris's throne                             │
│  80-year legal battle before the tribunal of the Ennead         │
│  Multiple combats, trials, mutilations (Horus loses eye,        │
│  Set loses testicles in some versions)                          │
│  Eventually: Horus wins — becomes king of Egypt                 │
│  Set sent to sky as god of storms (necessary power, not pure evil)│
│                                                                  │
│  SIGNIFICANCE:                                                   │
│  The living king = Horus; the dead king = Osiris                │
│  Every pharaoh is Horus alive; becomes Osiris at death          │
│  Every tomb = Osiris's resurrection narrative enacted           │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Major Deities Beyond the Ennead

```
THOTH — Wisdom, writing, magic, moon, judgment
  Ibis-headed (or baboon); invented writing (hieroglyphics)
  Records the result of the weighing of the heart
  Arbitrates disputes; "the tongue of Ra"
  Greek equivalent: Hermes (→ Hermes Trismegistus)

ANUBIS — Embalming, funerary rites, guardian of cemeteries
  Jackal-headed (jackals near desert graves)
  Originally king of the dead (before Osiris myth unified them)
  Conducts the weighing of the heart; inventor of embalming
  Made the first mummy of Osiris

HORUS — Sky, kingship, the living pharaoh
  Falcon-headed; the sun = right eye, moon = left eye (Eye of Horus)
  The Eye of Horus (wedjat) = healing, protection, royal power
  Multiple Horus forms: Horus the Elder (Haroeris), Horus of Edfu,
  Horus the child (Harpocrates = "Horus the younger")

RA (also Re, Ra-Horakhty) — Sun, creator, king of gods
  Falcon-headed with sun disk; daily solar journey on the Barque of Ra
  Day: Ra; setting: Atum; night: Auf (ram-headed)
  Nightly battle: Ra battles Apep (Apophis) — chaos serpent in Duat
  Set helps fight Apep (redemptive role of disorder)

HATHOR — Love, music, dancing, fertility, beauty, motherhood
  Cow goddess (or woman with cow horns + sun disk)
  Rejoices the heart; drunken festival of Hathor
  Also goddess of death in some aspects (Eye of Ra)
  Greek equivalent: Aphrodite; Roman: Venus

BASTET — Cats, home, protection, fertility
  Cat-headed (was lioness, became domestic cat)
  Guardian of the home; fierce protector against evil
  Cult center: Bubastis; mass cat burials found there

SEKHMET — War, destruction, disease, healing (dual)
  Lioness-headed; "the powerful one"
  The Eye of Ra in its destructive aspect
  Nearly destroys all of humanity (Ra appeases with beer dyed red)

PTAH — Creation, crafts, architecture, fertility
  Mummiform god (in tight wrapping); beard
  Creates through heart and tongue (thought and word)
  Patron of craftsmen; his high priest was "Great Master of Craftsmen"

KHNUM — Potter, river, creation
  Ram-headed; potters wheel; creates human bodies on his wheel
  Associated with Nile cataracts at Elephantine

SOBEK — Nile, crocodile, military, fertility
  Crocodile-headed; strength and power
  Ambiguous: crocodiles eat people but also control Nile floods

ATUM — see Ennead; the self-created, setting sun

KHEPRI — Rising sun as scarab beetle
  Rolls the sun-disk across the sky like a dung beetle rolls dung
  Scarab amulets = solar protection, resurrection
```

## The Solar Theology — Ra's Daily Journey

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 RA'S SOLAR BARQUE                                │
│                                                                  │
│  DAY (above horizon):                                            │
│  Ra (or Ra-Horakhty — "Ra-Horus of the Two Horizons")            │
│  Sails across the sky in the Barque of Millions                  │
│  Accompanied by a divine crew                                    │
│                                                                  │
│  SUNSET → NIGHT JOURNEY (Duat/underworld):                       │
│  Ra enters as Auf (the ram-headed night sun — weak, needs rest)  │
│  12 hours = 12 gates / 12 divisions of the night (Amduat)        │
│  Each hour: divine beings greet Ra; he grants them life via his  │
│  divine light; without this passage, no sunrise                  │
│                                                                  │
│  THE CONFLICT:                                                   │
│  Each night: Apep (Apophis) — cosmic chaos serpent — attacks     │
│  Ra must be defended by Set, Mehen (snake who coils around Ra),  │
│  the crew, and ritual spells recited by priests above            │
│  Priests chanted anti-Apophis spells in every major temple daily │
│  → cosmic order maintained by ritual participation               │
│                                                                  │
│  DAWN:                                                           │
│  Ra emerges reborn as Khepri (scarab = self-creating)            │
│  The victory over Apep renewed each day                          │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## The Afterlife — The Duat and Judgment

### Geography of the Duat

```
ENTRANCE: the western horizon (where the sun sets)

TWELVE HOURS OF THE NIGHT (twelve divisions):
  Each is a separate realm with distinct inhabitants, serpents, gates
  The deceased must know the secret names and spells for each gate
  → This is the function of the Book of the Dead (and earlier Pyramid Texts)

THE FIELD OF REEDS (Aaru):
  The Egyptian paradise — lush, fertile, eternal
  The deceased plows, sows, reaps forever (or is magically aided by
  shabtis/ushabtis = small servant figurines buried with the dead)

LAKE OF FIRE (Amenti):
  Consumed by fire demons — fate of the unrighteous
```

### The Weighing of the Heart

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              THE HALL OF TWO TRUTHS — JUDGMENT SCENE             │
│                                                                  │
│  The most important afterlife ritual in Egyptian religion        │
│                                                                  │
│  SETTING: Great Hall before Osiris and 42 assessor deities       │
│                                                                  │
│  THE SCALE:                                                      │
│  One pan: the deceased's HEART (ib) — the seat of consciousness │
│           and moral record (the heart remembers all deeds)      │
│  Other pan: the FEATHER OF MA'AT (truth, justice, cosmic order) │
│                                                                  │
│  JUDGES:                                                         │
│  Anubis: conducts and watches the scale                         │
│  Thoth: records the result                                      │
│  Osiris: presides                                               │
│  Ammit: "The Devourer" — waits to eat the heart if heavy        │
│    (body of lion, head of crocodile, hindquarters of hippopotamus)│
│    → Second death: total annihilation (no afterlife)            │
│                                                                  │
│  THE 42 NEGATIVE CONFESSIONS:                                   │
│  Before the 42 assessor deities (one per sin), the deceased     │
│  recites "I have not..." for each transgression:                │
│  "I have not stolen."                                           │
│  "I have not killed."                                           │
│  "I have not committed adultery."                               │
│  "I have not stolen temple offerings."                          │
│  "I have not spoken evil against the king."                     │
│  "I have not stopped the flow of water."                        │
│  [The list is exhaustive — personal, social, and cosmic sins]   │
│                                                                  │
│  IF HEART = FEATHER: justified (maa-kheru = "true of voice")   │
│    → proceed to the Field of Reeds                              │
│  IF HEART HEAVIER: Ammit devours it → second death              │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Ma'at — The Cosmic Principle

```
MA'AT is not just a goddess — it is the fundamental principle of the cosmos:
  Truth, justice, cosmic order, balance, harmony, morality

  — The world is created in ma'at; entropy/chaos (isfet) always threatens
  — The pharaoh's primary duty: maintain ma'at
  — Every temple ritual is ma'at maintenance
  — The Negative Confessions are a ma'at declaration
  — Thoth represents ma'at as intellectual/divine order
  — Maat (goddess): woman with ostrich feather on her head

ISFET: disorder, falsehood, injustice — the opposite
  — Apep/Apophis: the ultimate isfet
  — Set: the disruptive sfet within cosmic order (necessary)
  — The pharaoh who fails in justice creates isfet

The Egyptian moral system does not simply divide good/evil;
it divides ordered-functional-sustaining (ma'at) from
chaotic-disruptive-dissolving (isfet).
```

## Common Confusion Points

**Egyptian gods have multiple forms and are not cleanly distinct**: Ra, Atum, and Khepri are the same solar deity at different times of day. Horus appears in multiple forms. Mut and Hathor overlap. Egyptian theology is **additive and syncretic** — entities could be merged (Amun-Ra, Ra-Horakhty) without contradiction. Don't impose Greek-style distinct-deity thinking.

**Set is not the Egyptian Satan**: Set murders Osiris and is the antagonist of Horus, but he also defends Ra against Apep every night. He is the god of storms and desert — useful, dangerous, necessary. He becomes more purely negative in the Late Period (after associations with foreign invaders), but classical Egyptian thought saw him as a necessary part of the cosmic order.

**The Book of the Dead is not one canonical text**: Individual copies were commissioned for the wealthy and varied considerably. "Spell 125" (the weighing of the heart) appears in nearly all copies; other spells are optional. It's more like a spell library than a bible.

**Mummification ≠ superstition**: The elaborate mummification process (70 days, organ removal, canopic jars, natron drying, linen wrapping) was a re-enactment of Isis and Anubis preparing Osiris's body. It was the ritual technology of resurrection — theologically necessary, not merely preservationist. The physical body (khat) was one of several "souls": the ka (vital double), ba (personality-bird), ib (heart), ren (name), shut (shadow), and akh (transfigured spirit) all needed to be maintained.
