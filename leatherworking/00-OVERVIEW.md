# Leatherworking: Tanning, Tooling, and Craft — Landscape

## The Big Picture

Leather is animal skin structurally transformed by tanning — a chemical process that stabilizes collagen proteins against decay, water, and mechanical stress. The craft of leatherworking spans everything from high-end saddlery and bookbinding to garment construction and fine goods. The tanning method used determines almost everything about what the leather can do.

```
HIDE STRUCTURE — ANATOMY OF LEATHER
======================================

CROSS-SECTION OF A HIDE:

  ═══════════════════════════  GRAIN LAYER (0.2–0.5mm)
  Hair follicle holes (pores)  Fine, tight collagen; surface appearance
  ─────────────────────────── grain/corium junction (no cut here)

  ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════  CORIUM (bulk)
  Interwoven collagen bundles                              80–95% of thickness
  Density varies: shoulder (loose) → bend (tight, dense)
  ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  FLESH LAYER (scraped off in processing)

TERMINOLOGY:
  Grain side:  The outer surface with original hair follicles
               → "full grain" leather = grain completely intact
               → "top grain" = grain surface but slightly buffed/corrected
  Flesh side:  Inner surface (was against the animal); fibrous, suede-like
  Split:       Horizontal slice through hide below grain
               → No grain surface; weak; used for suede and backing
```

## Hide Anatomy — Regional Variation

The same hide varies dramatically by anatomical region:

```
HIDE REGIONALITY
================

FULL HIDE TOP VIEW (spread flat):

  ←─────── BACK / BEND (center)────────→
  ┌────────────────────────────────────┐
  │        N E C K                     │
  │ ┌─────────────────────────────┐    │
  │ │  BACK / BEND: most uniform  │    │
  │ │  Tightest fiber; consistent │    │
  │ │  Flat; minimal stretch      │    │
  │ │  Best quality zone          │    │
  │ └─────────────────────────────┘    │
  │  SHOULDER    SHOULDER              │
  │  (wrinkles;  (wrinkles;            │
  │   looser)     looser)              │
  │  BELLY        BELLY                │
  │  (most        (most                │
  │   stretch;     stretch;            │
  │   loosest      loosest             │
  │   fiber)       fiber)              │
  └────────────────────────────────────┘

SHOULDER: More wrinkles; looser fiber; stretches more
           Often a distinct shade from the bend
           Use for: straps, belts (shorter length OK)
           Avoid for: carving (less uniform; won't tool as cleanly)

BACK/BEND: Tightest, most uniform
           Best for: wallets, card holders, belts, holsters
           Premium portion → often sold separately as "bend" or "butt"

BELLY: Loosest; stretchiest; most irregular
       Often discarded in high-end work; used for lacing, straps
       Acceptable for: large bag panels (stretch less critical)
```

## Leather Grades and Weight

```
LEATHER WEIGHT AND THICKNESS SYSTEM (US)
==========================================

Leather is sold by weight (ounces per square foot) in the US:

  1 oz = 1/64" thickness = ~0.4mm

  WEIGHT RANGE    THICKNESS          TYPICAL APPLICATIONS
  ------------    ---------          --------------------
  1–2 oz          1/64–2/64"         Garment, glove liners
  2–3 oz          2/64–3/64"         Garment, lining, thin bags
  3–4 oz          3/64–4/64"         Soft bags, garment weight belts
  4–5 oz          4/64–5/64"         Light wallets, card holders, straps
  5–6 oz          5/64–6/64"         Wallets, standard belt (thin)
  6–7 oz          6/64–7/64"         Wallets, sheaths, light holsters
  7–8 oz          7/64–8/64"         Belts (standard), holsters, gun belts
  8–9 oz          8/64–9/64"         Heavy belts, saddle components
  9–10 oz         9/64–10/64"        Soles, heavy straps, saddle parts
  10–12 oz        10/64–12/64"+      Sole leather, heavy saddle work

European metric: thickness in mm (straightforward)

TEMPER: Stiffness category
  Temper          Description
  -------         -----------
  Firm            Holds shape; minimal flex; belts, soles
  Medium          Moderate flex; wallets, holsters
  Soft / pliable  Drapes; garment, bags, upholstery
```

## Craft Traditions

```
LEATHERWORKING TRADITIONS
===========================

SADDLERY:
  Heaviest craft; 8–12 oz veg-tan; stitched + riveted
  Must withstand enormous mechanical loads (rider weight + movement)
  Hand-stitched saddle stitch; heavy thread; all hardware brass/steel
  Historical mastery of tooling and carving for decorative work
  Sheridan style: Western floral carving tradition; elaborate

BOOKBINDING:
  Very thin leather (1–2 oz); often goatskin or calf ("calf vellum")
  Pared extremely thin at edges; used as cover over boards
  Gold tooling: heated stamps on dry leather compress fibers (blind)
                or gilded with gold leaf
  Conservation concerns: historic leather preservation

GARMENT LEATHER:
  Chrome-tanned; very soft; no tooling; garments, gloves, shoes uppers
  Pattern-cutting skills from apparel; seam allowances; machine-sewn
  Skiving (thinning) edges is critical for clean seams

FINE GOODS (bags, wallets, watch straps):
  Veg-tan or chrome-tan depending on item
  Saddle stitch; polished edges; minimal hardware
  Heritage brands: Hermès, Goyard, Ghurka (handmade; veg-tan)
  Mass market: chrome-tan; machine-stitched
```

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Which tanning method enables tooling/carving? | Vegetable tanning (firm; wet-moldable) |
| Which tanning method makes the softest leather? | Chrome tanning (soft, supple, uniform) |
| What is full-grain leather? | Intact outer grain surface; strongest and most durable |
| What is a leather "split"? | Horizontal slice of hide below the grain layer; no grain surface |
| What is leather weight in oz mean? | Thickness: 1 oz ≈ 1/64" ≈ 0.4mm |
| Which hide region is highest quality? | Back/bend (tightest fiber; most uniform; least stretch) |
| What is a "side" of leather? | Half a hide (cut lengthwise); typical purchasing unit |
| What is temper? | Stiffness classification of leather (firm/medium/soft) |
| Why does veg-tan develop patina? | Tannins react with oils, light, and use → surface darkens and enriches |
| What is saddle stitch? | Two-needle hand stitch that forms a locking pattern; more durable than machine stitch |

## Common Confusion Points

**"Genuine leather" is a low-quality grade, not a certification of authenticity.** In marketing, "genuine leather" often means split leather with a painted grain surface — the lowest grade of actual leather. It's not fake, but it's the least desirable grade. The good grades are: full grain > top grain > corrected grain > split. Bonded leather (labeled "bonded leather" or "composite leather") is shredded leather scraps + adhesive + backing — not leather in any meaningful sense.

**Veg-tan and chrome-tan are not interchangeable.** Many beginners buy chrome-tan for tooling and are confused when the swivel knife doesn't leave a clean impression. Chrome-tan is too soft and springy for tooling; the impression closes up as the leather dries. You must use veg-tan for any stamping or carving.

**Leather weight (oz) is a thickness measure, not a quality measure.** A 3 oz full-grain veg-tan is far superior to an 8 oz split. The weight tells you how thick it is; you need to know separately what grade (full-grain vs. split) and what tanning method.

**A "side" vs. a "hide" vs. a "bend"**: A full hide is the whole animal; a side is half the hide (split lengthwise — the more common purchase unit); a bend is the back section of a side (the premium portion). When buying from a leather supplier, know which cut you're getting.

**Brain-tanned leather is distinct from modern commercial leathers.** Brain-tanned buckskin is a historical/artisanal product, very different from any commercial leather. It is machine-washable (uniquely), smoke-set, and extraordinarily soft. It is not available commercially at scale. It's a craft tradition in its own right, separate from commercial leatherworking.
