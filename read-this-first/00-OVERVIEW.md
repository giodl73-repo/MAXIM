## A letter to whoever finds this

You found a set of books.

This is the one to read first.

The other books hold everything humanity learned — about nature, science,
mathematics, medicine, engineering, history, and more. They were written
carefully by people who wanted that knowledge to survive.

But those books assume you already know the basics.

This book starts earlier. It starts with staying alive.

If you are cold, hungry, lost, or hurt — work through this book from the
beginning. Each chapter builds on the last. Each one assumes you finished
the one before it.

If someone near you cannot read — the pictures in each chapter tell the
story. Show them the pictures. Then find someone who can read to help with
the details.

You are not alone in this. Every person who came before you faced hard
times. Most of them survived. You can too.

Start with water.

## Your body as a ruler

You may not have measuring tools. Your body is enough.

```
  ▓             thumb width      ≈ widest part of your thumb
  ▓▓▓▓▓         hand span        ≈ spread hand, tip of thumb to tip of pinky
  ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓     cubit            ≈ elbow to tip of middle finger
  ──────────── arm span          ≈ fingertip to fingertip, arms out wide
  pace                           ≈ one double step (left foot + right foot)
```

Use these measurements throughout this book.

## The sun as a clock

You may not have a timepiece.

```
  ○  (just above the horizon)   →   sunrise or sunset
  ○  (one fist above horizon)   →   about 1 hour of daylight left or begun
  ○  (high, short shadows)      →   midday — the warmest time
  ○  (setting, long shadows)    →   evening — prepare shelter and fire now

  To estimate hours: hold your fist at arm's length.
  The width of one fist at the horizon = about 1 hour of sunlight.
  Four fists from horizon to midday position = about 4 hours.
```

## How to use the other 52 books

When you have worked through this book — when you are safe, sheltered,
fed, and have learned to read — the other books are for you.

They are organized into sections. Each section has four volumes.

The best order to begin:

```
  1.  Natural World       — what plants and animals are around you
  2.  Earth & Space       — weather, land, water, seasons
  3.  Life Sciences       — how living things work, including you
  4.  Material Culture    — how to make things
  5.  Medicine            — (inside Life Sciences) — healing in depth
  6.  Mechanics           — how machines work
  7.  History & Ideas     — where all of this came from

  Read one book per season. Apply what you learn before moving on.
  A skill practiced is worth ten skills memorized.
```

The books with numbers on the spine are the content volumes.
Each starts with an overview (00-OVERVIEW) that explains what is inside.
