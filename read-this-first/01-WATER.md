# 01 — Water

---

```
              WATER FIRST
              ────────────

  Without water, you die in about 3 days.
  This is faster than cold. Faster than hunger.

  Find it. Clean it. Drink it.
```

---

## Why water is first

Your body is mostly water. When you run low, your thinking gets cloudy
first. Then your muscles stop working. Then your heart.

Dark yellow urine means you need water now.
No urine for many hours means danger.
Confusion and dizziness mean you are already low.

Drink before you feel thirsty if you can.

**How much:**
At rest: drink about 1 cupped handful every hour.
Working hard: drink 2–3 cupped handfuls every hour.
Hot weather: drink more.

## Finding water

Some sources are safer than others.

```
SAFEST ──────────────────────────────────── MOST DANGEROUS

  Rain       Spring      Stream      Lake       Still pond
  collected  or seep     (moving)    (large)    or puddle

   ○○○○        ○           ≈≈          ≈≈≈         ○
   ○○○○       / ←          ~           ~~~        (mud)
   collected   from        flowing     big         small
   directly    ground      water       body        pool
```

**Always clean water before drinking** — except rain you catch directly
in a clean container as it falls from the sky.

---

### Signs that water is nearby

When you do not know where water is, look for these:

```
  ○ Animals and birds at dawn and dusk
    — they travel to water every morning and evening
    — follow them

  ○ Green vegetation in a dry landscape
    — a strip of green trees through brown land
    — water is near the roots

  ○ Low ground and valleys
    — water flows downhill
    — walk downhill and keep walking

  ○ Dry streambeds
    — dig in the lowest, outermost bend
    — water may be just below the surface

  ○ Insects in large numbers
    — bees fly no more than a few thousand paces from water
    — mosquitoes and midges stay near water

  ○ Cliffs and rock faces
    — look for dark stains or green moss
    — seeps form where rock layers meet
```

## Cleaning water

**Never drink water you found without cleaning it first.**

You cannot see bacteria, parasites, or poisons. They make you violently
sick. Sickness loses you water faster than not drinking does.

There are three ways to clean water.

---

### Method 1 — Boiling (most reliable)

```
  STEPS:

  1. Fill your container with water.

  2. Build a fire (see chapter 02).

  3. Heat water until it boils.
     A rolling boil looks like this:
                  ○  ○  ○
               ○     ○     ○
              ≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈
              (big bubbles rising from bottom)

  4. Keep it at a full rolling boil for 1 minute.
     If you are very high up (mountains above your knee-height
     when looking at the horizon), boil for 3 minutes.

  5. Let it cool. Cover it. Drink it.

  That is all. Boiling kills everything dangerous in water.
```

Do not let boiled water sit uncovered for long. Cover it.

---

### Method 2 — Solar disinfection (if you have a clear container)

```
  STEPS:

  1. Find a clear container — glass bottle, clear plastic bottle.

  2. Fill it with the clearest water you can find.
     (If cloudy, filter first — see below.)

  3. Lay it in full direct sunlight for 6 hours.
     ○  ←  full sun, no clouds
     [ bottle ]  ←  on a dark surface if possible (absorbs heat)

  4. If the sky is half cloudy or more: leave for 2 full days.

  5. Drink from it.

  This works because UV light from the sun kills bacteria and
  viruses. It does not remove chemicals or make cloudy water safe.
  Use boiling if you can.
```

---

### Method 3 — Filtering (makes water clearer, does NOT make it safe alone)

A filter removes mud, grit, and particles. It does not remove bacteria.
**Always boil or solar-disinfect after filtering.**

```
  BUILD A SIMPLE FILTER:

  Take any container with a small hole at the bottom,
  or a piece of cloth shaped into a cone.

  Fill in layers from top to bottom:

      [ grass or cloth ]     ← catches large debris
      [ charcoal from fire ] ← absorbs some chemicals and taste
      [ fine sand ]          ← filters small particles
      [ gravel ]             ← supports the sand
      ↓ ↓ ↓
      [ collection container ]

  Pour cloudy water in at the top.
  Collect what comes out at the bottom.
  Then boil it before drinking.
```

## Storing water

Once you have clean water, keep it clean.

```
  ○ Use a container with a lid or cover.
  ○ Do not put your hands or fingers inside.
  ○ Do not let animals drink from it.
  ○ Drink from the top, not the bottom.
  ○ Clean your container every few days by rinsing with boiling water.
```

If you have no container, a large folded leaf can hold water for
drinking right away. A clay vessel (see chapter 06) can store it.

---

## Water you must not drink

Some water looks safe and is not.

```
  DO NOT DRINK:

  Blue-green water or surface scum that looks like green paint
  — this is algae that can kill you within hours
  — do not touch it either

  Water with a chemical or petroleum smell
  — it has been poisoned by industry or leaking tanks

  Water that is downstream of a mine
  — mines release heavy metals that cannot be boiled out

  Water surrounded by dead animals
  — something killed them; assume the water is the cause

  Water that burns or irritates your skin when touched
  — very acid or very alkaline; dangerous

  Seawater (ocean water)
  — has too much salt; drinking it makes you more thirsty
  — you can purify seawater only by distillation (complex)
  — do not drink it directly
```

## Signs of dehydration — act now

```
  MILD (act within the hour):
    Dark yellow urine
    Dry mouth
    Headache
    Tired without reason

  SERIOUS (act immediately):
    No urine for half a day
    Dizziness when standing
    Rapid heartbeat
    Nausea
    Sunken eyes

  DANGEROUS (this is a medical emergency):
    Confusion
    Cannot stand
    Skin does not spring back when pinched
```

If someone is very dehydrated, give them small sips frequently.
Do not let them drink a large amount all at once — it causes vomiting,
which wastes the water.

---

## Simple summary

```
  1. Find moving water first. Collect rain if it is raining.
  2. Always boil or solar-disinfect before drinking.
  3. Filter if the water is cloudy, then still boil.
  4. Store in a covered container.
  5. Drink before you feel thirsty.
  6. Watch your urine color. Pale yellow = good. Dark = drink more.
  7. Never drink seawater, algae water, or water that smells chemical.
```
