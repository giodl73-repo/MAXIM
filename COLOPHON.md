# Colophon

---

This is a volume of the **MAXIM Encyclopedia**, a personal reference library in 52 bound volumes plus index.

## Edition

First edition, 2026.

## Typography

Body text is set in **EB Garamond** at 11 point, 14-point line height. EB Garamond is an open-source revival of Claude Garamond's type (c. 1540), designed by Georg Mayr and released under the SIL Open Font License.

Code, diagrams, and monospaced text are set in **JetBrains Mono** at 10 point. JetBrains Mono is designed for technical readability with ligatures disabled for print. Released under the SIL Open Font License.

Running headers are set in small capitals at 8 point: section name, directory, and filename. Page numbers are centered in the footer.

## Paper

Interior pages are printed on 24 lb acid-free bond (~90 gsm), lignin-free, meeting ANSI/NISO Z39.48-1992 (*Permanence of Paper for Publications and Documents in Libraries and Archives*). Alkaline reserve exceeds 2% calcium carbonate. Rated for 500+ years under library storage conditions.

## Binding (Archival Edition)

Case-bound in library buckram over rigid boards. Signatures are Smyth-sewn with thread — mechanically independent of adhesive. Endsheets are acid-free. Head and tail bands in gold and navy. Spine and front cover foil-stamped in gold with the 12-ray MAXIM mark.

## Binding (Standard Edition)

Case-bound with PUR (polyurethane reactive) adhesive over rigid boards. Printed matte-laminated covers. Acid-free interior.

## The Mark

The MAXIM mark is a 12-ray radial symbol. Twelve rays at 30-degree intervals, each terminating at equal radius, with a filled center point. Twelve rays plus one center = thirteen sections. Thirteen sections times four volumes = fifty-two. A deck of cards.

The center point is People. Every other ray is a domain of knowledge. The human is at the center of the mark.
