## Coverage Notes

Paleontology reconstructs the history of life on Earth through the fossil record, spanning 4 billion years from the earliest microbial mats to the megafauna extinctions of the Pleistocene. This directory covers the methodological foundations (stratigraphy, taphonomy, radiometric dating), the major eras in sequence (Precambrian through Cenozoic), the five mass extinctions and their causes and recovery patterns, the major evolutionary transitions in both animals (fish-to-tetrapod, dinosaur-to-bird, synapsid-to-mammal) and plants (algae through angiosperms), and paleoclimatology — the use of fossil and geochemical proxies to reconstruct ancient climates and inform understanding of current climate change.
