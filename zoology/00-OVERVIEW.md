# Zoology — Landscape and Taxonomy

## The Big Picture

Zoology is the study of animals — one of two major eukaryotic kingdoms alongside plants (plus fungi, protists, bacteria). Animals are heterotrophs with ingestive nutrition, typically motile, lacking cell walls, and undergoing sexual reproduction. The ~1.5 million described animal species span ~30 phyla and represent the most morphologically diverse domain of eukaryotic life.

## What Defines an Animal

```
ANIMAL DIAGNOSTIC CHARACTER SUITE
===================================

REQUIRED:
  1. Eukaryotic (membrane-bound nuclei)
  2. Multicellular (no single-celled animals)
  3. Heterotrophic (cannot fix own carbon; must ingest)
  4. No cell walls (vs plants + fungi)
  5. Most: motile at some life stage
  6. Most: sexual reproduction (haploid gametes fusing -> diploid)
  7. Most: embryonic development through blastula stage

DERIVED CHARACTERS (not universal):
  Nervous system (absent in Porifera, Placozoa)
  Muscles (absent in Porifera)
  Bilateral symmetry (absent in Porifera, Cnidaria, many Echinodermata)
  Coelom (absent or modified in many)
  Segmentation (present in Annelida, Arthropoda, Chordata; convergent)
```

## Protostome vs Deuterostome Development

```
PROTOSTOME vs DEUTEROSTOME
=============================

PROTOSTOME ("mouth first"):
  Blastopore -> mouth
  Spiral, determinate cleavage
  Schizocoelomate (coelom from solid mesoderm splitting)
  Groups: Arthropoda, Mollusca, Annelida, Nematoda, Platyhelminthes

DEUTEROSTOME ("mouth second"):
  Blastopore -> anus; mouth forms secondarily
  Radial, indeterminate cleavage
  Enterocoelous (coelom from gut outpocketings)
  Groups: Echinodermata, Chordata

DEVELOPMENTAL FATE:
  Protostome determinate cleavage:
    Each blastomere fate fixed early
    Remove one cell -> defect (mosaic development)
  Deuterostome indeterminate cleavage:
    Fate not fixed at first divisions
    Separate identical twins are possible
    Remove one cell at 2-cell stage -> two complete embryos
    (basis for natural twinning in mammals)
```

## Vertebrate Overview

```
VERTEBRATE DIVERSITY
=====================

SUPERCLASS PISCES (fish; paraphyletic):
  Agnatha (jawless): lampreys, hagfish (~120 spp)
  Chondrichthyes: cartilaginous fish (~1,100 spp)
    Sharks, rays, skates, chimaeras
  Osteichthyes (bony fish):
    Actinopterygii (ray-finned): ~33,000 spp (largest vertebrate class)
    Sarcopterygii (lobe-finned): ~8 species (coelacanths + lungfish)
      + all tetrapods evolved from this group

TETRAPODA (4-limbed descendants of Sarcopterygii):
  Amphibia: ~8,500 spp; aquatic larvae; terrestrial adults
  Reptilia (including birds):
    Lepidosauria (lizards, snakes, tuatara): ~10,000 spp
    Archosauria (birds + crocodilians): ~10,600 bird spp + 27 croc spp
    Testudines (turtles): ~360 spp
  Mammalia: ~5,600 spp
    Monotremata (egg-laying): platypus + echidnas (5 spp)
    Marsupialia (pouched): ~330 spp
    Placentalia: ~5,200 spp (orders: Rodentia, Chiroptera, Primates...)
```

## Common Confusion Points

**Fish is paraphyletic**: A clade called "fish" including all fish but excluding tetrapods doesn't exist monophyletically. Coelacanths and lungfish are more closely related to tetrapods than to sharks. "Fish" is a grade, not a clade.

**Reptiles is also paraphyletic in traditional use**: Classic Reptilia excluded birds. Modern Reptilia = Amniota minus Mammalia (still paraphyletic) OR = Sauropsida including birds (monophyletic). The inclusion of birds in Reptilia is the phylogenetically correct modern treatment.

**Invertebrate is a negative definition**: "Invertebrate" = animal without a vertebral column. This includes 95%+ of animal species and multiple unrelated lineages. Not a natural group — just a convenient term for "not a vertebrate."

**Nematodes are massively undescribed**: ~25,000 species described but estimated >1 million exist. A teaspoon of good agricultural soil contains ~1 million nematodes. The most abundant animal on Earth by individual count is probably a nematode, not an insect.

**Sponges are animals**: Porifera (sponges) lack true tissues and muscles, have no nervous system, and are sessile as adults. Yet they are animals — multicellular, heterotrophic, no cell walls, with a blastula stage in development. They represent the most divergent animal body plan.
