# 11 — Evolution & Population Genetics

## Natural Selection, Drift, Speciation, Phylogenetics, Macroevolution

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Darwin's insights: variation + heritability + differential reproduction → natural selection
- Modern synthesis: Darwinian selection + Mendelian genetics + population genetics (Fisher, Wright, Haldane)
- Extended evolutionary synthesis: developmental plasticity, niche construction, epigenetic inheritance, evolvability
- Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium: p² + 2pq + q² = 1; assumes no selection/mutation/drift/migration/non-random mating
- Deviations from HWE as signal: selection, drift, inbreeding, population structure
- Types of natural selection: directional, stabilizing, disruptive, balancing (heterozygote advantage)
- Sexual selection: intrasexual (male-male competition) and intersexual (female choice); Zahavian handicap
- Genetic drift: random allele frequency change; stronger in small populations (N_e)
- Effective population size N_e: bottlenecks, founder effects, sex ratio, variation in reproductive success
- Mutation-selection balance: equilibrium frequency of deleterious alleles; genetic load
- Neutral theory (Kimura 1968): most molecular variation is selectively neutral; molecular clock
- Nearly neutral theory (Ohta): slightly deleterious mutations; N_e determines fate
- Coalescent theory: time to MRCA, genealogies, Kingman coalescent, θ = 4N_e·μ
- Speciation: allopatric (geographic isolation), sympatric (niche divergence, polyploidy), parapatric
- Reproductive isolation: prezygotic (habitat, temporal, behavioral, mechanical, gametic) and postzygotic (hybrid inviability/sterility)
- Phylogenetics: parsimony, maximum likelihood, Bayesian inference, model selection
- Molecular phylogenetics: sequence alignment, substitution models (JC, HKY, GTR), rate variation (Γ)
- Molecular clock: absolute dating with fossil calibrations; relaxed vs strict clocks
- Macroevolution: patterns in the fossil record; stasis, punctuated equilibrium (Eldredge-Gould), adaptive radiations
- Mass extinctions: five big ones (end-Ordovician, Late Devonian, P-T, T-J, K-Pg); kill mechanisms
- Evo-devo: Hox genes, developmental toolkits, modularity, evolvability, heterochrony, homeosis
- Horizontal gene transfer (HGT): common in prokaryotes; complicates tree of life; ~8% of human genome from viruses (ERVs)
- Major transitions in evolution: chromosomes, eukaryotes, multicellularity, sex, development, language (Maynard Smith & Szathmáry)
