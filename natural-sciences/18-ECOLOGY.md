# 12 — Ecology

## Ecosystems, Population Dynamics, Food Webs, Nutrient Cycles, Biomes

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Levels of ecological organization: individual → population → community → ecosystem → biosphere
- Niche: Hutchinsonian hypervolume; fundamental vs realized niche; niche overlap and competition
- Population ecology: birth rate, death rate, immigration, emigration → population change equation
- Exponential growth: dN/dt = rN, r = intrinsic rate of increase; J-curve
- Logistic growth: dN/dt = rN·(K−N)/K, carrying capacity K; S-curve, logistic equation
- Age structure: survivorship curves (Type I/II/III), life tables, Leslie matrix
- Species interactions: competition (−/−), predation (+/−), herbivory (+/−), mutualism (+/+), commensalism (+/0), parasitism (+/−), amensalism (0/−)
- Competition: interspecific vs intraspecific, competitive exclusion principle (Gause), character displacement
- Predator-prey dynamics: Lotka-Volterra equations, cycles, paradox of enrichment
- Keystone species: disproportionate ecosystem effect relative to biomass (Paine's sea star experiment)
- Community ecology: trophic levels, food chains vs food webs, trophic cascade
- Energy flow: 10% rule (trophic efficiency), primary productivity (NPP vs GPP), secondary productivity
- Biogeochemical cycles: carbon cycle (photosynthesis, respiration, decomposition, ocean sink), nitrogen cycle (fixation/nitrification/denitrification), phosphorus cycle (no gaseous phase), water cycle
- Decomposers: fungi, bacteria — detritivores, nutrient mineralization, soil organic matter
- Succession: primary succession (bare rock → climax), secondary succession, facilitation/tolerance/inhibition models
- Island biogeography (MacArthur-Wilson): species richness = balance of immigration and extinction; area and distance effects
- Biodiversity: α/β/γ diversity, Shannon index H', Simpson's D; species-area relationship (S = cA^z)
- Biomes: tropical rainforest, temperate forest, grassland, desert, taiga, tundra, ocean zones
- Marine ecology: photic vs aphotic zones, thermocline, upwelling, coral reefs, deep sea vents
- Freshwater ecology: lake stratification, eutrophication, nutrient loading, hypoxia
- Conservation biology: minimum viable population, metapopulation dynamics (Levins model), SLOSS debate
- Invasive species: traits, pathways, ecological impacts, management
- Climate change ecology: range shifts, phenological mismatch, coral bleaching, acidification (pH from 8.2 to 8.1)
- Planetary boundaries: Rockström framework — 9 boundaries, 6 breached as of 2023
