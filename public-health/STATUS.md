## Coverage Notes

Public health operates at the population level — preventing disease and promoting health through surveillance, policy, and intervention rather than treating individual patients. This directory covers the epidemiological methods that underpin evidence-based public health (study designs, causation criteria, confounding), disease surveillance infrastructure and outbreak response, the mechanics and immunology of vaccination programs, the growing burden of non-communicable diseases, environmental and occupational exposures, global health governance and financing, comparative health systems analysis, pandemic preparedness frameworks, and the quantitative metrics (DALYs, QALYs, GBD) used to measure population health and prioritize interventions.
