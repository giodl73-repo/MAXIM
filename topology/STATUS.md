## Coverage Notes

Topology studies properties of spaces preserved under continuous deformation — a discipline that begins with metric spaces (the concrete) and generalizes to abstract topological spaces, then deploys algebraic invariants (fundamental group, homology, cohomology) to distinguish spaces that mere continuity arguments cannot separate. This directory covers point-set topology (the foundational machinery of open sets, compactness, connectedness), algebraic topology (homotopy groups, homology/cohomology, exact sequences), and differential topology (manifolds, tangent bundles, de Rham theory). A final module bridges to applications: topological data analysis via persistent homology, topological phases of matter in condensed matter physics, and configuration spaces in robotics.
