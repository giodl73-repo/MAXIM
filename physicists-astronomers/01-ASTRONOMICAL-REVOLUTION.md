# The Astronomical Revolution — Copernicus, Tycho, Kepler, Galileo

## The Problem They Were Solving

For 1,400 years, astronomy ran on Ptolemy's geocentric model (Earth at center,
everything else orbiting in circles and epicycles). It worked — it predicted
planetary positions well enough for navigation and calendars. Why change it?

```
THE STATE OF ASTRONOMY IN 1500
================================

PTOLEMAIC SYSTEM (Geocentric):
  - Earth fixed at center
  - Sun, Moon, planets orbit Earth in circular paths
  - "Epicycles": small circles whose centers move on larger circles
    (explains retrograde motion: planets appearing to move backward)
  - Works for prediction to ~1–2° accuracy (adequate for navigation)

PROBLEM:
  To make it work, kept adding epicycles. By 1500, the model was
  baroque — 40+ circles for all the planets. It felt "wrong."

  More precisely: it had no predictive depth. You could fit the data
  but the model had no explanatory structure.

ALTERNATIVE: Aristarchus (~270 BCE) had proposed a heliocentric model.
  Ignored for 1,800 years. Copernicus revived it.
```

## Nicolaus Copernicus (1473–1543)

### Who He Was

Polish canon (church administrator) and amateur astronomer. Spent decades
working on his heliocentric model. *De revolutionibus orbium coelestium*
(1543) was published the year he died — reportedly he received the first
printed copy on his deathbed.

The book was placed on the Catholic Church's Index of Forbidden Books in
1616 (73 years later), after Galileo made it famous.

### The Contribution: Heliocentrism and the Copernican Principle

**The Heliocentric Model**

```
COPERNICUS'S HELIOCENTRIC SYSTEM
==================================

Key claims:
  1. The Sun is (approximately) at the center of the solar system
  2. Earth rotates on its axis daily (explains rising/setting of stars)
  3. Earth orbits the Sun annually (explains seasons and stellar parallax)
  4. Retrograde motion is an ILLUSION created by relative speeds:
     When Earth "passes" a slower outer planet, that planet appears
     to move backward against the stars.

RETROGRADE MOTION EXPLAINED:
  Earth (inner, faster)
    ───→ Orbit →
  Mars (outer, slower)
    ─→ Orbit →

  As Earth passes Mars, Mars appears to move backward from Earth's perspective.
  No epicycles needed for retrograde — it's purely geometric.

WHAT COPERNICUS KEPT WRONG:
  - Circular orbits (required epicycles STILL for accuracy)
  - He kept 48 circles — nearly as many as Ptolemy
  - The real improvement came from Kepler abandoning circular orbits
```

**The Copernican Principle**

The deeper implication: Earth is not special. No position in the universe is
privileged. This is now called the Copernican Principle (or mediocrity principle):
we should not assume our location, time, or situation is typical.

In modern cosmology: the universe is (on large scales) homogeneous and isotropic.
No preferred location. This is the **cosmological principle** — the Copernican
Principle applied to the entire universe.

## Tycho Brahe (1546–1601)

### Who He Was

Danish nobleman astronomer. Had part of his nose cut off in a duel in 1566
and wore a metal prosthetic for the rest of his life. Built the greatest
pre-telescope observatory in history (Uraniborg, on the island of Hven).
Spent 20 years measuring planetary positions to unprecedented accuracy.

### The Contribution: Precision Measurement

**What Tycho Actually Did**

Tycho did not accept Copernicus's model (mainly for religious reasons and
because he couldn't detect stellar parallax — the apparent shift in star
positions due to Earth's motion). He proposed a hybrid: planets orbit the
Sun, the Sun orbits Earth.

But his greatest contribution was DATA:

```
TYCHO'S OBSERVATIONAL PRECISION
=================================

Pre-Tycho accuracy: ~10 arcminutes (1/6 of a degree)
Tycho's accuracy: ~1–2 arcminutes

How: Designed instruments (quadrants, sextants) that eliminated
     systematic errors. Repeated measurements, calibrated instruments.
     Kept meticulous records of 20 years of observations.

Key data: Mars orbit measured to unprecedented precision.

This data, in Kepler's hands, was the tool that destroyed the
circular orbit model and led to elliptical orbits.

LESSON: Great theory requires great data.
  Copernicus theorized without good data. Kepler had Tycho's data.
  The combination was what worked.
```

Tycho was also the first to observe a supernova (1572 — "Tycho's star") and
a comet (1577) that moved through the supposedly crystalline spheres, breaking
the Aristotelian model of solid celestial spheres.

## Johannes Kepler (1571–1630)

### Who He Was

German mathematician and astronomer. Worked for Tycho Brahe in Prague and
inherited his data. Used that data to determine the actual shape of planetary
orbits. Deeply mystical (spent years trying to fit planetary orbits to nested
Platonic solids) but also rigorous in his mathematical analysis.

### The Contribution: Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

**The Three Laws**

```
KEPLER'S THREE LAWS (1609, 1619)
==================================

LAW 1 (1609): Planets orbit the Sun in ELLIPSES with the Sun at one FOCUS.

  Ellipse: The set of points P where |PF₁| + |PF₂| = constant.
  Two foci F₁, F₂.
  A circle is a special ellipse (F₁ = F₂ = center).

  Earth's eccentricity: e = 0.017 (nearly circular)
  Mars's eccentricity: e = 0.093 (noticeably elliptical)
  This small deviation from circle was visible in Tycho's data —
  only because Tycho's data was accurate to 2 arcminutes.

LAW 2 (1609): A line from the Sun to a planet sweeps equal areas
  in equal times. (Conservation of angular momentum — Newton's later explanation)

  When closer to Sun (perihelion): planet moves faster.
  When farther from Sun (aphelion): planet moves slower.
  The area swept per unit time is constant.

LAW 3 (1619): T² ∝ a³
  The square of the orbital period T is proportional to
  the cube of the semi-major axis a of the ellipse.

  Mathematically: T²/a³ = constant for all planets orbiting the same star.

  Earth: T = 1 year, a = 1 AU → T²/a³ = 1
  Mars:  T = 1.88 years, a = 1.52 AU → (1.88)²/(1.52)³ = 3.53/3.51 ≈ 1 ✓

Newton later derived all three laws from his law of gravitation.
The Kepler laws are what you get from F = GMm/r² for orbits.
```

**Why Kepler's Work Was a Breakthrough**

Kepler's Third Law, in particular, revealed that **a mathematical relationship
governs all the planets simultaneously** — a hint that a single force law
might explain everything. This is what Newton built on.

The shift from "describe the observations" to "find the mathematical law
governing all observations" is the key step into modern science.

## Galileo Galilei (1564–1642)

### Who He Was

Italian physicist and astronomer. Developed the telescope (improved a Dutch
design) and turned it on the sky in 1609. Discovered Jupiter's moons,
Saturn's rings (saw them as "ears"), sunspots, and mountains on the Moon.
Put on trial by the Inquisition in 1633 for supporting Copernicanism;
sentenced to house arrest for the rest of his life.

### The Contribution: Dynamics, Telescope, and Scientific Method

**Mechanics — The Galilean Foundation**

```
GALILEO'S MECHANICS
====================

Aristotle: Objects fall at speed proportional to their weight.
  Heavier falls faster. "Natural motion" toward center of Earth.
  Forced motion requires a force to maintain it.

Galileo (experiments at Pisa, ~1590–1604): WRONG.
  Objects fall at the SAME rate regardless of weight (ignoring air).
  Famous (possibly apocryphal): dropped two cannon balls from Leaning Tower.

What Galileo discovered:
  1. Free fall: d = ½gt²  (distance proportional to time squared)
  2. Independence of horizontal and vertical motion
     (projectile motion = parabola)
  3. Principle of Inertia: A moving object continues moving unless
     a force acts on it. (Newton's First Law is Galileo's insight.)

GALILEAN RELATIVITY:
  The laws of mechanics are the same in all UNIFORMLY MOVING frames.
  There is no mechanical experiment that can tell if you're moving
  at constant velocity or standing still.

  Example: Drop a ball inside a ship moving at constant speed.
    It falls straight down (relative to the ship).
    An observer on shore sees it follow a parabola.
    Both descriptions are correct — they're in different frames.

  EINSTEIN later extended this: the laws of ALL physics (not just mechanics)
  are the same in all inertial frames. Special relativity.
```

**Telescope and Observations**

```
GALILEO'S TELESCOPE DISCOVERIES (1609–1610)
=============================================

Jupiter's moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto — now "Galilean moons"):
  These moons orbit Jupiter, not Earth. PROOF that Earth is not the center
  of everything. Something in the solar system that doesn't orbit Earth.

Phases of Venus:
  Venus shows full phases (full, crescent, gibbous).
  In Ptolemy's model (Venus always between Earth and Sun), Venus could
  only show crescent phases. The FULL phases are impossible in geocentrism.
  Direct observational proof of heliocentrism.

Sunspots:
  The Sun is not perfect. Aristotle said celestial objects are perfect.
  Sunspots disprove it.

Mountains on the Moon:
  The Moon is not a smooth perfect sphere. It's a world with terrain.

Saturn's "ears" (rings):
  He couldn't resolve them — later astronomers (Huygens, 1655) saw rings.
```

**The Scientific Method**

Galileo's deepest contribution may be methodological:
1. **Quantitative measurement** over qualitative description
2. **Experiment** designed to test specific hypotheses
3. **Mathematical formulation** of natural laws
4. **Rejection of authority** (Aristotle, Scripture) in favor of evidence

The phrase "the book of nature is written in the language of mathematics"
is Galileo's. It is the founding statement of mathematical physics.

## Common Confusion Points

**"Copernicus was the first to propose heliocentrism"** — Aristarchus of Samos
proposed a heliocentric model around 270 BCE. Copernicus knew about Aristarchus
(cited him in an early draft, then removed the citation). Copernicus is important
for providing the mathematical model that could be tested, and for the timing
(in a Renaissance context where it could spread and be developed).

**"Galileo dropped balls from the Leaning Tower of Pisa"** — Possibly apocryphal.
The experiment is described only by Galileo's student Viviani. Galileo's actual
experimental method was inclined planes (rolled balls to slow down motion) and
pendulums. The equivalence principle (all objects fall at the same rate) was what
mattered, not the specific experiment.

**"The Church rejected Copernicus immediately"** — Copernicus's book was published
in 1543. The Church didn't put it on the Index until 1616 — 73 years later, in
reaction to Galileo's aggressive public campaign. Copernicus himself was a
church official. The opposition was political and social, not purely theological.

**"Kepler's laws are approximate"** — They are exact for a two-body problem (one
planet, one star). For real solar systems with multiple planets, the Kepler laws
are approximate: each planet perturbs the others slightly. These perturbations
were calculated by Laplace, Lagrange, and Gauss (File 03). GR also adds tiny
corrections (Mercury's perihelion precession, explained by Einstein in 1915).

**"Galileo proved Earth moves"** — He proved it is NOT the center of everything
(phases of Venus, Jupiter's moons). The actual proof that Earth moves (stellar
parallax) came in 1838 (Friedrich Bessel), after telescopes became precise enough
to measure the tiny apparent shifts of nearby stars.
