# Nuclear and Particle Physics — Rutherford, Chadwick, Fermi, Lawrence, Gell-Mann, Weinberg

## The Journey Into the Nucleus

```
PROBING MATTER'S STRUCTURE
============================

1897: Thomson — discovers the electron. Plum-pudding model of atom.

1909–1911: Rutherford-Geiger-Marsden — alpha scattering.
  Most alphas pass through gold foil. A FEW bounce almost backward.
  Conclusion: atoms have a tiny, massive, positively charged NUCLEUS.
  Rutherford: "It was almost as incredible as if you fired 15-inch shells
  at tissue paper and they came back and hit you."

1919: Rutherford — first artificial nuclear reaction.
  α + ¹⁴N → ¹⁷O + p (proton).
  Identifies protons.

1932: Chadwick — discovers the neutron.
  Completes the nuclear model: nucleus = protons + neutrons.

1932: Anderson — discovers the positron (Dirac's antimatter).

1934: Fermi — beta decay theory. Invents the weak force.

1938–1945: Nuclear fission. The bomb. The reactor.

1947: Pions discovered in cosmic rays. New particles.

1950s–1960s: Particle accelerators. Hundreds of new "particles."
  The "particle zoo."

1961–1974: Gell-Mann + Zweig — quarks organize the zoo.
  Weinberg + Salam + Glashow — electroweak unification.
  QCD (quantum chromodynamics).

1974: "November Revolution" — charm quark discovered.
  Confirms quark model. Standard Model established.

1983: W and Z bosons discovered (UA1, UA2 experiments at CERN).
2012: Higgs boson discovered (CMS, ATLAS at LHC).
```

## Ernest Rutherford (1871–1937)

### Who He Was

New Zealand-British physicist. Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1908) — awarded for
chemistry, not physics, because the committee didn't know what to do with
radioactivity. He later quipped: "All science is either physics or stamp collecting."

Trained the most productive group of experimental physicists in history:
Chadwick, Geiger, Cockcroft, Walton, Kapitza among his students and collaborators.

### The Contribution: The Nuclear Atom

**The Rutherford Scattering Experiment (1909–1911)**

```
THE GOLD FOIL EXPERIMENT
========================

Setup: Alpha particles (from radium) directed at thin gold foil.
  Scintillation screens around the foil detect where alphas go.

EXPECTED (Thomson plum-pudding model):
  Positive charge spread throughout the atom.
  Alphas would be barely deflected.

OBSERVED:
  Most alphas: pass straight through (foil is mostly empty space).
  Some alphas: deflected at large angles.
  A few: bounce almost backward.

RUTHERFORD'S ANALYSIS:
  Back-scattering requires a very strong force at close range.
  The positive charge must be concentrated in a tiny nucleus.

  Rutherford scattering formula (quantum mechanics version: Mott formula):
    dσ/dΩ = (Z₁Z₂e²/4E_cm)² · 1/sin⁴(θ/2)

  For gold: nucleus radius ~ 10⁻¹⁴ m (nucleus/atom ≈ 10⁻⁵ in linear scale)

  The atom is ~99.999...% empty space.
  The dense nucleus makes up ~99.97% of the atom's mass.
```

**Transmutation and Identifying Protons (1919)**

Rutherford bombarded nitrogen with alpha particles:
α + ¹⁴N → ¹⁷O + p

He observed fast particles with the same charge/mass ratio as hydrogen nuclei.
He named them PROTONS (from Greek "first").

This was the first deliberate nuclear reaction — "splitting the atom" in a
meaningful sense (though not fission).

## James Chadwick (1891–1974)

### Who He Was

British physicist, student of Rutherford. Nobel Prize 1935.
Led the British contribution to the Manhattan Project.

### The Contribution: The Neutron

**Discovering the Neutron (1932)**

```
THE NEUTRON DISCOVERY
=====================

PROBLEM: Atomic masses are roughly integers × proton mass.
  But atomic numbers (charge = proton count) are about half the mass.
  Nitrogen: mass ≈ 14, charge = 7. Where does the other mass come from?

  Wrong answer (1920s): bound proton-electron pairs inside nucleus.
  This violated the uncertainty principle: electron in nucleus would need
  ΔxΔp > ℏ/2 → kinetic energy >> observed beta decay energies.

Walther Bothe and Herbert Becker (1930):
  Beryllium bombarded by alphas produced a penetrating neutral radiation.
  Irène Joliot-Curie and Frédéric Joliot (1932): this radiation knocked
  protons from paraffin. Interpreted as gamma rays.
  But: the momentum transfer was too large for gamma rays.

CHADWICK (1932):
  If the neutral radiation consisted of massive neutral particles
  with approximately the same mass as the proton, the kinematics
  work out exactly.

  Neutron mass ≈ 1.675 × 10⁻²⁷ kg ≈ proton mass (slightly heavier)
  Electric charge: 0

  From conservation of energy and momentum in:
    ¹⁹Be + α → ¹²C + n

  Chadwick measured the mass, proving a new particle.
```

**The Neutron's Importance**

The neutron completed the nuclear model: nucleus = Z protons + N neutrons.
But beyond that:
- Neutrons have no electric charge → can approach nuclei without Coulomb repulsion
- Thermal neutrons (slow neutrons) are effective at inducing fission
- This is why nuclear reactors use neutron moderators (water, graphite)
  to slow neutrons down to thermal energies

## Enrico Fermi (1901–1954)

### Who He Was

Italian-American physicist. Nobel Prize 1938. Fled Italy (Mussolini's anti-Jewish
laws threatened his Jewish wife Laura) immediately after the Nobel ceremony in
Stockholm. Went to Columbia, then Chicago. Led CP-1 (first nuclear reactor, 1942)
under the University of Chicago football stands.

The element fermium (Fm) and the unit of length fermi (fm = 10⁻¹⁵ m) are named for him.

### The Contribution: Weak Force, Beta Decay, and the First Reactor

**The Theory of Beta Decay (1934)**

```
BETA DECAY — FERMI'S THEORY
============================

Problem: An unstable nucleus emits an electron (beta particle).
  But: nucleus = protons + neutrons. Where does the electron come from?
  Heisenberg's idea: neutrons are proton-electron bound states → wrong (UP violation)

Pauli (1930): Something else must be emitted with the electron.
  To conserve energy, momentum, and angular momentum.
  He called it the neutron (later renamed neutrino by Fermi).

FERMI (1934): Full theory.
  n → p + e⁻ + ν̄_e  (neutron → proton + electron + anti-neutrino)

  This is mediated by a NEW FORCE: the WEAK NUCLEAR FORCE.
  Fermi wrote the interaction Hamiltonian:
    H_weak ∝ G_F (ψ̄_p γᵘ ψ_n)(ψ̄_e γ_μ ψ_ν)

  G_F = Fermi constant = 1.166 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻² (coupling strength of weak force)

  Fermi's theory was wrong at high energy (non-renormalizable)
  but was the correct low-energy effective theory.
  It was later replaced by the electroweak theory (W and Z bosons).
```

**Chicago Pile-1 (1942)**

```
THE FIRST NUCLEAR REACTOR — CP-1 (DECEMBER 2, 1942)
======================================================

PHYSICS OF FISSION:
  ²³⁵U + n → fission products + 2–3 neutrons + ~200 MeV energy

  Each fission releases ~2.5 neutrons.
  If each of those causes another fission: chain reaction.
  Control: absorb excess neutrons (cadmium, boron rods).

CP-1 DESIGN:
  238 tons of uranium and uranium oxide
  Graphite moderator (slows neutrons to thermal energies)
  Cadmium control rods

  On December 2, 1942: control rods pulled out.
  Self-sustaining chain reaction achieved. 0.5 watts.
  Code sent to Washington: "The Italian navigator has just landed in the
  new world."

SIGNIFICANCE:
  First controlled nuclear chain reaction in history.
  The physics of reactors — same basic design used in all nuclear plants.
  The bomb followed from understanding the uncontrolled chain reaction.
```

## Ernest Orlando Lawrence (1901–1958)

### Who He Was

American physicist, University of California Berkeley. Nobel Prize 1939.
Invented the cyclotron (1930). Built progressively larger accelerators.
The element lawrencium (Lr) and Lawrence Livermore and Lawrence Berkeley
National Laboratories are named for him.

### The Contribution: The Cyclotron and Particle Acceleration

**The Cyclotron (1930)**

```
THE CYCLOTRON
=============

PRINCIPLE:
  A charged particle in a magnetic field moves in a circle.
  Period of circular motion: T = 2πm/(qB) — independent of speed!
  (Cyclotron resonance — works because slower particles have smaller radius)

  Use this: two D-shaped electrodes ("dees") with alternating voltage.
  Frequency = 1/T = qB/(2πm) (cyclotron frequency).
  Particle accelerated each time it crosses the gap between dees.
  Radius grows with each kick → spiral outward.

Lawrence's 1930 cyclotron: 4 inches diameter, energies up to ~80 keV.
By 1939: 60-inch cyclotron, protons to 20 MeV.
Cosmotron (1952): 3 GeV. Bevatron (1954): 6.2 GeV.

LIMITATION: At relativistic speeds, T is no longer constant.
  The particle gets out of phase with the alternating voltage.
  Solution: Synchrotron (vary the frequency to match relativistic mass increase).
  → Modern accelerators (LHC) are synchrotrons.
```

## Murray Gell-Mann (1929–2019)

### Who He Was

American physicist, Caltech. Nobel Prize 1969. Extraordinarily broad intellectual
interests (linguistics, natural history, complexity). Named quarks. Coined the
"eightfold way." Co-developed QCD.

### The Contribution: Quarks and the Eightfold Way

**The Particle Zoo and the Eightfold Way**

```
THE PARTICLE ZOO (1950s–1960s)
================================

Accelerators discovered hundreds of "particles."
  Pions, kaons, lambdas, sigmas, xis, omegas, rhos, deltas...
  They seemed to form patterns but nobody understood why.

GELL-MANN (1961) and NISHIJIMA independently:
  The EIGHTFOLD WAY — classify particles by SU(3) symmetry.

  Mesons (quark + antiquark): fit into octets of SU(3).
  Baryons (3 quarks): also fit into octets and decuplets.

PREDICTION:
  The baryon decuplet had 9 known particles and a gap.
  Gell-Mann predicted: the Omega minus (Ω⁻) should exist with specific mass.
  Discovered at Brookhaven 1964. Mass = predicted. Triumph.

QUARKS (Gell-Mann + Zweig, 1964):
  The SU(3) symmetry is explained if hadrons are made of QUARKS.
  Three types (flavors): up (u), down (d), strange (s)
  Charges: u = +2/3, d = -1/3, s = -1/3

  Proton = uud (charge = 2/3 + 2/3 - 1/3 = 1)
  Neutron = udd (charge = 2/3 - 1/3 - 1/3 = 0)
  Pion π⁺ = uᾱd (quark + antiquark = meson)

  Quarks are confined — they cannot be isolated (confinement).
  "Quark" from Finnegans Wake: "Three quarks for Muster Mark!"
```

**QCD — Quantum Chromodynamics**

```
QCD: THE THEORY OF THE STRONG FORCE
=====================================

Yang-Mills with gauge group SU(3):
  Three color charges: red, green, blue
  Quarks carry color. Gluons carry and mediate color.

  8 gluons (SU(3) has 8 generators).
  Gluons interact with each other (self-coupling — non-abelian).

KEY PROPERTIES:
  Asymptotic freedom (Gross, Politzer, Wilczek — Nobel 2004):
    At high energies (short distances), quarks interact weakly.
    This is why deep inelastic scattering works at high energy.

  Confinement:
    At low energies (large distances), the strong force INCREASES.
    Like a spring: the further you pull quarks apart, the stronger the force.
    Eventually enough energy to create a quark-antiquark pair from the vacuum.
    You can never isolate a single quark.

This explains why quarks weren't found as free particles in cyclotron experiments:
  The energy to separate them creates new quarks instead.
```

## Steven Weinberg (1933–2021)

### Who He Was

American physicist, University of Texas Austin. Nobel Prize 1979 (with Salam and
Glashow). Authored *The First Three Minutes* (1977), the best popular account
of Big Bang nucleosynthesis. *Gravitation and Cosmology* (1972) and *Quantum
Theory of Fields* (3 volumes) are definitive textbooks.

### The Contribution: Electroweak Unification

**Glashow-Weinberg-Salam Electroweak Theory**

```
ELECTROWEAK UNIFICATION (1967–1968)
=====================================

Three forces mediate particle interactions:
  Electromagnetism: infinite range, carried by massless photon
  Weak force: very short range (~10⁻¹⁸ m), carried by W±, Z⁰ bosons

  These look very different. But at high energies (E >> 80 GeV):
  They are the SAME force — the electroweak force.

Weinberg + Salam (1967):
  Gauge group: SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y
  Fields: W¹,W²,W³ (weak isospin) + B (hypercharge)

  Higgs mechanism spontaneously breaks SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y → U(1)_EM
  Gives mass to W and Z; photon stays massless.

  Physical bosons:
    W± = (W¹ ∓ iW²)/√2  (charged weak bosons)
    Z⁰ = W³cos θ_W - B sin θ_W  (neutral weak boson)
    γ  = W³sin θ_W + B cos θ_W  (photon)

  θ_W = Weinberg angle (≈ 28.7°) — a free parameter of the model.

PREDICTIONS:
  W mass: M_W = 80.4 GeV/c²
  Z mass: M_Z = 91.2 GeV/c²
  Neutral currents (new type of weak interaction): Z⁰ exchange.
  Discovered at CERN in 1973 (Gargamelle bubble chamber).
  W and Z discovered in 1983 (UA1 and UA2 at CERN). Carlo Rubbia.
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Nuclear fission and nuclear fusion are the same thing"** — Fission: a heavy
nucleus (U-235, Pu-239) splits into smaller pieces, releasing energy (used in bombs
and reactors). Fusion: light nuclei (hydrogen isotopes) combine to form heavier
ones, releasing energy (used in H-bombs, hoped for in tokamaks). The physics is
related (binding energy per nucleon curve) but the processes are opposite.

**"Quarks were discovered in accelerator experiments"** — Free quarks were NOT
discovered. Deep inelastic scattering at SLAC (1968–1969) showed protons have
internal structure (point-like scatterers = "partons" = quarks). But the quarks
could not be isolated — confinement prevents it. The quark model explains
the structure, but direct isolation is impossible.

**"The Standard Model predicts everything about particle physics"** — It doesn't
include gravity. It has ~19 free parameters that must be measured (quark masses,
mixing angles, coupling constants). It doesn't explain: dark matter (no candidate
particle), dark energy, the matter-antimatter asymmetry, neutrino masses (though
extended with neutrino mixing to accommodate them), or why there are three
generations of quarks and leptons.

**"Weinberg's theory was immediately accepted"** — It wasn't. The paper was cited
once in 1967–1971. When 't Hooft proved in 1971 that Yang-Mills theories are
renormalizable (fixing the infinity problem), people went back and looked at
Weinberg and Salam's work. The "neutral current" discovery in 1973 confirmed it.
Good theory: 6 years of being ignored, then everything at once.
