## The Three Revolutions That Matter Most

Physics has had three conceptual revolutions that required abandoning classical
intuition entirely:

```
THE THREE REVOLUTIONS
======================

1. NEWTONIAN REVOLUTION (1687):
   Before: Aristotelian — different laws for heavens and Earth.
   After: Universal laws — same gravity governs apple and Moon.
   Cost: Accepting action-at-a-distance (unnerving).

2. RELATIVISTIC REVOLUTION (1905–1915):
   Before: Absolute time and space; geometry is fixed.
   After: Space and time are relative; spacetime is curved.
   Cost: Abandoning simultaneity, Euclidean geometry.
   Requires: Speed of light is constant for ALL observers.

3. QUANTUM REVOLUTION (1900–1935):
   Before: Deterministic evolution; properties have definite values.
   After: Probabilities are fundamental; measurement disturbs the system.
   Cost: Abandoning determinism at small scales, wave-particle duality.
   Requires: Action is quantized; Heisenberg uncertainty holds.

These are NOT approximations. Classical mechanics is an approximation
valid when v << c (slow speeds) and when objects are macroscopic.
Quantum mechanics and general relativity are both exactly right in
their domains; unifying them is the open problem.
```

## The Shape of Physical Laws

A deep theme: physical laws have become progressively more mathematical,
more symmetry-based, and more abstract — while remaining predictive.

```
MATHEMATICAL STRUCTURE OF PHYSICS
===================================

Epoch          Mathematical Language         Content
---------      ----------------------        -------
Newton         Differential equations        F = ma, gravity
Lagrange       Variational calculus          Action principle
Hamilton       Symplectic geometry           Phase space
Maxwell        Partial differential eq.      Field equations
Einstein (SR)  Pseudo-Riemannian geometry    Spacetime metric
Einstein (GR)  Differential geometry         Einstein equations
Schrödinger    Complex Hilbert spaces        Wave equation
Heisenberg     Matrix algebra               Observable operators
Dirac          Clifford algebra, spinors     Relativistic QM
Feynman        Path integrals               Quantum field theory
Yang-Mills     Principal fiber bundles       Gauge theory
Standard Model SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)            Gauge Lagrangian
String theory  Calabi-Yau manifolds,        (Not yet tested)
               conformal field theory
```

For an MIT Math/TCS background: what looks like physics is mostly
deep mathematics made concrete by experiment. Every entry in that table
has a mathematical home you know or will know.
