## Edwin Hubble (1889–1953)

### Who He Was

American astronomer, Mount Wilson Observatory. Had earned a law degree but
chose astronomy. Used the 100-inch Hooker telescope (world's largest at the time)
to resolve individual stars in the Andromeda nebula.

### The Contribution: Galaxies and the Expanding Universe

**Galaxies as "Island Universes" (1923–1924)**

```
THE GREAT DEBATE (1920)
========================

Were the "spiral nebulae" inside the Milky Way or external?
  Shapley: Inside — estimated Milky Way is huge, spirals are small.
  Curtis: Outside — "island universes" like the Milky Way.

HUBBLE'S RESOLUTION (1923–1924):
  Using Cepheid variable stars as "standard candles."

  CEPHEID PERIOD-LUMINOSITY RELATION (Henrietta Leavitt, 1912):
    Cepheid variables pulsate with period proportional to intrinsic brightness.
    Measure the period → know the luminosity.
    Measure apparent brightness → compute distance.
    (Standard candle: know intrinsic brightness, measure apparent → get distance.)

  Hubble found Cepheids in the Andromeda nebula (M31):
    Distance ~ 900,000 light years (modern: 2.5 million ly)
    Far beyond the Milky Way (~100,000 ly across).

  The Andromeda nebula is a GALAXY — a system of ~1 trillion stars like our own.
  The universe contains billions of galaxies.
```

**Hubble's Law (1929)**

```
HUBBLE'S LAW
=============

v = H₀ · d

  v = recession velocity of galaxy (from redshift: z = Δλ/λ = v/c for v << c)
  d = distance to galaxy
  H₀ = Hubble constant ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc (current best value: 67–73 km/s/Mpc)
       (1 Mpc = megaparsec = 3.09 × 10²² m ≈ 3.26 million light years)

INTERPRETATION:
  The universe is EXPANDING. Every galaxy is receding from every other.
  (Not because galaxies are moving through space, but because SPACE ITSELF expands.)

  Analogy: dots on a balloon being inflated.
    No dot is at the center. Every dot sees every other dot moving away.

  The Hubble "constant" is not constant in time:
    H(t) = ȧ/a  where a(t) is the scale factor of the universe.
    At the Big Bang: a → 0.
    Today: a = 1.

HUBBLE TENSION:
  H₀ from CMB (Planck satellite): 67.4 ± 0.5 km/s/Mpc
  H₀ from Cepheids/supernovae (local): 73.0 ± 1.0 km/s/Mpc
  4.5σ discrepancy. Systematic error or new physics? Active debate.
```

## Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar (1910–1995)

### Who He Was

Indian-American astrophysicist. Nobel Prize 1983 (shared with William Fowler).
Calculated the Chandrasekhar limit at age 19 on the boat from India to Cambridge
to study under Eddington. Eddington dismissed the result as "nonsense" at a
Royal Astronomical Society meeting in 1935. Chandrasekhar was vindicated 40 years later.

### The Contribution: The Chandrasekhar Limit

**White Dwarfs and Quantum Degeneracy Pressure**

```
STELLAR EVOLUTION ENDPOINT
============================

A star is a balance between:
  GRAVITY (pulling in) ←→ PRESSURE (pushing out)

When nuclear fuel is exhausted:
  Gravity wins. The star collapses.
  What stops the collapse?

For a Sun-like star → WHITE DWARF:
  ELECTRON DEGENERACY PRESSURE:
  Pauli exclusion: electrons resist compression because they can't
  all be in the ground state. The pressure comes from kinetic energy,
  not temperature. It doesn't fade as the star cools.

  But: there is a LIMIT.

CHANDRASEKHAR'S CALCULATION (1930):
  At relativistic speeds, the degeneracy pressure ∝ n^(4/3) (vs. n^(5/3) non-relativistic)
  Gravity ∝ n^(4/3) also.
  They balance at only ONE mass:

  M_Ch = 5.87 (ℏc/G)^(3/2) · m_H⁻² ≈ 1.4 M_☉

  If M > 1.4 M_☉: electron degeneracy pressure cannot stop collapse.
  The white dwarf will collapse further.
```

**What Happens When Mass Exceeds the Limit**

```
STELLAR COLLAPSE ENDPOINTS
============================

M < 1.4 M_☉:  WHITE DWARF — supported by electron degeneracy.
               Dense as iron, size of Earth.

1.4 < M < ~3 M_☉:  NEUTRON STAR — electrons + protons → neutrons.
                    Supported by NEUTRON degeneracy pressure.
                    Density ~ 10¹⁴ g/cm³ (nuclear density).
                    Diameter ~ 20 km.
                    Pulsars (rotating neutron stars), magnetars.

M > ~3 M_☉:  BLACK HOLE — nothing can stop the collapse.
              Event horizon forms. Singularity inside.

This classification of stellar remnants is Chandrasekhar's framework.
The Chandrasekhar limit is a direct consequence of special relativity
applied to degenerate matter.

Type Ia supernovae: White dwarfs accreting matter up to ~1.4 M_☉
  explode in a characteristic way. Used as STANDARD CANDLES for
  measuring cosmological distances.
  Perlmutter/Schmidt/Riess used these to discover dark energy (1998).
```

## Marie Skłodowska Curie (1867–1934)

### Who She Was

Polish-French physicist and chemist. The only person to win Nobel Prizes in
TWO different sciences (Physics 1903, Chemistry 1911). The first woman to win
a Nobel Prize. First woman to earn a PhD in physics in France.

Discovered polonium and radium. Coined the term "radioactivity." Developed mobile
X-ray units ("petites Curies") for WWI battlefield diagnosis.

Died of aplastic anemia from radiation exposure — this was not known to be
dangerous at the time.

### The Contribution: Radioactivity

**Discovering Radioactivity**

```
BECQUEREL AND CURIE ON RADIOACTIVITY
======================================

Becquerel (1896): Uranium spontaneously emits radiation that fogs photographic plates.
  Rays not caused by fluorescence (persist in darkness).
  Named the phenomenon — but didn't pursue it deeply.

Marie Curie (1897–1902):
  Systematic measurement using piezoelectric electrometer (invented by Pierre Curie).
  Key insight: radioactivity is an ATOMIC property — proportional to amount
    of element, independent of temperature, pressure, or chemical state.
  This meant: radioactivity is coming from INSIDE the atom.
  (Before quantum mechanics — a profound inference.)

  Discovered that thorium (in addition to uranium) is radioactive.
  In pitchblende (uranium ore): radioactivity STRONGER than expected from uranium alone.
  → There must be another highly radioactive element in the ore.

Isolation campaign (1898–1902):
  Processed tons of pitchblende.
  Isolated TWO new elements:
    Polonium (named for Poland) — 400× more radioactive than uranium
    Radium (from Latin "radius" = ray) — 1 million× more radioactive than uranium

  Radium: first isolated in metallic form 1910. 1 gram of Ra from 10 tons of pitchblende.
```

**The Three Types of Radiation**

```
RUTHERFORD AND CURIE'S CLASSIFICATION
=======================================

α (alpha) radiation: Helium-4 nuclei (2p + 2n). Charged, heavy.
  Range: ~5 cm in air. Stopped by paper.
  Dangerous if INGESTED (high ionizing power).

β (beta) radiation: Electrons (β⁻) or positrons (β⁺).
  Range: ~1 m in air. Stopped by aluminum.
  Moderate ionizing power.

γ (gamma) radiation: High-energy photons.
  Range: Significant penetration. Stopped by lead/dense material.
  Lower ionizing power per photon but highly penetrating.

NUCLEAR REACTIONS:
  α decay: ²³⁸U → ²³⁴Th + α (A decreases by 4, Z by 2)
  β⁻ decay: ¹⁴C → ¹⁴N + e⁻ + ν̄_e (Z increases by 1)
  γ decay: excited nucleus → ground state + photon (A, Z unchanged)
```

**The Energy Source of Stars**

Marie Curie's discovery that radioactivity produces heat (from radium decays) was
one of the first hints that nuclear processes release energy. This eventually
resolved Kelvin's wrong age-of-Earth calculation — radioactive decay provides
a heat source inside the Earth. And nuclear fusion (related physics) powers stars.

## Stephen Hawking (1942–2018)

### Who He Was

British theoretical physicist, Cambridge. Diagnosed with ALS at 21, given 2 years
to live. Lived 55 more years, eventually completely paralyzed, communicating through
a voice synthesizer. Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge (Newton's chair).
Most famous scientist of his era.

### The Contribution: Hawking Radiation and Cosmological Singularities

**Singularity Theorems (with Roger Penrose)**

```
PENROSE-HAWKING SINGULARITY THEOREMS
=======================================

PENROSE (1965): If a "trapped surface" (closed surface where all light rays
  converge inward) exists, there must be a singularity in the future.
  This proved: once the Schwarzschild radius is crossed, collapse to
  singularity is inevitable (not just a feature of symmetry).

HAWKING + PENROSE (1970): Extended to cosmology.
  IF the universe is expanding and matter satisfies energy conditions,
  THEN there was a singularity in the past: the Big Bang.
  GR inevitably predicts its own breakdown at the beginning.

CONSEQUENCE:
  GR is incomplete — it predicts singularities where it breaks down.
  A quantum theory of gravity is needed to describe the Big Bang
  and the interior of black holes.
```

**Hawking Radiation (1974)**

```
HAWKING RADIATION
==================

PROBLEM: Classical GR: black holes are black. Nothing escapes.
  But: Quantum mechanics + GR together?

HAWKING'S INSIGHT (1974):
  Quantum effects near the event horizon cause black holes to RADIATE.

MECHANISM (intuition — the exact argument uses QFT in curved spacetime):
  Near the event horizon, virtual particle-antiparticle pairs constantly
  form from vacuum fluctuations.
  Usually they annihilate immediately.
  Near the event horizon: one particle may fall in, one escape.
  The escaping particle = Hawking radiation.
  The infalling negative-energy particle reduces the black hole's mass.
  The black hole EVAPORATES.

HAWKING TEMPERATURE:
  T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B)

  For a solar-mass black hole: T_H ≈ 6 × 10⁻⁸ K (unmeasurably small)
  For a microscopic black hole (M ~ 10⁻⁵ kg): T_H ~ 10²⁸ K (extremely hot)

HAWKING EVAPORATION TIME:
  t_evap ∝ M³
  A solar-mass black hole: t_evap ~ 10⁶⁷ years (much longer than universe age)
  A microscopic primordial black hole (M ~ 10¹² kg):
  would evaporate now — might be observed (not yet confirmed)

THE BLACK HOLE INFORMATION PARADOX:
  If a black hole evaporates, what happens to the information
  about the matter that fell in?
  Hawking (1976): information is destroyed (violating QM unitarity).
  'tHooft, Susskind: information must be preserved (holographic principle).
  Hawking (2014): partially revised — information escapes in Hawking radiation.
  Still unsettled. One of the deepest open problems in theoretical physics.
```

## Roger Penrose (1931– )

### Who He Was

British mathematical physicist, Oxford. Nobel Prize in Physics 2020 (shared with
Reinhard Genzel and Andrea Ghez) "for the discovery that black hole formation
is a robust prediction of the theory of general relativity." Co-author of
singularity theorems with Hawking.

### The Contribution: Twistors, Penrose Tiles, and Cosmic Censorship

**Penrose Singularity Theorem (1965)**

Covered above: if a trapped surface exists (inside a black hole), a singularity
is unavoidable. This proved black holes are a robust prediction of GR.

**Cosmic Censorship Conjecture**

Penrose conjectured that naked singularities (singularities not hidden behind
event horizons) cannot form from realistic initial conditions.
"Nature abhors a naked singularity."
This is still unproven. Numerical relativity simulations have found potential
counterexamples in special situations.

**Penrose Tiles**

```
PENROSE TILING
==============

A tiling of the plane using two rhombus shapes (or kite/dart pairs)
that:
  - COVERS the plane without gaps or overlaps
  - NEVER REPEATS exactly (aperiodic)
  - Has 5-fold (pentagonal) symmetry

The existence of aperiodic tilings was proved by Penrose in 1974.
It was thought impossible before.

APPLICATION:
  Quasicrystals (Shechtman, 1982): metallic alloys with 5-fold symmetry
  in X-ray diffraction (impossible for periodic crystals).
  Their atomic structure is a 3D Penrose tiling.
  Nobel Chemistry Prize 2011 (Shechtman).
```

## Vera Rubin (1928–2016)

### Who She Was

American astronomer, Carnegie Institution of Washington. Systematically measured
galaxy rotation curves — how fast stars orbit the center of galaxies at various
distances. Her results provided the strongest observational evidence for dark matter.
Despite her work's importance, she was never awarded a Nobel Prize (she died in 2016).

### The Contribution: Dark Matter Evidence from Galaxy Rotation Curves

**The Rotation Curve Problem**

```
GALAXY ROTATION CURVES
========================

Expected (Newtonian gravity, visible mass only):
  For a galaxy with most mass in center:
    v(r) ∝ 1/√r  for r beyond the bulge  (like planets in solar system)
    Outer regions should orbit slower.

OBSERVED (Rubin + Ford, 1970s):
    v(r) = FLAT for large r
    Outer stars orbit at the SAME SPEED as inner stars.
    This continues as far as observations can reach.

  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │   v (rotation speed)                  │
  │   │         ___observed___________    │
  │   │        /                          │
  │   │       / expected                  │
  │   │      /          \                 │
  │   │     /             \               │
  │   └───────────────────────────── r    │
  └────────────────────────────────────────┘

TO PRODUCE v = constant:
  M(r) ∝ r (mass enclosed grows linearly with r)
  But: most of the visible matter (stars, gas) is in the inner disk.
  There must be INVISIBLE MASS spread in a halo around the galaxy.

  Dark matter halo: spherical, extends far beyond visible stars.
  Makes up ~85% of the galaxy's total mass.
```

**Dark Matter — What It Is (and Isn't)**

```
DARK MATTER PROPERTIES
=======================

What we KNOW from observations:
  - Interacts gravitationally (that's how we detect it)
  - Electrically neutral (doesn't emit, absorb, or scatter light)
  - Mass: ~5× more than ordinary matter in the universe
  - Concentrated around galaxies in halos
  - Required to explain: galaxy rotation curves (Rubin),
    galaxy cluster velocities (Zwicky 1933),
    gravitational lensing by clusters (Bullet Cluster 2006),
    Big Bang nucleosynthesis (baryonic matter can't be enough),
    large-scale structure formation.

What dark matter is NOT:
  - Not "dark stars" (brown dwarfs, neutron stars) — MACHO searches
    ruled out most of the required mass.
  - Not neutrinos (too hot — would wash out structure).
  - Not black holes in the right mass range (microlensing surveys limit).

What dark matter might BE (leading candidates):
  - WIMPs (Weakly Interacting Massive Particles): hypothetical particles
    interacting only via gravity and weak force. Heavy (1–1000 GeV).
    Not yet detected directly despite many experiments.
  - Axions: very light hypothetical particle (10⁻⁵–10⁻³ eV).
  - Sterile neutrinos: hypothetical heavier neutrinos.
  - Primordial black holes (some mass ranges not excluded).
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Hubble discovered the Big Bang"** — Hubble discovered the expanding universe
observationally. Lemaître derived theoretically from GR in 1927 that the universe
must be expanding and traced it back to a "primeval atom." Gamow and collaborators
worked out Big Bang nucleosynthesis in the 1940s. The CMB (cosmic microwave
background) was predicted by Alpher and Herman (1948) and discovered by Penzias
and Wilson (1965). The Big Bang theory was established incrementally.

**"Hawking radiation has been observed"** — It has not been observed from real
black holes (they're too cold). Analogue Hawking radiation (in condensed matter
systems that mimic event horizons) has been observed in the lab, but not from
astronomical black holes. The 2019 Event Horizon Telescope image showed the
shadow of M87*; no Hawking radiation was detected.

**"Dark matter is just a fudge factor"** — The evidence for dark matter comes
from at least five independent lines: rotation curves, cluster dynamics, gravitational
lensing, CMB power spectrum, and large-scale structure. These all point to
the same amount of dark matter. That consistency across independent methods
is strong evidence it's real. The Bullet Cluster (two galaxy clusters that
collided) shows the dark matter and ordinary matter separated — the dark matter
passed through while the gas slowed down. This is very hard to explain without
actual dark matter.

**"Chandrasekhar's limit was immediately accepted"** — Eddington publicly mocked
it at the 1935 RAS meeting. He thought it was absurd that a star could collapse
completely ("there should be a law of nature preventing it" — his actual quote).
Chandrasekhar was so discouraged he switched to other areas of astrophysics
(stellar structure, radiative transfer, etc.). He was vindicated decades later
when neutron stars and black holes were discovered. He received the Nobel Prize
in 1983, 48 years after his original calculation.
