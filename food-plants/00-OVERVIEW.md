# Food Plants — Overview: Plant Domestication as Technology

## The Big Picture

Plant domestication is the most consequential technological act in human history. More than fire, more than the wheel, more than writing — the ability to grow predictable, abundant food was the precondition for everything else.

```

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Near East          Wheat (emmer, einkorn), barley, lentil, pea,
(Fertile Crescent) chickpea, flax, rye | ~10,000-8,000 BP
                   ↓ Poaceae (grasses) + Fabaceae (legumes) dominant

China              Rice (O. sativa), soybean, millet (foxtail, broomcorn),
                   adzuki bean, peach, apricot | ~8,000-7,000 BP
                   ↓ Poaceae + Fabaceae + Rosaceae

Mesoamerica        Maize (corn), common bean, squash, chili pepper,
                   tomato, avocado, cacao, vanilla | ~7,000-3,500 BP
                   ↓ Poaceae + Fabaceae + Solanaceae + Cucurbitaceae

                   ↓ Solanaceae + Fabaceae + Poaceae

                   cucumber, sesame, jute | ~4,000-3,000 BP

  Palm oil (oil crop)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wild plants:            Domesticated crops:       Civilizational results:
Random distribution     Planted in fields         Surplus → storage
Variable yield          Predictable yield         Surplus → specialization
Mobile exploitation     Sedentary management      Specialization → cities

  species used)           feed 80% of humanity)   Narrow diet → vulnerability
```

The central irony: humanity traded dietary diversity and resilience for caloric abundance and civilizational complexity. 12 plant species now feed 80% of the world's population. Wild ancestors of each were managed across multiple continents in hundreds of local varieties. We traded resilience for scale.

**Vavilov's tragic postscript:** He identified these centers and built the world's first seed bank in Leningrad. When the city was besieged in WWII, his scientists died of starvation rather than eat the seeds. He died in a Soviet prison (1943), having refused to endorse Lysenko's pseudoscience.

## Domestication Theory

### What Domestication Actually Is

Domestication is evolutionary change under human selection pressure. It is not a single event — it is a process taking hundreds to thousands of years.

```
WILD ANCESTOR                    DOMESTICATED CROP
──────────────                   ──────────────────
Small seeds                  →   Large seeds (easier harvest)
Seed dormancy                →   No dormancy (germinates immediately)
Shattering rachis            →   Non-shattering (seeds stay on plant)
Distributed across season    →   Synchronized maturation
Bitter/toxic compounds       →   Reduced bitterness/toxicity
Perennial habit (some)       →   Annual habit (higher yield per season)
Self-incompatible (some)     →   Self-compatible (easier management)
Spreading/vining             →   Compact/upright
```

This cluster of traits = **domestication syndrome** (Daniel Zohary, Jack Harlan).

### Unconscious vs Deliberate Selection

```
UNCONSCIOUS SELECTION (most early domestication):
  Farmers harvest → replant best seeds → unconscious genetic shift
  No intentional breeding, just differential survival of traits
  humans found useful. Over 1,000+ years → substantial genetic change.

DELIBERATE SELECTION (post-understanding):
  Cross-pollination, hybridization, roguing (removing inferior plants)
  Documented in Roman agricultural writing (Columella, Varro)
  Systematic from 18th-19th c. with Mendelian genetics framework

SCIENTIFIC BREEDING (modern):
  F1 hybrid vigor, mutation breeding, genetic engineering, CRISPR
  See 09-MODERN-BREEDING.md
```

### Co-evolution Theory

Plants didn't passively submit to domestication. Many formed mutualistic relationships — seed dispersal via human cultivation trades protection and spread for nutritional reward. Teosinte that became maize is now one of the most abundant plants on Earth. Both species "won."

## Genetic Diversity and the Bottleneck Problem

Every domestication event creates a **genetic bottleneck** — the small founder population carries only a fraction of the wild ancestor's gene pool.

```
WILD ANCESTOR                      DOMESTICATE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enormous genetic diversity     →   Narrow genetic base
Many defensive toxin genes     →   Reduced toxicity (useful to humans)
Many pest/disease               →   Reduced resistance (vulnerable)
  resistance alleles
Many stress-tolerance           →   Reduced tolerance
  alleles
Diverse photosensitivity        →   Optimized for specific latitude

The narrower the genetic base, the higher the vulnerability.
Irish potato famine: one clone (Lumper), no disease resistance.
Gros Michel banana: one clone, wiped out by Panama disease.
```

Modern plant breeders and CGIAR institutes maintain **germplasm banks** (seed banks) as genetic insurance. The Svalbard Global Seed Vault holds 1.3M seed samples as backup.

## Common Confusion Points

**Vavilov centers ≠ where crops grow today.** Origin centers had genetic diversity; cultivation spread far beyond. Wheat originated in Turkey; Australia and the US are major producers. Coffee originated in Ethiopia; Brazil produces 40% of global supply.

**Domestication was slow and multi-step.** "The invention of agriculture" is a textbook convenience. It was a gradual process across many generations with no single inventor. Multiple independent domestication events occurred on different continents for the same crops (e.g., rice, beans).

**F1 hybrid seeds ≠ GMO.** F1 hybrids are conventional crosses between inbred lines — no genetic engineering. The "farmer can't save seeds" issue applies to both F1 hybrids and GM crops, but for different reasons (F1 = genetic degradation in F2 generation; GM = IP/legal constraints).

**Organic ≠ non-GMO ≠ heirloom.** Three independent axes. Organic = production method. Non-GMO = no recombinant DNA. Heirloom = open-pollinated variety predating ~1951 (F1 hybrid era). A plant can be any combination.
