## Brightness Ranking

Modes can be ordered by brightness (more major-like notes = brighter):

```
BRIGHTEST                                              DARKEST
  Lydian > Ionian > Mixolydian > Dorian > Aeolian > Phrygian > Locrian
  (вҷҜ4)    (natural)  (вҷӯ7)       (вҷӯ3,вҷӯ7) (вҷӯ3,вҷӯ6,вҷӯ7) (вҷӯ2,вҷӯ3,вҷӯ6,вҷӯ7) (вҷӯ2,вҷӯ3,вҷӯ5,вҷӯ6,вҷӯ7)

Each step left = raise one note by a semitone
Each step right = lower one note by a semitone
```

This is a direct consequence of the ZвӮҒвӮӮ structure: each mode adds one more "flat" alteration from the major scale template.

## Pentatonic Scales

Remove two notes from the major scale to eliminate half-step tensions:

### Major Pentatonic

```
Remove scale degrees 4 and 7 (the half-step pair 3-4 and 7-8):
C major: C  D  E  F  G  A  B  C
                   вҶ‘           вҶ‘ remove these
C major pentatonic: C  D  E  G  A  (5 notes)
Pattern: W  W  m3  W  m3
Intervals from root: 1  2  3  5  6
```

### Minor Pentatonic

```
Remove scale degrees 2 and 6 from natural minor:
A natural minor: A  B  C  D  E  F  G  A
                    вҶ‘           вҶ‘ remove these
A minor pentatonic: A  C  D  E  G  (5 notes)
Pattern: m3  W  W  m3  W
Intervals from root: 1  вҷӯ3  4  5  вҷӯ7

Note: A minor pentatonic = C major pentatonic (same notes, different center)
```

### Blues Scale

Minor pentatonic + the "blue note" (вҷӯ5 / вҷҜ4 = tritone from root):

```
A blues scale: A  C  D  DвҷҜ/Eвҷӯ  E  G  A
                              вҶ‘ added blue note

The Eвҷӯ creates half-step tension against both D and E
The вҷӯ5 is "bent" in performance вҖ” not cleanly 6 semitones but approached as a slide
This note is NOT in any diatonic scale вҶ’ the "outside" quality of blues
```

## Symmetric Scales

Scales built from repeating interval patterns вҖ” inherently equal-tempered:

### Whole-Tone Scale

```
Pattern: W W W W W W (6 notes, all whole steps)
C whole-tone: C  D  E  FвҷҜ  GвҷҜ  AвҷҜ  C

Properties:
  вҖў Only 2 distinct whole-tone scales (transpositions 0 and 1 semitone)
  вҖў Every interval is ambiguous вҖ” no perfect fifth, no leading tone
  вҖў Every triad is augmented
  вҖў Cannot establish tonal center
  вҖў Used by Debussy for dreamy, unanchored quality
  вҖў Dominant whole-tone scale = Lydian Dominant (1 2 3 вҷҜ4 5 6 вҷӯ7) often called "Lydian вҷӯ7"
```

### Octatonic (Diminished) Scale

```
Two versions alternating H and W:
Diminished (H-W): H W H W H W H W (8 notes)
Dominant diminished (W-H): W H W H W H W H

C diminished (H-W): C  Dвҷӯ  Eвҷӯ  E  FвҷҜ  G  A  Bвҷӯ  C
C dominant dim (W-H): C  D  Eвҷӯ  F  FвҷҜ  Aвҷӯ  A  B  C

Properties:
  вҖў Only 3 distinct octatonic scales
  вҖў Symmetry under transposition by minor thirds (3 semitones)
  вҖў Every diminished 7th chord lives in the same octatonic set
  вҖў Used for: jazz improvisation over dim7 / dom7вҷӯ9 chords,
    Stravinsky (Petrushka bitonal chord), BartГіk, late Romantic tension
  вҖў In jazz: play H-W over diminished chords, W-H over dominant chords
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"D Dorian" vs "transposing to D major":** D Dorian uses the same notes as C major but *centers on D*. Transposing to D major would give you D E FвҷҜ G A B CвҷҜ вҖ” completely different pitches. The mode is the context (what note is home), not a transposition of Ionian.

**The modes are not separate scales:** They're the same 7-note diatonic set heard through different ears. Every major scale contains all 7 modes simultaneously вҖ” which mode you're in depends entirely on which note functions as tonic.

**Pentatonic vs blues scale:** The blues scale is the minor pentatonic + one added chromatic note (the вҷӯ5 / blue note). It's often treated as a 6-note "blues hexatonic." The blue note is not a stable resting point вҖ” it's used as a passing or bent note.

**Whole-tone: two scales only:** Because the 6 whole steps divide the octave exactly into 6 equal parts, transposing a whole-tone scale by 1 semitone gives you the other one. Transposing by 2 semitones gives you the same scale. There are literally only 2 distinct whole-tone collections.
