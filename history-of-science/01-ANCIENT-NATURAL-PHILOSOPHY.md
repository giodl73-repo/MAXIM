# Ancient Natural Philosophy — From Babylon to Alexandria

## The Big Picture

The trajectory from Babylonian astronomical tables to Ptolemy's Almagest spans roughly a thousand years and represents the first systematic attempt to describe nature using mathematical and logical frameworks — without supernatural agency.

## Layer 1: Babylonian Astronomy

### What Made Babylonian Astronomy Distinct

Babylonian astronomy (600-200 BCE) was **arithmetical, not geometrical**. The Greeks asked "what is the geometric model of planetary motion?" The Babylonians asked "what is the numerical pattern that predicts planetary positions?" These are fundamentally different cognitive approaches.

```
BABYLONIAN APPROACH:          GREEK APPROACH (later):
Observe positions over        Build a geometric model
many centuries                of circular orbits and
         |                    epicycles
         v                         |
Find arithmetic patterns           v
for rising/setting dates      Derive predictions from
         |                    geometry
         v                         |
Generate tables of                 v
future positions              Qualitative + quantitative
(no physical model)           model of cosmos
```

### The Saros Cycle

The Saros cycle (6,585.3 days = 18 years 11 days) is the period after which lunar and solar eclipses repeat in nearly the same geometry. Babylonian astronomers discovered this empirically, through records going back to ~750 BCE.

**Why it works** (which Babylonians didn't know): The Saros period is the least common multiple of:
- Synodic month (lunar phase cycle): 29.53059 days
- Draconic month (Moon returns to same node): 27.21222 days
- Anomalistic month (Moon returns to perigee): 27.55455 days

235 synodic months ~= 251 draconic months ~= 239 anomalistic months ~= 6585.32 days

The Babylonians had no idea why this worked. They found it empirically and used it predictively — a purely algorithmic, model-free approach.

### Sexagesimal Mathematics

Babylonian base-60 positional notation was powerful:
- 60 has factors: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30, 60 — highly composite, simplifies fractions
- Our 60-minute hour, 60-second minute, 360-degree circle are Babylonian survivals
- They could represent numbers like 1/3 exactly (= 0;20 in base 60) which base-10 cannot

**Mathematical capability**: Babylonians could solve quadratic equations, knew Pythagorean triples (1500+ years before Pythagoras), computed square roots to 6+ significant figures.

## Layer 2: Milesian Naturalists — Removing the Gods

### The Significance of Naturalistic Explanation

The Milesian philosophers of Ionia (modern western Turkey) ~600 BCE made a move unprecedented in surviving records: explaining natural phenomena using natural causes, without invoking gods.

```
PRE-MILESIAN:               MILESIAN MOVE:
"Why does it thunder?"      "What is the arche (primary
"Zeus is angry"             substance) underlying all change?"

"Why do floods occur?"
"The gods will it"          Thales: Water (primordial substance,
                            all things arise from water)

                            Anaximander: The Apeiron (the
                            indefinite/boundless — a more
                            abstract principle)

                            Anaximenes: Air (condensation =
                            water and earth; rarefaction = fire)
```

This is not necessarily an advance in "correctness" — water, air, and the apeiron are wrong. The advance is **methodological**: natural explanations invoke natural mechanisms that can be questioned, tested, refined. Divine explanations cannot be wrong.

### Anaximander's Specific Contributions

Anaximander (~610-546 BCE) is arguably more important than Thales:

- **The apeiron** as arche: recognized that positing a specific substance (like Thales' water) raises the question "why that substance?" The arche must be indefinite to avoid this regress
- **Cosmological model**: Earth is a cylinder floating freely in space — not supported by water (contra Thales), held in place by its equidistance from all points (a symmetry argument — genuinely sophisticated)
- **Biological evolution precursor**: animals arose from sea creatures; humans descended from fish-like ancestors (because human infants are helpless, humans couldn't have started as humans)

The evolution idea is striking. The reasoning is wrong in detail but the logic is sound: humans as infants are helpless; therefore human life depended on prior non-helpless stages; therefore earliest humans were different.

## Layer 3: Atomism — Leucippus and Democritus

### The Atomic Theory

Leucippus (~480-420 BCE) and Democritus (~460-370 BCE) proposed:
- Matter consists of indivisible particles: **atoms** (atomon = uncuttable)
- Atoms move through the **void** (vacuum)
- Atoms differ in shape, size, and arrangement — all properties derive from these geometric differences
- Causation is purely mechanical: atoms collide, hook together, separate
- The cosmos is infinite; there are infinitely many worlds, some with life

```
DEMOCRITUS'S ONTOLOGY:

+--------+    +--------+    +--------+
| ATOMS  |    | ATOMS  |    | ATOMS  |    ...infinite variety
| (solid)|    | (hooks)|    | (round)|
+--------+    +--------+    +--------+
      \            |            /
       +---------------------------+
       |          VOID             |
       |    (infinite space)       |
       +---------------------------+

All phenomena = atoms moving through void.
Fire = fast, round atoms.
Soul = round atoms (material, no afterlife).
Perception = atom streams from objects entering sense organs.
```

### Why It Is Remarkably Modern

The Greek atomic theory anticipates:
- Avogadro/Dalton's atoms (though sizes wrong)
- Material monism: mind reduces to matter
- Determinism: "Everything existing in the universe is the fruit of chance and necessity"
- The void anticipates vacuum; "atoms + void" = matter + spacetime

### Why It Lost to Aristotle

Aristotle had decisive objections — and institutional weight:

| Aristotelian Argument | Why It Seemed Compelling |
|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Nature abhors a vacuum: two objects in contact can't be pulled apart without creating void, yet they can be pulled apart, therefore no void | Empirically, surfaces seem to resist separation |
| How do atoms explain qualities like hot/cold without invoking qualities? | Circularity: "round atoms explain fire" just names the association |
| No teleology: atoms can't explain purposive processes like growth, development | Biological development seems goal-directed |
| Infinite divisibility of continuum: mathematical continuum requires infinite divisibility | Mathematical argument seemed stronger |

Furthermore, Aristotle was **right about biology** (observation-based) while being wrong about physics. His general system — which included teleology, the four causes, hylomorphism — was internally coherent and was institutionally dominant through the Middle Ages via the Church's adoption of Aristotelian metaphysics.

## Layer 4: Plato — Forms and Mathematical Structure

### The Theory of Forms

Plato's metaphysics: sensible objects are imperfect copies of perfect, eternal, unchanging Forms (eidos). Mathematical objects are the clearest examples.

```
+---------------------------+        +---------------------------+
|    WORLD OF FORMS         |        |    SENSIBLE WORLD         |
|    (eternal, real)        |        |    (changing, apparent)   |
+---------------------------+        +---------------------------+
| The Form of Circle        | ---->  | All drawn circles         |
| The Form of Beauty        | ---->  | Beautiful things          |
| The Form of Justice       | ---->  | Just acts                 |
| Mathematical numbers      | ---->  | Counted objects           |
+---------------------------+        +---------------------------+

Knowledge (episteme) = grasping Forms
Opinion (doxa) = perceiving sensible objects

Implication for science: mathematics describes reality, not
appearances. Astronomy should study the mathematical structure
of perfect motion, not messy observable planets.
```

**Significance**: Plato's view that **mathematics is the deep structure of reality** underlies most physical theory. When Galileo says "the book of nature is written in mathematics," that is Platonism — and it has been enormously productive.

### The Timaeus Cosmology

Plato's *Timaeus* (~360 BCE): the demiurge (craftsman-god) constructed the cosmos by imposing mathematical form on formless matter.

```
PLATONIC SOLIDS AS ELEMENTS:
+----------+----------+----------+----------+----------+
| ELEMENT  | SOLID    | FACES    | WHY      |          |
+----------+----------+----------+----------+----------+
| Fire     | Tetrahedron | 4    | Sharpest | Prickly  |
| Air      | Octahedron  | 8    | Mobile   | Air moves|
| Water    | Icosahedron | 20   | Fluid    | Rolls    |
| Earth    | Cube        | 6    | Stable   | Stacks   |
| Cosmos   | Dodecahedron| 12   | ---      | Envelops |
+----------+----------+----------+----------+----------+

Kepler used Platonic solids in Mysterium Cosmographicum (1596)
to model planetary orbits — wrong, but shows Platonic tradition's
persistence into the Scientific Revolution.
```

### The Academy as Institution

Plato founded the **Academy** (~387 BCE) — the first institutionalized higher learning center in the West. Its significance:
- Created a model for research + teaching community
- Mathematical competence required ("Let no one enter who does not know geometry" — apocryphal but culturally accurate)
- Lasted ~900 years (closed by Justinian 529 CE — the longest-running educational institution in antiquity)
- Established precedent: learning happens in communities with shared norms

## Layer 5: Aristotle's Biology

### Why His Biology Was Good

Aristotle's biology (*History of Animals*, *Parts of Animals*, *Generation of Animals*) is his best empirical work and was not superseded until the 17th century.

**Scale**: Aristotle described ~500 species, with morphological, behavioral, and reproductive data. He dissected extensively, described the development of chick embryos day-by-day, classified dolphins correctly as mammals (not fish) 2000 years before most Europeans acknowledged this.

```
ARISTOTLE'S CLASSIFICATION PRINCIPLES:

Scala Naturae (Great Chain of Being):
Minerals --> Plants --> Animals (lower) --> Animals (higher) --> Humans

Distinguished by:
- Plants: nutritive soul only (grow, reproduce)
- Animals: + sensitive soul (perceive, move toward pleasure)
- Humans: + rational soul (reason, deliberate)

Empirical taxonomic criteria:
- Mode of reproduction (viviparous, oviparous, ovoviviparous)
- Blood vs bloodless (vertebrates vs invertebrates — correct distinction)
- Mode of locomotion
- Habitat
- Social organization
```

### The Four Causes

Aristotle's explanatory framework — **four causes** (aitiai) that together fully explain any natural phenomenon:

| Cause | Definition | Example: Bronze Statue |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Material cause** | What it is made of | Bronze |
| **Formal cause** | What shape/form it has | The shape of the athlete |
| **Efficient cause** | What produced the change | The sculptor's chiseling |
| **Final cause** | What it is for, its purpose | Honor at the Olympics |

**Final causation in biology**: for Aristotle, biological processes have intrinsic purposes — the eye is for seeing, not seeing-as-byproduct. This teleological thinking was highly productive for biology (functional explanations) and wrong for physics (falling bodies don't "want" to reach their natural place).

### Hylomorphism

Every physical object is a **composite of matter (hyle) and form (morphe)**. The soul is the form of the living body — which is why Aristotle's psychology is not dualist. Mind is not separate from body; it is the body's organizing principle.

**Why this mattered**: No Cartesian mind-body problem for Aristotle. His view is close to functionalism in philosophy of mind — arguably more sophisticated than Descartes.

## Layer 6: Alexandrian Science

### Euclid's Elements — Deductive System as Scientific Model

Euclid's *Elements* (~300 BCE) established the **axiomatic-deductive method** as the gold standard for knowledge. Start from definitions and postulates, derive theorems by pure logic.

```
EUCLIDEAN METHOD:

DEFINITIONS
(Point = that which has no part, Line = breadthless length, ...)
         |
         v
POSTULATES (5) -- assumed without proof
1. A straight line segment can be drawn joining any two points
2. A finite straight line can be extended to a straight line
3. A circle can be drawn with given center and radius
4. All right angles are equal
5. (Parallel postulate) -- the famous one
         |
         v
COMMON NOTIONS (axioms about equality)
         |
         v
THEOREMS -- derived with logical proof
465 propositions across 13 books
```

**The epistemological model**: Euclid showed that hundreds of truths could be derived from five postulates and five common notions. This became the aspirational model for all science — find the axioms and derive everything else. Descartes, Newton (*Principia* uses geometric proof form), Spinoza (*Ethics* written more geometrico) all applied this model.

**Non-Euclidean geometry (1830s)**: Gauss, Lobachevsky, Bolyai showed that denying the parallel postulate yields consistent geometries. Riemann generalized further. This destroyed the idea that Euclid had discovered necessary truths about space — and ultimately fed into general relativity.

### Archimedes — Mechanics and Infinitesimals

Archimedes of Syracuse (~287-212 BCE) produced work not significantly improved until the 17th century:

| Work | Content | Modern Equivalent |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| *On Floating Bodies* | Principle of buoyancy (Archimedes' principle) | Fluid statics |
| *On the Equilibrium of Planes* | Center of mass, lever principle | Static mechanics |
| *Measurement of a Circle* | pi bounded between 223/71 and 22/7 | Numerical methods |
| *On the Sphere and Cylinder* | Surface area and volume of sphere | Integral calculus precursor |
| *The Method* | Proto-infinitesimal reasoning | Cavalieri's principle |

Archimedes' **Method** (lost until 1906, rediscovered in Archimedes Palimpsest) reveals he used infinitesimal reasoning — conceptually similar to integration — to discover results, then proved them using exhaustion. He was doing calculus in 250 BCE.

Why no Scientific Revolution then? Social structure: Archimedes regarded his mechanical inventions (catapults, compound pulleys, Archimedean screw) as beneath mathematical dignity. He did not pursue systematic experimental mechanics.

### Ptolemy's Almagest — Predictive Success Without Realism

Ptolemy's *Almagest* (~150 CE) contains the most sophisticated astronomical model of antiquity:

```
PTOLEMAIC SYSTEM ELEMENTS:

DEFERENT
Large circle centered near (not on) Earth
         +
EPICYCLE
Small circle whose center moves on the deferent
         +
EQUANT POINT
Center of uniform angular motion (not center of deferent)
         +
ECCENTRIC
Deferent center offset from Earth

Why equant was controversial: it violated "uniform circular
motion" -- a Platonic aesthetic requirement. Copernicus
HATED the equant and partly motivated his heliocentric
system by wanting to eliminate it.
```

**The prediction problem**: Ptolemy's model predicted positions to within ~1/2 degree of arc — adequate for naked-eye astronomy. It was a **computational algorithm** of remarkable sophistication.

**Instrumentalism vs realism**: Medieval Islamic astronomers debated whether Ptolemy's model was "true" (the spheres physically exist) or merely a "hypothesis for calculating" positions. This exact debate recurs with Copernicus.

### Eratosthenes — Measuring the Earth

Eratosthenes (~276-194 BCE) measured Earth's circumference with remarkable accuracy:

```
METHOD:
At summer solstice in Syene (Aswan): Sun directly overhead
(shadow at zero depth in well)

At Alexandria (same meridian, ~5000 stadia north):
Sun casts shadow at 7.2 degrees from vertical

7.2 / 360 = 1/50 of full circle
Therefore: Earth circumference = 50 x 5000 stadia = 250,000 stadia

Modern equivalent: ~46,250 km (actual: 40,075 km)
Error: ~15% -- depending on which "stadia" he used, perhaps much less

Tools required:
- The gnomon (shadow stick)
- Basic geometry (alternate interior angles)
- Knowledge that Alexandria and Syene are on same meridian
- An approximation of the distance between them
```

## Why Greek Science Didn't Produce the Industrial Revolution

### Structural Reasons

```
SLAVE ECONOMY EFFECT:
Cheap labor --> no incentive to mechanize
No mechanization demand --> no applied mechanics development
No applied mechanics --> Archimedes' work stays theoretical

SOCIAL STATUS HIERARCHY:
Contemplation (theoria) > craft (techne) > manual labor
Mathematical discovery = high status
Building machines = low status
Result: Archimedes' practical inventions are apologized for

EXPERIMENTAL vs THEORETICAL SPLIT:
Greeks valued demonstration, not manipulation
Controlled experiment with systematic variation not developed
Aristotle's physics is observational, not experimental
No tradition of "torture nature to reveal its secrets" (Bacon)

ABSENCE OF KEY INSTITUTIONS:
No printing press (knowledge transfer limited)
No patent system (no economic incentive for invention)
No universities (Academy, Lyceum are limited)
No systematic cross-discipline collaboration
```

### What Did Transfer

| Greek Achievement | Modern Form |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Axiomatic mathematics (Euclid) | Mathematical proof culture |
| Atomism (Democritus) | Modern atomic theory (via Gassendi, Newton) |
| Heliocentric idea (Aristarchus) | Copernicus (explicitly credits Aristarchus) |
| Empirical biology (Aristotle) | Taxonomy, comparative anatomy |
| Ptolemaic computational astronomy | In use until Kepler, 1600 years |

## Common Confusion Points

**Aristotle was not a bad scientist.** His physics (falling bodies, natural motion) was wrong and retarded European mechanics. His biology was excellent and not superseded until Harvey (circulation, 1628) and Vesalius (anatomy, 1543). Judge him by the work, not the label.

**Atomism was not a "proto-scientific" lucky guess.** Democritus's atoms have nothing to do with Dalton's atoms except the name and the insight that matter has discrete constituents. The Greek atoms were metaphysical; Dalton's were empirically constrained units with measurable weights.

**Plato's Forms are not anti-scientific.** The Platonic intuition — that mathematics describes underlying reality — is one of the most productive ideas in physics history. The tension between "calculating mathematics" and "true physical model" runs through all of physics to the present.

**Ptolemy's model was genuinely useful.** It was used for navigation, calendars, and astrology for 1400 years. Its replacement by Copernicus was not because it was "wrong" in any obvious practical sense — the Ptolemaic model predicted positions as well as early Copernican models.

**The Alexandrian library was not simply "burned."** The library declined over centuries. Julius Caesar's fire in 48 BCE was a warehouse fire, not the library. The main scholarly institution declined under Aurelian (~270 CE) and then Theophilus (~390 CE). One incident doesn't explain the end of Alexandrian science.
