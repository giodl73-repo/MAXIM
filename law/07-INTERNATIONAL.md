# 07 — International Law

## Treaties, WTO, Sovereign Immunity, Cross-Border Enforcement, Arbitration

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Sources of international law: treaties (bilateral/multilateral), customary international law (state practice + opinio juris), general principles of law, ICJ/tribunal decisions, legal scholarship
- Treaty law: Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties — ratification, reservations, interpretation (ordinary meaning/context/object-purpose), suspension, termination; US treaty vs executive agreement distinction
- Sovereignty and sovereign immunity: FSIA (Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act) — exceptions (commercial activity, tort in US, waiver), act of state doctrine
- WTO/GATT: most-favored-nation principle (MFN), national treatment, TRIPS (IP protection), dispute settlement body (DSB), Appellate Body crisis (US blocking appointments)
- International arbitration: ICSID (investment disputes — bilateral investment treaties, ISDS), ICC/LCIA/SIAC (commercial), New York Convention (1958 — enforcement of arbitral awards in 170 countries)
- Jurisdiction and choice of law: personal (presence/domicile/consent), subject matter, long-arm statutes; choice of law clauses, enforcement of foreign judgments (contrast with arbitral awards)
- EU law as international law: direct effect, supremacy, preliminary reference procedure (Article 267 TFEU), EU as regulatory superpower (Brussels effect — global standard-setting)
- Sanctions law: OFAC SDN list, primary vs secondary sanctions, BIS export controls (EAR/ITAR), entity list; compliance implications for global tech companies
- Cybersecurity in international law: state responsibility for state-sponsored hacking, Tallinn Manual (NATO cyber norms), attribution problems, international humanitarian law in cyberspace
