# 06 — Employment Law

## At-Will, Discrimination, Wage/Hour, Non-Competes, Trade Secrets

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- At-will employment (US default): exceptions — implied contract (handbook language), implied covenant of good faith, public policy exceptions (retaliation, whistleblowing)
- Protected characteristics: Title VII (race/color/religion/sex/national origin), ADEA (age 40+), ADA (disability — interactive process, reasonable accommodation, undue hardship), GINA (genetic info), Pregnancy Discrimination Act; state law additions (sexual orientation/gender identity/political affiliation vary by state)
- Discrimination frameworks: disparate treatment (McDonnell Douglas burden shifting) vs disparate impact (Griggs — facially neutral policies with discriminatory effect)
- Harassment: hostile work environment (severe or pervasive standard), quid pro quo, employer vicarious liability (Faragher/Ellerth affirmative defense)
- FLSA: minimum wage, overtime (1.5x for non-exempt >40 hrs/week), exempt classifications (executive/administrative/professional/highly compensated), independent contractor vs employee (economic reality test, ABC test in California)
- Non-competes: California ban (Business & Professions Code 16600), FTC proposed nationwide ban (litigated), enforceability spectrum (geographic scope/duration/legitimate interest), blue-penciling
- Trade secret protection in employment: confidentiality agreements, DTSA (Defend Trade Secrets Act) federal cause of action, inevitable disclosure doctrine, exit interview practices
- WARN Act: 60-day notice for mass layoffs (100+ employees, 50+ affected), COBRA continuation coverage
- Classification issues: employee vs contractor (IRS 20-factor test, ABC test), joint employer liability (staffing agencies, gig economy)
- International: employment law varies enormously — EU works councils, mandatory notice periods (Germany: up to 7 months), TUPE (business transfers in UK/EU), works council consultation requirements
