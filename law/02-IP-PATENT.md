# 02 — IP & Patent Law

## Patent, Copyright, Trademark, Trade Secret, FOSS Licensing

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Patent: utility (novel/non-obvious/useful, 20-year term from filing), design (ornamental appearance, 15 years), plant; claims structure (independent vs dependent); prosecution at USPTO
- Patent eligibility (35 USC 101): Alice Corp v CLS Bank — abstract ideas/mathematical concepts not patentable; software patents post-Alice landscape; what survives
- Patent lifecycle: provisional → non-provisional → prosecution → grant → maintenance fees; continuation/CIP/divisional applications; IPR (inter partes review) as invalidity attack
- Patent trolls (NPEs): licensing vs litigation strategy, FRAND commitments (standard-essential patents), defensive patent aggregators (OIN)
- Copyright: automatic (no registration needed), life + 70 years, work-for-hire doctrine, fair use (four-factor test), DMCA safe harbor (Section 512), software copyright (Whelan/Altai filtration test, Oracle v Google APIs)
- Trademark: use-based (US) vs registration-based (EU), likelihood of confusion test, dilution (famous marks), trade dress, genericization risk (escalator/aspirin/thermos)
- Trade secret: no registration, indefinite duration, must take reasonable measures to protect; misappropriation; DTSA (federal); inevitable disclosure doctrine
- FOSS licensing spectrum: permissive (MIT/BSD/Apache 2.0 — patent grant) → weak copyleft (LGPL/MPL) → strong copyleft (GPL v2/v3 — viral effect); AGPL (network use triggers copyleft); CC licenses (content)
- Open source due diligence in M&A: license audit, GPL contamination risk, contributor agreements (CLA vs DCO)
