# 04 — Antitrust Law

## Sherman/Clayton Acts, EU Competition Law, Tech Antitrust Live Cases

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Sherman Act Section 1 (agreements in restraint of trade): per se illegal (price fixing/market allocation/bid rigging/group boycotts) vs rule of reason (weighing procompetitive vs anticompetitive effects)
- Sherman Act Section 2 (monopolization): two elements — monopoly power (>70% market share rule of thumb) + willful acquisition/maintenance; attempted monopolization
- Clayton Act: Section 7 (mergers that substantially lessen competition), Section 8 (interlocking directorates); Hart-Scott-Rodino pre-merger notification thresholds
- Market definition: relevant product market (SSNIP test — hypothetical monopolist), relevant geographic market; two-sided markets debate (Amex ruling implications)
- EU competition law: Article 101 TFEU (agreements — equivalent to Section 1), Article 102 TFEU (abuse of dominant position — broader than monopolization), DG COMP enforcement, commitment decisions vs fines
- Tech antitrust live cases: US v Google (search + ad tech), FTC v Meta (Instagram/WhatsApp acquisitions), EU Digital Markets Act (DMA — gatekeeper obligations), Apple App Store (Epic v Apple, DOJ suit)
- Tying and bundling: per se vs rule of reason, platform bundling (Microsoft browser tying precedent from 1990s → today)
- Vertical restraints: exclusive dealing, most-favored-nation clauses, resale price maintenance (now rule of reason in US)
- Network effects and antitrust: when scale becomes monopoly power, multi-homing, essential facilities doctrine (its limits)
- Merger remedies: behavioral (firewalls/licenses) vs structural (divestitures); why DOJ/FTC prefer structural
