# 03 — Fittings & Joints

## Sweat, Compression, Push-Fit, Threaded, Solvent-Weld — How Pipes Connect

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Sweat/solder** (copper): heat fitting with torch until flux activates and solder draws in by capillary action; capillary action requires close-fitting joint (clearance ~0.001-0.004"); flux (water-soluble paste flux — H₂O-soluble after cure, preferred; aggressive flux OK for drains but clean thoroughly); heat fitting NOT the pipe; lead-free solder (95/5 tin-antimony, or tin-silver, or Hercules Stay-Brite); pipe must be dry (residual water vaporizes, prevents solder from flowing); test: push solder to joint without flame → if it melts, hot enough; remove heat, let solidify undisturbed; MAPP gas vs propane: MAPP hotter, faster; oxyacetylene for large pipe (2"+)
- **Press-fit** (ProPress, Viega): copper, stainless steel, carbon steel; no flame, no torch; insert pipe fully, press with tool; EPDM O-ring compressed creates seal; auditing ring identifies unpressed fittings; fast (plumbing inspections now require tool tracking); code-accepted in most jurisdictions; fitting has leak-before-press feature on some products (slightly open before pressing → can leak until pressed properly); cost of fittings higher than solder fittings; tool rental available
- **Compression fittings**: ferrule (olive/ring) + nut compresses onto pipe OD; no soldering; reusable (remove nut, ferrule stays on pipe — pipe cut beyond ferrule for reuse); for rigid pipe only (copper, CPVC, PVC); supply stops, shutoffs, appliance connections; not typically used for long pipe runs (multiple potential leak points); brass body standard; angle stops (supply shutoffs at fixtures) are typically compression
- **Flare fittings**: 45° flare on tube end (copper/aluminum/steel); fitting nut compresses flared end to mating fitting; fuel gas applications (copper for LP gas in some regions, not natural gas in all codes); refrigeration; not for water (overtightening cracks flare); dedicated flaring tool required; soft copper Type K or L only (Type M may crack)
- **Push-fit (SharkBite and equivalents)**: EPDM O-ring seals pipe OD; stainless steel teeth grip pipe; insert and done; compatible with copper, CPVC, PEX; demountable with removal tool; permanent or temporary use; excellent for repairs without torch; higher fitting cost; O-ring life ~25 years (some controversy about long-term permanence vs soldered joints); not allowed buried or in concrete without accessibility
- **Threaded** (NPT — National Pipe Taper): tapered thread that wedges as tightened; thread sealant required (Teflon tape = PTFE tape: 2-3 wraps clockwise from pipe end; or pipe dope/compound, superior for large/male brass threads); gas: use yellow Teflon tape (thicker, specific for gas) plus thread sealant in many codes; overtightening cracks fittings (especially plastic); male thread into female: hand tight + 2-3 turns with wrench; check for leaks after pressurization; not for copper (no NPT copper — use fittings with NPT x copper ends)
- **Solvent welding (PVC/CPVC/ABS)**: chemical fusion — solvent softens both surfaces, they fuse together; NOT gluing (no separate adhesive — the materials themselves bond); purple primer (required for PVC in most codes — cleans and preps); grey or clear primer for CPVC; PVC cement, CPVC cement (different chemistry — do not substitute), ABS cement (black), transition cement (ABS to PVC); cure time before pressurizing; temperature-dependent (cold weather — use cold-weather cement or warm surfaces); do not use PVC cement on CPVC — CPVC specific chemistry required
- **PEX fittings**:
  - Crimp (PEX-b): copper ring crimped over fitting barb with crimp tool; go/no-go gauge verifies; most common
  - Clamp/cinch: stainless steel clamp, single-ear crimper; cheaper tool
  - Expansion (PEX-a only): expand pipe end with Uponor/Rehau expander, slide fitting in, ring contracts over time; highest flow (fitting inside diameter = pipe ID, not barb); most expensive, best long-term performance
  - Push-fit (SharkBite): works on PEX, see above
- **Grooved/Victaulic**: clamp-on coupling for steel/copper; large commercial pipe (2"+); no threading, no welding; fast installation; mechanical groove cut or roll-grooved into pipe end; used in commercial HVAC, fire suppression, industrial
