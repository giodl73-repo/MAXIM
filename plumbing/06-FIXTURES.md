# 06 — Fixtures

## Toilets, Faucets, Showers — Mechanisms, Water Efficiency, Code

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Toilets — anatomy**:
  - Fill valve (ballcock → modern float-cup fill valve): controls water inlet to refill tank after flush; float drops as water drains → valve opens; float rises as tank fills → valve closes; Fluidmaster 400A = dominant product; runs continuously = fill valve or flapper issue
  - Flapper: rubber seal at bottom of tank; lifts when flush handle pulled (chain lift); water rushes into bowl; drops when tank empties to seal; deteriorates over time (chloramine damage in municipal water), mineral deposits; running toilet (flapper not sealing) = most common toilet problem
  - Flush valve (overflow tube + flapper seat): if tank water level rises above overflow tube → water runs into bowl constantly; adjust fill valve float to keep water line 1" below top of overflow tube
  - Trap: integral siphon trap in toilet bowl; flushing creates siphon → bowl empties; trapway size determines clog resistance (large trapway = important spec)
- **Toilet water efficiency**: federal max 1.6 GPF (gallons per flush) since 1994; WaterSense certified ≤1.28 GPF; high-efficiency toilets (HET) using 1.0-1.28 GPF; dual-flush toilets (0.8 GPF liquid waste / 1.6 GPF solid waste); pressure-assist toilets (compressed air in sealed inner tank adds force — noisier but fewer clogs, common in commercial); California's 1.28 GPF standard adopted by other states
- **Toilet types**: gravity-flush (standard), pressure-assist (commercial and residential), macerating/up-flush (Saniflo — pump and grinder, allows below-grade installs without breaking slab), composting (off-grid), tankless/flushometer (commercial, requires 1" supply at 25 PSI minimum)
- **Faucet types — cartridge mechanisms**:
  - Compression (oldest): rubber washer screws down against seat; eventually seat wears → leak; each hot/cold handle; serviceable but dated
  - Ball (Price Pfister/Delta ball-type): rotating ball with holes controls flow and temperature; spring-loaded rubber seats and springs around ball ports; spring/seat kits for repair
  - Ceramic disc (quarter-turn): two ceramic discs (machined to extreme flatness) rotate relative to each other; extremely durable (essentially never wear); single lever or side-by-side; require no lubrication; Kohler and others; susceptible to mineral damage if ceramic surface scratched
  - Cartridge (pull-type): Moen, Delta, Kohler standard; plastic or brass cartridge with rubber seals; replace whole cartridge as one unit; Moen cartridge (1225/1222) = most repaired cartridge in US
- **Shower valves**: pressure-balance valve (ASSE 1016) — required for shower since 1992 code; senses pressure change (if toilet flushed, cold pressure drops) and reduces hot proportionally to maintain temperature ±3°F; compensates for pressure changes, not temperature changes; one-handle scald prevention; Moen Posi-Temp, Delta Monitor as examples
- **Thermostatic shower valves**: maintains true mixed water temperature regardless of supply temperature changes; separate volume and temperature controls; more accurate scald protection than pressure-balance; more expensive; European standard; multi-body shower systems use thermostatic
- **Faucet finishes and corrosion**: chrome (electroplated, durable), brushed nickel (electroplated, PVD or lacquer over), oil-rubbed bronze (varies — lacquer over brass, or PVD; lacquer wears off exposing brass), polished brass (requires periodic polishing), matte black (painted, PVD, or powder-coated — check durability), brushed gold/PVD gold (durable)
- **ADA accessibility**: lever handles (not knobs) required for ADA-accessible fixtures; grab bars at specific locations; roll-under clearances; deck-mount faucets on accessible lavatories; accessible toilet height 17-19" (vs standard 14-17")
