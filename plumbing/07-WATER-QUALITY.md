# 07 — Water Quality

## Hardness, Scaling, Softeners, Filtration, Lead Pipes

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Water hardness**: dissolved calcium (Ca²⁺) and magnesium (Mg²⁺) bicarbonate/carbonate/sulfate; measured in grains per gallon (GPG), parts per million (PPM), or mg/L as CaCO₃; classification: soft <1 GPG, slightly hard 1-3.5, moderately hard 3.5-7, hard 7-10.5, very hard >10.5; 85% of US has hard water; problem: CaCO₃ scale deposits in hot water heaters, pipes, fixtures → reduced efficiency, eventual failure; soap doesn't lather in hard water (calcium precipitates soap)
- **Scale chemistry**: at elevated temperatures (>140°F), calcium bicarbonate → calcium carbonate (precipitates) + CO₂ + H₂O; explains why water heaters fail first; scale thermal insulation = 1/4" scale reduces heater efficiency 39% (DOE estimate); tankless water heaters extremely vulnerable → require annual descaling with vinegar/citric acid
- **Water softeners (ion exchange)**: resin beads with sodium ions; Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺ exchange onto resin, releasing Na⁺ into water; effective scaling prevention; resin periodically regenerated with concentrated NaCl brine (salt tank); sodium addition: ~460 mg sodium per liter for very hard water → concern for sodium-restricted diets; potassium chloride (KCl) alternative to NaCl (more expensive); bypass valve allows un-softened water to outdoor hose bibs (salt not appropriate for plants/irrigation); softened water should not sit in lead pipes (soft water more corrosive)
- **Salt-free water "softeners"** (actually scale inhibitors, not softeners): template-assisted crystallization (TAC) or electromagnetic/magnetic devices; do not remove hardness minerals (Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ stay in water); TAC modifies crystal structure to prevent scale adhesion; no waste water, no salt; EPA and independent testing show TAC effective for scale prevention but not ion exchange; "conditioned" not "softened"; useful for scale prevention without sodium addition; magnetic devices — no credible evidence of effectiveness
- **Sediment filtration**: particle filtration; 20-micron pre-filter (removes sediment), 5-micron (most visible particles), 1-micron (removes most protozoa like Giardia/Cryptosporidium cysts); cartridge (replace periodically) vs backwashing (self-cleaning media filter); essential before UV or RO systems; flow restriction increases with loading
- **Activated carbon filtration**: adsorption of organic compounds onto carbon surface; removes chlorine, chloramines, taste/odor, VOCs, pesticides, radon; does NOT remove: nitrates, heavy metals, microbes, hardness; granular activated carbon (GAC) vs carbon block (finer filtration, slower flow); replace when "breakthrough" occurs (odor/taste returns); NSF/ANSI 42 (taste/odor) and 53 (health effects) certifications
- **Reverse osmosis (RO)**: semi-permeable membrane under pressure passes water molecules but rejects dissolved solids, heavy metals, nitrates, fluoride, most pharmaceuticals; 95-99% TDS reduction; reject-to-permeate ratio 3:1 to 5:1 (3-5 gallons wasted per gallon produced); slow production rate → storage tank under sink; remineralization filter sometimes added after RO (RO water is slightly acidic, lacks beneficial minerals); NSF/ANSI 58 certification
- **UV disinfection**: UV-C light (254nm wavelength) damages DNA of bacteria/viruses/protozoa → prevents reproduction; effective at kill/inactivation; requires pre-filtration (turbid water blocks UV); no chemical addition/no taste change; does not remove chemicals or metals; requires power; NSF/ANSI 55 standard; used in wells and as post-RO polishing
- **Lead in pipes**: Lead and Copper Rule (EPA); action level 15 ppb; Flint, Michigan crisis (2014-2019) — change in water source without corrosion inhibitor (orthophosphate) → dissolution of lead from pipes; corrosion control treatment (orthophosphate forms insoluble lead phosphate coating inside pipes); service lines (utility-side and customer-side) still lead in many older cities; EPA Lead and Copper Rule Revisions (2021) — 10-year replacement mandate; NSF/ANSI 61 certification for plumbing products in contact with drinking water; at-faucet filters certified for lead removal (NSF/ANSI 53)
- **Well water specific**: annual testing recommended (coliform bacteria, nitrates, pH, hardness); arsenic in certain geologies (Bangladesh, Pakistan, Maine/New England, Western US); radon (dissolved in groundwater, outgasses in home); iron bacteria (sulfur smell, orange staining); iron removal (oxidation/filtration, greensand filter); shock chlorination for bacterial contamination
