# 00 — Plumbing: Overview

## Three Trees — Supply, Drain-Waste-Vent, Gas

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Plumbing as three separate, largely independent systems sharing a building:
  - **Supply** (pressurized, 40-80 PSI): potable cold water from municipal or well source, distributed to fixtures; hot water from heater; under pressure throughout — any leak sprays
  - **Drain-Waste-Vent (DWV)**: gravity-dependent drainage from fixtures to sewer/septic; atmospheric pressure maintained by vent pipes; traps seal each fixture from sewer gas; completely different pipe sizing logic (flow by gravity, not pressure)
  - **Gas** (fuel gas — natural gas or propane): black iron or CSST (flexible corrugated stainless steel tubing); separate from water systems; code-governed by gas code (NFPA 54/IRC Chapter 24)
- Why these systems don't mix: cross-connection control is life-safety; backflow prevention (backflow preventer, vacuum breaker) prevents contaminated water from entering potable supply; failure examples: garden hose left in pool, irrigation systems connected without backflow prevention → bacteria/chemicals siphon back into supply
- Pressure vs gravity: supply pipe can run any direction (pressurized); drain pipe must slope (1/4" per foot for horizontal runs, 3/16" minimum acceptable); vent pipe can be vertical or slope upward to atmosphere; understanding this prevents most amateur installation errors
- Code framework: International Plumbing Code (IPC) or Uniform Plumbing Code (UPC) as base; adopted by states/municipalities with local amendments; Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) = local building department; permit required for almost all new work; rough-in inspection (before walls closed) + final inspection; inspector's word is law
- Key sizing principles: supply — pipe sized for pressure drop and velocity (GPM demand × pressure available); DWV — sized by drainage fixture units (DFU), each fixture assigned a value; DFU drives minimum pipe diameter; gas — sized for BTU demand at end of run + pressure available
