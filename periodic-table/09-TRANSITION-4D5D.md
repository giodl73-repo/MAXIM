## Relativistic Effects вҖ” Why Au Is Yellow and Hg Is Liquid

Heavy elements (Z > ~70) require relativistic quantum mechanics to explain their properties.
The "standard" chemistry intuition fails here.

```
THE RELATIVISTIC EFFECT:

  For electron in 1s orbital of hydrogen-like atom:
  v/c вүҲ Z/137 (velocity as fraction of speed of light)

  At Z = 79 (Au): v/c вүҲ 0.58 вҶ’ Lorentz factor Оі вүҲ 1.22
  Relativistic mass increase вҶ’ effective Bohr radius contracts

  CONSEQUENCES:
  в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
  в”Ӯ  1. s and p orbitals contract and stabilize (direct effect)     в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ     вҶ’ 6s orbital in Au/Hg/Tl contracts significantly           в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ                                                                   в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ  2. d and f orbitals expand (indirect effect вҖ” s/p better        в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ     screen nucleus вҶ’ d/f feel less nuclear charge)              в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ                                                                   в”Ӯ
  в”Ӯ  3. Spin-orbit coupling increases dramatically with Z            в”Ӯ
  в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ

WHY GOLD IS YELLOW:
  Non-relativistic Au: 5d вҶ’ 6s transition would be in UV (invisible)
  Relativistic Au: 6s contracts and stabilizes; 5d expands and destabilizes
  вҶ’ Energy gap 5dвҶ’6s DECREASES вҶ’ transition moves from UV into visible blue range (~450 nm)
  вҶ’ Au absorbs blue light вҶ’ reflects yellow/orange вҶ’ appears yellow
  Silver and copper: dвҶ’s transition in UV for Ag, just barely absorbs blue for Cu (explains copper's reddish tint)

WHY MERCURY IS LIQUID:
  Non-relativistic Hg: would be a solid metal (like Cd above it)
  Relativistic Hg: 6s orbital contracts dramatically вҶ’ 6sВІ filled pair strongly stabilized
  вҶ’ Very hard to promote 6s electrons into bonding вҶ’ weak metallic bonding
  вҶ’ Much lower cohesive energy вҶ’ low melting point (вҲ’38.8В°C)
  This is literally relativistic quantum mechanics observable on a kitchen bench.
```


## Molybdenum (Mo, Z=42) вҖ” Unique Biology

### The Nitrogen Fixation Connection

```
NITROGENASE вҖ” THE ONLY BIOLOGICAL NвӮӮ FIXER:
  NвӮӮ + 8HвҒә + 8eвҒ» + 16 ATP вҶ’ 2 NHвӮғ + HвӮӮ + 16 ADP + 16 Pбөў

  FeMo-cofactor (FeMoco): [Mo-7Fe-9S-C-homocitrate]
  NвӮӮ binds to the Mo (or adjacent Fe вҖ” still debated) and is reduced stepwise:
  NвӮӮ вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮӮ вҶ’ NвӮӮHвӮ„ вҶ’ 2 NHвӮғ

  Mo is the ONLY metal known to fix atmospheric NвӮӮ in biology
  (There are Fe-only and V-Fe nitrogenases but Mo is most abundant and efficient)
  When Mo is scarce, organisms can switch to V-nitrogenase (marine cyanobacteria)

  This means: Mo availability in ancient oceans may have limited biological N fixation
  вҶ’ linked to early Earth biogeochemistry and rise of complex life
```

### Molybdenum Industrial Uses

```

  Aerospace bearings, rifle breech mechanisms, anti-seize compounds
  "Molykote" brand solid lubricant

  Hydrodesulfurization (HDS): remove S from crude oil вҶ’ prevent SOвӮӮ emissions
  Catalyst: CoMoS or NiMoS on AlвӮӮOвӮғ support
  C-S bonds cleaved: R-SH + HвӮӮ вҶ’ R-H + HвӮӮS (then HвӮӮS вҶ’ S by Claus process)
```

## Technetium (Tc, Z=43) вҖ” No Stable Isotopes


## The Platinum Group Metals (PGMs)

Ru, Rh, Pd, Os, Ir, Pt вҖ” six metals with broadly similar properties, all extremely rare.

```
RARITY (crustal abundance, ppm):
  Ru: 0.001 | Rh: 0.0002 | Pd: 0.015 | Os: 0.0005 | Ir: 0.0004 | Pt: 0.005

Compare: Au: 0.004 ppm, Ag: 0.075 ppm
PGMs come primarily from sulfide ore deposits (South Africa 75% of Pt+Pd supply)
and as byproducts of Ni/Cu refining

COMMON PROPERTIES:
  High melting points, corrosion resistant, dense, catalytically active
  Tend to concentrate together in nature (siderophilic + chalcophilic)
  Market price: Rh ~$5000-30,000/oz (most variable), Ir ~$5000/oz, Pt ~$1000/oz
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Why does Pd/C work as a catalyst but Pd in solution doesn't?"**
Heterogeneous Pd/C: Pd nanoparticles on activated carbon вҶ’ surface Pd atoms act as active sites
for HвӮӮ dissociation and alkene adsorption. In solution, PdвҒ° as a lone metal is not stable.
Homogeneous Pd: ligand-stabilized PdвҒ° complexes (like Pd(PPhвӮғ)вӮ„) are the active cross-coupling
catalysts вҖ” they operate by the OA/TM/RE mechanism and are soluble. Different applications.

**"Why is the K-Pg iridium anomaly evidence for an asteroid, not a volcano?"**
Volcanic eruptions can release Ir, but the K-Pg layer is globally synchronous (same age everywhere)
and the Ir concentration matches chondritic (stony meteorite) composition. Volcanic Ir would be
local and have different isotope ratios. Plus: the impact crater was found (Chicxulub, 1991).

**"Mercury is liquid вҖ” can I touch it?"**
A brief skin contact with liquid Hg is not acutely dangerous (low dermal absorption of elemental
Hg). The danger is: (1) Hg vapor inhalation (elemental Hg is volatile at RT вҖ” don't heat it, don't
work with it in enclosed spaces), (2) methylmercury formation in environment, (3) chronic low-level
exposure accumulates (Hg half-life in brain is years). The old "roll it around in your hand" demos
in schools were concerning primarily for vapor exposure.
