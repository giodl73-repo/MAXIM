## Coverage Notes

Demography is the scientific study of human populations — their size, composition, distribution, and dynamics through birth, death, and migration. This directory covers formal demographic methods (life tables, cohort-component projections, rate decomposition), the three fundamental demographic processes (fertility, mortality, migration), the classic and second demographic transitions, population aging and its fiscal implications, global urbanization dynamics, UN population projection methodology and scenario variants, and the demographic dividend — the growth window that opens when a young age structure shifts toward a working-age bulge. The field bridges sociology, economics, epidemiology, and policy in ways that make it indispensable for long-range planning.
