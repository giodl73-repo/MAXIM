## Isostasy — Crustal Adjustments

```
ISOSTASY CONCEPT:
  Earth's crust floats isostatically on viscous mantle (asthenosphere)
  Load applied → crust sinks (subsidence)
  Load removed → crust rises (rebound)

  Relaxation timescale: 1,000–10,000 years (depends on mantle viscosity)
  Upper mantle viscosity: ~10²¹ Pa·s (10²¹ times more viscous than water)

GLACIAL ISOSTATIC ADJUSTMENT (GIA):
  Last Glacial Maximum (LGM): ~21,000 yr BP
    Ice sheets up to 3–4 km thick over Scandinavia, Canada
    Mass pushed crust DOWN by hundreds of meters
    Mantle material flowed outward (peripheral bulge rose)

  Post-LGM ice melting:
    Scandinavia, Canada uplift: ~200–400 m total, ongoing at 5–10 mm/yr
    Scotland, Fennoscandia: rising 1–6 mm/yr today
    Peripheral bulge collapses: SE England, US Mid-Atlantic coast SINKING ~1 mm/yr
      (this is the forebulge collapse → amplifies relative sea level rise along US East Coast)

  GIA also drives gravitational effects:
    Large ice sheets have gravitational pull → sea level HIGHER adjacent to ice
    As Greenland melts → gravity decreases → sea level DROP near Greenland
    → sea level RISE far from Greenland (Southern Ocean, US East Coast, Australia)
    This "sea level fingerprint" is unique to each melting glacier

SEDIMENT LOADING SUBSIDENCE:
  Deltas: rapid sediment deposition → compaction → subsidence
  Mississippi Delta: 5–10 mm/yr natural compaction + 10–20 mm/yr anthropogenic
    (groundwater extraction, oil/gas extraction) → total 25–30 mm/yr
  Bangkok: 20–30 mm/yr subsidence (groundwater pumping)
  Jakarta: up to 25 cm/yr in worst areas (near-term flood risk catastrophic)
```

## Eustasy — Global Mean Sea Level (GMSL) History

```
GLACIAL-INTERGLACIAL CYCLES:
  LGM (~21,000 BP): sea level ~125 m BELOW present
    → exposed continental shelves (Dogger Bank fishing grounds were dry land)
    → Britain attached to Europe via "Doggerland"
    → Beringia: land bridge between Alaska and Siberia
    → Indonesia: Sundaland (most of Java, Borneo, Sumatra exposed)

  Last Interglacial (~125,000 BP): sea level ~6–9 m ABOVE present
    → global temperature ~0.5–1°C warmer than today
    → significant Greenland + some West Antarctic ice melted

HOLOCENE SEA LEVEL:
  Deglaciation: sea level rose from -125 m to ~0 m between 21,000–6,000 BP
  Average rate: ~10–15 mm/yr during rapid melt phases
  Meltwater Pulse 1A (~14,600 BP): +20 m in ~340 years = 50 mm/yr!!
    (possibly from sudden Antarctic ice collapse)
  Holocene Highstand: in some places, sea level slightly higher than present ~6000 BP
    (due to GIA — rebounding land lagging behind; ice load removed, land still rising)

20TH CENTURY:
  Rate: +1.7 mm/yr over 1900–2018 average (tide gauge record)
  1993–2024: +3.6 mm/yr (satellite altimetry, Jason/Sentinel series)
  Acceleration observed: rate increasing ~0.1 mm/yr per year since 1993
```

## Components of Modern Sea Level Rise

```
OBSERVED GLOBAL MEAN SEA LEVEL RISE (2006–2018 budget):

  Component                        Contribution (mm/yr)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Thermal expansion (steric)            1.4
  Glaciers (mountain/small ice caps)    0.9
  Greenland ice sheet                   0.7
  Antarctic ice sheet                   0.4
  Land water storage (dams, aquifers)  -0.2 (uncertain)
  TOTAL (budget)                        3.2
  OBSERVED (altimetry)                  3.6
  Closure gap: ~0.4 (within uncertainty)

GREENLAND ICE SHEET:
  Total ice volume: 2.9 million km³ → sea level equivalent: 7.2 m
  Current mass loss: ~280 Gt/yr (2010s–2020s)
  Mechanisms: surface melting (68%), iceberg calving (32%)
  Jakobshavn, Helheim, Kangerdlugssuaq: major outlet glacier retreats
  Tipping point: sustained 1.5–2.5°C warming may trigger irreversible loss
    → committed to several meters over centuries even if warming stops

WEST ANTARCTIC ICE SHEET (WAIS):
  Marine ice sheet: grounded below sea level on retrograde slope
  Total SLE: ~3.3 m
  Marine Ice Sheet Instability (MISI): once grounding line retreats from ridge crest
    → accelerating retreat → potential runaway collapse
  Amundsen Sea Embayment (Thwaites Glacier): "Doomsday Glacier"
    Currently losing ~50 Gt/yr, accelerating
    Thwaites alone SLE: ~0.65 m, but destabilization could unlock rest of WAIS
  Timeline: decades to centuries for significant committed SLR

EAST ANTARCTIC ICE SHEET (EAIS):
  Total SLE: ~52 m (most ice on Earth)
  Currently near-neutral to slight mass gain (snowfall) in interior
  Coastal glaciers accelerating (Totten Glacier: may destabilize ~3°C warming)
  Generally considered more stable than WAIS for this century
```

## IPCC AR6 Sea Level Projections (2021)

```
PROJECTED GLOBAL MEAN SEA LEVEL RISE BY 2100 (relative to 1995–2014 mean):

  Scenario         Likely range (m)     Upper end (m)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
  SSP1-1.9 (1.5°C)  0.28–0.55           0.62
  SSP2-4.5 (2.5°C)  0.33–0.61           0.74
  SSP5-8.5 (4.5°C)  0.63–1.01           1.16
  Low likelihood but high impact (ice cliff instability, MISI):
    Up to 2 m not ruled out for high-emissions scenario
    Beyond 2100: several meters possible under high emissions

REGIONAL VARIATION — RSL ≠ GMSL:
  +/- 20–30 cm from GMSL depending on location:
    Amplified: US Atlantic Coast (forebulge collapse + ocean dynamics),
               deltaic coasts (subsidence), Indonesia, Bangladesh
    Reduced or opposite: Scandinavia, Scotland, Canada (still rebounding from ice)

COMMITTED SEA LEVEL RISE:
  Even if GHG emissions stop today:
    ~0.4–0.5 m of thermal expansion already "locked in" from existing ocean heat
    GIA ongoing for millennia
    Ice sheet response lasts centuries to millennia
    ~0.5–1 m committed even under optimal mitigation
```

## Storm Surge and Extreme Sea Levels

```
COMPOUND FLOODING:
  Extreme water level = tide + storm surge + wave setup + river flood
  Probability of compound event >> probability of any individual component
  Example: Hurricane Sandy 2012:
    Astronomical tide: +0.5 m (at high tide)
    Storm surge: +2.9 m
    Waves on top: +1–2 m
    Total peak: ~4 m above MWSL at Battery Park, NYC

RETURN PERIODS AND RISK:
  "100-year flood" = 1% annual exceedance probability (AEP)
  NOT "occurs once every 100 years" — it's a probabilistic statement
  With 0.5 m SLR: today's 100-year event becomes ~10-year event
  With 1 m SLR: today's 100-year event may become annual

  Compound effect: SLR shifts the entire distribution
    → events that were rare become frequent → threshold crossings
    → infrastructure designed for 100-yr events fails regularly

TIDE GAUGE RECORD CONTEXT:
  ~2000 long-term tide gauges globally (PSMSL database)
  Coverage: biased toward developed coastlines
  Corrections needed: GIA, local subsidence, datum changes
  Longest records: Amsterdam (1700), Brest (1807), Stockholm (1774)
  These show acceleration of sea level rise in 20th century
```

## Historic Sea Level Transgressions and Regressions

```
MAJOR HISTORIC SEA LEVEL EVENTS:

  Permo-Carboniferous glaciation (~300 Ma):
    Sea level drop 100–150 m (major ice caps) → major exposure of shelves
    Precursor to massive coal deposition in swamp forests just before this

  Cretaceous Highstand (~80 Ma):
    Sea level ~200–250 m ABOVE present → 80% of North America flooded
    Western Interior Seaway: Kansas under shallow sea
    Maximum of seawater flooding of continents in Phanerozoic

  Messinian Salinity Crisis (~5.96–5.33 Ma):
    Mediterranean Sea isolated from Atlantic → completely evaporated!
    2–3 km thick evaporite deposits (halite, anhydrite)
    Sea level in Atlantic dropped ~8–10 m (volume removed into Mediterranean)
    Then "Zanclean Flood" — Atlantic broke through Gibraltar sill
    Mediterranean refilled in ~100–1000 years

  Pleistocene glacial cycles (2.6 Ma–11,700 BP):
    Sea level oscillating ±120 m with ~100,000-yr cycle
    Shaped coastlines, enabled migration across land bridges
    Doggerland, Sundaland, Beringia — submerged post-LGM

  Black Sea reflooding (~7,500 BP):
    Fresh-water lake → connected to Mediterranean as sea level rose
    Candidate for "Noah's Flood" myth (Ryan & Pitman hypothesis, debated)
```

## Paleosea Level Reconstruction Methods

```
PROXIES FOR PAST SEA LEVEL:

  CORALS:
    Acropora palmata (elkhorn coral): grows in 0–5 m water
    Dated by U-Th series → precise ages for specific sea level positions
    Last Interglacial corals at +6–9 m → SL was higher when warmer

  FORAMINIFERA:
    Benthic forams: depth-specific assemblages
    δ¹⁸O of planktonic forams: proxy for ice volume (hence sea level)
    Ice volume ↑ → ocean water ¹⁸O-enriched (light ¹⁶O evaporates, stays in ice)
    → foram shells record heavy δ¹⁸O during glacials

  SEDIMENT CORES:
    Transgressive sequences: sea level rise → shelf sedimentation over terrestrial
    Regressive sequences: sea level fall → progradation of deltaic/coastal systems

  SUBMERGED SPELEOTHEMS:
    Cave deposits (stalagmites/stalactites) form only in air, not water
    Submerged speleothems in Yucatan blue holes: mark when caves were above sea level
    → precise sea level reconstruction at specific past times

  SEDIMENT STRATIGRAPHY:
    Sequence stratigraphy (seismic + drill): maps shoreline positions through time
    Type-1 unconformities = sea level falls
    Transgressions = sea level rises
```

## Common Confusion Points

**Sea level rise is not uniform**: Global Mean Sea Level is a volume-weighted average. Locally, RSL can be rising 3× faster (US Mid-Atlantic, some delta coasts) or actually falling (Scandinavia, Hudson Bay) depending on GIA and tectonic setting.

**Thermal expansion is not just near-surface warming**: The entire water column expands. Most new ocean heat is stored in the 0–700 m layer, but deep ocean (700–2000 m) is also warming slowly. Full thermal equilibration takes centuries — committed expansion is already "baked in" even without further emissions.

**Ice shelf melting doesn't raise sea level directly**: Ice shelves (e.g., Ross Ice Shelf, Filchner-Ronne) are already floating. Melting floating ice doesn't raise sea level (Archimedes principle — displacing water equal to their weight already). But ice shelves act as BUTTRESSES — when they collapse (Larsen B in 2002), the land-based glaciers behind them accelerate → land ice flows to sea → THAT raises sea level.

**"Committed" vs. "projected" sea level**: Committed SLR is the rise that will occur even under immediate zero emissions (from existing ocean heat + GIA + short-term ice dynamics). Projected SLR includes all future emissions. Commitments are already ~0.5 m+ for this century. Future emissions determine whether we get 0.5 m or 2+ m.

**Return periods are not memoryless in non-stationary climate**: A "100-year event" is defined relative to a stationary historical distribution. As sea level rises, that distribution shifts — the return period of the same flood level decreases. An event that was 100-year in 1990 may be a 10-year event by 2050 with 30 cm SLR. Engineering designed for 1% AEP becomes inadequate without recalibration.
