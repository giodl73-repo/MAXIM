# Small Bodies: Asteroids, Comets, and KBOs

## The Big Picture

Small bodies are the leftover building blocks of planet formation — frozen records of the early solar system's chemistry, dynamics, and composition. They are not debris; they are the unfinished business of accretion.

```
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                    SMALL BODY POPULATIONS                         |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                                  |
|  INNER SOLAR SYSTEM         OUTER SOLAR SYSTEM                   |
|  -------------------        --------------------                 |
|                                                                  |
|  Near-Earth Asteroids (NEAs) Centaurs (Saturn-Neptune zone)      |
|  ~2200 known >1 km           ~3000 known                         |
|                                                                  |
|  Main Belt Asteroids (MBA)   Kuiper Belt Objects (KBOs)          |
|  ~2-3.5 AU; ~800,000 known   30-50 AU; ~100,000 known >100 km   |
|  2× Earth mass total                                             |
|                                                                  |
|  Jupiter Trojans             Scattered Disk Objects (SDOs)        |
|  L4 and L5 points; ~9800     50-100+ AU; highly eccentric        |
|  known; equal C+P types                                          |
|                                                                  |
|  Mars Trojans, Earth Trojans  Oort Cloud (hypothetical)          |
|  few known                    ~2000-100,000 AU; ~10¹²-¹³ comets  |
|                                                                  |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

## Asteroid Taxonomy

### By Orbit

```
ORBITAL CLASSES
===============

  MAIN BELT (2.0-3.5 AU):
    Inner:  2.0-2.5 AU  (S, E types dominate)
    Middle: 2.5-2.82 AU
    Outer:  2.82-3.5 AU (C, B types dominate)
    Kirkwood Gaps: resonances with Jupiter clear specific zones
      3:1 resonance: 2.50 AU
      5:2 resonance: 2.82 AU
      7:3 resonance: 2.96 AU
      2:1 resonance: 3.28 AU

  NEAR-EARTH OBJECTS (NEOs):
    Atiras:    entirely inside Earth's orbit (q < 0.983 AU)
    Atens:     cross Earth's orbit, short semi-major axis (a < 1 AU)
    Apollos:   cross Earth's orbit, long semi-major axis (a > 1 AU)
    Amors:     orbit just outside Earth's; 1.017 < q < 1.3 AU

  TROJANS:
    Lead (L4) or trail (L5) a planet by 60° in its orbit
    Jupiter has ~7000 known; also Mars, Neptune, Earth (minimally)
    Jupiter's L4 "Greek" camp, L5 "Trojan" camp
```

### By Composition (Spectral Type)

```
ASTEROID SPECTRAL TAXONOMY
===========================

  TYPE    COMPOSITION           LOCATION       METEORITE ANALOG
  ----    -----------           --------       ----------------
  C       Carbon-rich, dark     Outer belt     Carbonaceous chondrites
          hydrated silicates    (C, B, G, F)   (CI, CM types)
          low albedo (~0.03-0.10)

  S       Silicate (olivine,    Inner belt     Ordinary chondrites
          pyroxene) + metal     (S, Q, A, K)   (H, L, LL types)
          moderate albedo (0.15-0.25)

  M       Metallic iron/Ni      Outer S zone   Iron meteorites
          (nickel-iron)                        (not well sampled)
          moderate albedo

  V       Basaltic crust        Vesta family   HED meteorites
          (evolved differentiated)             (howardite, eucrite, diogenite)

  D       Dark, red, organic    Outer belt     No match (primitive?)
          (Jupiter Trojans)     + Trojans

  P       Very dark, red        Outer belt     No meteorite analog
          + Trojans

  KEY: Compositional gradient from inner (rocky/metallic) to outer (dark/carbon-rich)
       = record of the original condensation gradient in the solar nebula
```

## Comets: Structure and Behavior

### Comet Anatomy

```
COMET STRUCTURE
===============

  NUCLEUS (1-50 km)
    +------------------------------------------+
    |  Porous, icy aggregate ("dirty snowball") |
    |  50-80% empty space                      |
    |  Composition: H₂O ice (dominant)         |
    |  + CO₂, CO, CH₄, NH₃, complex organics  |
    |  + silicate dust, organics, CHON grains  |
    +------------------------------------------+
         |
         |  When inside ~3 AU: solar heating
         v
  COMA (10,000-100,000 km)
    Gas + dust sublimated from nucleus
    H₂O vapor, CO₂, CO, HCN, complex molecules
    Halo of neutral gas

  HYDROGEN CLOUD (1-10 million km)
    Lyman-α emission; photodissociation of H₂O → H

  DUST TAIL (curved)
    Pushed by solar radiation pressure
    Trails behind along orbit
    White/yellow: scattered sunlight

  ION/PLASMA TAIL (straight)
    Pushed by solar wind
    Always points directly AWAY from Sun
    Blue: CO⁺, CO₂⁺ fluorescence
    Straight because solar wind moves radially outward
```

### Comet Populations and Orbits

```
COMET POPULATION ORIGINS
=========================

  SHORT-PERIOD COMETS (P < 200 yr)
    Jupiter-family: P < 20 yr; prograde, low-inclination orbits
    Source: Kuiper Belt / Scattered Disk
    Scattered inward by Neptune
    Example: Halley (75.3 yr), 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko

  LONG-PERIOD COMETS (P > 200 yr)
    Isotropic distribution of inclinations
    Source: Oort Cloud (50,000-100,000 AU)
    Perturbed inward by passing stars / Galactic tides
    First apparition: "dynamically new" comets
    Example: Hale-Bopp (2520 yr period)

  INTERSTELLAR COMETS:
    'Oumuamua (2017): first confirmed interstellar object
      cigar-shaped, non-gravitational acceleration, no coma
      now leaving the solar system
    Borisov (2019): first confirmed interstellar COMET
      coma, CN gas, dust — normal comet composition from another star

  DORMANT/DEAD COMETS:
    Exhausted all volatile ices; look like asteroids
    Some "asteroids" in Apollo group may be extinct comet nuclei
    3200 Phaethon (Geminid meteor source) — active with dust, probably dead comet
```

## Kuiper Belt Objects (KBOs)

```
KUIPER BELT STRUCTURE
=====================

  CLASSICAL KBOs (cold): near-circular orbits; 42-48 AU
    Low-inclination; red surface; pebble-pile structure (streaming instability)
    Arrokoth (MU69) — first KBO visited by spacecraft (New Horizons 2019)
    Cold classical KBOs: never perturbed; pristine dynamically

  RESONANT KBOs (Plutinos): locked in resonance with Neptune
    3:2 resonance (same as Pluto): ~39.5 AU → "Plutinos"
    Pluto, Ixion, Huya: ~1000+ known
    2:1 resonance: ~47.8 AU → "Twotinos"

  SCATTERED DISK: highly eccentric; Neptune-scattered
    q ~35-40 AU, a ~50-1000 AU
    Source of Jupiter-family comets (scattered in by Neptune)
    Eris (~2330 km) found here; ~same size as Pluto

  DETACHED (SEDNOIDS):
    Large perihelion (q > 50 AU); not Neptune-scattered
    Sedna: q = 76 AU, a = 506 AU — too far for Neptune scattering
    Hypothesized: shepherd → Planet Nine? Or stellar encounter?

  PLUTO:
    Pluto-Charon system (Charon is 1/8 Pluto's mass — a double system)
    Heart-shaped Tombaugh Regio: nitrogen ice plain (Sputnik Planitia)
    Bladed terrain (methane ice); mountains (~3000 m) = water ice
    Subsurface ocean? (moment of inertia + geology suggests yes)
    New Horizons (2015) flyby revolutionized KBO science
```

## Meteors, Meteorites, and Impactors

```
IMPACT HIERARCHY
================

  METEOROID (< 1 m)
    Small particle in space
    → in atmosphere: METEOR (shooting star)
    → on surface: METEORITE

  BOLIDE / FIREBALL (1 m - 20 m)
    Spectacular fireball; may reach surface
    Example: Chelyabinsk 2013: ~20 m; ~500 kt; shocked 1600+

  SMALL IMPACTOR (20 m - 1 km)
    < 25 m: atmospheric breakup (Tunguska 1908: ~50 m, ~10-15 MT)
    25-1000 m: regional devastation
    1 km: global effects (cooling, crop failure)

  LARGE IMPACTOR (> 1 km)
    > 10 km: mass extinction risk
    Chicxulub: ~10-15 km; ~100 million MT; K-Pg extinction
    Vredefort, Sudbury, etc.: ancient >200 km craters

METEORITE TYPES:
  CHONDRITES (80%): undifferentiated; primitive
    CC (carbonaceous): CI, CM, CR — most primitive; organics, water
    OC (ordinary): H, L, LL — most common; S-type asteroid material
    EC (enstatite): reduced; formed close to Sun

  ACHONDRITES (8%): from differentiated bodies
    HED: from Vesta (basaltic, gabbroic, ultramafic)
    SNC: from Mars (shergottites, nakhlites, chassignites)
    Lunar meteorites: from Moon (ejected by impacts)

  IRONS (5%): core material of differentiated asteroids
  STONY-IRONS (1%): pallasites (mantle-core boundary), mesosiderites
```

## The Oort Cloud

```
OORT CLOUD (HYPOTHETICAL)
==========================

  DISTANCE: ~2,000-200,000 AU (~1-4 light-years from Sun)
  STRUCTURE: Inner Oort Cloud (donut-shaped, 2,000-20,000 AU)
             Outer Oort Cloud (spherical, 20,000-200,000 AU)

  ESTIMATED POPULATION: 10¹¹-10¹³ comets > 1 km
  TOTAL MASS: ~1-5 M_Earth (very uncertain)

  EVIDENCE: Inferred from long-period comet orbital distribution
    Long-period comets have isotropic inclinations → spherical reservoir
    Aphelion distances cluster around 10,000-100,000 AU

  HOW IT FORMED:
    Planetesimals scattered outward by forming giant planets
    Jupiter: most efficient scatterer → inner Oort population
    Neptune: responsible for most Oort Cloud population
    Stellar encounters and galactic tides then circularized orbits

  PERTURBATION MECHANISMS (sending comets inward):
    Passing stars (most important at inner distances)
    Galactic tidal field (disk tide; most important at outer distances)
    Giant molecular cloud encounters (rare but catastrophic)
```

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What is the asteroid belt's total mass? | ~4% of the Moon's mass (~3×10²¹ kg); mostly empty space despite being "a belt" |
| Why is there a gap at 2.5 AU? | Jupiter 3:1 mean-motion resonance (Kirkwood gap); repeated perturbations scatter asteroids out |
| What are C-type asteroids made of? | Hydrated silicates + carbon-rich material; most primitive; likely sources of Earth's water via late accretion |
| What drives comet activity? | Sublimation of ices when nucleus warms inside ~3 AU; CO₂ can drive activity at larger distances |
| Why is the ion tail straight? | Solar wind blows directly away from the Sun (radially); ion tail aligns with the solar wind direction |
| How old are chondrites? | Chondrules date to 4.563-4.567 Ga — essentially the age of the solar system |
| What is the Tisserand parameter? | Orbital parameter T_J relative to Jupiter; T_J < 3 → Jupiter-family comet; T_J > 3 → asteroid (roughly) |
| Why is Pluto not a planet? | Doesn't clear its orbital neighborhood — the Kuiper Belt is full of similar objects; fails IAU 2006 criterion 3 |

## Common Confusion Points

**The asteroid belt is mostly empty**: If you flew through it, you'd statistically never hit anything. Total mass is ~4% of the Moon. The danger in science fiction is wildly exaggerated; Voyager, Pioneer, and all outer planets missions passed through without incident.

**Comets do not last forever**: Each pass near the Sun ablates ~1 m of surface. A typical comet nucleus is completely consumed after 100-1000 close passes. Short-period comets have geological lifetimes of ~tens of thousands of years.

**'Oumuamua is not understood**: The non-gravitational acceleration (without visible coma) is unexplained. Leading hypothesis: H₂ outgassing from amorphous ice (invisible). Alien artifact is not supported by evidence and is not the leading scientific hypothesis, despite media attention.

**Meteorites from Mars exist**: SNC meteorites (Shergottites, Nakhlites, Chassignites) are confirmed Martian meteorites — ejected by large impacts, traveled millions of years in space, fell to Earth. ALH84001 (Antarctic Martian meteorite) caused a biosignature controversy in 1996; features are now attributed to non-biological processes.

**Kuiper Belt vs Oort Cloud**: KBOs are in a disk (30-50 AU); the Oort Cloud is a sphere (2000-200,000 AU). Jupiter-family comets come from the Kuiper Belt/Scattered Disk; long-period comets come from the Oort Cloud.
