# Giant Planets and Ice Giants

## The Big Picture

The four outer planets dominate the solar system's mass and angular momentum. They formed beyond the frost line where solids were abundant, accreted massive atmospheres, and then reshuffled the entire solar system through gravitational migration.

## Gas Giants: Jupiter and Saturn

### Jupiter: Internal Structure

```
JUPITER INTERIOR
================

  +---------------------------+
  | Atmosphere                |  H₂, He, CH₄, NH₃, H₂O clouds
  | (top 1000 km)             |  Zone/belt structure; winds ±150 m/s
  +---------------------------+
  | Molecular H₂              |  ~10,000 km deep; liquid hydrogen
  | (hydrogen layer)          |  Transitions gradually
  +---------------------------+
  | Metallic Hydrogen         |  >1 Mbar pressure → H becomes conductor
  | (electron sea)            |  Depth: ~10,000-50,000 km
  +---------------------------+  Source of Jupiter's massive B field
  | Inner core               |  Rock + ice; ~10-20 M_Earth
  | (dilute or compact)       |  Core may be "dilute" — dissolved
  +---------------------------+  (Juno gravity data 2021)

  TOTAL RADIUS: 71,492 km
  CENTRAL PRESSURE: ~40-70 Mbar
  CENTRAL TEMPERATURE: ~35,000-40,000 K

  METALLIC HYDROGEN:
    Not a distinct phase transition (no sharp boundary in Jupiter conditions)
    Electrical conductivity increases continuously with depth
    "Metallic" = behaves like a metal (free electrons)
    This is the dynamo layer → generates Jupiter's magnetic field
```

### Jupiter's Atmosphere

```
CLOUD STRUCTURE (VERTICAL)
===========================

  Altitude  Temp    Composition        Feature
  --------  ----    -----------        -------
  0 km      165 K   NH₃ ice           White zone clouds (top)
  -50 km    213 K   NH₄SH             Brown belt clouds
  -130 km   273 K   H₂O ice / water   Water clouds (deep)
  -200 km   >300 K  Clear             Deep atmosphere

  BELT-ZONE STRUCTURE:
    Zones: bright, high, cold, upwelling (anticyclonic in S hemisphere)
    Belts: dark, low, warm, downwelling (cyclonic)
    Jets at boundaries: alternating east-west; ±150 m/s
    Source: differential rotation + latent heat + rotation

  GREAT RED SPOT:
    Persistent anticyclone; ~400 years old
    Currently: ~14,000 km wide (shrinking from ~40,000 km in 1800s)
    Wind speeds at perimeter: ~500 km/hr
    Color: unknown origin (photochemical products? sulfur? P?)
    Depth: extends ~200 km below cloud tops (MWR instrument, Juno)

  LIGHTNING:
    Detected by Galileo probe, Juno, Voyager
    Much more energetic than Earth lightning
    Associated with deep water clouds → moist convection
```

### Saturn: Structural Differences from Jupiter

```
SATURN vs JUPITER COMPARISON
==============================

                Jupiter            Saturn
  Mass          318 M⊕             95 M⊕
  Mean density  1.33 g/cm³         0.69 g/cm³  (LESS than water!)
  Oblateness    0.065              0.097  (more flattened)
  Heat excess   1.7× solar input   1.8× solar input
  He depletion  Slight             Strong (helium rain)

  HELIUM RAIN (Saturn-specific):
    Saturn is cool enough that helium condenses out of H/He mixture
    Helium droplets form at ~2-4 Mbar depth → rain down toward core
    Effect:
    (1) Atmosphere depleted in He relative to solar (confirmed)
    (2) Releases gravitational potential energy → explains Saturn's
        excess heat (still radiating energy from 4.6 Ga formation heat
        plus ongoing helium rain heating)

  SATURN'S LOW DENSITY:
    Saturn is 95× Earth's mass but only 0.69 g/cm³
    Diameter is 9× Earth's → mass spread over huge volume
    Mostly H/He by mass; no equivalent terrestrial solid core
```

## Ice Giants: Uranus and Neptune

### The "Ice Giant" Distinction

```
GAS GIANT vs ICE GIANT INTERIOR MODELS
========================================

  GAS GIANT (Jupiter/Saturn)     ICE GIANT (Uranus/Neptune)
  --------------------------     --------------------------
  +------------------+           +------------------+
  | H/He atmosphere  |           | H/He atmosphere  | thin
  +------------------+           +------------------+
  | Molecular H      |           | "Icy" mantle     | H₂O+CH₄+NH₃
  +------------------+           | (hot dense fluid)|
  | Metallic H       |           +------------------+
  +------------------+           | Rock/ice core    |
  | Rock core (small)|           +------------------+
  +------------------+

  "Ice" in "ice giant" refers to bulk composition (water, methane,
  ammonia ices) — not temperature state. The interior is a hot,
  supercritical, ionically conducting fluid.

  Ice giant mass:  ~15-17 M_Earth
  Gas giant mass:  ~95-318 M_Earth
  Key: Not enough mass to capture massive H/He envelope
```

### Uranus: The Tilted Planet

```
URANUS FACTS
============

  Obliquity:  97.77° — ROLLS on its side
              → poles face the Sun each 42-year "season"
              → extreme seasons: 42 years of continuous day/night at poles

  Why tipped? Giant impact likely tipped it during formation
              OR interaction with a large moon (now gone)

  Internal heat: NONE detected (Uranus emits ~0.97× solar input)
    vs Neptune: emits 2.6× solar input (internal heat source)
  WHY? Unknown — possibly trapped by a thin stable layer in the interior
       preventing convection (the mantle stratification hypothesis)
       Or: lost heat rapidly in an early impact

  MAGNETIC FIELD:
    Dipole tilted 59° from rotation axis (Earth: 11°)
    Dipole offset from center by 0.3 R_Uranus
    → Non-dipole field is very complex
    Cause: Dynamo in the ionic conduction layer (not metallic H)

  RINGS: 13 narrow dark rings (Uranian rings)
    First observed 1977 by Kuiper Airborne Observatory (stellar occultation)
    Dark: carbon-rich material; optical depth ~0.1-1
    Narrow: maintained by shepherd moons

  MOONS: 27 known; named after Shakespeare/Pope characters
    Major: Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, Oberon
    Miranda: bizarre geology (Verona Rupes cliff: 20 km high)
             Possibly disrupted and reassembled
```

### Neptune: The Windy Ice Giant

```
NEPTUNE FACTS
=============

  Most distant gas/ice giant: 30 AU, 165-year orbit
  Only "discovered" by math: Le Verrier predicted position from Uranus
  orbital perturbations; Galle found it in 1846

  WINDS: Strongest in solar system
    Great Dark Spot (Voyager 2, 1989): wind speeds ~2100 km/hr
    (since dissipated; Hubble shows new spots form and dissolve)
    Equatorial retrograde jets: wind direction OPPOSITE to rotation

  TRITON (largest moon):
    Retrograde orbit → not formed in situ; CAPTURED from Kuiper Belt
    Nitrogen geysers detected by Voyager 2
    Tidally decaying orbit: will cross Roche limit in ~3.6 Gyr → new ring system
    Surface: nitrogen ice + methane ice; "cantaloupe" terrain
    Temperature: ~38 K (one of coldest surfaces in solar system)

  INTERNAL HEAT:
    Emits 2.6× solar input; significant internal heat source
    Contrast to Uranus: puzzling; same formation region?
    Possible: Uranus has stratified interior that traps heat

  MAGNETIC FIELD:
    Dipole tilted 47° from rotation axis; offset 0.55 R_Neptune
    Similar to Uranus → both have dynamos in "superionic water" layer?
    "Superionic water": high-pressure phase where O forms lattice, H flows freely
```

## Major Moon Systems

```
GALILEAN MOONS (Jupiter)
=========================

  IO          Innermost; tidal heating → most volcanically active body
              ~400 active volcanoes; sulfur plains; no impact craters
              (surface renewal rate >> crater accumulation rate)
              Tidal flexing from Laplace resonance: Io:Europa:Ganymede = 1:2:4

  EUROPA      Ice shell overlying liquid water ocean (confirmed ~2020s)
              Tidal heating maintains liquid ocean beneath ~10-30 km ice
              Magnetic induction signal confirms conducting layer = saltwater
              E-CLIPPER mission (2024 launch, 2030 arrival) will probe this
              Highest-priority astrobiology target in solar system

  GANYMEDE    Largest moon in solar system (bigger than Mercury)
              Own magnetic field (unique among moons)
              Ice shell + subsurface ocean + rocky interior
              ESA JUICE mission targeting Ganymede (arrives 2034)

  CALLISTO    Heavily cratered; no evidence of internal activity
              Subsurface ocean likely (magnetic induction)
              Outermost; least tidal heating; geologically dead surface

  LAPLACE RESONANCE: Io:Europa:Ganymede = 1:2:4 orbital periods
    → Maintained by tidal dissipation + exchange of angular momentum
    → Keeps Io's orbit eccentric → drives tidal heating → Io volcanism
    → Europa's eccentric orbit → tidal heating → liquid ocean
```

```

=====================================

  SPECIES     Jupiter   Saturn    Uranus    Neptune

  H₂          89.8%     96.3%     82.5%     80%
  He          10.2%     3.25%     15.2%     19%
  CH₄         0.3%      0.45%     2.3%      1.5%

  H₂O         ~1000 ppm (deep)    (deep)    (deep)
  Heavy elem.  2-4× sol  5-6× sol 25-50× sol 25-50× sol

  KEY: Uranus/Neptune are MORE enriched in heavy elements
       → consistent with higher rock/ice core mass fraction
       → methane blue color: CH₄ absorbs red → reflects blue/green

  "METHANE BLUE":
    Uranus: pale cyan-blue
    Neptune: deeper blue (more CH₄ + different haze)
    Saturn: pale yellow (phosphine + NH₃ + photochemical haze)
    Jupiter: complex bands (multiple chromophores; not just NH₃)
```

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What is metallic hydrogen? | At pressures >1 Mbar, hydrogen becomes electrically conducting (electrons delocalized); it's the dynamo source in Jupiter/Saturn |
| Why does Saturn have lower density than water? | Saturn's 95 M_Earth is spread over a volume 9× Earth's radius; mostly hydrogen, which is very low density even under compression |
| Why does Uranus have no internal heat? | Unknown — possibly suppressed by a stably stratified interior layer preventing heat from escaping; the ice giant formation event may have cleared heat faster |
| What drives Io's volcanism? | Tidal heating from the 1:2:4 Laplace resonance with Europa and Ganymede; the eccentric orbit forces repeated tidal flexing → frictional heat |
| Why is Triton captured? | Retrograde orbit is impossible for an in-situ-formed moon; it was captured from the Kuiper Belt (similar composition to Pluto) |
| Why are ring particles in Saturn so white? | Very young surface (cosmically speaking); ice not yet darkened by meteoroid contamination; rings are estimated <100 Myr old |
| How was Neptune discovered? | By Newtonian prediction — Le Verrier (and Adams) noticed Uranus's orbit deviated from predictions → postulated a perturbing planet → found in 1846 |
| What is the Roche limit? | Distance inside which tidal forces exceed a body's self-gravity; moons inside the Roche limit are torn apart → form rings |

## Common Confusion Points

**Ice giants are not cold ice**: The term "ice giant" describes composition (H₂O/CH₄/NH₃ ices in bulk), not temperature. The interior of Neptune is ~7000 K and at GPa pressures — a hot, supercritical fluid.

**Jupiter's Great Red Spot is not a storm in the Earth sense**: It's a persistent anticyclonic high-pressure system 1.3× Earth's diameter. It has been shrinking for centuries; it may eventually disappear. The red color source is not definitively identified.

**Saturn's rings are not permanent**: They're likely <100-200 Myr old — Cassini data suggests they're very young relative to the 4.6 Ga solar system age. They're also being actively lost (ring rain into Saturn's atmosphere at ~10,000 kg/s).

**Ganymede vs Mercury**: Ganymede is larger than Mercury in radius (2631 km vs 2440 km) but has much lower mass/density because it's half ice. Mercury is denser rock+metal.

**Neptune's magnetic tilt is not a coincidence with Uranus's**: Both have highly tilted, offset magnetic dipoles — but the physical cause is thought to be the dynamo geometry in their ionic/superionic mantles, not their rotation axis tilts (which are very different: Uranus 97°, Neptune 28°).
