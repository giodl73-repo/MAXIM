# 01 — Engineering Statics

## Equilibrium, Free Bodies, Trusses, Frames, Distributed Loads

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Fundamental concepts: scalar vs vector, SI/US unit systems, dimensional homogeneity
- Vector operations: addition (parallelogram law), dot product, cross product, moment as r × F
- Equilibrium in 2D: ΣFx = 0, ΣFy = 0, ΣM = 0 — three independent equations
- Equilibrium in 3D: six equilibrium equations, right-hand rule conventions
- Free body diagrams: isolating the body, modeling support conditions (pin, roller, fixed, cable)
- Support reactions: 2D reaction types and unknowns; 3D ball-and-socket, journal bearing
- Determinacy and stability: comparing equations vs unknowns for beams, trusses, frames
- Moment of a force: scalar (d × F) and vector (r × F) formulations, moment about an axis
- Couples: pure moments, resultant of a couple
- Distributed forces: resultant force and centroid for rectangles, triangles, parabolas
- Center of gravity / centroid: composite shapes, method of integration
- Area moment of inertia (second moment of area): Ix, Iy, polar J; parallel axis theorem
- Truss analysis — method of joints: joint equilibrium, tension/compression sign convention
- Truss analysis — method of sections: Ritter's method, cutting up to three members
- Space trusses: 3D joint method, member forces from 3D equilibrium
- Frames: multi-force members, disassembly into free bodies, pin forces
- Machines: mechanical advantage, efficiency, linkages
- Friction: Coulomb friction model (F_f ≤ μN), angle of friction, wedges, screws, bearings
- Belt friction: T₂/T₁ = e^(μβ) for flat belts and V-belts
- Virtual work principle: virtual displacements, principle of virtual work for equilibrium
