# 04 — Geotechnical Engineering

## Soil Mechanics, Foundations, Slopes, Earth Pressure

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Soil composition: minerals, grain size distribution, Atterberg limits (LL, PL, PI)
- Soil classification: USCS (SM, SW, CL, CH, etc.), AASHTO classification
- Phase relationships: void ratio e, porosity n, degree of saturation, unit weights
- Permeability: Darcy's law, hydraulic conductivity K, Hazen empirical formula
- Seepage: flow nets, equipotential lines, uplift pressure, piping and internal erosion
- Effective stress: Terzaghi's principle σ' = σ − u, pore water pressure
- Compressibility: consolidation theory (1D Terzaghi), settlement time rate, Cv
- Primary vs secondary consolidation: compression index Cc, preconsolidation pressure
- Stress distribution in soil: Boussinesq point load, Westergaard, stress contours
- Shear strength: Mohr-Coulomb failure criterion τ_f = c + σ' tan φ
- Laboratory tests: direct shear, triaxial (UU, CU, CD), unconfined compression
- Field tests: SPT (N-value), CPT (tip resistance q_c, sleeve friction), vane shear
- Shallow foundations: bearing capacity (Terzaghi, Meyerhof, Hansen), shape/depth/inclination factors
- Settlement analysis: immediate elastic settlement, consolidation settlement, differential settlement
- Deep foundations: driven piles, bored piles, pile capacity (static analysis, dynamic formulas)
- Retaining walls: gravity walls, cantilever walls, sheet pile walls; active/passive/at-rest pressures
- Rankine and Coulomb earth pressure theories; wedge analysis
- Slope stability: limit equilibrium methods (Swedish circle, Bishop's simplified), factor of safety
- Reinforced earth: geosynthetics, mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) walls
- Seismic considerations: liquefaction susceptibility, Seed-Idriss simplified method, NACG
