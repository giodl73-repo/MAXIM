# 02 — Mechanics of Materials

## Stress, Strain, Bending, Shear, Torsion, Deflection

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Stress: normal stress σ = P/A, shear stress τ = V/A, bearing stress σ_b = P/A_b
- Strain: normal ε = δ/L, shear γ, volumetric strain e = ΔV/V
- Hooke's law: σ = Eε, τ = Gγ, bulk modulus K
- Poisson's ratio: ν = −ε_lat/ε_axial, relation G = E/[2(1+ν)]
- Axial loading: deformation δ = PL/AE, statically indeterminate axial members
- Thermal stresses: δ_T = αΔTL, thermal stress when constrained
- Torsion of circular shafts: τ = Tr/J, angle of twist φ = TL/GJ, polar moment J
- Torsion of non-circular sections: thin-walled tubes (Bredt), solid rectangular cross sections
- Beam bending: sign conventions, shear and moment diagrams from load functions
- Flexure formula: σ = −My/I, neutral axis, section modulus S = I/c
- Shear stress in beams: τ = VQ/(Ib), shear flow q = VQ/I
- Built-up beams: fastener spacing from shear flow
- Beam deflection: differential equation EIy'' = M(x), direct integration
- Macaulay bracket notation for discontinuous loads
- Moment area method: first and second moment-area theorems
- Superposition: combining standard cases (tables), beam deflection formulae
- Stress transformation: plane stress, Mohr's circle for 2D, principal stresses and planes
- Strain transformation: strain Mohr's circle, principal strains, strain rosettes
- Failure theories: maximum normal stress (Rankine), Tresca, von Mises — with worked examples
- Column buckling: Euler critical load P_cr = π²EI/(kL)², slenderness ratio, effective length
- Inelastic bending: elastic-perfectly plastic, plastic neutral axis, shape factor, plastic moment
- Curved beams: correction factor for non-straight members (Winkler-Bach theory)
- Thick-walled cylinders: Lamé equations for hoop and radial stress, shrink fits
