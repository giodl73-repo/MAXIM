# 06 — Hallmarks & Standards

## Karat, Fineness, Assay Offices — The Consumer Protection System

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **What hallmarking is**: a system of official marks stamped onto precious metal items to indicate metal content and verify its accuracy; consumer protection against adulteration; one of the oldest consumer protection systems in the world — English hallmarking dates to 1300 (Statute of Edward I requiring gold/silver to be assayed and marked); system predates modern consumer protection law by 700 years
- **UK hallmarking** (one of the most comprehensive systems): legally mandatory for items above minimum weight sold as precious metal; four required marks: (1) maker's mark (sponsor's mark — registered symbol of manufacturer/assayer submitter); (2) standard mark (purity symbol); (3) assay office mark (which office tested — London: leopard's head; Edinburgh: castle; Birmingham: anchor; Sheffield: York rose); (4) date letter (year of hallmarking — unique letter style per cycle per office); optional: traditional fineness marks (lion passant = sterling silver, crown = 18K gold historically)
  - UK standard marks: gold — 999/990/916 (22K)/750 (18K)/585 (14K)/375 (9K); silver — 999/958 (Britannia)/925 (sterling)/800; platinum — 999/950/900/850; palladium — 999/950/500
- **US marking**: no mandatory hallmarking system (voluntary); FTC regulations require that any quality mark must be accurate to within 0.5 karat for gold; minimum legal gold = 10K (417); stamps: "14K," "18K," "925," "Sterling," "Plat," "Pt950," etc.; maker's trademark should accompany purity mark; no independent verification required (self-declaration); this creates more risk of misrepresentation than European systems
- **Italian hallmarking**: mandatory; Italian gold mostly 18K (750); fineness shown as three-digit number in geometric frame; provincial marks (each Italian province had its own assay office and mark historically); still common to see "750" in hexagonal frame
- **French hallmarking**: owl mark = imported goods (assayed after import); eagle head = 18K gold guaranteed by Paris assay; dolphin = gold guaranteed in some provinces; mercury head = silver
- **Convention on the Control and Marking of Articles of Precious Metals (Vienna Convention, 1972)**: international hallmarking mutual recognition agreement; currently 22 member states including UK, most of Europe, not US or China; the "common control mark" (CCM) = stylized balance scale in pentagon shape; member states' marks accepted by all other member states
- **GIA grading reports**: not hallmarks but the de facto gemstone certification standard; GIA Diamond Grading Report: 4Cs grades, plot of inclusion locations, proportions (table %, depth %, crown angle, pavilion angle), fluorescence, comment on treatments; report number laser-inscribed on girdle (microscope readable); GIA Colored Stone Report: color description, weight, shape/cut, measurements, treatment disclosure; GIA origin report (for high-value rubies/sapphires/emeralds — distinguishes geographic origin)
- **Kimberley Process Certification Scheme (KPCS)**: international certification for rough diamonds to prevent trade in conflict diamonds (diamonds used to fund rebel movements against legitimate governments — Sierra Leone, Angola, DRC, Côte d'Ivoire civil wars); established 2003; requires government certificates for rough diamond shipments; criticism: limited enforcement, does not cover diamonds funding government atrocities (Zimbabwe Marange diamonds controversy), not applicable to lab-grown diamonds; participation by 85 countries representing 99.8% of global diamond production
- **Responsible jewelry practices**: Responsible Jewellery Council (RJC) certification for members of jewelry supply chain (mining through retail) — covers human rights, labor, environment, business ethics; Fairtrade gold (licensed Fairtrade mines in Africa/South America — certified premiums paid to miners); Fairmined gold (independent certification for artisanal/small-scale miners); "recycled gold" (metal from refiner → no new mining but provenance of original mining unknown)
- **Diamond quality marks by sellers**: "ideal cut" (GIA Excellent + AGS 0 in combination — not a single standard); "hearts and arrows" (precision-cut round brilliants showing 8 hearts and 8 arrows in special viewer — indicates high optical precision); "triple excellent" (GIA Excellent cut + Excellent polish + Excellent symmetry); these are marketing terms, not independent standards
