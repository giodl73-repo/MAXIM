## Materials Hierarchy

```
JEWELRY MATERIALS
=================

METALS
├── Precious metals
│   ├── Gold (Au) — 24k pure, alloyed 18k/14k/9k for hardness
│   ├── Silver (Ag) — fine 999, jewelry standard 925 sterling
│   ├── Platinum (Pt) — Pt950/Pt900; densest common metal
│   └── Palladium (Pd) — lighter PGM; white gold alloys
├── Base metals — brass, bronze, copper (fashion/costume jewelry)
└── Specialty — titanium, niobium, stainless (modern/hypoallergenic)

GEMSTONES
├── Natural — mineral or organic origin
│   ├── Native elements  — Diamond (C, cubic, sp³ bonds)
│   ├── Oxides           — Corundum (Al₂O₃): ruby, sapphire
│   ├── Silicates        — Beryl (emerald), tourmaline, garnet
│   ├── Other minerals   — Turquoise, malachite, lapis lazuli
│   └── Organic          — Pearl, amber, coral, jet
└── Synthetic/Simulant
    ├── Synthetics — same chemistry as natural (lab diamond, synthetic ruby)
    └── Simulants  — different chemistry (CZ ≠ diamond, moissanite = SiC)
```

## Key Physical Properties Driving Design

```
HARDNESS (Mohs)          DENSITY              REFRACTIVE INDEX
================         =======              ================
Diamond    10.0          3.52 g/cm³           2.417  ← brilliance
Corundum    9.0          4.00 g/cm³           1.762–1.770
Topaz       8.0          3.53 g/cm³           1.619–1.627
Quartz      7.0          2.65 g/cm³           1.544       ← airborne dust
Feldspar    6.0                               ~1.52
Opal        5.5–6.5                           1.37–1.47   ← fragile
Pearl       2.5–4.5      2.60–2.85            1.53–1.69

METALS (much softer than gemstones)
 Fine gold:    ~2.5 Mohs     Fine silver: ~2.5 Mohs
 Platinum:     ~4–4.5 Mohs

Key insight: minimum Mohs 7 for rings (airborne quartz dust).
Opal, pearl, coral require protective settings or reserved
for pendants/earrings (lower abrasion exposure).
```

## Purity Systems

```
GOLD
====
24k = 999/1000 = pure (too soft — gold leaf, investment)
18k = 750/1000 = 75% Au  (EU/Asia fine jewelry standard)
14k = 585/1000 = 58.5% Au (US dominant)
10k = 417/1000 = 41.7% Au (US legal minimum for "gold")
 9k = 375/1000 = 37.5% Au (UK/Australia entry-level)

Color via alloy:
 Yellow: Cu + Ag    White: Pd or Ni (+ Rh plate)
 Rose:   high Cu    Green: high Ag (electrum)

SILVER
======
Fine:      999    Sterling: 925 (92.5% Ag + 7.5% Cu)
Britannia: 958    Argentium: 935 (Ge addition, tarnish-resistant)

PLATINUM
========
Pt950: 95% + Ru/Co/Ir   Pt900: 90%   Pt850: 85%
All marked in millesimal fineness (parts per thousand)
```

## Common Confusion Points

**Karat vs Carat**: Two completely different measurements sharing etymology.
- Karat (k) = gold purity (18k = 18/24 parts gold)
- Carat (ct) = gemstone weight (1 ct = 0.2 grams)

**Gold-filled vs gold-plated vs solid gold**:
- Solid gold: gold alloy all the way through
- Gold-filled: 1/20 by weight gold, mechanically bonded (durable, 5–20 µm)
- Gold-plated: electroplated 0.5–2.5 µm (wears off in months/years)
- Vermeil: ≥2.5 µm gold plate over sterling silver base

**Natural vs Synthetic vs Simulant**:
- Natural ruby and synthetic ruby: same Al₂O₃+Cr³⁺ composition — different origin
- CZ simulant: ZrO₂ — different composition entirely, looks like diamond

**Hallmark vs Stamp**:
- Hallmark: independently verified mark from assay office
- Maker's stamp: self-applied, not independently verified

## Decision Cheat Sheet

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Which gold purity for daily-wear ring? | 14k (US) or 18k (international); 18k preferred fine jewelry |
| Hardest natural gem? | Diamond (10 Mohs) |
| Minimum hardness for ring wear? | 7 Mohs (quartz dust in air) |
| Best tarnish-resistant silver? | Argentium 935 or rhodium-plated sterling |
| Platinum vs white gold? | Platinum: heavier, durable, no re-plating; WG: lighter, cheaper |
| Lab diamond vs natural? | Chemically identical; lab ~80% cheaper; near-zero resale for lab |
| Hardest non-diamond gem? | Corundum (ruby/sapphire) at 9 Mohs |
| What is a hallmark? | Legal purity mark from independent assay office |
| How are diamonds graded? | GIA 4Cs: Color, Clarity, Cut, Carat weight |
