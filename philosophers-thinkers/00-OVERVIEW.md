# Philosophers & Thinkers — Overview

## The Big Picture

Philosophy is not one discipline — it is several that share a method: rigorous argument about fundamental questions. The fields split roughly into:

```
PHILOSOPHY
├── METAPHYSICS ─── what exists? (ontology, substance, causation, time)
├── EPISTEMOLOGY ── how do we know? (justification, skepticism, truth)
├── LOGIC ──────── what follows from what? (validity, inference, formal systems)
├── ETHICS ─────── what should we do? (normative theory, meta-ethics)
├── POLITICAL ───── how should societies be organized?
├── PHILOSOPHY OF MIND ── what is consciousness? (qualia, intentionality)
├── PHILOSOPHY OF LANGUAGE ── how does language mean? (reference, truth)
└── PHILOSOPHY OF SCIENCE ── what makes knowledge scientific?
```

Every major philosopher touches several simultaneously. The history of philosophy is not a list of answers — it is a conversation where each generation responds to the previous generation's problems.

## Why This Matters to Someone with MIT TCS Background

Philosophy and theoretical computer science share more machinery than most people recognize:

| CS Concept | Philosophical Origin |
|------------|---------------------|
| Formal logic / proof systems | Frege's Begriffsschrift (1879) — first predicate logic |
| Type theory | Russell's ramified theory of types (response to his own paradox) |
| The halting problem / undecidability | Hilbert's Entscheidungsproblem — the question Church/Turing answered |
| Lambda calculus | Church — grew directly from philosophical logic tradition |
| Modal logic / possible worlds | Kripke — now standard in program verification, epistemic logic |
| Formal semantics | Frege's sense/reference → Tarski's truth theory → model theory |
| Gödel incompleteness | Response to Russell/Hilbert's logicist program |
| Computability as epistemology | Turing's machines as models of effective procedure |
| Hard problem of consciousness | Chalmers — directly engaged in AI consciousness debates |

The analytic tradition (File 08) is the most directly relevant. Frege invented quantifier logic; Russell tried to reduce mathematics to logic (the project that birthed type theory); Wittgenstein's Tractatus is a formal theory of representation; Quine's critique of analyticity echoes debates about specification and semantics.

| **Analytic / synthetic** | True by meaning alone / true by fact about world | Kant; contested by Quine |
| **Substance** | What exists independently; substrate of properties | Aristotle, Descartes, Spinoza |
| **Form (eidos)** | Abstract essence; what makes a thing what it is | Plato, Aristotle |
| **Dialectic** | Reasoning through contradiction to higher synthesis | Plato (dialogue), Hegel |
| **Categorical imperative** | Act only on maxims you could will to be universal law | Kant |
| **Will to power** | Not domination but self-overcoming, creative drive | Nietzsche |
| **Intentionality** | Consciousness is always directed at / about something | Husserl, Brentano |
| **Being-in-the-world** | Existence is embedded, not a detached observation | Heidegger |
| **Language games** | Meaning is use in a practice, not reference to objects | Wittgenstein (PI) |
| **Veil of ignorance** | Design justice principles without knowing your social position | Rawls |
| **Sense / reference** | Two aspects of meaning: descriptive content vs. what is named | Frege |

## Common Confusion Points

**"Philosophy is just opinions"** — Philosophy makes arguments: premises, valid inferences, conclusions. Bad philosophy is bad argument; good philosophy is rigorous argument about questions that resist empirical resolution.

**Rationalism vs. Empiricism** — The standard framing (reason alone vs. experience alone) is too clean. Descartes did controlled experiments; Hume used careful reasoning. The split is really about what provides certainty: innate rational intuitions or sensory experience?

**Plato vs. Aristotle as permanent polarity** — Raphael's "School of Athens" (Plato pointing up, Aristotle gesturing down) is a useful shorthand but misleads. Aristotle studied with Plato for twenty years; his logic and metaphysics are deeply shaped by Platonic problems. The split is in method and emphasis, not opposition.

**"Kant is impossible"** — The first Critique has a clear architecture: transcendental aesthetic (space/time as forms of intuition) → transcendental analytic (categories of understanding) → transcendental dialectic (where reason overreaches into antinomies). The difficulty is terminological. The argument is: we bring structure to experience; experience doesn't just print itself on a passive mind.

**Hegel vs. Marx** — Marx's historical materialism explicitly inverts Hegel: Hegel said history is the development of Geist (Mind/Spirit through contradiction); Marx said it's the development of material productive forces and class conflict. Marx called this "standing Hegel on his head."

**Wittgenstein early vs. late** — TLP (1921) and PI (1953) are near-opposite positions. TLP: language pictures the world via logical form; the limits of language are the limits of the world; what can't be said must be passed over in silence. PI: meaning is use in practices (language games); there is no single underlying logical form; philosophy's job is therapeutic, not constructive. Wittgenstein publicly repudiated TLP.

**Analytic vs. Continental split** — This is a 20th-century institutional division, not a deep philosophical divide. Wittgenstein is claimed by both traditions. Rawls is analytic in method but engages deeply with political theory that has continental roots. The split tracks academic culture (journals, hiring, methods) more than philosophical substance.
