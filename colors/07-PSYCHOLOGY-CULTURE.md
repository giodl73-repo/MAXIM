# 07 — Color Psychology & Culture

## Symbolism, Marketing, Synesthesia, Cross-Cultural Meaning

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Cross-cultural color symbolism — divergence and convergence:
  - **White**: purity/weddings in Western tradition; mourning/death in China, Japan, India, Korea; ambiguity (white as blank slate vs absence); hospital/clinical associations
  - **Red**: danger/stop in West (fire, blood); luck/celebration in China (red envelopes, red wedding dress); socialism/revolution; passion/love; warning in nature (aposematism — poison dart frogs, ladybugs)
  - **Black**: mourning/death in West; formality/elegance; evil/darkness; power suits; Japan — black for celebration in some contexts; most widespread negative connotations but context-dependent
  - **Blue**: trustworthiness/reliability in West (why Facebook/LinkedIn/Twitter chose blue); sadness ("feeling blue"); masculinity (historically gendered: pink for boys/blue for girls in US until ~1940, then reversed); in ancient Egypt and Islamic tradition — protection; blue doors in Morocco
  - **Yellow**: happiness/optimism vs cowardice ("yellow"); caution/warning (traffic signs); Asian royalty (imperial yellow in China); Hindu sacred color
  - **Green**: nature/environment/eco; Islam (green in the Prophet's banners); money (US dollars, envy); nausea ("looking green"); Ireland; many cultures associate with growth
  - **Purple**: royalty/power (from Tyrian purple scarcity); religious (Lent); creativity; LGBTQ+ spectrum
- **Color in marketing**: blue = trustworthy (financial institutions); red = urgency/appetite (fast food — McDonald's, KFC, Wendy's red); green = natural/healthy; orange = energy/affordability; purple = premium/luxury; yellow/orange = call to action; black = luxury/exclusivity — Keller's brand color research; A/B testing of button colors; note: correlation vs causation problems in color-marketing research (brands succeed for many reasons, color a small factor)
- **Color in wayfinding and safety**: OSHA safety color standards (red = fire equipment/danger, orange = dangerous parts/warning, yellow = caution/hazardous, green = safety equipment/first aid, blue = information/PPE required); hospital color-coded wristbands (red = allergy, yellow = fall risk, purple = DNR — NOT standardized nationally → patient safety issue); airport/transit wayfinding
- **Synesthesia**: automatic, involuntary sensory cross-activation; grapheme-color synesthesia (letters/numbers have specific colors — consistent for individual, varies between synesthetes); ~4% of population; letters typically vowels more colored than consonants; higher prevalence in artists (Wassily Kandinsky, David Hockney, Pharrell Williams); MRI evidence of cross-activation between grapheme and color areas
- **Baker-Miller pink** (Drunk Tank Pink): specific shade of pink (Pepto-Bismol pink, Pantone 507) claimed by US Naval researchers (1970s) to reduce aggression; used in prison holding cells; subsequent research failed to replicate; widely cited as example of poorly conducted color psychology research; symbolic of oversimplified color-behavior claims
- **Ecological valence theory** (Palmer & Schloss): people like colors associated with liked objects; blue = liked (clear sky, clean water → liked); brown = disliked (feces, decay → disliked); explains universal preference for blue and dislike of olive/dark yellow better than other theories
- **Color and temperature perception**: rooms painted blue/green feel 3-4°F cooler than rooms painted red/orange at same temperature; not just perception — affects comfort ratings; warm colors advance (appear closer), cool colors recede (appear farther) — Albers-influenced design principle
