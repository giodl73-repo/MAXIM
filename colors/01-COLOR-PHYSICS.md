# 01 — Color Physics

## Light, Wavelength, and Why Objects Have Color

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Light as electromagnetic radiation: photon energy E = hc/λ; visible range 380–700nm; color as wavelength of reflected/transmitted/emitted light; the spectrum as continuum (not discrete bands — the named colors are human conventions)
- Why objects have color — four mechanisms:
  1. **Selective absorption/reflection**: chromophores in material absorb specific wavelengths; complementary wavelengths reflected; red tomato absorbs blue/green, reflects red; dominant mechanism for pigments and dyes
  2. **Structural color (interference)**: thin-film interference (soap bubbles, oil slicks, beetle elytra — wavelength-dependent constructive/destructive interference); photonic crystal arrays (opal, morpho butterfly wings — periodic nanostructure scatters specific wavelengths); diffraction (CD surface, hummingbird feathers); no absorption — color from geometry
  3. **Fluorescence/phosphorescence**: absorb UV, re-emit visible; "optical brighteners" in laundry detergent absorb UV → emit blue → whiter appearance; more under dedicated nature file
  4. **Emission**: incandescent (blackbody radiation — temperature determines color, ~2700K warm white, ~6500K daylight); LEDs (semiconductor electron-hole recombination at specific energy = specific wavelength)
- Spectral power distribution (SPD): the physics of a light source described as power vs wavelength; objects described by spectral reflectance curve (0–100% at each wavelength); perceived color = SPD × reflectance × observer response functions
- Blackbody radiation and color temperature: Planck's law; stars and tungsten filaments as approximate blackbodies; correlated color temperature (CCT) for non-blackbody sources; warm (2700-3000K) vs cool (5000-6500K) light; counterintuitive naming (higher Kelvin = "cooler" looking = bluer)
- Rayleigh scattering: λ⁻⁴ scattering (small particles scatter shorter wavelengths more); why sky is blue (nitrogen/oxygen scatter blue more than red); why sunsets are red (long path through atmosphere, blue scattered away); why clouds are white (water droplets large → Mie scattering, wavelength-independent)
- Transmission: colored glass and filters; Beer-Lambert law (absorbance proportional to concentration × path length); stained glass = colorant ions in silica matrix; colored solutions
