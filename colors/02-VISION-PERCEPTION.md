# 02 — Vision & Perception

## Retinal Cones, Opponent Process, Metamerism, Simultaneous Contrast

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Retinal photoreceptors: rods (~120M, scotopic/low-light vision, no color, peak sensitivity ~498nm) vs cones (~6M, photopic/color vision); three cone types: L (long, peak ~564nm, "red"), M (medium, peak ~534nm, "green"), S (short, peak ~420nm, "blue"); massive overlap between L and M (why red-green discrimination is hardest for color blindness)
- Trichromacy (Young-Helmholtz): any color can be matched by three primary lights; basis of all color reproduction systems (RGB screens, CMYK print); the LMS cone responses are the foundation of CIE colorimetry
- Opponent process theory (Hering): after cones, visual system encodes color in three opponent channels: L-M (red vs green), (L+M)-S (yellow vs blue), L+M+S (light vs dark); explains: why reddish-green and yellowish-blue don't exist as colors; why complementary afterimages occur; why certain color pairs "vibrate" (simultaneous contrast)
- **Metamerism**: two objects that have different spectral reflectance curves but appear identical under one light source, different under another; critical for paint/textile/print matching; industrial problem — color match under fluorescent in factory doesn't hold under incandescent in customer's home; metameric index quantifies degree
- **Simultaneous contrast** (Chevreul): same gray appears lighter on dark background, darker on light background; simultaneous color contrast — gray on red background appears greenish, gray on green appears reddish; opponent channel mechanism; Albers' *Interaction of Color* (1963) — systematic exploration; used and abused in design
- **Color constancy**: brain compensates for light source color so that objects appear same color despite different illumination (white paper looks white in both tungsten and daylight); mechanism: ratio comparison across image, context clues; "The Dress" (blue/black vs white/gold internet debate 2015) = color constancy failure under ambiguous illumination prior
- **Color blindness**: red-green (8% of males, 0.5% of females — X-linked; dichromacy: protanopia/deuteranopia, or anomalous trichromacy: protanomaly/deuteranomaly), blue-yellow (tritanopia, rare, autosomal), achromatopsia (rod monochromacy, very rare); Ishihara test; design implications (colorblind-safe palettes — never use red/green alone for information)
- **Tetrachromacy**: ~12% of women may have 4 cone types (anomalous trichromacy in men can produce tetrachromat daughters); ability to distinguish colors that appear identical to trichromats; poorly understood; rarely tested
- Lightness perception: Weber's law (just-noticeable difference proportional to magnitude); gamma correction in displays (human perception non-linear — we're more sensitive to dark differences); perceptual lightness (L* in CIE Lab) vs physical luminance
