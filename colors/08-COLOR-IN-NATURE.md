# 08 — Color in Nature

## Structural Color, Animal Pigments, Bioluminescence, Camouflage

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Pigment-based color in animals** — chromophores:
  - Melanins (eumelanin — black/brown; phaeomelanin — red/yellow): most widespread animal pigment; hair/feathers/skin; made from tyrosine; UV-absorbing (photoprotection); same pigment as human skin color; melanin in squid ink
  - Carotenoids: red/orange/yellow; cannot be synthesized by animals — must be obtained from diet (plants/fungi/bacteria); flamingo pink from carotenoids in algae/crustaceans eaten; salmon pink/orange (astaxanthin from krill); autumn leaf colors (unmasking of carotenoids as chlorophyll breaks down); canary yellow (diet-dependent)
  - Porphyrins: ring pigments; hemoglobin (red, iron-containing porphyrin), chlorophyll (green, magnesium porphyrin), biliverdin (green — eggshell color of many birds), bilirubin (yellow/orange — jaundice); turacin (red porphyrin unique to turacos) water-soluble — birds' red actually washes off in rain
  - Pterins: yellow/white/orange in butterfly wings (xanthopterin, leucopterin); separate from melanins and carotenoids
- **Structural color** (physical mechanisms, no pigment):
  - **Thin-film interference**: soap bubbles, oil slicks, beetle elytra (*Chrysochroa fulgidissima* — Japanese jewel beetle); iridescent variation with angle (same wavelength not reflected at all angles); mechanism: partial reflection at each interface → constructive interference for wavelengths where 2nd reflection is in phase
  - **Photonic crystals**: periodic nanostructure (~100-500nm spacing, comparable to visible light wavelengths); morpho butterfly wings (multilayer lamellae → intense iridescent blue); opal (random close-packed silica spheres → play of color); hummingbird feathers (melanin nanorods in hexagonal arrays)
  - **Quasi-ordered nanostructures**: blue bird feathers (Eastern bluebird, kingfisher) — NOT thin-film; sponge-like nanostructure scatters blue coherently; incoherent vs coherent scattering
  - **Diffraction**: CD/DVD surfaces; beetle *Serica sericea* — diffraction from scale microstructure
- **Bioluminescence**: light production by living organisms via oxidation of luciferin (substrate) by luciferase (enzyme); ~80% of deep-sea species; fireflies (flash pattern species-specific, mate signaling); Vibrio fischeri (squid light organ, quorum sensing); Noctiluca (sea sparkle — red tide); deep sea uses: counter-illumination camouflage, lure (anglerfish), communication; GFP (green fluorescent protein from jellyfish — Shimomura/Chalfie/Tsien Nobel 2008) revolutionized molecular biology
- **Camouflage**: background matching (octopus chromatophore control — millisecond color change via muscle-controlled pigment cells); disruptive coloration (zebra stripes — disputed: anti-predator, thermoregulation, or tsetse fly deterrence); countershading (dark back, light belly — Thayer's principle, 1896); crypsis vs mimicry; Batesian mimicry (palatable species mimics unpalatable); Müllerian mimicry (two unpalatable species converge on same warning pattern — reinforced learning for predators)
- **Autumn leaf colors**: green from chlorophyll (masked other pigments); in autumn, chlorophyll breaks down revealing pre-existing carotenoids (yellow/orange); red anthocyanins actively synthesized in autumn (why? photoprotection during nutrient resorption, honest signal to aphids, anti-freeze mechanism — debated); maple reds require glucose (bright autumns in years with warm days + cold nights)
