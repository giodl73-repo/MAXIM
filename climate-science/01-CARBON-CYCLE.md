# 01 — The Carbon Cycle

## Reservoirs, Fluxes, Ocean Chemistry, Anthropogenic Perturbation

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Carbon reservoirs: atmosphere (~870 GtC), terrestrial biosphere (~2,600 GtC vegetation+soil), ocean (~38,000 GtC), lithosphere/fossil fuels (~5,000 GtC accessible); residence times in each
- Natural fluxes: photosynthesis/respiration (~120 GtC/yr terrestrial exchange), ocean-atmosphere gas exchange (~80 GtC/yr), volcanic outgassing (~0.1 GtC/yr), weathering (geological timescale)
- Anthropogenic perturbation: current emissions ~10 GtC/yr fossil fuels + ~1.5 GtC/yr land use change; ~50% stays in atmosphere annually, ~25% land sink, ~25% ocean sink; airborne fraction
- Ocean carbon chemistry: CO₂(aq) + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻ ⇌ H⁺ + CO₃²⁻; carbonate system alkalinity and DIC (Dissolved Inorganic Carbon); Revelle factor (buffer capacity); ocean acidification (pH drop ~0.1 since industrial revolution)
- Biological pump: photosynthesis in photic zone → export flux of organic carbon → remineralization at depth; efficiency (f-ratio); deep ocean carbon sequestration; iron fertilization hypothesis
- Terrestrial carbon sinks: CO₂ fertilization (diminishing returns), vegetation greening, permafrost carbon (frozen organic matter ~1,500 GtC — largest terrestrial carbon pool after deep soils)
- Methane cycle: wetlands (largest natural source), agriculture (rice/cattle/manure), fossil fuel leakage, permafrost (thermokarst lakes), atmospheric lifetime ~9 years (CO₂ ~centuries); GWP-100 = 28, GWP-20 = 80
- Nitrous oxide: agriculture (N-fertilizer nitrification/denitrification), atmospheric lifetime ~114 years, GWP-100 = 265; third most important anthropogenic greenhouse gas
- Carbon feedbacks: permafrost thaw (CO₂ + CH₄ release), soil respiration acceleration (Q₁₀), Amazon dieback (forest → savanna carbon release), tropical peatland fire
