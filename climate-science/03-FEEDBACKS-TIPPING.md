# 03 — Feedbacks & Tipping Points

## Climate Feedbacks, Tipping Elements, Hysteresis, Cascade Risk

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Feedback framework: feedback parameter λ = λ₀ + Σfᵢ; Planck response (λ₀ ≈ −3.2 W/m²/K — negative/stabilizing, blackbody emission increase); net feedback must be negative for stable climate
- Water vapor feedback (+1.8 W/m²/K): strongest positive feedback; specific humidity increases ~7%/K (Clausius-Clapeyron); tropospheric moistening amplifies initial warming; well-constrained
- Ice-albedo feedback (+0.3-0.5 W/m²/K): ice/snow loss → lower albedo → more absorption; Arctic amplification (2-4× global average warming); sea ice extent decline
- Lapse rate feedback (±): tropics negative (moist adiabat steepens), high latitudes positive; partially offsets water vapor feedback in tropics
- Cloud feedbacks (−0.4 to +0.8 W/m²/K): largest uncertainty; low cloud (SW) vs high cloud (LW); marine boundary layer clouds (stratus → cumulus transition); CMIP6 models show mostly positive
- Carbon cycle feedbacks: land and ocean sinks weakening as CO₂ rises; permafrost thaw (β and γ feedbacks); tropical forest vulnerability
- Tipping elements (Lenton et al. 2008, updated 2022): self-reinforcing components with critical thresholds:
  - Greenland Ice Sheet (~1.5-2°C): 7m SLR over centuries-millennia, marine ice sheet instability
  - West Antarctic Ice Sheet (~1.5°C): marine ice sheet instability, ~3-5m SLR
  - Amazon dieback (~2-3°C + deforestation): forest → savanna, ~90 GtC release, regional rainfall disruption
  - AMOC slowdown (~2-4°C): thermohaline circulation disruption, European cooling, monsoon shifts
  - Boreal forest dieback, coral reef die-off (~1.5°C already at risk), permafrost abrupt thaw
- Tipping point cascades: interconnected tipping elements may trigger each other; "Hothouse Earth" pathway (Steffen et al. 2018)
- Hysteresis: many tipping elements have different forward (tipping) and backward (recovery) thresholds; once tipped, restoring CO₂ to pre-industrial may not restore the system
