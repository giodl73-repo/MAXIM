# 04 — Emissions & Pathways

## RCP/SSP Scenarios, Carbon Budget Math, Sector Contributions, Net Zero

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Scenario framework: Representative Concentration Pathways (RCPs — radiative forcing by 2100: 2.6/4.5/6.0/8.5 W/m²) → Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs — socioeconomic narratives + emission levels); SSP1-2.6 (sustainability/~1.8°C), SSP2-4.5 (intermediate/~2.7°C), SSP3-7.0 (regional rivalry/~3.6°C), SSP5-8.5 (fossil fuel development/~4.4°C)
- Carbon budget math: cumulative CO₂ emissions determine peak temperature (approximately linear relationship); remaining budget from 2023 — ~380 GtCO₂ for 1.5°C (50% probability), ~1230 GtCO₂ for 2°C; annual emissions ~40 GtCO₂ → ~9 years at 1.5°C budget
- Sector contributions (global): energy systems ~34% (electricity/heat), transport ~16%, industry ~24% (steel/cement/chemicals — hard to decarbonize), agriculture ~22%, buildings ~6%
- Decarbonization difficulty spectrum: easy (utility solar/wind — already cheapest), medium (road transport — EVs accelerating), hard (aviation/shipping/steel/cement/agriculture)
- Net-zero definition: gross emissions ≠ zero; residual emissions offset by carbon dioxide removal (CDR); "net-zero by 2050" — timing of CDR vs mitigation matters enormously
- Overshoot pathways: most 1.5°C scenarios involve temperature overshoot + drawdown via CDR; risk of locking in irreversible changes during overshoot period
- Country commitments: NDCs (Nationally Determined Contributions — Paris Agreement mechanism), net-zero pledges vs NDC implementation gap, global stocktake (2023 — not on track)
- Carbon pricing mechanisms: carbon tax (price certainty, quantity uncertain), cap-and-trade (quantity certainty, price uncertain), EU ETS as largest example, border carbon adjustments (CBAM)
- Just transition: distributional impacts of decarbonization, energy poverty, fossil fuel worker displacement, green development vs CBDR (Common But Differentiated Responsibilities)
