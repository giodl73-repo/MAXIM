# 06 — Mitigation & Geoengineering

## Renewables, Grid Decarbonization, CDR, Solar Radiation Management

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Solar PV physics: p-n junction photoelectric effect, Shockley-Queisser limit (33% single-junction efficiency), multi-junction cells, current module efficiency (commercial ~22%, lab record ~29%); LCOE decline (80% cost reduction 2010-2023, now cheapest electricity in history in most regions)
- Wind energy: Betz limit (59.3% maximum power extraction), capacity factor (onshore ~35%, offshore ~45%), wake effects, grid integration challenges (intermittency)
- Grid decarbonization: variable renewable integration (storage/transmission/demand response/flexibility), capacity adequacy vs energy adequacy, HVDC transmission, nuclear as firm power, natural gas as bridge vs lock-in
- Energy storage: lithium-ion (chemistry/costs/grid-scale), pumped hydro (90% of deployed storage), flow batteries (vanadium redox), hydrogen (seasonal storage), thermal storage
- Transport decarbonization: BEVs (battery cost curve mirroring solar), grid emissions intensity matters (lifecycle emissions), shipping (ammonia/methanol/green hydrogen), aviation (SAF — sustainable aviation fuel, ~10% lifecycle reduction only, cost premium)
- Industry decarbonization (hard sectors): green steel (hydrogen DRI replacing coking coal), cement (clinker chemistry — CO₂ from calcium carbonate calcination, not just energy), industrial heat (electrification limits, green hydrogen), chemicals (Haber-Bosch for ammonia)
- Carbon Dioxide Removal (CDR): natural (afforestation/reforestation — permanence risk, land use competition), BECCS (bioenergy + CCS — land/water competition, negative emissions potential ~1-5 GtCO₂/yr), DAC (direct air capture — current cost ~$300-1000/tCO₂, theoretical minimum ~$50, Climeworks/Carbon Engineering plants), enhanced weathering, ocean alkalinization
- Solar Radiation Management (SRM): Stratospheric Aerosol Injection (SAI) — sulfate aerosols in stratosphere mimicking volcanic cooling (~Pinatubo 1991 = 0.5°C cooling from 20 Mt SO₂); modeling shows rapid cooling but termination shock risk (abrupt warming if stopped), precipitation pattern changes, ozone impacts; Marine Cloud Brightening (MCB — sea salt to increase cloud albedo)
- Governance of geoengineering: moral hazard concern (reduces mitigation pressure), transboundary effects without consent, no international governance framework, research vs deployment distinction
