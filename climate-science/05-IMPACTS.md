# 05 — Climate Impacts

## Sea Level, Extreme Events, Agriculture, Ecosystems, Risk Framing

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Sea level rise: two components — thermal expansion (~40% of current SLR) + ice mass loss (Greenland + West Antarctica + mountain glaciers ~60%); current rate ~3.7 mm/yr (accelerating); committed SLR already locked in (~0.5m by 2100 even under aggressive mitigation); IPCC likely range 2100 ~0.3-1.0m; low-probability/high-impact tail (marine ice sheet instability → 2m+)
- Extreme heat: wet-bulb temperature (Tw) as survival limit (35°C Tw = unsurvivable for 6 hrs); compound heat-humidity events; urban heat island amplification; heat mortality statistics; 2003 European heatwave / 2021 Pacific Northwest as attribution studies
- Flooding and precipitation: Clausius-Clapeyron (7%/K moisture increase → heavier precipitation events); but regional patterns complex (wet-get-wetter/dry-get-drier oversimplification); atmospheric rivers, flood frequency changes
- Drought and water stress: PDSI (Palmer Drought Severity Index), soil moisture deficit vs meteorological drought vs hydrological drought; snowpack as water tower (western US/Himalayas/Andes); groundwater depletion
- Wildfire: fire weather (temperature/humidity/wind/drought compound), fire season lengthening, fuel accumulation from fire suppression legacy, smoke health impacts (PM2.5)
- Agricultural impacts: yield response curves (optimal temperature for crops → losses above threshold), growing season shifts, CO₂ fertilization partially offsets (smaller effect than models suggest), regional winners vs losers, food system resilience
- Ocean impacts: coral bleaching (3-4°C SST anomaly triggers bleaching, >1.5°C risks 70-90% coral loss), deoxygenation (oxygen minimum zones expanding), fisheries range shifts
- Ecosystem disruption: phenology mismatches (earlier springs decoupling predator-prey), range shifts (poleward/upslope), extinction risk estimates, biome boundary shifts
- Human systems: climate migration projections (wide range 200M-1B by 2100), conflict risk (resource scarcity amplifier), health (malaria range, heat mortality, air quality), infrastructure damage
- Risk framing: reasons for concern (IPCC "burning embers"), fat tails in climate sensitivity distribution, expected value vs risk aversion framing for policy
