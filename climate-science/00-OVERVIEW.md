# 00 — Climate Science Overview

## Earth's Energy Budget, Greenhouse Effect Physics, Climate Sensitivity

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Earth's energy budget: solar irradiance (1361 W/m²), albedo (~0.30), effective emission temperature (255K vs actual 288K — the 33K greenhouse effect), energy balance at TOA (top of atmosphere)
- Greenhouse effect physics: molecular absorption spectra (CO₂ bends at 15μm, H₂O rotational bands, CH₄/N₂O), Beer-Lambert law, saturation of absorption bands, why adding CO₂ still matters despite saturation (wings of absorption bands), Planck function shift with temperature
- Radiative forcing: W/m² perturbation to energy balance; CO₂ forcing = 5.35 ln(C/C₀); forcing vs feedback distinction; current CO₂ forcing ~2.3 W/m² vs pre-industrial
- Climate sensitivity: Equilibrium Climate Sensitivity (ECS) — warming per CO₂ doubling, likely range 2.5–4°C (IPCC AR6); Transient Climate Response (TCR) — warming at CO₂ doubling under 1%/yr increase, faster timescale; Earth System Sensitivity includes slower feedbacks
- Historical climate record: instrumental record (1850–), proxy records (ice cores/tree rings/speleothems/coral), Holocene stable period, glacial-interglacial cycles (Milankovitch forcing), Paleocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum (PETM) as rapid warming analog
- Attribution science: detection (statistical significance of observed trend) vs attribution (fraction due to anthropogenic forcing); Fraction of Attributable Risk (FAR) for extreme events; IPCC confidence language
- The carbon budget to 1.5°C/2°C: remaining budget ~400 GtCO₂ for 1.5°C (as of 2023, ~10 years at current emissions), budget definition uncertainties
