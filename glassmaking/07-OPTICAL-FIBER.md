# 07 — Optical Fiber

## Total Internal Reflection, Drawing, Attenuation, Single/Multi-Mode

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Total internal reflection (TIR)**: light traveling from denser medium (higher RI) to less dense medium (lower RI) bends away from normal; at the critical angle (θ_c = arcsin(n₂/n₁)), light is refracted along the interface; above critical angle, all light is reflected back (total internal reflection); for silica glass (n≈1.444) to air (n=1.0): θ_c ≈ 43.7°; optical fiber exploits TIR to guide light along a glass fiber with near-zero losses at the glass-cladding interface
- **Fiber structure**: core (higher RI, where light travels), cladding (lower RI, surrounds core → TIR keeps light in core), primary coating (soft acrylate, 250μm total OD, protects glass from mechanical damage), secondary coating/buffer; single-mode fiber: core ~8-10μm diameter, cladding 125μm; multimode: core 50μm or 62.5μm, cladding 125μm; the core-cladding RI difference is small (~0.3%) — achieved by doping the silica core with germanium (GeO₂, increases RI) or doping cladding with fluorine (decreases RI)
- **Single-mode vs multimode**:
  - **Multimode (MMF)**: larger core (50 or 62.5μm); multiple light paths (modes) travel through the fiber; modal dispersion — different modes travel at slightly different velocities → pulse spreading over distance → limits bandwidth × distance; used for short-distance applications (data center interconnects <300m, campus networks); LED or VCSEL (vertical cavity surface emitting laser) sources; OM3, OM4, OM5 grades (aqua jacket); cheaper transceivers
  - **Single-mode (SMF)**: narrow core allows only one mode (the fundamental mode); no modal dispersion; capable of very high bandwidth over very long distances; all long-haul telecommunications; submarine cables; chromatic dispersion remains (different wavelengths travel at slightly different speeds) but manageable with dispersion-shifted fiber and dispersion compensation; expensive laser light sources required; OS1, OS2 grades (yellow jacket); the global internet backbone
- **Attenuation**: signal power loss per unit length (dB/km); key loss mechanisms:
  - **Rayleigh scattering**: fundamental limit at short wavelengths; scales as λ⁻⁴; light scattered by refractive index fluctuations at molecular scale; irreducible in silica at room temperature; dominates below 1,000nm
  - **Absorption**: IR absorption (molecular vibration of Si-OH bonds — overtones in telecom window); metal ion impurities; water peak (OH⁻ ions at 1,383nm — "water peak" that defines the low-loss windows at 1,310nm and 1,550nm); modern low-water-peak fiber (E-band) reduces OH content to nearly eliminate this peak
  - **Rayleigh + IR absorption minimum**: the two mechanisms cross near 1,550nm where standard single-mode fiber has minimum attenuation ~0.18 dB/km; this is why all long-haul telecom uses 1,550nm (C-band); second window at 1,310nm (~0.35 dB/km)
  - **Bending losses**: macro-bend (tight bends allow light to escape), micro-bend (microscale perturbations from coating/handling)
- **Fabrication — preform and draw tower**: optical fiber manufactured in two steps:
  1. **Preform manufacture**: cylindrical glass rod (preform) with correct core/cladding RI profile, typically 10-15cm diameter × 1m long; two main methods: MCVD (Modified Chemical Vapor Deposition — layers of SiCl₄ + GeCl₄ oxidized inside silica tube by traversing torch, layers collapsed to solid rod), and OVD/VAD (Outside Vapor Deposition / Vapor-phase Axial Deposition — layers deposited on outside of rotating target rod by flame hydrolysis, then sintered)
  2. **Draw tower**: preform fed into furnace zone at top of draw tower (20-30m tall); temperature ~2,100°C; fiber drawn at 10-30 m/s; diameter monitored by laser gauge → feedback to drawing speed maintains 125.0±0.1μm; UV-cured acrylate coating applied online; fibers wound onto spools; one preform → 1,000-5,000 km of fiber
- **DWDM (Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing)**: modern fiber uses wavelength multiplexing to send many channels simultaneously; C-band (1,530-1,565nm) divided into 40-96+ channels at 50 or 25 GHz spacing; EDFA (erbium-doped fiber amplifier) amplifies all channels simultaneously at 1,550nm without optical-electrical-optical conversion; modern single fiber pair capacity = 100+ Tb/s; transoceanic cable capacity enabling global internet; Gordon Bell's famous prediction about bandwidth exceeded in every way
- **Corning's breakthrough** (1970): Robert Maurer, Donald Keck, Peter Schultz at Corning achieved first fiber with attenuation below 20 dB/km (theoretical threshold for practical communication, proposed by Charles Kao 1966); prior fibers had attenuation >1,000 dB/km; pure silica core + titanium-doped cladding (later switched to germanium); Charles Kao (Hong Kong) received Nobel Prize in Physics 2009 for theoretical work predicting that pure silica could achieve low-loss transmission
