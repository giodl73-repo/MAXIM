## Coverage Notes

The history of printing and publishing traces humanity's evolving methods for recording, reproducing, and distributing information — from the first clay tablets of Mesopotamia through papyrus scrolls, the medieval manuscript codex, East Asian movable type, Gutenberg's press revolution, the industrialization of printing, and the digital disruption of the last four decades. This directory covers writing materials and technologies in sequence, examines why the same invention had radically different social impacts in different cultures, traces the economics and regulation of the book and newspaper trade, and ends with the digital publishing transformation (desktop publishing, PDF standardization, ebooks, print-on-demand, and open-access scholarly publishing).
