# 05 — Population & Urban Geography

## Demographic Transition, Urbanization, Megacities, Migration, Zipf's Law

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Demographic transition model (DTM): Stage 1 (high birth rate/high death rate — pre-industrial), Stage 2 (death rate drops → population explosion — sanitation/medicine), Stage 3 (birth rate drops — education/urbanization/women's rights), Stage 4 (low birth/death rates — developed countries), Stage 5 (birth rate below replacement → population decline — Japan/Germany/South Korea)
- Population projections: UN medium variant ~10.4B by 2100 (plateau), high variant 12B, low variant 9B; regional divergence (Africa 4B, Europe/Japan decline, India largest); demographic dividend
- Urbanization: global >55% urban (2018), projected 68% by 2050; fastest urbanization in Africa/Asia; urbanization and economic development correlation (causation debated)
- City size distributions: Zipf's Law (rank × size ≈ constant in many countries — 2nd city = 1/2 of largest, etc.); primacy (one dominant city → capital = much larger than #2); national capital vs economic capital separation (e.g., DC vs NYC, Canberra vs Sydney)
- Megacities (>10M population): ~35 as of 2024, mostly in Global South; Tokyo (37M), Delhi, Shanghai, Dhaka — governance challenges, informal settlements, infrastructure
- Urban internal structure: concentric zone model (Burgess), sector model (Hoyt), multiple nuclei (Harris/Ullman), polycentric suburban sprawl; edge cities; gentrification mechanics; informal settlements (slums — UN-Habitat 1B+ people)
- Migration: push factors (violence/poverty/climate) vs pull factors (economic opportunity), internal (rural-urban most common), international (refugees + economic migrants), remittances ($800B+ globally > ODA), brain drain/brain gain
- Aging societies: dependency ratio (workers supporting elderly/young), pension system stress, migration as partial solution, automation as alternative, Japan as leading case study
- Urban heat islands: cities 1-3°C warmer than surroundings (dark surfaces/reduced evapotranspiration/waste heat), impacts on energy demand and health
