# 07 — Economic Geography

## Agglomeration, Core-Periphery, Global Value Chains, Ports, Logistics

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Agglomeration economies: localization (same-industry clustering → shared labor pool/suppliers/knowledge spillovers) vs urbanization (diverse industries → cross-sector innovation); Marshall's industrial districts; why Silicon Valley/Wall Street/Hollywood persist despite high costs
- Location theory: von Thünen land use rings (distance from market → intensity of use), Weber industrial location (minimize transport costs to inputs + market), Christaller central place theory (hexagonal market areas, hierarchy of places)
- New Economic Geography (Krugman 1991 Nobel): core-periphery models, increasing returns + transport costs → agglomeration; why manufacturing concentrates; import substitution vs export-led development
- World systems theory (Wallerstein): core (capital-intensive, high-skill, high-wage production) vs periphery (labor-intensive, low-wage, commodity export) vs semi-periphery; critique (static, teleological)
- Global value chains (GVCs): Apple as canonical example (designed USA, components Japan/Korea/Taiwan, assembled China, sold globally); value capture by geography; smile curve (R&D + design + brand vs manufacturing — value concentration at ends)
- Special Economic Zones (SEZs): from Shenzhen (1980) to 5,000+ worldwide; preferential tax/regulatory treatment, export processing zones, FDI attraction; success vs failure factors
- Port geography: container revolution (Malcom McLean 1956), TEU (twenty-foot equivalent unit), port concentration (Rotterdam/Singapore/Shanghai top volumes), hinterland connectivity, port-city relationships
- Logistics and supply chain geography: just-in-time → single-source vulnerability (COVID/Suez/Taiwan risk), nearshoring/friendshoring trends, cold chain requirements, last-mile delivery economics
- Tourism geography: carrying capacity, over-tourism (Venice/Barcelona/Amsterdam backlash), heritage tourism, ecotourism, MICE (meetings/incentives/conferences/exhibitions), economic multiplier
- Economic corridors: BRI land corridors (CPEC/Bangladesh-China-India-Myanmar), US PGII (Partnership for Global Infrastructure and Investment), Trans-African Highway, economic corridor theory
