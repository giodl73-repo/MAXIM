# 03 — Ocean-Atmosphere Coupling

## Thermohaline Circulation, Gyres, ENSO, Monsoons, Teleconnections

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Ocean circulation drivers: surface currents (wind-driven, Ekman transport → geostrophic flow), deep circulation (thermohaline — driven by density differences from temperature + salinity)
- Global Ocean Conveyor Belt (thermohaline circulation/AMOC): North Atlantic Deep Water (NADW) formation at high latitudes → sinks → flows south → upwells in Southern Ocean/Pacific/Indian → surface return as warm shallow current; timescale ~1000 years; AMOC slowdown as tipping point (see climate-science/03)
- Ocean gyres: subtropical gyres in each ocean basin (5 total), clockwise in Northern Hemisphere/counter-clockwise in Southern (Coriolis), western boundary intensification (Gulf Stream/Kuroshio much stronger than eastern equivalents), garbage patches in gyre centers
- Upwelling zones: coastal upwelling (California/Humboldt/Benguela/Canary/Somalia currents) — cold nutrient-rich water → productive fisheries + fog; equatorial upwelling; high productivity but cold/dry nearby climate
- ENSO (El Niño-Southern Oscillation): normal state (trade winds push warm water west, cold upwelling off Peru), El Niño (trade winds weaken → warm water spreads east → Peru/Ecuador floods, Indonesia/Australia drought, Atlantic hurricane suppression, jet stream shifts), La Niña (enhanced normal → Indonesia/Australia floods, drought SE USA); PDO (Pacific Decadal Oscillation) as multi-decadal modulation
- Monsoon systems: South Asian monsoon (ITCZ migrates north in summer → onshore moisture flow → June-September rainfall over India — 80% of annual precipitation), East Asian monsoon, West African monsoon, Australian monsoon; failure → famine (historical famines largely monsoon-driven)
- Teleconnections: climate anomalies correlating across large distances; North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO — pressure difference Iceland-Azores), Pacific North American pattern (PNA), Arctic Oscillation (AO) → polar vortex disruption → cold air outbreaks
- Ocean heat content: ocean absorbs 90%+ of anthropogenic heat; sea surface temperature (SST) trends; marine heatwaves (MHW) intensifying, bleaching coral, disrupting fisheries
