# 00 — Geography Overview

## Physical vs Human Geography, Geographic Determinism, Frameworks

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Physical geography scope: geomorphology (landforms), climatology (climate patterns), biogeography (species/biome distribution), hydrology (water systems), oceanography (ocean systems), pedology (soils)
- Human geography scope: population/urban geography, economic geography, political geography, cultural geography, historical geography (see historical-geography/ for the specialist track)
- Geographic determinism debate: Montesquieu (climate → character → government), Ratzel (environmental determinism — crude), Huntington (civilization shaped by climate — discredited), Ellen Churchill Semple (possibilism — environment sets limits, humans choose within them), Blaut's critique
- Diamond's "Guns, Germs, and Steel" (1997): geographic luck (Eurasian east-west axis → crop/animal diffusion → population density → state capacity → military advantage); detailed critiques (ignores agency, overpredicts from environment, selection on outcomes); what survives and what doesn't
- Scale and spatial analysis: local vs regional vs global scale, modifiable areal unit problem (MAUP), spatial autocorrelation (Tobler's First Law — near things more similar), GIS as analytical tool, remote sensing
- Physical geography and human history: river valleys as civilization cradles (Nile/Tigris-Euphrates/Indus/Yellow), isthmus and peninsula effects on trade, mountain barriers (Alps/Himalayas), island isolation effects
- Modern geography's contributions: urbanization patterns, climate vulnerability mapping, supply chain geography, electoral geography (gerrymandering), disease geography (epidemiology + spatial)
- Key concepts: absolute vs relative location, place vs space, human-environment interaction, movement/flows, regions as analytical construct
- Index of modules: physical geography, climate zones, ocean-atmosphere, biogeography, population/urban, geopolitics/resources, economic geography
