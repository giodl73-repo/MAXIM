# 02 — Climate Zones & Biomes

## Köppen Classification, Climate Controls, Biome Distribution

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Köppen classification (1900, updated Trewartha 1968): five main groups — A (Tropical: Af/Am/Aw), B (Dry: BWh/BWk/BSh/BSk), C (Temperate: Csa/Csb/Cfa/Cfb/Cfc), D (Continental: Dfa/Dfb/Dfc/Dfd/Dwa/Dwb), E (Polar: ET/EF); criteria based on temperature + precipitation thresholds
- Climate controls: latitude (solar angle → insolation), altitude (lapse rate 6.5°C/km), continentality (ocean moderates temperature), ocean currents (warm currents → mild/wet: Gulf Stream; cold currents → cool/dry: California/Humboldt/Benguela), ITCZ migration (drives tropical wet-dry seasonality), orographic effect (windward wet, leeward dry)
- Tropical zone (A): tropical rainforest (Af — no dry season, >60mm/month all months, Amazon/Congo/SE Asia), tropical monsoon (Am), tropical savanna (Aw — distinct dry season, African savanna), biodiversity peak
- Dry zone (B): deserts (BW — hot: Sahara/Arabian/Atacama, cold: Gobi/Patagonia) and steppes (BS — semi-arid, interior continental, Kazakh/Great Plains); evapotranspiration exceeds precipitation
- Temperate zone (C): Mediterranean (Cs — dry summer, wet winter, fire-adapted shrubland), humid subtropical (Cfa — hot summer/mild winter, SE USA/China/SE South America), oceanic/maritime (Cfb/Cfc — mild/cloudy, W Europe/Pacific NW)
- Continental zone (D): humid continental (Dfa/Dfb — hot/cold summer, cold winter, Great Plains/Midwest, boreal climate transition), subarctic/boreal (Dfc — short cool summer, long cold winter, taiga forest belt)
- Polar zone (E): tundra (ET — brief summer, permafrost) and ice cap (EF — no melting month)
- Biome distribution and climate: tropical rainforest/tropical dry/savanna/desert/chaparral/temperate grassland/temperate deciduous forest/boreal forest (taiga)/tundra/ice; climate envelope concept; why biome map ≈ climate zone map
- Altitude as latitude analog: tropical mountain zones (Andean pisos altitudinales — tierra caliente/templada/fría/helada); treeline; alpine tundra
