# 06 — Geopolitics & Resource Geography

## Oil, Water, Rare Earths, Chokepoints, Arctic, Food Security

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Oil geography: Persian Gulf concentration (~40% of world reserves), major producers (Saudi Arabia/Russia/USA/Iraq/UAE/Kuwait), OPEC+ production agreements, resource curse mechanisms (Dutch disease, rentier state, authoritarian durability)
- Strategic chokepoints: Strait of Hormuz (21M bbl/day — 20% of global oil, 25% of LNG; Iran threat), Strait of Malacca (15M bbl/day — shortest sea route Asia-Europe/Middle East; alternative: Lombok Strait), Bab-el-Mandeb (Red Sea access; Houthi disruption 2023-), Suez Canal (12% of world trade; 2021 Ever Given blockage), Turkish Straits (Bosporus/Dardanelles — Black Sea access, Montreux Convention), GIUK Gap (Greenland-Iceland-UK anti-submarine warfare corridor)
- Water stress: UN definition (>1700m³/capita/yr = water stressed; >1000 = scarcity), aquifer depletion (Ogallala/Indus/Arabian), interstate water treaties (Colorado River/Nile/Jordan/Mekong), virtual water trade, desalination economics (Saudi Arabia/Israel/UAE)
- Food geography: caloric surpluses vs deficits by region, grain export concentration (Ukraine/Russia/USA/Argentina/Australia — "breadbaskets"), comparative advantage in agriculture, food miles vs carbon footprint tradeoff, GMO geography (regulatory fragmentation)
- Rare earth geography: China produces ~60% of REEs and processes ~90%; critical minerals for energy transition (lithium in Chile/Australia/Argentina "Lithium Triangle", cobalt in DRC ~70%, nickel, manganese); US/EU supply chain diversification efforts; Environmental impacts of mining
- Timber and deforestation: tropical timber from SE Asia/Congo/Amazon, REDD+ (Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Degradation), FSC certification
- Arctic competition: Northern Sea Route (shortens Europe-Asia by ~40% when ice-free in summer), Northwest Passage (Canadian vs international waters dispute), submarine cable routes, overlapping continental shelf claims (Russia/Canada/Denmark/Norway/USA), UNCLOS Article 76 + Arctic Council
- Resource conflicts: resource-war thesis (oil → Gulf Wars, diamonds → Sierra Leone, coltan → Congo), institutional mediation, curse vs blessing (Norway vs Nigeria comparison)
