# 05 — Strategy

## Porter Five Forces, Competitive Advantage, OKRs, Balanced Scorecard

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Porter's Five Forces: threat of entry (barriers: economies of scale/switching costs/capital requirements/incumbency advantages), buyer power, supplier power, threat of substitutes, rivalry intensity; industry attractiveness vs competitive position
- Porter's generic strategies: cost leadership (operational efficiency, scale), differentiation (unique value, premium price), focus (niche target); stuck-in-the-middle trap; value chain as analytical tool
- Resource-based view (Barney): VRIN framework (Valuable/Rare/Inimitable/Non-substitutable), sustained competitive advantage from resources vs market positioning; capabilities vs resources
- Dynamic capabilities (Teece): sensing (opportunities/threats), seizing (mobilizing resources), transforming (reconfiguring) — especially relevant for tech companies in fast-moving markets
- Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim/Mauborgne): value innovation (create uncontested market space), Strategy Canvas, ERRC grid (eliminate/reduce/raise/create); critique (survivorship bias)
- OKRs (Objectives and Key Results): Intel origin (Grove), Google scaling (Doerr "Measure What Matters"); objective (aspirational direction) vs key results (quantifiable outcomes); committed vs aspirational OKRs; common failure modes (too many, sandbagging, gaming metrics)
- Balanced Scorecard (Kaplan/Norton): four perspectives (financial/customer/internal process/learning&growth), strategy maps, lagging vs leading indicators; critique (complexity, not predictive)
- Strategy execution: McKinsey 7-S (strategy/structure/systems/style/staff/skills/shared values), Execution (Bossidy/Charan three processes — people/strategy/operations)
- Platform strategy: network effects (direct/indirect/two-sided), winner-take-all vs winner-take-most, envelopment, platform vs pipeline businesses (Parker/Van Alstyne)
- Corporate strategy vs business unit strategy: portfolio management (BCG matrix — cash cow/star/question mark/dog), M&A as strategy, core vs context (Moore)
