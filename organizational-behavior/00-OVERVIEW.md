# 00 — Organizational Behavior Overview

## Levels of Analysis, History of Management Theory, What Has Evidence

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Levels of analysis: individual (personality/motivation/perception) → group (teams/leadership/conflict) → organizational (structure/culture/change) → environment (strategy/institutional)
- History of management thought: Scientific Management (Taylor — time-motion, piece-rate), Administrative Management (Fayol — 14 principles), Human Relations (Hawthorne studies — worker attitudes matter), Systems Theory (organization as open system), Contingency Theory (no universal best way)
- What actually has evidence: meta-analytic evidence base for OB interventions; what's replication-crisis casualties vs what survived; effect sizes that matter vs statistical significance theater
- The OB-economics interface: principal-agent theory (incentive alignment), transaction cost economics (Williamson — make vs buy), property rights theory (Coase)
- Culture: Schein's three levels (artifacts/espoused values/basic assumptions), Hofstede dimensions (power distance/individualism/uncertainty avoidance/masculinity/long-term orientation), culture as competitive advantage or constraint
- Key journals and evidence standards: Journal of Applied Psychology, Administrative Science Quarterly, Academy of Management Journal; what "meta-analysis" means for evidence quality
- Index of all modules: motivation, leadership, teams, org design, strategy, change
