# 01 — Motivation

## SDT, Herzberg, Expectancy Theory, Goal-Setting, Intrinsic vs Extrinsic

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan): three basic psychological needs — autonomy (volition, not independence), competence (efficacy), relatedness (connection); intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation spectrum (amotivation → external → introjected → identified → integrated → intrinsic); cognitive evaluation theory (how extrinsic rewards crowd out intrinsic motivation)
- Herzberg two-factor theory: hygiene factors (salary/supervision/working conditions — prevent dissatisfaction but don't motivate) vs motivators (achievement/recognition/responsibility/growth — actually motivate); job enrichment implications; critique (methodology issues) but useful framework
- Expectancy theory (Vroom): Motivation = Expectancy (effort → performance) × Instrumentality (performance → outcome) × Valence (value of outcome); practical implications for incentive design
- Goal-setting theory (Locke & Latham): specific + difficult goals outperform "do your best"; the SMART framework (and its limitations); goal conflict; distal vs proximal goals; OKRs as goal-setting theory applied
- Equity theory (Adams): perceived fairness of outcome/input ratio vs referent others; underpayment inequity (anger/underperformance) vs overpayment (guilt, less motivating than expected)
- Intrinsic vs extrinsic reward design: when performance pay works (algorithmic tasks) vs backfires (heuristic/creative tasks); Dan Pink's Drive popularization vs research base
- Pay and motivation: efficiency wage theory, tournament theory (promotion-based incentives), pay transparency effects, base vs variable pay research
- Motivation across cultures: SDT universality claim vs cultural variation evidence
