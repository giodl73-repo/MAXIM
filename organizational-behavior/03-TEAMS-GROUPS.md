# 03 — Teams & Groups

## Group Dynamics, Social Loafing, Groupthink, Psychological Safety, Hackman

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Group vs team distinction: group (shared identity), team (interdependent tasks, shared accountability); task interdependence types (pooled/sequential/reciprocal/intensive)
- Tuckman stages: forming/storming/norming/performing/adjourning — useful mental model; critique (not linear, not universal); punctuated equilibrium model (Gersick) as alternative
- Social loafing: Ringelmann effect (individual effort declines in groups), identifiability as solution, accountability mechanisms; when groups outperform individuals (additive vs conjunctive vs disjunctive tasks)
- Groupthink (Janis): eight symptoms, antecedents (high cohesion/insulation/directive leadership/stress), classic cases (Bay of Pigs/Challenger/Columbia), prevention (devil's advocate, outside experts, anonymous critique)
- Psychological safety (Edmondson): interpersonal risk-taking climate, measurement (7-item scale), Google Project Aristotle as evidence, distinction from trust, how leaders create/destroy it
- Team effectiveness models: Hackman's inputs-processes-outputs (IPO), GRPI (goals/roles/processes/interpersonal), Lencioni's five dysfunctions (trust/conflict/commitment/accountability/results)
- Decision-making in groups: nominal group technique, Delphi method, structured debate (dialectical inquiry), pre-mortem (Klein), when to use consensus vs authority
- Diversity in teams: information elaboration hypothesis (task-relevant diversity helps), fault lines (subgroup formation), surface vs deep-level diversity distinctions
- Virtual teams: trust building without FtF, communication richness (media synchronicity theory), documentation practices, timezone management
- Team composition: complementary skills vs similar cognitive styles, optimal size (Dunbar numbers applied), role clarity
