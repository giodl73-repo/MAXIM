# 02 — Leadership

## Transformational, Situational, LMX, Dark Triad, Predictors

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Trait theories: early (great man) → modern — meta-analytic evidence for Big Five predictors (extraversion strongest, conscientiousness second), intelligence (GMA) as predictor, dark triad executives
- Transactional vs transformational leadership: transactional (contingent reward, management-by-exception), transformational (idealized influence/inspirational motivation/intellectual stimulation/individualized consideration — Bass's 4 I's); full range model; meta-analytic effects
- Leader-Member Exchange (LMX): in-group vs out-group differentiation, relationship quality as predictor of outcomes, equity concerns with high LMX differentiation
- Situational leadership (Hersey & Blanchard): directing/coaching/supporting/delegating based on follower development level; critique (low empirical support but widely used framework)
- Servant leadership: follower-first orientation, community building; growing evidence base; contrast with narcissistic leadership
- Authentic leadership: self-awareness, relational transparency, balanced processing, internalized moral perspective; positive psychology roots; critique (self-report bias)
- Dark triad in leaders: narcissism (charisma + entitlement + exploitation), Machiavellianism (strategic manipulation), subclinical psychopathy (fearlessness + ruthlessness); research on when dark triad "works" short-term vs organizational costs
- Leadership vs management: Kotter's distinction (management = planning/organizing/controlling; leadership = direction/alignment/motivation); why both matter; common confusion
- Psychometric predictors: structured interviews, situational judgment tests, 360-degree feedback validity; what actually predicts leadership effectiveness
- Remote/hybrid leadership: proximity bias, documentation as leadership practice, async-first communication
