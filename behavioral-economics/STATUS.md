## Coverage Notes

Behavioral economics integrates psychology into economic models, challenging the classical assumption of perfectly rational utility-maximizing agents. This directory covers the theoretical critique of expected utility theory (Allais and Ellsberg paradoxes, Kahneman and Tversky's prospect theory, Simon's bounded rationality), the major heuristics and biases (availability, representativeness, anchoring), mental accounting anomalies, social preferences revealed in experimental games, intertemporal choice failures and hyperbolic discounting, and nudge/choice-architecture interventions. Two applied modules bridge to real-world policy (government behavioral insight teams, default interventions) and product/UX design (persuasive patterns, variable reward schedules, ethical design considerations).
