# Exoplanets
## Detection Methods, Demographics, Characterization, Habitability

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Detection methods: transit (Kepler/TESS geometry, limbdarkening), radial velocity (Doppler, m sin i degeneracy), direct imaging (coronagraph, angular separation limit), microlensing, astrometry (GAIA)
- Population demographics: hot Jupiters, super-Earths/mini-Neptunes, radius gap (Fulton gap ~1.7 R_E), occurrence rates
- Mass-radius diagram: rocky vs water world vs gas dwarf degeneracy; EOS families
- Habitable zone: classical (liquid water), updated (CO₂/H₂O limits), moist greenhouse, maximum greenhouse
- Transmission spectroscopy: JWST NIRSpec/MIRI — TRAPPIST-1b, TRAPPIST-1c, K2-18b (DMS controversy)
- Emission spectroscopy: thermal phase curves, dayside/nightside temperature contrast
- TRAPPIST-1 system deep dive: 7 Earth-sized planets, resonance chain, tidal locking, atmospheric retention
- Hot Jupiter mysteries: migration, magnetic inflation, ultra-hot Jupiters (iron vapor)
- Future: PLATO (2026), Roman (coronagraph), HWO (direct imaging rocky planets ~2040)
