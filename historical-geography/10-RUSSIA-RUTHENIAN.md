# Kievan Rus to Muscovy — Russia's Medieval Arc

## The Big Picture

```
EASTERN SLAVIC POLITICAL EVOLUTION:

  ~750-850: Varangian (Swedish Norse) traders/settlers
            along river routes (Ladoga → Dnieper → Black Sea)
            |
  862:     Rurik at Novgorod (traditional date)
            Rurikid dynasty begins
            |
  ~880:    Oleg moves capital to Kyiv
            Kievan Rus: loose confederation under Kyiv
            |
  980-1015: Vladimir I — Christianized Rus (988)
  1019-1054: Yaroslav the Wise — cultural peak
            |
  1054:    Death of Yaroslav → rota succession → fragmentation
            |
  1237-1240: Mongol invasion; Batu Khan
             Kyiv sacked 1240
             Most principalities become Mongol tributaries
            |
  1240-1480: THE MONGOL YOKE
             Golden Horde overlordship
            |
  1147:     Moscow first mentioned (under Yuri Dolgorukiy)
  1263:     Moscow becomes separate principality (Daniel)
  ~1300-1480: Rise of Moscow
              Ivan I Kalita ("Moneybag") — tribute collector
              Ivan III — throws off Mongol yoke (1480)
              Ivan IV "the Terrible" — first Tsar (1547)
            |
  1598:     Rurikid dynasty ends; Boris Godunov
  1598-1613: Time of Troubles
  1613:     Michael Romanov elected → Romanov dynasty
```

## Kievan Rus — The Foundation

### Rurikids and the Rota System

**Rurik** (r. ~862–879): Traditional founder. Invited by Novgorod Slavs to rule (the "Calling of the Varangians" narrative). He was Varangian (Swedish Norse). Whether this actually happened or is legendary: debated, but the Rurikid dynasty was real.

**Oleg "the Prophet"** (r. ~882–912): Moved the capital from Novgorod to Kyiv ~882. Raided Constantinople 907; obtained favorable trade treaty — Rus merchants got significant trading privileges.

**Vladimir I "the Great"** (r. 980–1015): The pivotal ruler.

```
VLADIMIR'S CONVERSION 988:
  Vladimir was pagan (had multiple wives + 800 concubines reportedly)
  Legend: sent envoys to sample religions
    Jews: "Your God took your temple and scattered you — pass"
    Muslims: "No alcohol — definitely pass"
    Germans (Catholics): "No beauty in their worship — pass"
    Byzantines at Hagia Sophia: "We knew not whether we were
      in heaven or on earth... we only know God dwells there"
  (This is a chronicle — probably shaped after the fact)
  REAL REASONS: alliance with Byzantium; Byzantine bride (Anna Porphyrogenneta)
                prestige of Orthodox civilization; trade relationships

  Vladimir received Princess Anna (sister of Byzantine emperors)
  in exchange for: baptism + military aid (suppressed revolt in Crimea)
  Mass baptism of Kiev in the Dnieper 988
  → Eastern Orthodox Christianity established in Rus
  → Cyrillic alphabet → literacy in Slavonic
  → Byzantine art, architecture (churches), law → Rus culture
```

**Yaroslav the Wise** (r. 1019–1054): Cultural peak of Kievan Rus.
- Built St. Sophia Cathedral in Kyiv (modeled on Hagia Sophia)
- Codified Rus law (Russkaya Pravda)
- Established ecclesiastical connections with Constantinople
- Married his children into every major European royal family
  (his daughter Anna married Henry I of France)

### The Rota System — Catastrophic Succession

Yaroslav the Wise's biggest mistake: dividing Rus among his sons with a seniority rotation.

```
ROTA (LADDER) SYSTEM:
  Yaroslav divided territories among sons:
  Eldest son: gets Kyiv (senior throne)
  Others: get other cities in seniority order
  ON DEATH: everyone moves up one step
  Youngest brother of Kyiv ruler: gets least important city

WHAT WENT WRONG:
  Assumes all brothers cooperate
  Assumes no one dies out of order
  Assumes no branching (cousins, nephews — do they participate?)

  IN PRACTICE:
  Constant warfare over who moves where
  Every death triggered a succession crisis
  Cousins fought with uncles; brothers fought brothers
  Princes built up LOCAL power rather than rotating loyally

  Within 80 years of Yaroslav's death:
  Kievan Rus was fragmenting into competing principalities
  The unity of the rota system was purely theoretical by 1100
```

## The Major Principalities (c. 1100–1240)

```
PRINCIPAL TERRITORIES AFTER FRAGMENTATION:
  Novgorod Republic (north):
    De facto republic; elected posadnik (mayor) to govern
    Prince invited as military commander only (paid; could be expelled)
    Veche (assembly) of leading citizens made decisions
    Hanseatic trade partner; enormous fur wealth
    Independence maintained even under Mongols (too far north;
    paid tribute but never sacked)

  Vladimir-Suzdal (northeast):
    Yuri Dolgorukiy: founded Moscow 1147 (as small fort)
    Andrei Bogolyubsky: moved capital from Kyiv to Vladimir
    → Center of gravity shifting northeast
    Mongols sacked Vladimir 1238

  Galicia-Volhynia (southwest):
    Closest to Catholic West; strongest "Western" orientation
    Roman of Volhynia: briefly powerful; killed 1205
    Daniel Romanovich: title "King of Rus" from Pope 1253
    (attempted crusade against Mongols — never materialized)
    Eventually absorbed by Poland and Lithuania

  Chernigov, Pereyaslavl, Smolensk:
    Major principalities that competed for Kyiv
    All devastated by Mongols 1238-1240

  Kyiv itself:
    Symbolic senior city; increasingly a prize rather than a power
    Sacked so many times (by Andrei Bogolyubsky 1169!) that its
    prestige was already declining before the Mongols
```

## The Mongol Invasion (1237–1242) and the Yoke

```
BATU KHAN'S INVASION:
  Subutai: the tactical genius
  Fall/Winter 1237: northeastern Rus
  Vladimir-Suzdal: Ryazan burned Dec 1237; Vladimir Feb 1238
  Battle of the Sit River 1238: Grand Prince Yuri II of Vladimir killed
  1239: Southern Rus principalities
  Dec 1240: KYIV SACKED
    Plano Carpini (Papal envoy 1245) described: "countless skulls
    and bones of dead men lying on the field"
  1241: Mongols into Poland + Hungary
  Battle of Legnica (Poland): Teutonic Knights + Poles defeated
  Battle of Mohi (Hungary): Hungarian army destroyed
  Then: withdrawal (death of Ögedei Khan)

THE YOKE (1240–1480):
  Golden Horde: the western khanate, centered on lower Volga (Sarai)
  Russian princes → Sarai to receive yarlik (patent of rule)
  Khan confirmed who ruled; could remove princes
  Tribute: regular; collection often sub-contracted to Russian princes
  Census: Mongols conducted census for tax purposes

  LIFE UNDER THE YOKE:
  Initially: brutal devastation; population collapse
  Later: princes adapted; many went to Sarai; intermarried
  Trade: Pax Mongolica (briefly) benefited merchants
  Religion: Mongols initially tolerant of Orthodox Church
  Church: exempt from tribute under most khans
```

## The Rise of Moscow

### Why Moscow?

```
MOSCOW'S ADVANTAGES:
  Geography: center of river network in northeastern Rus
             (Moskva River → Oka → Volga)
  Protected: not the obvious first target for steppe raids
             (unlike exposed southern principalities)
  Small: started insignificant → no reason for others to destroy it

DANIEL OF MOSCOW (r. 1263–1303):
  Youngest son of Alexander Nevsky
  Got Moscow as a minor appanage (smallest portion)
  Began aggressive territorial acquisition: took Kolomna 1301
  The Muscovite expansion began with him

IVAN I "KALITA" (Money-bag) (r. 1325–1340):
  The key early figure
  Strategy: become the most reliable tribute collector for the Golden Horde
  Metropolitan of the Russian Church moved to Moscow 1325
  (Church seat had been in Vladimir; now Moscow)
  → Moscow = religious capital of Orthodox Rus
  Khan granted Ivan right to collect tribute from ALL Russian princes
  Ivan kept some for himself (built Moscow treasury)
  Bought additional territories from impoverished princes

RESULTS:
  Moscow grew wealthy while other principalities were impoverished
  Other princes resented but couldn't challenge Horde-backed Moscow
  Church endorsement gave Moscow spiritual prestige
```

### From Tribute Collector to Independent Power

**Dmitry Donskoy** (r. 1359–1389): Won the Battle of Kulikovo Field (1380).

```
BATTLE OF KULIKOVO 1380:
  Mamai (not the Khan, but the power behind the Horde) invaded
  Dmitry assembled largest Russian army since Mongol invasion
  Battle at Kulikovo Field (confluence of Don + Nepryadva)
  Russian victory — first major defeat of a Mongol/Tatar force
  Significance: proved Russia could win. Symbolic, not decisive
  Aftermath: Tokhtamysh (actual Mongol Khan) sacked Moscow 1382
  → Yoke resumed
  But Kulikovo became the foundational myth of Russian resistance
```

**Ivan III "the Great"** (r. 1462–1505): The architect of the Russian state.

```
IVAN III'S ACHIEVEMENTS:

  THE YOKE THROWN OFF (1480):
  "Stand on the Ugra River" — two armies faced each other
  Neither attacked; Akhmat Khan withdrew
  → Conventional date of Mongol yoke's end
  (Really: Moscow had been de facto independent for years)

  TERRITORIAL EXPANSION:
  Incorporated: Novgorod (1478 — abolished the veche; brought 300 noble
    families to Moscow to break their power)
  Tver (1485)
  Pskov, Ryazan, Vyazma, Chernigov area
  Moscow from city-state to multi-regional power

  MARRIAGE TO SOPHIA PALAIOLOGINA (1472):
  Sophia: niece of last Byzantine emperor (Constantine XI)
  Arranged by Pope (hoping Moscow would join anti-Ottoman crusade)
  Ivan used the Byzantine connection:
  → Adopted the double-headed eagle (Byzantine imperial symbol)
  → Elaborate court ceremony copied from Byzantine model
  → Claimed succession to Rome's imperial tradition
  → Eventually: "Moscow = Third Rome" doctrine (formalized by monk Filofei c. 1510)

  TITLE:
  Ivan III used "Gosudar vsea Rusi" (Lord of all Rus)
  His grandson Ivan IV would take "Tsar" formally
```

**Vasily III** (r. 1505–1533): Completed the consolidation — took Pskov, Smolensk.

## Ivan IV "the Terrible" (r. 1547–1584)

```
FIRST RUSSIAN TSAR:
  Ivan IV crowned Tsar and Autocrat of all Russia 1547
  (First to use the title formally from the start of reign)
  The title "Tsar" = Caesar; deliberately imperial

EARLY ACHIEVEMENTS:
  Conquered Kazan Khanate 1552 (major celebration; St. Basil's Cathedral built)
  Conquered Astrakhan 1556
  → Both former Mongol khanates absorbed
  → Russia begins expansion toward Siberia

THE OPRICHNINA (1565–1572):
  Ivan's paranoid response to real and imagined treason
  Russia divided: Oprichnina (Ivan's personal realm) and
                  Zemshchina (rest, under boyar administration)
  Oprichniki: Ivan's personal police/terror force
              Black robes; black horses; brooms and dog heads on saddle
              (symbolizing: sweeping away and sniffing out treason)
  Massacred noble families; sacked Novgorod 1570 (paranoid accusation)
  Killed his own son Ivan Ivanovich 1581 in a rage
  Ended 1572 after Oprichniki failed against Crimean Tatar raid
  → Destabilized Russian society; weakened the state
  → Contributed to Time of Troubles after his death

THE TERRIBLE:
  "Grozny" in Russian means: awesome, fearsome, terrifying
  The "terrible" translation is imprecise — more like "formidable/fearsome"
  In early years, Grozny was used admiringly
  By later years, it had shifted to dread
```

## Time of Troubles (1598–1613)

```
RURIKID DYNASTY ENDS:
  Ivan IV's son Theodore I: mentally limited, ruled in name only
  Boris Godunov: brother-in-law, actual ruler during Theodore's reign
  Theodore died without heir 1598
  → Rurikid dynasty extinct
  Boris Godunov elected Tsar

CHAOS:
  False Dmitry I (1605–1606): claimed to be Theodore's brother Dmitry
    (who had died 1591 under suspicious circumstances)
    Polish-backed; supported by many Russians who wanted change
    Killed in Moscow revolt 1606
  Vasily Shuisky (boyar tsar, 1606–1610):
    Constant rebellions; Polish invasion
  False Dmitry II: another pretender; chaos
  Polish occupation of Moscow 1610–1612
  National militia (Pozharsky + Minin) expelled Poles 1612

RESOLUTION:
  Zemsky Sobor (Assembly of the Land) elected Michael Romanov 1613
  Michael: 16 years old; grandnephew of Ivan IV's first wife
  His father Filaret (Patriarch of Moscow) was the real power
  → Romanov dynasty; ruled until 1917
```

## Common Confusion Points

**"Kievan Rus" ≠ Russia**
Kievan Rus was a multi-ethnic state spanning modern Ukraine, Belarus, and Russia. All three countries claim it as their founding state. The capital (Kyiv) is now in Ukraine. This makes "Kievan Rus was Russia" politically fraught — it was the origin of all three states' cultures, not just Russia's.

**The Varangians were not just raiders**
The eastern Varangians (Swedes primarily) were primarily traders, not raiders. The river route from Scandinavia to Constantinople was the richest trade route in early medieval Europe. Furs, amber, and slaves went south; silk and silver came north.

**Alexander Nevsky: hero or collaborator?**
Alexander Nevsky (Ivan III's grandfather's grandfather) defeated the Swedes (Battle of the Neva, 1240 — gave him "Nevsky") and the Teutonic Knights (Battle on the Ice, 1242). Soviet propaganda made him a nationalist hero. But he was also the Mongol Khan's loyal servant and tribute collector, who put down Russian anti-Mongol rebellions. Both are true.

**Moscow vs Russia vs Muscovy**
"Muscovy" is the Latin/Western name for the Principality of Moscow. It wasn't called "Russia" until after Ivan IV — and even then the name was contested. What expanded from the Principality of Moscow was not identical to "Russia" in any ethnic or geographic sense.
