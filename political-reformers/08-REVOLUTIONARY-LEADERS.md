# Revolutionary Leaders: Lenin, Mao, Castro, Guevara

## The Big Picture

```
  THE COMMUNIST REVOLUTIONARY TRADITION: 1917–1989
  ==================================================

  1917   Russian Revolution
         Lenin + Bolsheviks seize power
         World War I collapses Tsarist regime

  1921   Soviet state consolidating
         NEP (New Economic Policy) — tactical retreat to market
         Lenin controls; Stalin rising

  1924   Lenin dies; Stalin outmaneuvers Trotsky
  1929   Stalin's dictatorship consolidated; collectivization
  1936-38 Great Terror; 750,000 executed; millions to gulags

  1949   Chinese Revolution
         Mao Zedong and CCP defeat Chiang Kai-shek's KMT
         "China has stood up"

  1958-62 Great Leap Forward — 30-45M dead in famine
  1966-76 Cultural Revolution — targeted terror against intellectuals

  1959   Cuban Revolution
         Castro + Guevara oust Batista (New Year's Day)
         First communist state in Western Hemisphere

  1967   Guevara killed in Bolivia attempting to export revolution
  1975   North Vietnam reunifies Vietnam (Ho Chi Minh's movement)
  1979   Nicaragua (Sandinistas); Cambodia aftermath (Khmer Rouge)
  1989   Berlin Wall falls; Soviet bloc collapses
  1991   USSR dissolved

  STRUCTURAL PATTERN:
  Marxist theory (class struggle → inevitable revolution)
  + Leninist vanguard party (professional revolutionaries lead)
  + Peasant mobilization (Mao's adaptation)
  → Revolutionary seizure of state
  → Party dictatorship
  → [Either: reform and survive (China, Cuba) OR
              stagnation and collapse (USSR, Eastern Europe)]
```

## Vladimir Ilyich Lenin (1870–1924)

### The Theory: Vanguard Party

Marxism predicted that the working class (*proletariat*) would develop "class consciousness" — understand their exploitation and organize politically. In practice, Russian workers were developing trade union consciousness (better wages and conditions) rather than revolutionary consciousness (overthrow of capitalism). Lenin's *What Is to Be Done?* (1902) argued that left to themselves, workers would only achieve "trade union consciousness." The revolution required a *vanguard party* of professional revolutionaries — intellectuals who understood Marxist theory and could lead the workers.

```
  LENINIST ORGANIZATIONAL MODEL (1902)
  =====================================

  Orthodox Marxism:
  Proletariat → develops class consciousness → organizes →
  revolution (inevitable through economic forces)

  Leninism:
  Vanguard Party → educates and leads proletariat →
  organizes workers + peasants → seizes power →
  "dictatorship of the proletariat" (transitional state)
  → eventually withers away (never happened)

  Key features:
  - Democratic centralism: debate within party; unity in action
  - Professional full-time revolutionaries
  - Underground organization (necessary under Tsarist repression)
  - Party disciplines individual members

  Consequence: the party IS the revolution; the party cannot
  be questioned; the party's survival = the revolution's survival
  This logic led directly to the Cheka (secret police),
  Red Terror, and eventually Stalin's apparatus.
```

### The October Revolution (November 7, 1917 — Old Style Calendar: October 25)

WWI had destroyed the Romanov regime. The February Revolution (March 1917 in modern calendar) produced a Provisional Government (constitutional liberals + socialists) but it continued the war. The Soviets (workers' councils) held independent power. Lenin arrived from Swiss exile in April 1917 (Germany facilitated his travel — they wanted Russia out of the war).

Lenin's *April Theses*: Bolsheviks should not support the Provisional Government; they should call for immediate peace, land redistribution, and transfer of power to soviets. The slogan: "Peace, Land, Bread."

**The seizure of power** was a coup, not a mass revolution. The Military Revolutionary Committee of the Petrograd Soviet, controlled by Trotsky, seized key communications and infrastructure. The Provisional Government dissolved without significant resistance. It was over in hours.

### What Lenin Built and What It Became

```
  LENINIST STATE → STALINIST STATE
  ==================================

  1917-18  Soviet Republic
           - Constituent Assembly elected → dissolved when
             Bolsheviks got 24% (Socialist Revolutionaries got 40%)
           - "Dictatorship of the proletariat" = Bolshevik monopoly
           - Cheka (secret police) established December 1917

  1918-21  Civil War + Red Terror
           - White Army + foreign intervention
           - Cheka: mass executions of class enemies
           - Estimated 50,000-200,000 executed; thousands in camps
           - This is Lenin's Red Terror, not Stalin's

  1921     NEP (New Economic Policy):
           - Partial market restoration after War Communism failure
           - Famine of 1921-22: 5 million dead; Lenin calls for help

  1922     Stalin appointed General Secretary; begins building
           personal apparatus

  1924     Lenin dies aged 53 (strokes)
           Lenin's Testament: criticized Stalin; recommended removal
           Stalin suppressed it

  1929-53  Stalin:
           - Collectivization: 5-7M deaths (Ukrainian Holodomor)
           - Great Terror: 750,000 executed; 1.8M arrested
           - GULAG: 1.8M peak population
           - WWII: 27M Soviet dead
```

**Lenin's Responsibility**: Lenin built the one-party state, the Cheka, the Red Terror, and the logic that the party's survival justified any violence. Stalin used that apparatus at a scale Lenin had not. Whether Stalin was Lenin's "logical conclusion" or a divergence from Lenin's vision is contested. Lenin did try to warn against Stalin personally. He had already built the structure Stalin would operate.

## Mao Zedong (1893–1976)

### The Strategic Innovations

Mao's theoretical contribution to revolutionary politics: adapting Leninism to a peasant society. Russia had an industrial proletariat; China in 1921 did not. Mao argued that the peasantry could be the revolutionary class if properly organized.

```
  MAO'S PEASANT WAR THEORY
  =========================

  Lenin: urban workers + revolutionary party
  Mao:   rural peasants + party + people's army
         "Political power grows out of the barrel of a gun"

  Three phases of People's War:
  1. Strategic defense: guerrillas in base areas; avoid decisive battles
  2. Strategic stalemate: build strength; expand liberated zones
  3. Strategic offensive: conventional forces attack and win

  The Long March (1934-35):
  KMT encirclement → CCP abandons Jiangxi Soviet
  6,000 miles on foot; 100,000 started, 8,000-10,000 finished
  Mao consolidates leadership during the march
  → Yan'an base area; develops theory; consolidates party control

  Anti-Japanese War (1937-45):
  CCP and KMT both fight Japan
  CCP uses war to expand base areas from SE China to N. China
  KMT bleeds fighting Japan; CCP expands
  → Civil War resumes 1946; CCP wins 1949
```

**The People's Republic (1949–1976)**:

| Period | Policy | Deaths | Economic effect |
|--------|--------|--------|-----------------|
| 1950–52 | Land Reform | 1–2M landlords killed | Land redistributed |
| 1950–53 | Korean War | 180,000 Chinese KIA | International isolation |
| 1956–57 | Hundred Flowers Campaign | — | Intellectuals lured into criticizing party |
| 1957–58 | Anti-Rightist Campaign | 300,000 labeled "rightists" | Intellectuals purged |
| 1958–62 | Great Leap Forward | 30–45M dead (famine) | Industrial + agricultural collapse |
| 1966–76 | Cultural Revolution | 0.5–2M killed; millions persecuted | Cultural destruction; economic disruption |
| 1976 | Mao dies | — | Deng Xiaoping opens reforms |

**The Great Leap Forward** is the deadliest policy failure in human history in terms of direct death toll. Mao mandated steel production through backyard furnaces (which produced useless pig iron), requiring peasants to abandon harvests. Grain production collapsed. Local officials falsified production figures upward because reporting failure meant punishment. Famine resulted. Mao knew by 1959; continued the policy for two more years.

### Mao's Lasting Influence

The legacy outside China is as a theory and symbol more than as a model:
- Maoist movements: Peru (Sendero Luminoso), Nepal (Maoist insurgency that transformed into coalition government), India (Naxalites)
- "Maoism" in 1960s–70s Western student movements: revolutionary romanticism; Cultural Revolution as anti-establishment
- China since 1978: economic reforms under Deng explicitly rejected Maoist economics; the CCP maintains political continuity with Mao while rejecting most of his economic program

Xi Jinping has rehabilitated Mao's image for domestic legitimacy purposes while maintaining Deng's economic model — an unstable combination.

## Ernesto "Che" Guevara (1928–1967)

### What He Did

Guevara was an Argentine physician who became a Marxist revolutionary through his *Motorcycle Diaries* tour of Latin America (1952), witnessing poverty and a CIA-backed coup (Guatemala, 1954). He joined Castro's Cuban revolutionary movement in Mexico in 1955.

**Cuba (1956–59)**: Guevara led the guerrilla column that was decisive in the battle for Santa Clara (December 28, 1958). Castro entered Havana on January 8, 1959. Batista had fled New Year's Eve.

**Post-revolution role**: As head of the National Bank of Cuba and Minister of Industry, Guevara attempted to industrialize Cuba on a Soviet model. The results were poor (sugar production collapsed; industrialization failed; Cuba became dependent on Soviet subsidies).

**Foco theory**:

```
  GUEVARA'S FOCO THEORY (Regis Debray "Revolution in the Revolution," 1967)
  ===========================================================================

  Orthodox Leninism: vanguard party first → build mass support →
                     revolutionary situation → insurrection

  Foco theory:       small guerrilla group ("foco") establishes
                     itself in favorable terrain → actions create
                     revolutionary situation → masses follow

  Key claims:
  1. You don't need to wait for "objective conditions"
  2. The guerrilla band itself creates the conditions
  3. Rural base + mountain terrain + moral example
  4. "The duty of every revolutionary is to make the revolution"

  PRACTICAL RECORD:
  Cuba (1956-59): worked (special conditions — Batista's
    weak regime; US pressure; Havana organized support)
  Bolivia (1966-67): failed completely
    - Peasants didn't support the foreign guerrillas
    - Party discipline broke down
    - CIA + Bolivian Army located and killed the group
    October 9, 1967: Guevara captured and executed

  The Cuban success was not a generalizable model.
  The conditions that made Cuba work didn't apply elsewhere.
```

### Guevara as Symbol

Guevara the symbol (Alberto Korda's 1960 photograph; the t-shirt) diverged completely from Guevara the actual political theorist. The symbol represents romantic revolutionary spirit, anti-imperialism, anti-corporate sentiment. The actual person:
- Supervised execution of hundreds in Cuba after the revolution
- Argued for nuclear confrontation with the US (during the Cuban Missile Crisis he wanted the missiles used)
- Applied command economy theory with poor results
- Died in a military failure in Bolivia

The gap between the romantic image and the historical figure is one of the largest in modern political iconography.

## Fidel Castro (1926–2016)

### The Revolution

Castro organized the attack on the Moncada Barracks (July 26, 1953) — the birth of the 26th of July Movement. The attack failed; Castro was imprisoned, then amnestied, then exiled to Mexico. There he met Guevara. He landed in Cuba with 82 men on the *Granma* in December 1956; most were killed immediately; 12–20 survivors including Castro and Guevara fled to the Sierra Maestra mountains.

The two-year guerrilla campaign was effective less because of military victories and more because the Batista regime was losing support across Cuban society — urban underground, labor, middle class, even military elements. Batista fled December 31, 1958.

```
  CUBAN REVOLUTION: KEY FACTS VS. POPULAR MISCONCEPTION
  =======================================================

  MISCONCEPTION: Guerrilla war in the mountains won
  REALITY: Urban underground (llano) and middle-class
           opposition (students, urban workers, professional
           class) was as important as the Sierra Maestra
           campaign. Castro coordinated both.

  MISCONCEPTION: Castro always planned a communist state
  REALITY: He explicitly denied Communist alignment until
           after the Bay of Pigs (1961). The turn to
           Soviet alignment was partly driven by US hostility
           (Bay of Pigs, assassination attempts, embargo).

  MISCONCEPTION: Cuba's healthcare and literacy gains
                 justify the political system
  REALITY: Cuba did achieve near-universal literacy (1961
           campaign) and functional healthcare. The economy
           stagnated; political repression was real
           (UMAP labor camps for gays, dissidents, etc.);
           200,000+ political prisoners over the decades.
           Outcomes on some social metrics ≠ justification
           for the political system.
```

**Castro's Institutional Achievement**: Castro built a stable one-party state that survived:
- Bay of Pigs invasion (1961)
- CIA assassination attempts (600+, reportedly)
- US trade embargo (1962–present)
- Soviet collapse (1991) — the "Special Period" of severe deprivation
- Transition to Raúl Castro (2008) and Miguel Díaz-Canel (2018) — maintained system continuity beyond the founder

This institutional durability is unusual for a Latin American revolution. Most didn't survive their founders.

## Common Confusion Points

**"Lenin didn't intend Stalinism."**
Lenin built the apparatus — Cheka, one-party monopoly, Red Terror, dissolution of the Constituent Assembly. He tried to prevent Stalin personally. But the institutional structure he created was the structure Stalin operated. Whether the outcome was logically inevitable from Lenin's premises is contested; that Lenin created the conditions is not.

**"Mao unified China and that alone justifies him."**
Sun Yat-sen started the nationalist revolution. The KMT under Chiang Kai-shek had already unified most of China by 1928. Mao defeated Chiang and established CCP rule — the question is whether that is better or worse than what the alternative (KMT government) would have produced. Chiang's Taiwan became a prosperous democracy. Mao's China produced 30–45 million famine deaths before Deng's reforms created growth. These are not equivalent outcomes.

**"Guevara's t-shirt version is the real Guevara."**
The romantic icon is a marketing phenomenon. The historical Guevara organized executions, presided over economic failure, and died in a military failure based on a flawed theory. His genuine intellectual contribution (the foco theory) has been largely falsified by subsequent attempts to apply it.

**"Cuba's healthcare system proves socialism works."**
Cuba achieves good health outcomes relative to its income level — this is real and worth acknowledging. The mechanisms: trained doctors (the state invested heavily in medical education) and a public health infrastructure. The economic cost: food insecurity, housing shortage, emigration of productive population. Cuba's healthcare is not evidence for or against socialism in general; it's evidence that a particular set of priorities (medical education + public health infrastructure) produces particular outcomes regardless of the broader economic system's failures.

**"The Cuban Missile Crisis was resolved by diplomacy."**
It was also resolved by a secret deal: the US publicly pledged not to invade Cuba; the US also secretly agreed to remove Jupiter missiles from Turkey within 6 months. Khrushchev could sell the outcome as not simply capitulating. Guevara, by contrast, was furious — he wanted the missiles used.
