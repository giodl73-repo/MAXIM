# 03 — Flight Mechanics

## Equations of Motion, Stability, Control

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Reference frames: earth axes (NED), body axes, wind axes, stability axes
- Euler angles: roll/pitch/yaw (φ,θ,ψ), rotation matrix, gimbal lock
- Quaternion representation: advantages for simulation, singularity-free
- 6-DOF equations of motion: translational (F=ma) and rotational (M=Iα) in body axes
- Forces in flight: weight, thrust, lift, drag — longitudinal equilibrium
- Trim conditions: straight-and-level, climbing, turning flight
- Static stability: longitudinal (pitch), lateral (roll), directional (yaw)
- Neutral point and static margin: CG position, stick-fixed vs stick-free
- Dynamic stability: eigenvalues, phugoid mode, short-period mode, spiral mode, Dutch roll
- Control surfaces: elevator authority, aileron roll rate, rudder authority
- Fly-by-wire: advantages (envelope protection, carefree handling), quadruple redundancy
- Handling qualities: Level 1/2/3, Cooper-Harper rating scale, MIL-SPEC requirements
- V-n envelope: structural limit load, ultimate load, gust envelope, design dive speed
- Maneuver loads: steady turns, pull-outs, roll maneuvers
- Performance: range (Breguet equation), endurance, climb rate, ceiling
- Takeoff and landing analysis: ground roll, obstacle clearance, balanced field length
