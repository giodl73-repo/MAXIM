# 01 — Aerodynamics

## Lift, Drag, Boundary Layers, Compressible Flow

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Fluid mechanics foundations: continuity equation, Euler equations, Navier-Stokes for aerodynamics
- Bernoulli equation: derivation, applicability limits, Venturi, pitot tube
- Circulation and lift: Kutta-Joukowski theorem Γ→L, vortex sheets, Kutta condition
- Thin airfoil theory: camber line, angle of attack, lift curve slope (2π per radian)
- Boundary layer: displacement/momentum thickness, Blasius solution (laminar), log-law (turbulent)
- Transition and separation: Reynolds number, adverse pressure gradient, stall types
- Drag decomposition: pressure drag, skin friction drag, induced drag, wave drag
- Compressible flow: Mach regimes (subsonic/transonic/supersonic/hypersonic)
- Normal shocks: Rankine-Hugoniot relations, total pressure loss
- Oblique shocks and expansion fans: shock angle, Mach angle, Prandtl-Meyer function
- Transonic aerodynamics: critical Mach number, drag divergence, supercritical airfoils
- Supersonic area rule: Whitcomb rule, wave drag minimization
- Wings in 3D: finite wing theory, Prandtl lifting line, induced angle of attack
- Aspect ratio and efficiency: Oswald efficiency factor, winglets
- Swept wings: design rationale, Mach sweepback, divergence vs flutter
- Delta wings and low-speed leading edge vortices
- Computational aerodynamics: panel methods, RANS CFD, turbulence models (k-ε, k-ω, SST)
- Experimental methods: wind tunnels, pressure taps, PIV, force balances
