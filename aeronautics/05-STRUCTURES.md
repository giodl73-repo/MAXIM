# 05 — Aerospace Structures

## Loads, Stress Analysis, Materials, Fatigue, Aeroelasticity

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Airframe structural concepts: semi-monocoque, stressed skin, spars, frames, stringers
- Load paths: bending, shear, torsion in wing box and fuselage
- Stress analysis: normal stress, shear stress, principal stresses, Mohr's circle
- Thin-walled structures: shear flow, torsion of closed sections (Bredt-Batho), open sections
- Beam theory: Euler-Bernoulli vs Timoshenko, deflection and slope equations
- Column buckling: Euler critical load, effective length, crippling of panels
- Finite Element Method (FEM) in aerospace: stick models → box beam → detailed shell models
- Design loads: limit load, ultimate load (1.5x), proof load
- Material selection: aluminum alloys (2xxx, 7xxx), titanium (Ti-6Al-4V), CFRP, GFRP
- Composites mechanics: ply theory, Classical Laminate Theory (CLT), failure criteria
- Fatigue: S-N curves, stress concentration factors (Kt), crack initiation vs propagation
- Fracture mechanics: stress intensity factor K, Paris law da/dN, critical crack size
- Damage tolerance: safe life vs fail-safe vs damage tolerant design philosophies
- Aeroelasticity: flutter (coupled bending-torsion), divergence, aileron reversal
- Thermal loads: aerodynamic heating, coefficient of thermal expansion mismatch
- Certification testing: static tests, fatigue tests, ground vibration test (GVT)
