# 01 — Antibiotics

## Beta-Lactams, Macrolides, Quinolones, Aminoglycosides, Resistance

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Antibiotic targets: cell wall (β-lactams/glycopeptides), cell membrane (polymyxins/daptomycin), protein synthesis 30S (aminoglycosides/tetracyclines) vs 50S (macrolides/chloramphenicol/linezolid), DNA gyrase (quinolones), folate synthesis (sulfonamides/TMP)
- β-lactams: penicillins → cephalosporins (gen 1–5) → carbapenems → aztreonam (monobactam); mechanism (PBP binding → murein synthesis blockade); penicillinase vs ESBL vs carbapenemase resistance
- Glycopeptides: vancomycin (D-Ala-D-Ala binding), MRSA coverage, VRE resistance (D-Ala-D-Lac substitution)
- Macrolides: erythromycin/azithromycin/clarithromycin — 50S 23S rRNA binding, atypical coverage, drug interactions (CYP3A4)
- Fluoroquinolones: ciprofloxacin/levofloxacin/moxifloxacin — DNA gyrase/topoisomerase IV; broad spectrum; CNS/tendon toxicity
- Aminoglycosides: gentamicin/tobramycin/amikacin — 30S 16S rRNA, synergy with β-lactams, concentration-dependent killing, nephrotoxicity/ototoxicity
- Tetracyclines: doxycycline/minocycline/tigecycline — 30S, broad spectrum including atypicals/rickettsiae, photosensitivity
- Clindamycin: anaerobic coverage, 50S, C. diff risk, MRSA skin/soft tissue
- Metronidazole: anaerobes + protozoa (Giardia/Trichomonas/Entamoeba), free radical mechanism
- TMP-SMX: folate pathway double block, PCP prophylaxis/treatment, UTI, Nocardia
- Antibiotic stewardship principles: right drug/dose/duration/route, de-escalation, culture-guided therapy
