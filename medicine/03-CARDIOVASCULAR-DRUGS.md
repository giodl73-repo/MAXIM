# 03 — Cardiovascular Drugs

## Statins, Antihypertensives, Anticoagulants, Antiarrhythmics

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Lipid-lowering: statins (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors — LDL reduction ~50%, pleiotropic effects, myopathy/rhabdomyolysis), ezetimibe (NPC1L1 cholesterol absorption), PCSK9 inhibitors (mAbs → LDL receptor recycling → dramatic LDL reduction), fibrates (PPAR-α → TG lowering), niacin
- Antihypertensives first-line:
  - ACE inhibitors (-pril): bradykinin accumulation, dry cough, teratogenic, renal protection in DM
  - ARBs (-sartan): same indications as ACEi, no cough, also teratogenic
  - CCBs: dihydropyridines (amlodipine — vasodilate, peripheral) vs non-DHP (verapamil/diltiazem — cardiac conduction)
  - Thiazide diuretics: HCTZ, chlorthalidone — NCC transporter, K+ wasting, hyperuricemia
  - β-blockers: ISA/selectivity, heart failure utility (carvedilol/metoprolol succinate/bisoprolol), contraindications
- Heart failure drugs: ACEi/ARB/ARNI (sacubitril-valsartan), β-blockers, MRAs (spironolactone/eplerenone), SGLT2 inhibitors (gliflozins — HFrEF and HFpEF), ivabradine, hydralazine/nitrates
- Anticoagulants: warfarin (vitamin K antagonist, INR monitoring, drug interactions), DOACs (apixaban/rivaroxaban — factor Xa; dabigatran — thrombin), heparins (UFH/LMWH/fondaparinux)
- Antiplatelets: aspirin (COX-1/TXA2), clopidogrel/ticagrelor/prasugrel (P2Y12 inhibitors), dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) indications
- Vaughan-Williams antiarrhythmic classification (Class I-IV) — mechanism, indications, proarrhythmic risk
- Digoxin: Na/K-ATPase inhibition, vagotonic, narrow therapeutic window, toxicity signs
