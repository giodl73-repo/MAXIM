# 08 — Respiratory & GI Drugs

## Bronchodilators, Steroids, PPIs, Antiemetics, Motility

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Respiratory:**
  - SABA (salbutamol/albuterol): β2-agonist, bronchodilation, rescue inhaler, hypokalemia at high doses
  - LABA (salmeterol/formoterol/indacaterol): long-acting β2, maintenance, never without ICS
  - SAMA (ipratropium): muscarinic M3 blockade, COPD > asthma, less effective than β2
  - LAMA (tiotropium/umeclidinium): COPD cornerstone, once daily, urinary retention
  - Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS): fluticasone/budesonide/beclomethasone — NF-κB suppression, local anti-inflammatory, HPA suppression at high doses
  - Methylxanthines: theophylline/aminophylline — narrow TI, PDE inhibitor + adenosine antagonist, bronchodilator + mild anti-inflammatory; monitoring required
  - Biologics for severe asthma: omalizumab (anti-IgE), mepolizumab/benralizumab (anti-IL-5), tezepelumab (anti-TSLP)
  - Mucolytics/antifibrotics: N-acetylcysteine, pirfenidone/nintedanib (IPF)
- **GI:**
  - PPIs (omeprazole/lansoprazole/esomeprazole): irreversible H+/K+-ATPase inhibition; peptic ulcer disease, GERD, H. pylori triple therapy, Zollinger-Ellison; long-term: Mg2+ malabsorption, C. diff, fractures
  - H2 blockers (famotidine/ranitidine): competitive histamine H2 blockade; less potent than PPI; tolerance
  - Prokinetics: metoclopramide (D2 + 5-HT4), erythromycin (motilin agonist), domperidone
  - Antiemetics: ondansetron/granisetron (5-HT3 antagonists), prochlorperazine (D2), promethazine (H1/D2), NK1 antagonists (aprepitant — chemotherapy)
  - Laxatives: osmotic (lactulose/PEG), stimulant (bisacodyl/senna), bulk-forming (psyllium), secretagogues (linaclotide/lubiprostone)
  - Antidiarrheals: loperamide (μ-opioid, peripheral only), bismuth, cholestyramine (bile acid sequestrant for bile acid malabsorption)
  - IBD-specific: mesalazine (5-ASA — topical anti-inflammatory), azathioprine/6-MP, biologics (see immunomodulators)
