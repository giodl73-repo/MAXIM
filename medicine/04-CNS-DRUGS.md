# 04 — CNS Drugs

## Antidepressants, Antipsychotics, Anxiolytics, Opioids

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Antidepressants:
  - SSRIs (fluoxetine/sertraline/escitalopram): serotonin reuptake blockade, 2–4 week onset, sexual dysfunction, serotonin syndrome risk, discontinuation syndrome
  - SNRIs (venlafaxine/duloxetine): + norepinephrine, neuropathic pain indication
  - TCAs (amitriptyline/nortriptyline): multiple receptor blockade (5-HT/NE/histamine/muscarinic/α1), cardiotoxic in overdose
  - MAOIs (phenelzine/tranylcypromine): irreversible MAO-A/B blockade, tyramine hypertensive crisis ("cheese reaction"), drug interactions
  - Atypicals: bupropion (DA/NE, no sexual SEs, seizure risk, smoking cessation), mirtazapine (α2 antagonist + 5-HT2/3, sedating, appetite increase), vortioxetine
- Antipsychotics:
  - First generation (typical): haloperidol/chlorpromazine — D2 blockade, EPS (akathisia/dystonia/parkinsonism/tardive dyskinesia), NMS
  - Second generation (atypical): clozapine (D4/5-HT2, agranulocytosis, seizures, most effective), olanzapine/risperidone/quetiapine/aripiprazole — metabolic SEs
- Mood stabilizers: lithium (Na+ mimic, narrow TI, polyuria/hypothyroid/tremor/teratogen), valproate, lamotrigine (Stevens-Johnson risk), carbamazepine
- Anxiolytics/Sedatives: benzodiazepines (GABA-A positive allosteric modulator, tolerance/dependence, antidote flumazenil), buspirone (5-HT1A partial agonist, no dependence), z-drugs
- Opioids: MOR/DOR/KOR agonism, pain relief + respiratory depression + constipation + miosis; full agonists vs partial (buprenorphine) vs antagonists (naloxone/naltrexone); OUD treatment
- Anticonvulsants: mechanisms (Na+ channel — carbamazepine/phenytoin/lamotrigine), GABA enhancement (valproate/benzodiazepines/phenobarbital), Ca2+ channel (ethosuximide), SV2A (levetiracetam)
