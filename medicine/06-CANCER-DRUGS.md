# 06 — Cancer Drugs

## Cytotoxics, Targeted Therapy, Immunotherapy

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Cytotoxic chemotherapy mechanisms:
  - Alkylating agents (cyclophosphamide/cisplatin/carboplatin): DNA cross-linking, not cell-cycle specific, nephrotoxicity/neurotoxicity
  - Antimetabolites (methotrexate/5-FU/gemcitabine/cytarabine): S-phase specific, folate antagonism, thymidylate synthase
  - Topoisomerase inhibitors: Topo I (irinotecan/topotecan) vs Topo II (doxorubicin/etoposide); anthracycline cardiotoxicity
  - Microtubule drugs: taxanes (paclitaxel — stabilize, prevent depolymerization), vinca alkaloids (vincristine — prevent polymerization)
- Targeted therapy:
  - Kinase inhibitors (-nibs): imatinib (BCR-ABL/GIST, first targeted cancer drug), erlotinib/gefitinib (EGFR), vemurafenib (BRAF V600E), ibrutinib (BTK), CDK4/6 inhibitors (palbociclib)
  - Monoclonal antibodies (-mabs): trastuzumab (HER2), rituximab (CD20), cetuximab (EGFR), bevacizumab (VEGF), anti-HER2 ADCs
  - PARP inhibitors (olaparib/rucaparib): synthetic lethality in BRCA1/2-mutated tumors
  - BCL-2 inhibitors (venetoclax): apoptosis restoration in CLL/AML
- Immunotherapy:
  - Checkpoint inhibitors: anti-PD-1 (nivolumab/pembrolizumab), anti-PD-L1 (atezolizumab), anti-CTLA-4 (ipilimumab); immune-related adverse events (irAEs)
  - CAR-T cells: autologous T cells engineered with chimeric antigen receptor; CD19 (B-cell malignancies); CRS/ICANS toxicities
  - Bispecific antibodies: blinatumomab (CD3×CD19), engaging T cells to tumor
- Hormone therapy: tamoxifen/SERMs/aromatase inhibitors (breast cancer), enzalutamide/abiraterone (prostate cancer)
- Antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs): antibody + linker + cytotoxic payload; T-DM1, enfortumab vedotin
