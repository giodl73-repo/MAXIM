# 10 — Diagnostics & Imaging

## Lab Panels, X-ray/CT/MRI/US/PET — Physics and Clinical Use

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- **Laboratory diagnostics:**
  - CBC: WBC differential, RBC indices (MCV/MCH/MCHC — anemia classification), platelets
  - BMP/CMP: Na/K/Cl/CO2 (anion gap), BUN/Cr (GFR estimation), glucose, Ca, LFTs (AST/ALT/ALP/GGT/bilirubin/albumin/PT)
  - Coagulation: PT/INR (extrinsic/factor VII), aPTT (intrinsic/VIII/IX/XI), fibrinogen, D-dimer, thromboelastography
  - Cardiac biomarkers: troponin I/T (high-sensitivity), BNP/NT-proBNP, CK-MB, myoglobin — time course
  - Inflammatory markers: CRP (IL-6 stimulated), ESR (acute-phase proteins), ferritin, procalcitonin (bacterial infection)
  - Urinalysis: dipstick + microscopy, urine protein:creatinine ratio, urine electrolytes
  - Blood gases: ABG vs VBG, Henderson-Hasselbalch, A-a gradient
- **Imaging modalities:**
  - X-ray: ionizing radiation, air vs soft tissue vs bone attenuation, CXR approach (ABCDE), bone density assessment
  - CT: helical acquisition, Hounsfield units (−1000 air / 0 water / +1000 bone), contrast phases (non/arterial/portal/delayed), radiation dose considerations
  - MRI: T1 (fat bright, water dark, anatomy) vs T2 (water bright, pathology), gadolinium contrast, diffusion-weighted imaging (stroke), FLAIR, MR spectroscopy
  - Ultrasound: piezoelectric transducer, frequency vs penetration trade-off, echogenicity, Doppler, no ionizing radiation, bedside POCUS applications
  - Nuclear medicine/PET: radiotracers (FDG-PET/CT for cancer metabolism, sestamibi for cardiac), SPECT, radiation protection
  - Interventional: fluoroscopy, angiography, image-guided procedures (biopsy/drainage/ablation)
- **Diagnostic reasoning:** pre-test probability, LR+ and LR−, Bayesian updating, sensitivity vs specificity trade-offs for screening vs diagnosis
