# 09 — Anesthesia & Surgery

## General, Regional, Local Anesthesia, Perioperative Care

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Anesthesia types: general (unconscious/amnesia/analgesia/muscle relaxation) vs regional (nerve block — epidural/spinal/peripheral) vs local (lidocaine/bupivacaine infiltration)
- Induction agents: propofol (GABA-A, lipid formulation, pain on injection), ketamine (NMDA antagonist, dissociative, maintains airway reflexes, bronchodilator — useful in asthma/trauma), etomidate (GABA-A, hemodynamically stable, adrenal suppression), thiopental (barbiturate, historical)
- Volatile anesthetic agents: sevoflurane/desflurane/isoflurane — MAC (minimum alveolar concentration), potency = 1/oil:gas coefficient, malignant hyperthermia trigger (ryanodine receptor), hepatotoxicity
- Nitrous oxide: incomplete anesthetic, adjunct, analgesic, vitamin B12 oxidation (avoid in prolonged use/B12 deficiency)
- Opioids in anesthesia: fentanyl/remifentanil (ultra-short)/sufentanil — respiratory depression, bradycardia, pruritus
- Muscle relaxants: depolarizing (succinylcholine — ACh mimic → Phase I/II block, fasciculations, hyperkalemia risk, contraindicated in burns/crush/denervation) vs non-depolarizing (rocuronium/vecuronium/cisatracurium — competitive NMJ blockade, reversed by sugammadex or neostigmine/glycopyrrolate)
- Regional anesthesia: spinal (intrathecal LA — fast, reliable, hypotension risk, PDPH), epidural (catheter — titratable, used in labor/thoracic surgery), nerve blocks (ultrasound-guided — interscalene/femoral/popliteal sciatic/TAP)
- Local anesthetics: mechanism (Na+ channel blockade), ester vs amide (amino-amide -caine names have two 'i's), toxicity (CNS → cardiac), maximum doses
- Perioperative care: fasting guidelines (2/4/6/8 rule), VTE prophylaxis (LMWH/sequential compression), surgical site infection prevention, enhanced recovery after surgery (ERAS)
- ASA physical status classification (I–VI); anesthetic risk factors
