# 05 — Endocrine & Metabolic Drugs

## Insulin, Metformin, Thyroid Drugs, Steroids, Sex Hormones

> **STUB** — outline only, content to be authored

**Planned coverage:**
- Insulin analogs: rapid-acting (lispro/aspart/glulisine — meal coverage), regular (short-acting IV drip), NPH (intermediate), glargine/detemir/degludec (long-acting basal); pharmacokinetic profiles; U-100 vs U-500
- Oral antidiabetics:
  - Metformin: AMPK activation → hepatic gluconeogenesis suppression; first-line; lactic acidosis risk; hold before contrast
  - SGLT2 inhibitors (gliflozins): glycosuria + osmotic diuresis; HF/CKD/CV benefits; DKA risk (euglycemic)
  - GLP-1 receptor agonists (−glutides): incretin effect + satiety; weight loss + CV protection; GI SEs; semaglutide for obesity
  - DPP-4 inhibitors (gliptins): prolong endogenous GLP-1; weight neutral; pancreatitis risk
  - Sulfonylureas: K-ATP channel closure → insulin secretion; hypoglycemia risk; weight gain
  - Pioglitazone (TZD): PPAR-γ → insulin sensitization; fluid retention/CHF risk; bladder cancer signal
- Thyroid drugs: levothyroxine (T4 replacement), liothyronine (T3), PTU/methimazole (peroxidase inhibition ± peripheral conversion), radioiodine (I-131), β-blockers for symptom control in hyperthyroidism
- Corticosteroids: glucocorticoids (prednisolone/dexamethasone/hydrocortisone — dose equivalents, mineralocorticoid activity table), HPA suppression and tapering, Cushing's iatrogenic vs endogenous
- Mineralocorticoids: fludrocortisone (Addison's replacement), spironolactone/eplerenone (MRA in HF/HTN/hyperaldosteronism)
- Osteoporosis drugs: bisphosphonates (osteoclast apoptosis, jaw osteonecrosis risk, atypical femur fracture), denosumab (anti-RANKL mAb), teriparatide (PTH analog — anabolic), SERMs (raloxifene)
- Uric acid drugs: allopurinol (xanthine oxidase inhibitor), febuxostat, probenecid (uricosuric), colchicine (microtubule polymerization blockade for acute attacks), NSAIDs/steroids
